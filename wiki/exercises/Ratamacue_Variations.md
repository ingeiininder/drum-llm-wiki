# Ratamacue Variations
The Ratamacue family expands upon the basic [[Drag]] by adding triplet-based single strokes. These are essential for developing orchestral and rudimental snare vocabulary.

### Double Ratamacue (Ex 5.6)
Adds an extra drag to the start of the standard Ratamacue.
- **Sticking**: llR llR L R L
- **Feel**: Typically played with a triplet subdivision.

### Triple Ratamacue (Ex 5.7)
Adds two extra drags to the start of the standard Ratamacue.
- **Sticking**: llR llR llR L R L
- **Context**: Often used in military-style cadences and [[Charley Wilcoxon]] solos.

### Common Pitfalls
- **Crushing the Drags**: Avoid "pressing" the grace notes into the head. They should remain rhythmic and distinct.
- **Rhythmic Distortion**: Ensure the final three single strokes (L R L) maintain a consistent triplet feel regardless of how many drags precede them.
