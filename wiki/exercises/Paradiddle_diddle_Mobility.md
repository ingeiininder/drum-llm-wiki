# Paradiddle-diddle Drumset Mobility

The **Paradiddle-diddle** (`R L R R L L`) is a six-note rudiment that does **not** alternate hands. To play it starting with the left hand, you must explicitly switch to the `L R L L R R` sticking.

### Application Strategies
*   **Right-Hand Lead**: `R L R R L L`. Often used to move from the snare to the floor tom or cymbals on the right side.
*   **Left-Hand Lead**: `L R L L R R`. Useful for moving toward the high tom or cymbals on the left side.
*   **Orchestration**: Like the [[Six_Stroke_Roll_Variations]], the paradiddle-diddle is most effective when the singles are orchestrated and the diddles remain as ghost notes on the snare.

### Comparison
While the Six-Stroke Roll (`R L L R R L`) is often used for its "accent-sandwich" feel, the Paradiddle-diddle is frequently used for its "front-loaded" accent (`R L`), allowing for quick, aggressive bursts that resolve into smooth doubles.
