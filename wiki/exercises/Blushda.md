# Blushda
The **Blushda** is a popular hybrid rudiment that combines a [[Flam]] and a [[Drag]]. It is widely used in modern gospel, jazz, and fusion drumming for its "greasy" and fluid sound.

### Sticking Pattern
- **Basic Sticking**: rL RR L (Flam followed by a drag and a single stroke).
- **Alternative**: lR LL R.

### Performance Notes
- **The "Swoosh"**: The Blushda should have a continuous, rolling sound. The transition from the flam to the drag must be seamless.
- **Orchestration**:
    - **Triplet Feel (Ex 7.15)**: Often used in jazz "comping."
    - **16th-Note Feel (Ex 7.16)**: Used for rapid-fire fills.
- **Pro-Tip**: Think of the Blushda as one single motion rather than three separate components. The "RR" (drag) should bounce naturally off the primary stroke of the flam.
