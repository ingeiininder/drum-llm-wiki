**Practice Instructions:**
1.  **Start Slow:** Begin at a very slow tempo with a [[Metronome]], subdividing the beat into three (e.g., "1-trip-let, 2-trip-let").
2.  **Evenness:** Focus intensely on making each of the three notes within every triplet perfectly even in volume and spacing.
3.  **Flowing Feel:** Strive to maintain the "rounder" and "flowing" [[Eighth-Note_Triplet_Feel]], avoiding any stiffness or choppiness.
4.  **Relaxation:** Keep your hands, wrists, and arms relaxed to facilitate smooth, continuous motion.
5.  **Listen Critically:** Pay attention to the sound. Are both hands producing the same quality of stroke?

**Pro-Tip:**
This exercise is foundational. Don't rush through it. The goal is to build solid muscle memory for the triplet rhythm and to ensure both hands are equally developed. Think of the motion as a continuous, circular flow rather than individual, choppy strokes. This will set you up for success in more complex triplet patterns.
