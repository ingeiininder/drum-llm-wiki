# 16ths (One-Bar Phrases) - 2/4

This exercise focuses on reading and executing [[Sixteenth_Note]]s within one-bar phrases in the [[2_4_Time_Signature]]. It introduces the subdivision of each beat into four equal parts, a crucial skill for playing intricate rhythms and faster tempos.

**Purpose:**
*   To develop a strong internal feel for sixteenth note subdivisions.
*   To accurately place sixteenth notes within the two beats of a 2/4 measure.
*   To improve [[Sight_Reading]] of sixteenth note-based rhythms.

**Pro-Tips:**
*   **Vocalization:** Count "ONE-e-and-a TWO-e-and-a" to internalize the feel of the sixteenth notes.
*   **Evenness:** Focus on making all four notes in the sixteenth note grouping perfectly even. Avoid rushing or dragging any part.
*   **Metronome:** Start with the [[Metronome]] on the quarter note, then try setting it to click on the eighth note or even the sixteenth note subdivision for advanced practice.
*   **Relaxed Stroke:** Maintain a relaxed grip and stroke to ensure smooth and even sixteenth note execution.
*   **Common Pitfall:** Uneven sixteenth notes, often with the "e" or "a" being rushed or played softer.

Mastering sixteenth notes is fundamental for playing modern [[Groove]] and [[Fill]] in many contemporary styles.
