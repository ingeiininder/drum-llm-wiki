# Technique Control Exercises (Unit 3)
This set of exercises is designed to integrate various [[Accents (Drumming)]], [[Taps (Drumming)]], and [[Moeller Up Stroke]]s within the framework of the [[Moeller Technique]]. These exercises are crucial for developing dynamic control and fluidity.

## Instructions
*   **Tempo**: Begin each exercise at a slow enough tempo (e.g., 50-60 bpm) where you can focus on each individual stroke, whether it be an accent, tap, or up stroke.
*   **Stroke Identification**:
    *   If a note is not an accent, it is a tap.
    *   The [[Moeller Up Stroke]] (which is a tap) always precedes an accented note.
    *   If a tap follows an accent, that accented note will essentially utilize the same control/[[Down Stroke]] technique developed in Unit 1.
*   **Dynamic Gap**: Focus on developing the biggest dynamic gap between the accented and unaccented notes.

## Further Development
To further develop these techniques, incorporate studies from [[Joe Morello]]'s book, [[Master Studies]]. Specifically, focus on the "Accent Studies" (Exercises 1-12 on pages 7 and 8), which combine alternate stickings with one-handed stickings.

## Practice Suggestions
*   **Daily Repetition**: Play all of the Technique Control Exercises twenty times every day.
*   **Mirror/Video**: Use a mirror or video monitor to check the different angles of your technique for consistency and to ensure proper execution of each stroke type.
*   **Master Studies Integration**: Every day, play four of the twelve assigned [[Master Studies]] exercises for two minutes each, choosing a different set of four each day to ensure comprehensive practice.
