# Rudimental Recall: Ruffs
[[Rudimental_Recall_Ruffs]] refers to a practice segment or exercise dedicated to reviewing and reinforcing proficiency in various [[Ruff]] rudiments. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 6 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Purpose:**
*   **Retention:** Ensures that previously learned [[Rudimental Drumming]] concepts, specifically ruffs, are not forgotten.
*   **Refinement:** Provides an opportunity to improve the consistency, dynamics, and speed of ruffs.
*   **Application:** Encourages drummers to integrate ruffs into their playing more naturally.

**Ruffs Typically Included:**
*   [[Three-Stroke Ruff]]
*   [[Four-Stroke Ruff]]
*   [[Five-Stroke Ruff]]
*   [[Seven-Stroke Ruff]]

**Practice Suggestions:**
*   Practice each ruff individually, focusing on the evenness and quietness of the grace notes leading into the accented primary stroke.
*   Vary tempos, starting slowly and gradually increasing speed.
*   Practice leading with both the right and left hands.
*   Apply ruffs to different drums on the [[Drum Kit]] (e.g., snare, tom-toms) to develop versatility.

**Pro-Tip:** Regular "recall" sessions are vital for solidifying any technical skill. Instead of just playing them, try to *feel* the rhythmic placement of the grace notes and the impact of the main stroke. This helps to internalize the rudiment beyond just the sticking pattern.
