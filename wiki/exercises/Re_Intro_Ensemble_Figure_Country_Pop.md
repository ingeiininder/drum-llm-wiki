# Re-Intro Ensemble Figure (Country-Pop)
Following the second ending of the B section in the [[Country_Pop_Play_Along_Chart_Unit_5]], there are two bars that function as a re-introduction. These bars contain an [[Ensemble_Figures]].

**Characteristics**:
*   Both rhythms within this ensemble figure are [[Long Notes]].
*   A [[Crash_Cymbal]] should be played to accentuate these rhythms, providing sustain and impact.

**Execution Approaches**:
The text suggests two simple examples, implying variations in how the crash cymbal and other drums might be used to articulate these long notes. The core idea is to let the crash ring out to emphasize the sustained nature of the figure.

**Pro-Tip**: When playing long notes on a crash cymbal, ensure the strike is confident and allows the cymbal to fully resonate. Avoid muting it prematurely unless specifically indicated. The sustain of the cymbal helps to carry the long notes of the ensemble.
