# Home Base Motion

**Home Base Motion** is a specialized [[Drumset_Mobility]] concept designed to exhaust every possible drum-to-drum combination. This exercise ensures that a drummer can transition from any single drum to any other drum on the kit without hesitation or loss of rhythmic integrity.

### The Concept
In this exercise, one drum is designated as the "Home Base." The drummer alternates between the Home Base drum and every other drum in the setup. Once all combinations for that drum are exhausted, the "Home Base" designation moves to the next drum.

### Exercise Structure (Example: 16th Notes)
If the **Snare** is Home Base:
1.  Play 16th notes: Snare -> High Tom
2.  Play 16th notes: Snare -> Mid Tom
3.  Play 16th notes: Snare -> Floor Tom

The pattern then repeats with the **High Tom** as Home Base, then the **Mid Tom**, and so on.

### Key Objectives
*   **Note Placement**: Moving between different physical locations can often cause "rushing" or "dragging." The goal is to keep the subdivision (e.g., 16th notes or triplets) perfectly even.
*   **Physical Economy**: Find the most direct path between drums. Avoid unnecessary "looping" motions with the sticks.
*   **Sound Consistency**: Ensure the volume and tone of the "Home Base" drum remain identical regardless of which "target" drum you are moving toward.
