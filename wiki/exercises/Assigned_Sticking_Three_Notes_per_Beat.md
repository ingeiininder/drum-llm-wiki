# Assigned Sticking: Three Notes per Beat
This exercise applies the principles of [[Assigned_Sticking]] to rhythms containing three notes per beat. These patterns often involve combinations of [[16th_Note]]s and [[16th_Rest]]s, or sometimes [[16th_Note]] triplets (though the text focuses on 16th notes).

*   **Concept**: The three notes per beat are placed within the four-part 16th-note subdivision (1 e & a). The [[Sticking_Patterns]] will follow the [[Alternating_Hands_Sticking]] principle based on where these three notes fall.
*   **Example**: If the pattern is a 16th note on "1", a 16th note on "e", and a 16th note on "&" (1 e &), the sticking would be R L R (assuming [[Right_Hand_Lead_Assigned_Sticking]]). If the pattern is a 16th note on "e", a 16th note on "&", and a 16th note on "a" (e & a), the sticking would be L R L.

**Pro-Tip**: These patterns can be tricky because they leave a gap in the 16th-note subdivision. Maintaining the mental subdivision of "1 e & a" is crucial to ensure the three notes are placed accurately and evenly, and that the correct hand is used for each stroke. This helps develop rhythmic precision and [[Muscle_Memory_Drumming]].
