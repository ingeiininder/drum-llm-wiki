# Four-Bar Bass Drum Pattern
A [[Four-Bar Bass Drum Pattern]] is an example of a [[Multiple-Bar Phrases]] where the [[Bass Drum]] part repeats every four measures. This allows for more complex and evolving rhythmic ideas within the groove, contributing to the overall [[Song Structure]] and feel.

**Example Structure**:
*   **Bar 1**: First bar of the phrase.
*   **Bar 2**: Repeats the pattern from Bar 1.
*   **Bar 3**: Repeats the pattern from Bar 1.
*   **Bar 4**: Introduces a new pattern or variation, completing the four-bar cycle before repeating from Bar 1.

**Pro-Tip**: When practicing four-bar patterns, focus on the continuity and flow across the bar lines. The challenge is to make the entire four-bar phrase feel like one cohesive statement, rather than four disconnected one-bar loops. Experiment with different rhythmic figures in the "new pattern" bar to create tension, resolution, or a sense of forward motion. This approach significantly enhances your [[Groove]] and [[Phrasing]].
