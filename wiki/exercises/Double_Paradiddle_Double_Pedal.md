# Double Paradiddle (Double Pedal)

Applying the **Double Paradiddle** [[Rudiment]] to the feet is a specialized coordination exercise used to build dexterity and rhythmic interest in [[Double Pedal]] playing.

### Pattern Breakdown
The standard Double Paradiddle is a 6-note pattern: **R L R L R R** (or **L R L R L L**).
When applied to the feet:
1.  **Right Foot (R)**
2.  **Left Foot (L)**
3.  **Right Foot (R)**
4.  **Left Foot (L)**
5.  **Right Foot (R)**
6.  **Right Foot (R)**

### Technical Benefits
*   **Breaking the Alternation**: Most double bass playing relies on constant alternating singles. The double stroke at the end of the paradiddle forces the drummer to develop "twitch" muscles in the ankles.
*   **Metric Displacement**: Because it is a 6-note pattern, it can be phrased as triplets or as sixteenth notes, creating interesting over-the-barline rhythms.

### Pro-Tips
*   **Lead with the Weak Foot**: Practice starting the pattern with the left foot (**L R L R L L**) to ensure symmetry in power and timing.
*   **Integration**: Try playing a steady 8th-note pattern on the [[Ride Cymbal]] while executing the double paradiddles on the feet to test four-way coordination.
