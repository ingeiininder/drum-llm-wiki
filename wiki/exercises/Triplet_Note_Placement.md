# Triplet Note Placement Exercise

This technical exercise (Ex 2.10) is designed to develop precision and [[Limb Independence]] within a triplet subdivision.

### Execution Instructions
1.  **Lead Limb**: Play continuous [[Eighth-Note Triplets]] with the right hand on the Hi-Hat.
2.  **Secondary Limb**: Play specific triplet-based rhythmic phrases with the left hand on the Snare Drum.
3.  **Ambidexterity**: Once comfortable, switch hands (Left hand on Hi-Hat, Right hand on Snare) to develop **Open-Handed Playing**.

### BPM Checklist
*   Begin at **60 BPM** for clinical accuracy.
*   Progress through 70, 80, and 90 BPM.
*   Target **100 BPM** for professional-level fluency.

### Goal
The objective is to ensure the snare drum notes align perfectly with the hi-hat grid without the hi-hat pattern "flinching" or losing its steady flow. This builds the foundation for complex [[Ghost Notes]] and linear triplet patterns.
