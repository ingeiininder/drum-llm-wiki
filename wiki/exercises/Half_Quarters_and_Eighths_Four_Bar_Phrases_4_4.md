# Half, Quarters and Eighths (Four-Bar Phrases) - 4/4

This exercise focuses on fundamental [[Rhythm Reading]] within the [[4-4_Time_Signature]], utilizing combinations of [[Half Note]]s, [[Quarter Note]]s, and [[Eighth Note]]s. The patterns are presented as four-bar phrases, allowing for focused practice on basic rhythmic building blocks and continuity in the most common time signature.

**Purpose:**
*   To solidify the understanding and execution of basic [[Note Values]] in 4/4 time.
*   To develop a steady internal [[Pulse]] and accurate rhythmic placement within a four-beat measure.
*   To improve [[Sight Reading]] ability for common rhythmic figures over extended phrases in 4/4.

**Pro-Tips:**
*   **Consistent Tempo:** Start at a slow, comfortable [[Tempo]] with a [[Metronome]] and gradually increase speed only when accuracy is maintained.
*   **Vocalization:** Count and vocalize each rhythm before playing it. For 4/4, you might count "ONE-and TWO-and THREE-and FOUR-and" for eighth notes.
*   **Relaxation:** Maintain a relaxed grip and stroke to ensure evenness and prevent tension over the longer phrases.
*   **Common Pitfall:** Rushing the eighth notes or shortening the duration of half and quarter notes. Ensure each note receives its full value.

Mastering these basic phrases in 4/4 is essential before moving on to more complex rhythmic challenges and applying them to [[Groove]].
