# Double Stroke Roll Ostinato Study

This exercise series integrates standard [[Rudimental_Rolls]] with a fixed [[Four_Way_Coordination]] ostinato. The goal is to achieve "Rudimental Recall"—the ability to execute complex hand patterns while maintaining a rock-solid foot foundation.

### The Ostinato
The feet maintain a "very common" pattern:
- **Bass Drum**: Usually on quarter notes or specific syncopated hits.
- **Hi-Hat**: Typically "chicked" on beats 2 and 4 (standard [[Jazz_Independence]] footwork).

### Exercises
1. **5-Stroke Roll (Ex 6.15)**: Focus on the transition between the double strokes and the single accented note.
2. **7-Stroke Roll (Ex 6.16)**: Requires careful placement of the seven notes to avoid [[Flamming]] with the bass drum.
3. **9-Stroke Roll (Ex 6.17)**: A longer duration roll requiring sustained evenness.
4. **6-Stroke Roll (Ex 7.6 - 7.8)**: Practiced leading with the Right, then Left, then alternating.
5. **8-Stroke Roll (Ex 7.9)**: Naturally alternating sticking.

### Technical Requirements
- **Articulation**: Bring out the accents in the snare drum clearly.
- **Alignment**: Strive for perfection in vertical alignment; the snare and bass drum must hit simultaneously without "flamming."
- **Balance**: Ensure the volume of the roll does not overpower the foot ostinato, and vice versa.

### Pro-Tips
- **Seat Height**: If you struggle with balance during these rolls, check your seat height. Proper ergonomics are essential for [[Coordinated_Independence]].
- **Slow Accuracy**: Practice these meticulously at slow tempos before increasing speed. The articulation of the subdivisions within the accented triplets is the priority.
