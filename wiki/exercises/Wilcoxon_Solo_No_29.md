# Wilcoxon Solo No. 29

Solo No. 29 is a technical study by [[Charley Wilcoxon]] focused on the integration of [[Double_Stroke_Roll]] variations within a [[2/4 Time]] signature. This 17-bar solo (including first and second endings) serves as a benchmark for metering short rolls against a 16th-note grid.

### Primary Components
The solo is primarily constructed using:
- [[Five_Stroke_Roll]]
- [[Six_Stroke_Roll]] (including inverted variations)
- [[Ten-Stroke_Roll]]
- [[Flam Tap]]
- [[Seven-Stroke_Roll]] (appearing in the first ending)

### Technical Requirements
- **Subdivision**: All rolls are based on a 16th-note framework. Use **four-part counting** (1-e-&-a) to ensure the rolls are metered accurately.
- **The "Slide" Pitfall**: When performing inverted [[Six_Stroke_Roll]]s, maintain a strict 32nd-note feel. Avoid the common error of "sliding" into a [[16th-Note Triplet]] subdivision, which alters the rhythmic intent.
- **Dynamics**: Maintain a clear distinction between accented roll starts/ends and the unaccented double strokes.

### Practice Progression
1. Master the [[Roll_Check_Patterns]] (Ex 8.1 - 8.5) to internalize the transition from single strokes to double strokes.
2. Apply the 32nd-note feel used in [[Wilcoxon_Solo_No_29]] to the 7-stroke rolls in this solo.
3. Practice at a slow tempo to ensure the "inner" notes of the double strokes are rhythmic and even.

> **Pro-Tip**: Use a metronome set to an 8th-note click to help stabilize the 2/4 pulse while navigating the dense 32nd-note roll textures.
