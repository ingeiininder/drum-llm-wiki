# Saturday Night in Memphis

"Saturday Night in Memphis" is a funk-based performance piece written by [[Jeff Salem]], Josh Harris, and Dave DiMarco. The track is characterized by melodic bass lines and intricate [[Horn_Section]] arrangements that require the drummer to balance a deep groove with precise [[Comping_Horn_Figures]].

### Performance Focus
- **Bass Synchronization**: The primary objective is to lock in tightly with the [[Bass_Guitar]]. Pay close attention to the distinction between **staccato** and **legato** figures within the bass line to ensure the rhythm section breathes as a single unit.
- **Dynamic Building**: The song features significant crescendos, particularly leading into section C2.
- **Ensemble Figures**: Section C2 concludes with a critical ensemble horn run. These figures must be supported with sharp, clean accents.
- **Cymbal Control**: When catching [[Horn_Shots]], it is vital to keep them "tight." Avoid allowing cymbals to ring over the rests; use hand choking or shorter-decay cymbals to maintain the staccato nature of the horn hits.

### Structure and Comping
- **Section A1/A2**: Focus on comping the trumpet and saxophone figures.
- **Section B**: The groove should shift to specifically mirror the melodic contours of the bass line.
- **Ending**: The track features an unpredictable ending with specific shots that must be memorized or tracked closely on the [[Drum_Chart]].

> [!TIP]
> **Pro-Tip**: Use the last bar of A2 and C2 as "setup" moments. Your [[Drum_Fill]] should not just be rhythmic decoration but should "lead" the listener and the band into the next dynamic level or ensemble figure.
