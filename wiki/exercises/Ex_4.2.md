`Ex 4.2` is a specific exercise illustrating a 'B' section `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]` within the context of `[[Intro_to_Two-Handed_16ths]]`. *   **Two-Bar Bass Drum Pattern**: The `[[Bass_Drum]]` part cycles every two measures, creating a driving and repetitive feel.
*   **Hi-Hat Opening**: The inclusion of a `[[Hi-Hat_Opening]]` on specific beats adds a bright, open articulation, enhancing the `[[Rhythmic_Textures]]` and providing a distinct character for the 'B' section.
*   **Application**: This groove serves as a foundational element for the 'B' section of the `[[ABAB_32-Bar_Form]]`, demonstrating how a shorter bass drum pattern and hi-hat articulation can create a contrasting musical phrase.

**Pro-Tip**: Focus on the coordination between your hands and foot, especially for the `[[Hi-Hat_Opening]]`. Ensure the open hi-hat sound is clean and precisely timed, and that the `[[Two-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` is locked in with the overall `[[Groove]]`.
