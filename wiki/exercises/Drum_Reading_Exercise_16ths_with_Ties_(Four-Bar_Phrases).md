# Drum Reading Exercise: 16ths with Ties (Four-Bar_Phrases)
This exercise focuses on reading and performing rhythms that combine [[Sixteenth Notes]] with [[Ties (Music)]] within a four-bar phrase structure. It is part of a series of drum reading exercises designed to develop rhythmic literacy and coordination.

**Exercise Focus:**
*   **Rhythmic Interpretation:** Accurately reading and playing complex rhythms involving both rapid sixteenth-note subdivisions and tied notes.
*   **Phrasing:** Understanding and executing musical phrases that span four bars.
*   **Syncopation:** Ties within sixteenth-note patterns are a common source of syncopation, requiring a strong internal clock and precise timing.

**Note on Source Material:** The musical notation for this specific exercise in the provided OCR (IMG_0502) is highly garbled and unreadable, preventing a detailed transcription of the exact rhythms. However, the title indicates its rhythmic focus.

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing tied sixteenth notes, maintain the underlying sixteenth-note subdivision. Counting "1-e-&-a" and mentally holding the tied notes will help ensure rhythmic accuracy, especially when ties cross beat or bar lines.
