# Rudimental Recall: Rolls
[[Rudimental Recall: Rolls]] refers to a practice segment or exercise dedicated to reviewing and reinforcing proficiency in various [[Drum Roll]] rudiments. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 9 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Purpose:**
*   **Retention:** Ensures that previously learned [[Rudimental Drumming]] concepts, specifically rolls, are not forgotten.
*   **Refinement:** Provides an opportunity to improve the consistency, evenness, and speed of rolls.
*   **Application:** Encourages drummers to integrate rolls into their playing more naturally and musically.

**Rolls Typically Included:**
*   [[Five-Stroke Roll]]
*   [[Six-Stroke Roll]]
*   [[Seven-Stroke Roll]]
*   [[Ten-Stroke Roll]]
*   Potentially other rolls like the [[Double Stroke Roll]] or [[Multiple Bounce Roll]].

**Practice Suggestions:**
*   Practice each roll individually, focusing on the evenness and clarity of all the strokes within the roll.
*   Vary tempos, starting slowly and gradually increasing speed, ensuring the roll sounds smooth and continuous.
*   Practice leading with both the right and left hands.
*   Apply rolls to different drums on the [[Drum Kit]] (e.g., snare, tom-toms) to develop versatility in fills.

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing rolls, think about the *flow* and *sound* of the roll rather than just the individual stickings. The goal is often to create a sustained sound. Use a metronome to ensure rhythmic accuracy and consistency, and record yourself to critically evaluate the evenness and quality of your rolls.
