# Drag Paradiddle #2
The **Drag Paradiddle #2** is an advanced variation of the standard [[Paradiddle]] that incorporates two consecutive [[Drag]] (didder) strokes before the primary accented note. 

### Technical Breakdown
In a standard 4/4 or 2/4 context, this rudiment is often felt with a syncopated lean. The sticking pattern is:
- **Right-hand lead**: llR llR L R R
- **Left-hand lead**: rrL rrL R L L

### Performance Notes
- **Grace Notes**: Ensure the double-stroke grace notes (the drags) are played tight and low to the drumhead, providing a "buzz" or "snap" immediately preceding the primary stroke.
- **Accents**: The primary stroke following the two drags should be clearly accented to define the rhythmic structure.
- **Pro-Tip**: Focus on the "rebound" of the second drag to transition smoothly into the single strokes of the paradiddle.
