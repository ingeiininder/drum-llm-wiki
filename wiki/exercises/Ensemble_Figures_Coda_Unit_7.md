# Ensemble Figures (Coda, Unit 7)

[[Ensemble Performance|Ensemble figures]] are rhythmic patterns played by the entire band in unison, and they are particularly impactful when used in a [[Coda_Music|coda]] to create a strong, unified ending.

## Location and Rhythms

In the Unit 7 chart, ensemble figures are present in the coda section (at the very end of the chart). These figures occur on:
*   **First bar of coda**:
    *   The "&" of 1
    *   Downbeats of 2, 3, and 4
*   **Last measure**:
    *   The first four eighth notes ("1 & 2 &")

## Drummer's Orchestration

As a drummer, you need to accentuate these rhythms alongside the band to create a powerful and cohesive ending. A good orchestration option is:
*   **Play the rhythm on the [[Snare Drum]]**: This provides a clear and articulate articulation for the figures.
*   **Last hit on the "&" of 2**: Play this final hit with a [[Bass Drum]]/[[Crash Cymbal]] combination. This creates a definitive and impactful final statement.

## Importance

*   **Definitive Ending**: Ensemble figures in the coda provide a clear and strong signal that the song is concluding, ensuring everyone ends together.
*   **Impact**: They add a sense of finality and often a dramatic flair to the ending.
*   **Communication**: Precise timing and listening are crucial for all band members to execute these figures in unison.

## Pro-Tips

*   **Precision**: Practice hitting these figures with absolute precision, locking in with the rest of the band. The ending needs to be tight.
*   **Dynamic Emphasis**: Ensure your hits on these figures are strong and clear, especially the final crash/bass drum combination, which should be the peak of the ending.
*   **Listen to the Band**: Pay close attention to the other musicians to ensure you are perfectly in sync with their hits.
*   **Anticipate the Coda**: Be ready for the [[Cue (Music)|cue]] to take the coda and transition smoothly into these final figures.
*   **Experiment with Orchestration**: While the suggested orchestration is effective, you can experiment with other combinations (e.g., adding a [[Tom-Tom]] to the final hit) as long as it supports the ensemble and creates the desired impact.
