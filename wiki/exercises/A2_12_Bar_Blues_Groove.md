# A2 12-Bar Blues Groove
The A2 section of the [[12_Bar_Blues_Structure]] chart introduces a variation on the blues [[Groove]], shifting the primary timekeeping element and introducing a new [[Snare_Drum]] articulation. This groove is also played for 12 bars.

**Key Elements**:
*   **Hi-Hat**: Played on a tightly closed [[Hi-Hat]], providing a crisp, defined pulse.
*   **Snare Drum**: Utilizes a [[Cross_Stick]] articulation, offering a softer, woodier backbeat compared to a [[Rimshot]].
*   **Bass Drum**: Maintains a strong, standard sound (either [[Bury_the_Beater_Bass_Drum_Technique]] or [[Off_the_Head_Bass_Drum_Technique]]).
*   **Bass Drum Pattern**: Features a two-bar pattern, expanding on the one-bar pattern from the [[A1_12_Bar_Blues_Groove]] by adding a bar.

**Performance Insights**:
*   The shift to a tightly closed hi-hat and cross-stick creates a more subdued and sophisticated feel compared to the A1 section.
*   The two-bar bass drum pattern requires attention to ensure its consistency and integration with the rest of the groove.

**Pro-Tip**: Focus on the consistency of your cross-stick sound; it should be clear and even. The tightly closed hi-hat should provide a steady, precise pulse. Pay close attention to the new two-bar bass drum pattern, ensuring it flows smoothly and doesn't sound disjointed. This groove often implies a slightly more laid-back or "cool" blues feel.
