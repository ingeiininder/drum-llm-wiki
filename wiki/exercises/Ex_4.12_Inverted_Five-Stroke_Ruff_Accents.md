# Ex 4.12 Inverted Five-Stroke Ruff Accents
This exercise applies the [[Inverted Five-Stroke Ruff]] within a rhythmic context where the overall accents of the phrase are placed on specific beats (beats 2 and 4). This requires careful alignment of the rudiment's initial accent with these target beats.

**Objective:**
To develop rhythmic flexibility and control when applying the [[Inverted Five-Stroke Ruff]], specifically by aligning its inherent accent with designated beats within a measure.

**Musical Context (as described in source):**
The exercise is designed such that the accents will fall on beats 2 and 4. This means the initial accented stroke of the [[Inverted Five-Stroke Ruff]] will align with these beats.

**Practice Instructions:**
1.  **Master the Basic Rudiment:** Ensure you can play the [[Inverted Five-Stroke Ruff]] cleanly with good dynamic contrast before attempting this exercise.
2.  **Identify Accent Points:** Clearly mark or mentally note where beats 2 and 4 fall in the measure.
3.  **Align Rudiment Accents:** Practice placing the initial accented stroke of the [[Inverted Five-Stroke Ruff]] precisely on beats 2 and 4. This will require starting the rudiment at different points relative to the beat.
4.  **Maintain Flow:** Despite the shifted accents, strive to maintain the smooth, flowing feel of the rudiment, especially the transition from the accent to the grace notes.

**Pro-Tip:**
This exercise is a great test of your internal clock and ability to manipulate rudiments musically. Use a [[Metronome]] and count the beats aloud. If you struggle with the placement, isolate the target beat and practice just the rudiment starting on that beat, then gradually add the surrounding notes. This helps in developing a strong internal sense of rhythm and accent placement.
