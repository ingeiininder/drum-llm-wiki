# Ostinato Reading Technique

The **Ostinato Reading Exercise** is designed to develop four-way independence by maintaining a fixed pattern in the hands while reading syncopated rhythms with the feet.

### The Pattern (Ex 3.19)
- **Right Hand**: Plays a steady eighth-note pattern on the [[Hi-Hat]].
- **Left Hand**: Plays a steady eighth-note pattern on the [[Snare Drum]].
- **Combined**: This creates a hand-to-hand [[Ostinato]] that defines the eighth-note grid.

### The Reading Task (Ex 3.20)
While the hands maintain the ostinato, the **Bass Drum** is used to read a 32-bar rhythmic exercise based on eighth-note subdivisions.

### Execution Steps
1. **Establish the Pulse**: Start with an eighth-note click at **140 BPM**.
2. **Count Out Loud**: Counting the eighth notes (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6) is mandatory to lock the bass drum into the hand grid.
3. **Incremental Speed**: Gradually increase the tempo to 150 and 160 BPM.
4. **Open-Handed Playing**: Once comfortable, switch hands (Left Hand on Hi-Hat, Right Hand on Snare) to develop [[Open-Handed Playing]] and hemispheric brain balance.

### Pro-Tip
Focus on the "unison" hits. Identify exactly which bass drum notes fall simultaneously with the hand ostinato and which (if any) fall between. This exercise is the foundation for advanced [[Linear Drumming]].
