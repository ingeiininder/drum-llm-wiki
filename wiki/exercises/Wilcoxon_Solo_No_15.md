# Wilcoxon Solo No. 15

Solo No. 15 by [[Charley Wilcoxon]] introduces the challenge of performing complex rudiments within [[6/8 Time]]. Unlike previous solos in 2/4, the eighth note now serves as the primary pulse.

### Rhythmic Structure
- **Time Signature**: 6/8 (Compound Duple).
- **Form**: 17 bars with a repeated first 8-measure section and first/second endings.
- **Metering Variations**: A critical aspect of this solo is that rolls are not metered uniformly:
    - **[[Five-Stroke Roll]]**: Generally metered as 32nd notes. *Exception*: In Bar 15, they are metered as 16th notes.
    - **[[Ten-Stroke Roll]]**: Always metered as 16th notes in this context.
    - **[[Double Paradiddle]]**: Used to navigate the 6/8 flow.

### Technical Focus
- **6/8 Roll Check Patterns**: Exercises 9.1 through 9.5 are essential for understanding how to "fit" double strokes into a triple-based meter.
- **Pulse Internalization**: Because the pulse is the eighth note, the player must be careful not to rush the 16th-note and 32nd-note subdivisions.

### Practice Suggestions
- **Check Patterns First**: Do not attempt the solo until the [[Roll Check Patterns (6/8)]] are comfortable.
- **Subdivision Awareness**: Pay close attention to Bar 15; the shift from 32nd-note rolls to 16th-note rolls is a common point of failure in performance.
- **Counting**: Use "1 2 3 4 5 6" or "1 & a 2 & a" counting to maintain the compound feel.
