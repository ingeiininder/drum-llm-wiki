# Ostinato Reading Exercise

This exercise focuses on developing **four-way independence** by maintaining a repetitive hand pattern (**Ostinato**) while reading independent rhythms with the feet.

### Setup
1. **Hand Ostinato**: Play a consistent pattern between the Hi-Hat (Right Hand) and Snare Drum (Left Hand).
2. **Reading Material**: Use a 32-bar eighth-note based exercise (e.g., Ex 2.22).
3. **Execution**: Play the written rhythms on the **Bass Drum**.

### Progression Steps
- **Step 1**: Count eighth notes out loud ("1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &").
- **Step 2**: Start at 70 BPM.
- **Step 3**: Increase to 80 and 90 BPM.
- **Step 4 (Open-Handed)**: Switch hands (Left Hand on Hi-Hat, Right Hand on Snare) to develop [[Open-Handed Playing]].

### Technical Focus
The challenge is to prevent the Bass Drum from "pulling" the hands off the ostinato. This is a foundational skill for [[Jazz Drumming]] (comping) and [[Latin Drumming]] (clave-based independence).
