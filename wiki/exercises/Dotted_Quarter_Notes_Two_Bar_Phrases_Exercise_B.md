# Dotted Quarter Notes (Two Bar Phrases) - Exercise B
This exercise provides practice in reading and performing rhythms composed primarily of [[Dotted_Quarter_Note]]s within two-bar phrases. It emphasizes the consistent execution of the 1.5-beat duration and its implications for rhythmic flow across measures.

**Key Learning Points:**
*   **Consistent Duration**: Accurately feel and play the 1.5-beat duration of each dotted quarter note.
*   **Rhythmic Continuity**: Maintain a smooth and connected rhythmic flow over the two-bar phrases, especially when patterns cross the [[Bar_Lines]].
*   **Subdivision Awareness**: Continuously subdivide into [[Eighth_Note]]s to ensure precise placement of attacks and accurate duration.

**Pro-Tip:** Listen for the inherent [[Syncopation]] created by dotted quarter notes. They often create a "push" or "pull" feel against the main [[Beat]], which is a key characteristic of many grooves.
