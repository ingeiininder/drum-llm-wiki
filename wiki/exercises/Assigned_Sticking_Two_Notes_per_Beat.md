# Assigned Sticking: Two Notes per Beat
This exercise applies the principles of [[Assigned Sticking]] to rhythms containing two notes per beat. These often involve [[Eighth Note]]s or combinations of [[16th Note]]s and [[16th Rest]]s that sum to an eighth-note value.

*   **Concept**: The underlying [[Alternating Hands (Sticking)]] of continuous [[16th Note]]s is still the mental framework. The two notes per beat will fall on their respective assigned hands according to the 16th-note grid.
*   **Example**: For two [[Eighth Note]]s per beat (1 &), the first note (1) would be played by the right hand (following [[Right Hand Lead (Assigned Sticking)]]), and the second note (&) would also be played by the right hand. If the pattern was 16th note on '1' and 16th note on 'e', it would be R L.

**Pro-Tip**: Pay close attention to the exact placement of the two notes within the beat. Are they on "1 &"? "1 e"? "& a"? Each placement will dictate a different sticking based on the 16th-note grid. This precision is vital for accurate [[Drum Reading]] and rhythmic execution.
