# Nine-Stroke Roll

The **Nine-Stroke Roll** is an essential rudiment found extensively in orchestral, rudimental, and drumset literature.

### Structure and Sticking
- **Composition**: Four sets of doubles followed by one single-stroke accent.
- **Alternating Nature**: This is an **alternating rudiment**.
- **Counting**: Like the eight-stroke roll, it can be conceptualized in five hand motions (RR-LL-RR-LL-R).

### Technical Focus
- **Sticking Pattern**: `R R L L R R L L R` / `L L R R L L R R L`.
- **Resolution**: The final single stroke usually lands on a strong beat (downbeat or backbeat), providing a definitive conclusion to the roll.

### Pro-Tips
- **Rhythmic Evenness**: Avoid "galloping" the doubles. The space between the RR and the LL should be identical to the space between the two Rs themselves.
