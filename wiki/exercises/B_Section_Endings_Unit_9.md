# B Section Endings (Unit 9)
In the B section of this particular piece, the eighth bar of the section requires a different musical outcome each time it is played. This is achieved through the use of three separately notated endings, a common practice in musical scores to guide repetition and navigation.

*   **1st Ending**: After playing through the B section and reaching the 1st ending, the instruction is to `[[Travel back to A]]`. This means the performer will repeat the A section.
*   **2nd Ending**: Upon reaching the 2nd ending, the instruction is also to `[[Travel back to A]]`. This implies a specific repetition structure where the A section is revisited after both the first and second passes of the B section.
*   **3rd Ending**: When the 3rd ending is encountered, the instruction is to `[[Continue on to the next section]]`. This typically leads to a new part of the song, such as a [[Coda_Section_Unit_9]] or another distinct musical phrase.

The notation `[[D. S. al Coda]]` (Dal Segno al Coda) indicates that after certain repetitions, the performer should go back to the "Segno" sign (S.) and then proceed to the Coda when instructed. In this context, "Repeats are good" suggests that the repetitions are integral to the song's form and should be embraced for the full musical effect.

**Pro-Tip**: Pay close attention to the road map of the song. Misinterpreting endings or navigation signs like `D.S. al Coda` can disrupt the entire performance. Practice the transitions between sections until they are seamless.
