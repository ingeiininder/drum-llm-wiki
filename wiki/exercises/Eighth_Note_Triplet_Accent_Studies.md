# Eighth-Note Triplet Accent Studies

Developing control over [[Eighth_Note_Triplets]] involves shifting accents across the three subdivisions (1-trip-let). This study emphasizes the development of the **[[Up Motion]]** and musical phrasing.

### Practice Progression
1.  **Static Accents**: Playing accents on the first, second, or third partial of the triplet.
2.  **Moving Accents**: Shifting the accent pattern every measure.
3.  **Orchestration**:
    *   **Two Toms**: Moving accents between high and low toms.
    *   **Cymbal/Bass Combo**: Playing accents on cymbals (Right/Left) with the Bass Drum reinforcing the accent, while the Hi-Hat foot maintains beats 2 and 4.

### Technical Focus: The Up Motion
To play consecutive accents or accents on the "off" subdivisions, the hand must utilize an "up motion" (lifting the stick immediately after a low tap) to prepare for the next high-velocity stroke.

### Pro-Tip
When playing triplets hand-to-hand (RLR LRL), the accent will naturally shift between the right and left hands. This is excellent for identifying "weak hand" deficiencies in volume and timing.
