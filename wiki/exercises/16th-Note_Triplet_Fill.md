# 16th-Note Triplet Fill

The **16th-note triplet fill** is a versatile rhythmic embellishment used across nearly all genres of music. In a rock context, it provides a rapid, rolling texture that can lead effectively into new sections.

### Rhythmic Placement
To execute this fill with precision, the first note of the triplet must start exactly on the **"&" of beat 3** (in a 4/4 context). 

### Sticking and Execution
The standard sticking for this fill is:
*   **R L R** (Right, Left, Right)

### Practice Method
1.  **Isolate the Primary Strokes**: Focus on the eighth-note pulse (1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &) and ensure the triplet fits perfectly within the space of the half-beat.
2.  **Articulation**: The fill must be played in an articulate and clear manner. Avoid "smearing" the notes; each of the three notes in the triplet should have equal volume and spacing.
3.  **Repetition**: Practice the fill as a repeating loop to make the motion second nature.

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Rushing**: Starting the triplet too early or "cramming" the notes so they finish before the next downbeat.
*   **Lopsidedness**: Uneven volume between the Right and Left hands.
