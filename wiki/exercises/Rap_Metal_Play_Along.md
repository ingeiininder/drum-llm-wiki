# Rap-Metal Play-Along (Alt Rock 102)

This performance unit focuses on the **Rap-Metal** genre, specifically targeting the transition between different hi-hat textures and the execution of solo fills within a heavy rock context.

### Key Performance Elements
*   **Tight Hi-hat**: Used in the primary groove sections (e.g., Section A) to provide a crisp, articulate foundation.
*   **Loose Hi-hat**: Transitioned to in Section B to increase the energy and "wash" of the groove, typical of chorus sections in Rap-Metal.
*   **Solo Fills**: The chart includes designated areas for "Solo Fills," requiring the drummer to maintain the aggressive stylistic integrity of the track while showcasing individual creativity.

### Practice Parameters
*   **Tempo**: The track is designed to be practiced at **82, 92, and 102 BPM**.
*   **Dynamics**: Focus on the contrast between the "Tight" and "Loose" sections. The loose hi-hat sections often require a slight increase in overall volume and intensity.
*   **Recording**: As per the MI curriculum suggestions, students should record their performance of this chart to evaluate their "stylistically correct drum sound."
