# 16th, Eighth and Quarter Notes with Quarter Rests (One Bar Phrases)
This exercise type introduces [[Quarter Rests]] into rhythmic patterns that already combine [[Sixteenth Notes]], [[Eighth Notes]], and [[Quarter Notes]] within a single measure. The inclusion of longer rests requires precise timing and a strong internal pulse to re-enter the rhythm accurately.

**Practice Guidance:**
1.  **Sustained Pulse:** During the [[Quarter Rests]], maintain a clear mental subdivision of the beat to ensure you re-enter on time.
2.  **Anticipation:** Mentally prepare for the next note during the rest period.
3.  **Counting:** Counting aloud, including the rests (e.g., "1-e-&-a, 2-rest, 3-e-&, 4-a"), can be very helpful.

**Pro-Tip:** [[Quarter Rests]] can sometimes feel like a break, but they are active parts of the rhythm. Think of them as "played silence." This exercise is crucial for developing your ability to play [[Grooves]] with space and syncopation.
