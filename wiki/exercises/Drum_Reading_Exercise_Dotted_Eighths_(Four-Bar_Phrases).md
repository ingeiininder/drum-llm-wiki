# Drum Reading Exercise: Dotted Eighths (Four-Bar_Phrases)
This exercise focuses on reading and performing rhythms that incorporate [[Dotted Eighth Notes]] within a four-bar phrase structure. It is part of a series of drum reading exercises designed to develop rhythmic literacy and coordination.

**Exercise Focus:**
*   **Rhythmic Interpretation:** Accurately reading and playing rhythms involving dotted eighth notes, which extend for three [[Sixteenth Notes]].
*   **Phrasing:** Understanding and executing musical phrases that span four bars.
*   **Syncopation:** Dotted eighth notes frequently create syncopated patterns, requiring precise placement against the main beat.

**Pro-Tip:** To accurately play dotted eighth notes, it's often helpful to subdivide the beat into [[Sixteenth Notes]]. A dotted eighth note will typically be followed by a sixteenth note to complete the beat, creating a common "dotted eighth-sixteenth" rhythm.
