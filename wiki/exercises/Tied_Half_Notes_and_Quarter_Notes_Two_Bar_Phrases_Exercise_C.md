# Tied Half Notes and Quarter Notes (Two-Bar Phrases) - Exercise C
This exercise provides further practice in reading and performing [[Ties]] that connect [[Half_Note]] and [[Quarter_Note]] within two-bar phrases. It aims to solidify the understanding of how ties create longer rhythmic values and affect [[Phrasing]].

**Key Learning Points:**
*   **Consistent Pulse**: Maintain a steady internal [[Metronome]] pulse throughout the tied durations.
*   **Visual Interpretation**: Develop quick recognition of tied patterns and their combined rhythmic value.
*   **Musicality**: Strive for a musical execution, ensuring the sustained notes contribute to the overall flow of the phrase.

**Pro-Tip:** Try vocalizing the rhythm, counting the full duration of the tied notes. For example, if a half note on beat 1 is tied to a quarter note on beat 3, count "1-2-3" for the duration of that single attack. This reinforces the mental connection between notation and sound.
