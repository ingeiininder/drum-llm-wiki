# The A Groove (Eighth-Note Rock Groove)
"The A Groove" is an eighth-note rock groove found within Letter A of the chart, widely used in popular recordings. It combines familiar [[hi-hat]] and [[snare drum]] elements with a new [[bass drum]] pattern.

A key feature of this groove is the [[hi-hat]] employing an accent on every quarter note (beats 1, 2, 3, and 4).

## Performance Notes
It is crucial to ensure that each limb lines up precisely. Whenever a [[snare drum]] or [[bass drum]] hit coincides with a [[hi-hat]] hit, they should be played in unison, with no [[Flams]] present. Dedicated practice time is essential to refine this approach for proper feel and sound.
