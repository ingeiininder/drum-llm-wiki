# Ex 4.16 Inverted Seven-Stroke Ruff Accents
This exercise applies the [[Inverted_Seven-Stroke_Ruff]] within a rhythmic context where the overall accents of the phrase are placed on specific beats (beats 2 and 4). This requires careful alignment of the rudiment's initial accent with these target beats.

**Objective:**
To develop rhythmic flexibility and control when applying the [[Inverted_Seven-Stroke_Ruff]], specifically by aligning its inherent accent with designated beats within a measure. This is a challenging exercise due to the length of the rudiment and the inverted accent placement.

**Musical Context (as described in source):**
The exercise is designed such that the accents will fall on beats 2 and 4. This means the initial accented stroke of the [[Inverted_Seven-Stroke_Ruff]] will align with these beats.
`R L R` (The provided snippet is incomplete, but the description clearly states the accents are on beats 2 and 4.)

**Practice Instructions:**
1.  **Master the Basic Rudiment:** Ensure you can play the [[Inverted_Seven-Stroke_Ruff]] cleanly with good dynamic contrast and even grace notes before attempting this exercise.
2.  **Identify Accent Points:** Clearly mark or mentally note where beats 2 and 4 fall in the measure.
3.  **Align Rudiment Accents:** Practice placing the initial accented stroke of the [[Inverted_Seven-Stroke_Ruff]] precisely on beats 2 and 4. This will require starting the rudiment at different points relative to the beat.
4.  **Maintain Flow and Evenness:** Despite the shifted accents, strive to maintain the smooth, flowing feel of the rudiment, especially the evenness and dynamic control of the six grace notes following the accent.

**Pro-Tip:**
This exercise is a demanding test of your internal clock and ability to manipulate longer rudiments musically. Use a [[Metronome]] and count the beats aloud. If you struggle with the placement, isolate the target beat and practice just the rudiment starting on that beat, then gradually add the surrounding notes. This helps in developing a strong internal sense of rhythm and accent placement, crucial for advanced [[Drum_Technique_50]] and musicality.
