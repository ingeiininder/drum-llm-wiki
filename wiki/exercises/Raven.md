# Raven

"Raven" is an advanced progressive track performed by [[Todd_Sucherman]] (originally by The Falling Wallendas). It serves as a masterclass in [[Odd-Time_Signatures]] and [[Linear_Drumming]] textures.

### Rhythmic Structure
The main form of the song follows a cyclical time signature pattern:
- **Two bars of 6/8**
- **One bar of 9/8**

### The "Over-Ride" Concept
Todd Sucherman utilizes a [[Hi-Hat_Ostinato]] pumping eighth notes with an alternating open-closed "slosh" pattern. Because of the extra beats in the 9/8 bar, the relationship between the open hi-hat and the downbeat shifts:
- The open "slosh" occurs on the downbeats in the first cycle.
- Three bars later, due to the displacement, the "slosh" moves to the upbeats.
- This creates a constantly evolving "over-ride" feel that challenges the drummer's [[Coordination]].

### Texture and Timbre
- **Snares Off**: The song is played with the snare wires disengaged to provide a more "tribal" or percussive quality.
- **Ride Bell & Splash Accents**: These are integrated into the linear patterns to highlight specific melodic movements.
- **Tom Rims**: Sucherman uses the rims of the toms to add an "unusual texture," moving the part away from a standard backbeat-heavy groove into a more percussive soundscape.

> [!TIP]
> **Pro-Tip**: When practicing "Raven," master the [[Hi-Hat_Ostinato]] in isolation first. The displacement caused by the 9/8 bar is the most common point of failure for students.
