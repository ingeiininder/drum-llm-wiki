# Martellato Interpretation
**Martellato** (often referred to as a "rooftop" accent) indicates a note that is both accented and short—"short and fat."

### Performance Characteristics
*   **Set-up Fills**: Should be bold and aggressive.
*   **Orchestration**: Often played as **Double Stops** between the **Floor Tom** and **Snare Drum**.
*   **Bass Drum**: The bass drum is almost always added to provide the "fat" low-end punch required for this articulation.

**Technical Note**: Unlike a standard accent, the Martellato requires a quick "stop" or muffling if the tempo is slow enough to hear the ring, though usually, the choice of drums (Snare/Toms) handles the decay naturally.
