# Ex 4.18 Hard Rock Groove
This exercise, designated as Ex 4.18, is a [[Hard Rock]] groove focusing on [[Two-Handed 16th Notes]] on the hi-hat. It incorporates variations in [[Hi-Hat Sound Production]] and includes designated [[Drum Fill]] sections.

The exercise outlines specific hi-hat articulations:
*   **Tight; Thick Hi-Hat A**: Emphasizes a closed, articulate sound.
*   **Slightly Loose Hi-Hat B**: Introduces a more open, "sloshy" sound.
*   **Tight; Thick Hi-Hat A2**: Returns to the closed sound.
*   **Slightly Loose Hi-Hat B2**: Reintroduces the looser sound.

The structure also includes musical navigation instructions such as [[D.S. al Coda]].

**Pro-Tip:** When transitioning between different hi-hat articulations (tight vs. slightly loose), ensure the change is intentional and controlled. Practice these transitions slowly to maintain rhythmic integrity and avoid accidental "sloshes" when a tight sound is desired, or vice versa. The fills should complement the groove and maintain the overall feel of the hard rock style.
