# Ex 4.3
`Ex 4.3` is an exercise that demonstrates the complete `[[ABAB_32-Bar_Form]]` by combining the 'A' section `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]` (from `[[Ex_4.1]]`) and the 'B' section `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]` (from `[[Ex_4.2]]`) into a full 32-bar structure.

**Key Elements:**
*   **ABAB Structure**: The exercise clearly outlines the alternation of 'A' and 'B' sections, each comprising an `[[Eight-Bar_Phrase]]`.
*   **Groove Application**: It shows how the distinct `[[Different_Rhythm_(or_Groove)]]` of each section contributes to the overall form.
*   **Fills**: The exercise includes designated `[[Fill]]` sections, demonstrating how to transition smoothly between the 'A' and 'B' sections and within the phrases.
*   **Musical Context**: This example provides a practical application of understanding song form and how drum parts are constructed to support the `[[Particular_Guitar_Riff_and_Chord_Structure]]` of each section.

**Pro-Tip**: When practicing `Ex 4.3`, focus not just on playing the individual grooves correctly, but also on the seamless transitions between sections and the musicality of your `[[Fill]]`. Pay attention to the overall flow and how the contrasting 'A' and 'B' sections contribute to the complete musical statement.
