# Six-Stroke Roll Accent Displacement
This exercise involves moving accents through every partial of the [[Six-Stroke Roll]] to develop extreme control over [[Dynamic Nuance]] and [[Stick Control]].

### Single Accent Displacement
Start with an unaccented Six-Stroke Roll (**R L L R R L**). Move a single accent through each of the six partials:
1. **Partial 1**: **R** L L R R L (Downbeat)
2. **Partial 2**: R **L** L R R L (First note of the left double)
3. **Partial 3**: R L **L** R R L (Second note of the left double)
4. **Partial 4**: R L L **R** R L (First note of the right double)
5. **Partial 5**: R L L R **R** L (Second note of the right double)
6. **Partial 6**: R L L R R **L** (The "ah" or last partial)

### Double Accent Variations
In more advanced stages, two accents are applied to the roll:
- **Neighboring Accents**: Accenting partials 1 & 2, then 2 & 3, etc.
- **Separated Accents**: Accenting partials 1 & 3, 2 & 4, etc.

### Technical Requirements
- **The Throw**: For accents on the first note of a double, use a downward "throw" and immediately relax to allow the second note to be a soft rebound.
- **The Snap**: For accents on the second note of a double, use a wrist "snap" after the first soft stroke to produce the accent.

### Common Pitfalls
- **Sympathetic Accents**: Avoid accidentally accenting the single strokes when you are trying to accent the doubles.
- **Rhythmic Distortion**: Ensure the spacing of the [[Sextuplets]] remains even regardless of where the accent falls.
