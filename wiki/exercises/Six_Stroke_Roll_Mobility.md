# Six-Stroke Roll Drumset Mobility

Applying the [[Six_Stroke_Roll_Variations]] to the drumset requires a systematic approach to orchestration. The goal is to use the rudiment to "shape" rhythmic ideas rather than just playing a pattern.

### Orchestration Strategy
*   **The Snare Anchor**: Keep all double strokes on the snare drum at a low dynamic level.
*   **Single Stroke Movement**: Orchestrate the single strokes between the snare and the toms. This allows the singles to define the "melody" of the fill or groove.
*   **Dynamic Intent**: All single strokes should be accented. This creates a clear, driving rhythmic structure while the doubles provide a "rolling" texture underneath.

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Tightness**: Avoid becoming "choppy." The six-stroke roll should feel relaxed and open.
*   **Inconsistent Doubles**: Ensure both notes of the double stroke sound identical in volume and timbre.
