# Tied Quarter Notes and Eighth Notes (One-Bar Phrases) - Exercise F
This exercise combines [[Ties]] with both [[Quarter_Note]] and [[Eighth_Note]] within one-bar phrases. It challenges the drummer to interpret and execute rhythms that blend different note values and their extended durations.

**Key Learning Points:**
*   **Mixed Duration Interpretation**: Accurately read and perform rhythms where quarter notes and eighth notes are tied together, creating varied sustained lengths.
*   **Rhythmic Interplay**: Understand how these tied combinations affect the overall rhythmic feel and [[Phrasing]] within a single measure.
*   **Precision**: Ensure precise timing of attacks and accurate feeling of the combined durations.

**Pro-Tip:** When encountering mixed tied values, mentally convert everything to the smallest common subdivision (e.g., eighth notes). This helps in accurately counting and feeling the precise start and end points of each tied group.
