# Groupings of Five (Over-the-Bar)

Groupings of five are more complex than groupings of three because they take longer to resolve back to the "1" of a measure in 4/4 time.

### Basic Grouping (Ex 9.3)
- **Pattern**: Accent every fifth 16th note.
- **Sticking**: R-l-r-l-R | l-r-l-R-l | r-l-R-l-r | L-r-l-r-l
- **Key Feature**: Because 5 is an odd number, the accent will naturally alternate between the right and left hands if using continuous 16th notes.

### Expanded Grouping (Ex 9.4)
- **Pattern**: Add a second accent two 16th notes after the first accent within the grouping of five.
- **Rhythm**: **1** e **&** a (2) e **&** a (3) e **&** a... (where bold indicates accents).
- **Benefit**: This creates a more melodic, syncopated feel that is highly effective for modern drum fills and soloing.

### Pro-Tip
Tap your foot on the quarter notes. The "conflict" between your foot (on the beats) and your hands (on the groupings of five) is the core of the exercise. If your foot starts following your hands, slow down the tempo.
