# Dotted Half Rests and Dotted Quarter Rests (One Bar Phrases) - Exercise G
This exercise focuses on reading and accurately observing [[Dotted Half Rest]]s and [[Dotted Quarter Rest]]s within single-bar phrases. It emphasizes the importance of silence as a precise rhythmic element.

**Key Learning Points:**
*   **Rest Duration**: Accurately interpret the 3-beat duration of a dotted half rest and the 1.5-beat duration of a dotted quarter rest.
*   **Silent Subdivision**: Maintain a strong internal [[Metronome]] and mentally subdivide through the rests to ensure precise timing of the next attack.
*   **Rhythmic Control**: Develop the discipline to hold silence for its exact duration without rushing or anticipating.

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing, try to "feel" the full duration of the rest, just as you would a note. For a dotted half rest, count "1-2-3" silently before playing on beat 4 (if applicable). This mental engagement helps maintain rhythmic continuity.
