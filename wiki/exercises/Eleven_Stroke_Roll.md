# Eleven-Stroke Roll

The **Eleven-Stroke Roll** is a unique member of the [[Double-Stroke Roll]] family because it does **not** naturally alternate.

### Structure and Sticking
- **Composition**: Five sets of doubles followed by one single-stroke accent.
- **Hand Motions**: Comprised of six hand motions (e.g., RR-LL-RR-LL-RR-L).
- **Practice Method**: Because it doesn't alternate, it must be practiced "one way" (Right-hand lead), then the "other way" (Left-hand lead), and finally forced into an alternating pattern by adding or subtracting a beat in the context of a phrase.

### Technical Focus
- **Sticking Pattern**: `R R L L R R L L R R L`.
- **Symmetry**: Pay close attention to the left-hand lead version, as the five doubles can often become uneven when the non-dominant hand leads.
