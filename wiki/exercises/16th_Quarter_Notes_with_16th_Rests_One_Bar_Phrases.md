# 16th and Quarter Notes with 16th Rests (One Bar Phrases)
This exercise type (O and P) combines [[16th_Notes]] and [[Quarter_Note]] with [[16th_Rest]] within single-bar phrases. This presents a unique challenge of accurately placing both very short and relatively long note values, interspersed with precise short rests.

**Practice Guidance:**
1.  **Note Value Contrast:** Clearly articulate the difference in duration between the [[16th_Notes]] and [[Quarter_Note]].
2.  **Sustained Pulse:** Maintain a strong internal [[Sixteenth_Note]] subdivision, especially during the [[Quarter_Note]], to ensure accurate re-entry after the longer note.
3.  **Rhythmic Clarity:** Focus on making each note and rest distinct and well-defined.

**Pro-Tip:** These patterns can be tricky because the [[Quarter_Note]] might tempt you to relax your subdivision. Keep the internal 16th-note pulse active throughout the entire measure. This is great for developing precision in [[Groove]] that mix fast and slow elements.
