# Paradiddle Pyramid Inversions

The **Paradiddle Pyramid** is a structured exercise that cycles through [[Single_Paradiddle]], [[Double_Paradiddle]], and [[Triple_Paradiddle]] patterns, specifically utilizing **Inversions**.

### Structure
- **Single Paradiddle Inversion**: (e.g., R L L R L R R L)
- **Double Paradiddle Inversion**: (e.g., R L R L L R L R L R R L)
- **Triple Paradiddle Inversion**: (e.g., R L R L R L L R L R L R L R R L)

### Coordination Elements
- **Foot Pattern**: Bass drum and Hi-Hat are played on all quarter notes.
- **Accents**: The accents move relative to the quarter-note pulse as the paradiddle length changes.
- **Repetition**: Perform four of each paradiddle type before moving to the next level of the pyramid.

### Practice Goals
- **Dynamic Contrast**: Ensure the accents are significantly louder than the unaccented "inner notes."
- **Vertical Alignment**: The accented notes must align perfectly with the quarter-note bass drum and hi-hat.
- **Flow**: Transition smoothly between the single, double, and triple variations without breaking the foot ostinato.

### Pro-Tips
- **Inversion Benefit**: Practicing inversions helps move the "double" portion of the paradiddle to different parts of the beat, which is a common requirement in [[Drumset_Orchestration]].
- **Metronome Range**: Start at 72 BPM and work up to 104 BPM.
