# Burn the Bridge

"Burn the Bridge" is a funk composition by [[Jeff_Salem]] that emphasizes [[Syncopation]] and pocket playing. The track is built around a highly syncopated bass line and requires the drummer to maintain a consistent feel with minimal fill interference.

### Technical Requirements
- **Two-Bar Patterns**: The core grooves for sections A and B are constructed as two-bar phrases. Consistency in these patterns is more important than technical flash.
- **Anticipated Bass Drum**: In the second bar of Section A, the [[Bass_Drum]] is anticipated (played on the "and" of 4 or the "e/a" of the preceding beat), which drives the forward motion of the groove.
- **Odd Phrasing**: Section B is a **7-bar pattern** rather than the standard 8-bar phrase. This requires heightened awareness of the [[Form_Awareness]] to avoid "turning the beat around" or missing the transition to Section C.
- **Ensemble Figures**: The end of Section C and the final bar of the tune contain specific horn and ensemble figures that require rhythmic reinforcement.

### Transition Strategies
- **B to A**: Use one-beat fills to provide a subtle "nudge" back into the main groove.
- **B to C**: Use two-beat fills to signal the larger dynamic build-up.

> [!WARNING]
> **Common Pitfall**: Drummers often overplay on this track. The curriculum notes that this song "doesn't require many fills." Focus on the [[Locking_with_the_Bass]] to ensure the syncopated figures feel grounded.
