# Eighth Note and Rest Application in 2/4 Time
This exercise focuses on the application of [[Eighth Notes]], [[Eighth Rests]], [[Tied Eighth Notes]], and [[Dotted Eighth Note]]s within the [[2/4 Time Signature]]. It develops precision in subdividing the beat and handling syncopated figures at the eighth-note level.

**Key Learning Points:**
*   **Subdivision Accuracy**: Accurately play and rest for the duration of half a beat (eighth note/rest).
*   **Tied Eighth Notes**: Understand how eighth notes are tied to extend their [[Duration]], often creating [[Syncopated Rhythms]].
*   **Dotted Eighth Notes**: Precisely execute the 0.75-beat duration of dotted eighth notes, requiring a strong internal [[Sixteenth Note]] pulse.

**Pro-Tip:** When playing eighth notes and rests in 2/4, consistently count "1 & 2 &" to maintain a steady [[Subdivision]]. For dotted eighth notes, remember they are equal to three sixteenth notes, so feel the "1 e &" or "2 e &" for their duration.
