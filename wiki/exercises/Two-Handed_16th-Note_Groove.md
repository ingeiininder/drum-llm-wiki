# Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove

The **Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove** is a technique where both hands alternate (RLRL) on the hi-hat to execute rapid 16th-note patterns.

### Execution
- **Sticking**: Standard alternating RLRL.
- **Backbeat**: In this specific curriculum application, the **right hand** moves from the hi-hat to the snare to play the backbeat on 2 and 4, while the left hand continues the 16th-note flow on the hi-hat.
- **Dynamics**: Focus on the accents. The unaccented notes should be played as [[Ghost Notes]] to maintain a driving but textured feel.

### Technical Challenges
A primary challenge is preventing the snare accent from rushing. Because the right hand must travel, there is a tendency to "straighten" the [[Swung 16th Phrasing]]. 

> [!TIP]
> If the tempo is high or the texture requires it, you can eliminate the unaccented hi-hat notes and play the backbeat with the left hand, effectively turning it into a one-handed lead groove.
