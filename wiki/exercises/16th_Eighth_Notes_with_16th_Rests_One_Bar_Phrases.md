# 16th and Eighth Notes with 16th Rests (One Bar Phrases)
This exercise type (K and L) combines [[16th_Notes]] and [[Eighth_Note]] with the precision requirement of [[16th_Rest]] within single-bar phrases. This introduces a common rhythmic challenge: accurately placing longer notes and rests amidst a rapid 16th-note subdivision.

**Practice Guidance:**
1.  **Subdivision Awareness:** Always be aware of the underlying [[Sixteenth_Note]] grid, even when playing [[Eighth_Note]] or encountering [[16th_Rest]].
2.  **Rest Placement:** Ensure that the 16th rests are observed precisely, creating clear rhythmic gaps.
3.  **Rhythmic Contrast:** Pay attention to the contrast between the shorter 16th notes and the longer 8th notes.

**Pro-Tip:** These patterns are very common in [[Funk]] and [[Pop]] drumming. Practice them with a strong emphasis on the clarity of each note and the crispness of each rest. This will help you develop a tight, articulate feel.
