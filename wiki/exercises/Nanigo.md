# Nanigo (Drumset Afro-Cuban 6/8)
The **Nanigo** is the standard drumset orchestration of the [[Afro_Cuban_6_8]] folkloric rhythm. It requires high-level coordination as it distributes the parts of multiple percussionists across the four limbs.

### Orchestration Mapping
To perform the Nanigo, the drummer voices the traditional instruments as follows:
*   **Right Hand**: Plays the [[6/8_Bell_Pattern]] on a [[Cowbell]] or the bell of the ride cymbal.
*   **Left Hand**: Plays the "Low Drum" part on the **Rack Tom** (often utilizing "open" and "slap" tones if played on a snare/tom).
*   **Right Foot**: Plays the "High Drum" part on the **Bass Drum**.
*   **Left Foot**: Maintains the pulse (dotted quarter notes) on the **Hi-Hat**.

### Technical Focus
The challenge of the Nanigo is maintaining the "triplet-based" flow without letting the bass drum or tom parts pull the bell pattern out of time. The bass drum often plays a syncopated role that can feel "counter" to the steady pulse of the hi-hat foot.

### Pro-Tip
Start by mastering the "Essentials":
1.  Bell Pattern (Right Hand)
2.  Pulse (Left Foot)
3.  Backbeat/Low Drum (Left Hand)
Add the Bass Drum last, as it is often the most syncopated element.
