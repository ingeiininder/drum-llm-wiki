# Drumset Mobility: Double Strokes

While previous mobility exercises focused on single strokes, this unit explores the application of [[Double_Strokes]] (doubles) around the drumset.

### Technical Challenges
- **Rebound Management**: Unlike the snare drum, [[Toms]] often have less rebound. Executing clean doubles on toms requires more wrist involvement and "pulling" the sound out of the drum.
- **Movement Patterns**:
    - **Clockwise**: Moving from high to low surfaces.
    - **Counter-Clockwise**: Moving from low to high surfaces.
    - **Combinations**: Mixing directions to challenge spatial awareness.

### Practice Variations
- **Left-Hand Development**: The curriculum suggests moving the left hand (which often plays the doubles on the snare) to a tom to increase the physical demand and balance the hands.
- **Sequential Play**: Play all variations (Ex 3.1 through 3.5) in a continuous sequence without stopping to appreciate the relationship between different note values and kit positions.

> **Pro-Tip**: Focus on the "second note" of the double. On toms, the second note tends to be quieter; ensure you use your fingers/wrists to give it equal volume to the first note.
