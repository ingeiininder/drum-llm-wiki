# Eighth Note to Quarter Note Modulation (Ballad to Reggae)

This modulation involves a transition from a **Rock Ballad** (80 BPM) to a **Reggae Groove**.

### The Modulation ($e = q$)
The [[Value Indicator]] states that the **Eighth Note** from the Ballad section becomes the **Quarter Note** in the Reggae section.
*   **Result**: The new tempo is effectively 160 BPM (double the original 80 BPM).
*   **Feel Shift**: While the tempo mathematically doubles, Reggae is often felt with a "Half Time" feel.

### Sectional Analysis
1.  **Letter A (Ballad)**: 16th-note based groove at 80 BPM. Requires consistent hi-hat work and a solid backbeat.
2.  **Letter B (Transition)**: Continues the ballad feel but introduces ensemble figures that require [[Setup_Fills]]. The final fill should be stylistically transitional (e.g., using eighth-note triplets).
3.  **Letter C (Reggae)**: The [[One-Drop]] groove. The bass drum and side-stick land on beat 3. Because of the $e = q$ modulation, the notation remains clean and readable despite the faster tempo.

### Performance Notes
*   **One-Drop Technique**: In the Reggae section, the "one" is often left empty (hence "one-drop"), with the emphasis shifting to beat 3.
*   **Return Transition**: At the end of the Reggae section, a second value indicator ($q = e$) is used to return to the original Ballad tempo.
