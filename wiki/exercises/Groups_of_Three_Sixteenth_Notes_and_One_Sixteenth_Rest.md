# Groups of Three Sixteenth Notes and One Sixteenth Rest
This category of rhythmic exercises focuses on patterns where three [[Sixteenth_Note]]s are combined with one [[Sixteenth_Rest]] within a single beat. These patterns are fundamental [[Rhythmic_Permutations]] derived from [[Mixing_Sixteenth_Notes_and_Sixteenth_Rests]].

**Variations within a Beat (1-e-&-a):**
The single [[Sixteenth_Rest]] can be placed on any of the four subdivisions within the beat, leading to four distinct patterns:
1.  **Rest on the downbeat:** `_ e & a` (e.g., silence on 1, notes on e-&-a)
2.  **Rest on the "e":** `1 _ & a` (e.g., note on 1, silence on e, notes on &-a)
3.  **Rest on the "&":** `1 e _ a` (e.g., notes on 1-e, silence on &, note on a)
4.  **Rest on the "a":** `1 e & _` (e.g., notes on 1-e-&, silence on a)

**Practice:**
*   Utilize [[Verbal_Counting_(Rhythm)]] (1-e-&-a) to accurately internalize and perform these patterns.
*   Employ the [[Imaginary_Bar_Line_(Study_Aid)]] to isolate each beat and focus on the precise timing of the rest.
*   Pay close attention to the exact duration of the silence, treating the rest as an active part of the rhythm.

**Pro-Tip:**
These patterns are building blocks for many [[Funk]] and [[Fusion (Music)]] grooves. Practice them on different drums (e.g., snare, bass drum, hi-hat) to develop coordination and musicality. The placement of the rest significantly changes the feel of the beat, so experiment with how each variation sounds and feels.
