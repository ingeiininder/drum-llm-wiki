# Eighth Notes with Eighth Rests (Four-Bar Phrases)
This exercise category extends the practice of reading and performing [[Eighth Notes]] and [[Eighth Rests]] into longer, four-bar phrases. It builds upon the foundational skills developed in [[Eighth Notes with Eighth Rests (Two-Bar Phrases)]].

**Purpose:**
*   To enhance [[Rhythmic Continuity]] and [[Phrase Development]] over more extended musical sections.
*   To improve the ability to maintain a steady tempo and [[Internal Clock (Drumming)]] across multiple measures.
*   To prepare for reading more complex musical forms.

**Pro-Tip:**
Focus on seeing the entire four-bar phrase as a single musical idea. While practicing, try to anticipate upcoming rhythms. If you encounter difficulty, break the phrase down into two-bar or even one-bar segments, then gradually reassemble them. Using a [[Metronome]] is crucial for maintaining a consistent tempo throughout the longer phrase.
