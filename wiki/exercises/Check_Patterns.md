# Check Patterns
[[Check_Patterns]] are rhythmic exercises or sequences designed to test and reinforce a drummer's internal clock, rhythmic accuracy, and ability to maintain a steady tempo. They often involve playing a pattern and then leaving a space (a "check") to ensure the internal timing remains consistent. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 8 of "[[Drum_Technique_1_(MI_Curriculum_Series)]]".

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Internal Clock Development:** Helps drummers internalize rhythmic subdivisions and maintain a steady pulse without external aids.
*   **Rhythmic Accuracy:** Identifies weaknesses in timing and helps to correct them.
*   **Consistency:** Improves the ability to play patterns evenly and consistently over time.
*   **Metronome Independence:** Gradually reduces reliance on a constant metronome click by forcing the drummer to "feel" the time.

**Typical Structure:**
A common [[Check_Patterns]] might involve playing a rhythmic phrase for a certain number of beats or measures, then resting for an equal number of beats or measures, and then re-entering precisely on time. For example:
*   Play 2 measures of [[16th_Notes]]
*   Rest 2 measures
*   Re-enter precisely on beat 1 of the next phrase.

**Benefits:**
*   Enhances rhythmic precision and confidence.
*   Develops a stronger internal sense of time.
*   Improves the ability to play with other musicians in an ensemble.

**Pro-Tip:** Start with simple [[Check_Patterns]] and gradually increase their complexity and the length of the "check" (rest period). Always use a metronome initially to verify your accuracy when re-entering. The goal is to feel the time so strongly that you can accurately re-enter even after a long silence.
