# Assigned Sticking: Four Notes per Beat
This exercise applies the principles of [[Assigned_Sticking]] to rhythms containing four notes per beat, which typically means continuous [[16th_Note]]s. This is the most direct application of the alternating hand concept.

*   **Concept**: Each of the four 16th-note subdivisions (1 e & a) within a beat is played, with the [[Sticking_Patterns]] strictly following [[Alternating_Hands_Sticking]].
*   **Example**: For continuous 16th notes (1 e & a), the sticking would be R L R L (assuming [[Right_Hand_Lead_Assigned_Sticking]]). This pattern repeats for every beat of continuous 16th notes.

**Pro-Tip**: This is a foundational exercise for developing speed, endurance, and evenness between the hands. Focus on making each stroke sound identical in volume and tone. Use a metronome and practice gradually increasing the tempo, ensuring that the [[Muscle_Memory_Drumming]] for this pattern is deeply ingrained. This is essential for playing fast fills and intricate grooves.
