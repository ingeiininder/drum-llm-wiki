# Bossa Nova Drumming Pattern

The standard Bossa Nova groove is a two-bar [[Ostinato]] that requires independence between all four limbs.

### Rhythmic Breakdown
*   **Hands (Top)**: Continuous 8th notes on the [[Hi-Hat]] or [[Ride_Cymbal]].
*   **Cross-Stick (Snare)**:
    *   Bar 1: Beat 1, the "&" of 2, and Beat 4.
    *   Bar 2: Beat 2 and the "&" of 3.
*   **Feet (Bottom)**:
    *   **Bass Drum**: Plays "1" and the "&" of 3 in every bar.
    *   **Hi-Hat Foot**: Traditionally played on beats 2 and 4 (the "backbeat" function) during solo sections or when moving to the ride cymbal.

### Technical Execution
*   **Bass Drum Sound**: Use an "unburied" stroke with a felt beater. To achieve the required quiet dynamic, "choke up" on the pedal by placing the toes just behind the chain/strap. The beater should only travel 2 to 2.5 inches from the head.
*   **Alignment**: The bass drum must strike exactly with the cymbal notes to avoid "flamming."

> [!WARNING]
> **Common Pitfall**: Many students accidentally accent the "&" of 3 on the bass drum. Keep both notes of the bass drum pattern even in volume to maintain a professional Brazilian feel.
