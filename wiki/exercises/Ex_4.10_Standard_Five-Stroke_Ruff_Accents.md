# Ex 4.10 Standard Five-Stroke Ruff Accents
This exercise builds upon the [[Standard_Five-Stroke_Ruff]] by shifting the placement of the accents within a rhythmic context. Instead of the accent always falling on the fifth stroke of the rudiment, this exercise places the accents on specific beats (beats 1 and 3).

**Objective:**
To develop flexibility in applying the [[Standard_Five-Stroke_Ruff]] while maintaining specific accent patterns, enhancing rhythmic control and musicality.

**Musical Context (as described in source):**
The exercise is designed such that the accents of the overall phrase will fall on beats 1 and 3. This means the accented fifth stroke of the [[Five-Stroke_Ruff]] will align with these beats.
`3] L R L` (This snippet indicates a phrase where the rudiment's accent is placed on beat 1 and then again on beat 3 within a 4/4 or similar context, requiring careful alignment.)

**Practice Instructions:**
1.  **Master the Basic Rudiment:** Ensure you can play the [[Standard_Five-Stroke_Ruff]] cleanly with good dynamic contrast before attempting this exercise.
2.  **Identify Accent Points:** Clearly mark or mentally note where beats 1 and 3 fall in the measure.
3.  **Align Rudiment Accents:** Practice placing the accented fifth stroke of the [[Five-Stroke_Ruff]] precisely on beats 1 and 3. This may require starting the rudiment at different points relative to the beat.
4.  **Maintain Flow:** Despite the shifted accents, strive to maintain the smooth, flowing feel of the rudiment.

**Pro-Tip:**
This exercise is excellent for developing rhythmic independence and understanding how rudiments can be manipulated within a musical phrase. Use a [[Metronome]] and count the beats aloud. If you find it challenging, slow down significantly and practice the alignment of the rudiment's accent with the target beat in isolation before connecting the full phrase. This helps in internalizing the rhythmic placement.
