# Assigned Sticking: One Note per Beat
This exercise applies the principles of [[Assigned Sticking]] to rhythms where only one note occurs per beat, typically a [[Quarter Note]]. While seemingly simple, it reinforces the foundational concept of hand assignment within the beat.

*   **Concept**: Even when playing a single note per beat, the underlying [[Alternating_Hands_Sticking]] of continuous [[16th Note]]s is mentally (or physically, if tapping) maintained. The actual played note falls on its assigned hand's stroke.
*   **Example**: For a series of Quarter Notes (1 - 2 - 3 - 4 -), if the [[Right_Hand_Lead_Assigned_Sticking]] is applied, the right hand would play each Quarter Note. The left hand would be "resting" or preparing for its turn on the "e" or "a" of the underlying 16th-note subdivision.

**Pro-Tip**: Even with sparse rhythms, maintaining the mental subdivision of 16th notes and the corresponding assigned sticking helps ensure precise timing and prepares you for more complex patterns. This builds a strong internal clock and reinforces [[Muscle_Memory_Drumming]].
