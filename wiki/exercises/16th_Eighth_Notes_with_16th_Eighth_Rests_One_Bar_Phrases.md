# 16th and Eighth Notes with 16th and Eighth Rests (One Bar Phrases)
This exercise type (M and N) further increases rhythmic complexity by combining [[Sixteenth Notes]] and [[Eighth Notes]] with *both* [[16th_Rest]] and [[Eighth Rests]] within single-bar phrases. This demands a high level of rhythmic precision and an acute awareness of varying rest durations.

**Practice Guidance:**
1.  **Discriminate Rests:** Clearly differentiate between the duration of a [[16th Rest]] and an [[Eighth Rest]]. A common mistake is to treat them as the same length.
2.  **Internal Subdivision:** Maintain a consistent [[Sixteenth Note]] subdivision throughout, using it as a reference point for all notes and rests.
3.  **Slow Practice:** Due to the increased complexity, starting at a very slow tempo is crucial for accuracy before gradually speeding up.

**Pro-Tip:** This type of exercise is excellent for developing your ability to play highly syncopated rhythms. The precise placement of different rest values is key to creating compelling rhythmic tension and release.
