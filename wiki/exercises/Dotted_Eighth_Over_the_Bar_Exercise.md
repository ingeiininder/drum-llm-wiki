# Dotted Eighth Over-the-Bar Exercise
This exercise applies the [[Over_the_Bar_Groove_Technique]] using a three-16th-note grouping (dotted eighth value) distributed between the [[Bass Drum]] and [[Snare Drum]].

### The Pattern
- **Bass Drum**: Plays on the start of every three-16th-note grouping.
- **Snare Drum**: Fills the remaining "holes" with [[Ghost Notes]].
- **Hi-Hat**: Plays steady 8th notes or quarter notes.

### Progression
1. **Unison Hands**: Play the dotted eighth rhythm with both hands on the snare and floor tom over quarter notes in the feet.
2. **Linear Distribution**: Move the rhythm to the Bass Drum, with the Snare Drum filling the 16th-note gaps.
3. **Layered Independence**: 
    - Add accents to the Hi-Hat on every downbeat.
    - Add accents to the Hi-Hat on every upbeat.
    - Apply a "1-e-&" Hi-Hat pattern over the 3-note grouping.

### 12-Bar Resolution
Because the pattern is based on 3-note groupings in a 4/4 (16-note) environment, the sequence takes **12 bars** to come "full circle" and land perfectly back on Beat 1.

### Pro-Tip
Avoid the "3/4 Trap." The tendency is to start feeling the pattern in 3/4 time. Force yourself to count "1, 2, 3, 4" out loud to ensure you are feeling the syncopation against 4/4.
