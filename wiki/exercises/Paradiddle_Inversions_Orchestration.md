# Paradiddle Inversions: Orchestration
Mastering [[Paradiddle Inversions]] on the drum set allows for fluid movement and complex-sounding fills that are based on simple, alternating stickings.

### The Four Inversions
1. **Standard**: RLRR LRLL
2. **Inversion #1**: RRLR LLRL
3. **Inversion #2**: RLLR LRRL
4. **Inversion #3**: RLRL LRLR (Note: This is essentially single strokes with a shifted start point).

### Creative Development Steps
- **Step 1: Stick Height**: Maintain a strict contrast between [[Accents_Drumming]] (9"-12") and [[Ghost_Notes]] (1"-3").
- **Step 2: Orchestration**: 
    - Move accents to the [[Tom-Toms]].
    - Move accents to [[Cymbals]] with [[Bass_Drum]] support.
- **Step 3: Switching**: Practice switching between different inversions every two beats without breaking the 16th-note flow.

### Pro-Tips
- **The "Ghost" Bed**: The secret to professional-sounding paradiddle fills is the volume of the unaccented notes. If the ghost notes are too loud, the orchestration loses its clarity.
- **Flow**: Use Inversion #2 (RLLR) to move easily between the snare and the floor tom, as the double-left allows the right hand time to travel.
