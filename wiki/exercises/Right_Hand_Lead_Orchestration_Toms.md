# Right-Hand Lead Orchestration (Toms)

This exercise applies [[Right-Hand Lead Application]] to the drum set by moving specific notes of a rhythmic pattern to the toms while keeping the remaining notes on the snare drum.

### Orchestration Rules
The primary rule is to play the **first right hand** of each rhythmic grouping on a tom-tom. All other notes (left hands and subsequent right hands in a 16th-note flow) remain on the snare.

### Variations (Ex 8.9)
1. **Rack Tom Focus**: The first right hand of every rhythm is played on the rack tom.
2. **Floor Tom Focus**: The first right hand of every rhythm is played on the floor tom.
3. **Alternating Toms**: 
   - Beats 1 and 3: First right hand on the rack tom.
   - Beats 2 and 4: First right hand on the floor tom.

### Practice Routine for Fills
Integrate these patterns into a musical context using the following phrasing exercises:
- **2-Bar Phrases**: 2 bars of [[Rock Groove]] + 2 bars of the orchestrated rhythm.
- **4-Bar Phrases**: 3 bars of [[Rock Groove]] + 1 bar of the orchestrated rhythm.
- **8-Bar Phrases**: 7 bars of [[Rock Groove]] + 1 bar of the orchestrated rhythm.

### Pro-Tip
Always count the [[Subdivision]] out loud. Hearing the "e-&-a" or the "1-trip-let" internally before striking the tom ensures the fill doesn't rush or drag relative to the groove.
