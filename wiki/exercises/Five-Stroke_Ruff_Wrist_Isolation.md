# Five-Stroke Ruff Wrist Isolation
This exercise is designed to isolate and strengthen individual wrist control while playing the [[Five-Stroke Ruff]]. It involves playing the entire rudiment with a single hand, focusing on the wrist's ability to produce both unaccented grace notes and an accented stroke.

**Objective:**
To develop independent wrist control and strength for both hands, ensuring that each hand can execute the [[Five-Stroke Ruff]] with precision, dynamics, and fluidity. This is a crucial step in achieving balanced technique.

**Method (based on Ex 4.9 played with one hand):**
The original [[Ex 4.9 Standard Five-Stroke Ruff Practice]] is adapted to be played entirely with one hand.
Example: `R R R R R` (all strokes with the right hand, with the fifth 'R' being accented).
Then repeat with the left hand: `L L L L L` (all strokes with the left hand, with the fifth 'L' being accented).

**Practice Instructions:**
1.  **Focus on One Hand:** Begin by playing the entire [[Five-Stroke Ruff]] (four grace notes, one accent) using only your right hand.
2.  **Wrist-Driven Motion:** Emphasize that all five strokes should primarily come from the wrist, with minimal arm involvement.
3.  **Dynamic Control:** Maintain the dynamic contrast: four soft grace notes followed by a strong accent, all produced by the same wrist.
4.  **Repeat with Other Hand:** Once comfortable with the right hand, switch to the left hand and repeat the process, ensuring equal attention and development for both hands.
5.  **Slow and Deliberate:** Start very slowly with a [[Metronome]] to ensure each stroke is intentional and controlled.

**Pro-Tip:**
This exercise is a direct application of [[Moeller_Up_Stroke]] principles, even if not explicitly named. The grace notes can be thought of as "taps" or "downstrokes" from a low position, while the accent is a "full stroke" or "upstroke" that prepares for the next set of grace notes. Pay close attention to the rebound of the stick; allow it to do some of the work, especially for the grace notes. This isolation helps identify and correct imbalances between your hands, leading to a more symmetrical and powerful drumming technique.
