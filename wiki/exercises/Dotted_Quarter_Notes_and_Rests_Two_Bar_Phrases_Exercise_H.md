# Dotted Quarter Notes and Rests (Two Bar Phrases) - Exercise H
This exercise combines [[Dotted Quarter Note]]s with [[Dotted Quarter Rest]]s (and potentially other quarter notes/rests) within two-bar phrases. It challenges the drummer to accurately interpret both played notes and periods of silence, often creating [[Syncopated Rhythms]].

**Key Learning Points:**
*   **Interplay of Sound and Silence**: Accurately execute the precise durations of both dotted quarter notes and dotted quarter rests.
*   **Rhythmic Precision**: Maintain a high level of accuracy in both attacks and silent durations.
*   **Phrase Continuity**: Ensure smooth transitions between notes and rests, and across the [[Bar Line]].

**Pro-Tip:** When a dotted quarter note is followed by a dotted quarter rest (or vice versa), pay extra attention to the exact subdivision where the sound stops and the silence begins, or where the silence ends and the sound begins. This requires meticulous subdivision.
