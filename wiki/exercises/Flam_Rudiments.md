# Flam Rudiments

Flam rudiments integrate the [[Flam Technique]] into various rhythmic patterns and sticking permutations. The primary goal when practicing these is **control over speed**, specifically maintaining low stick heights for unaccented notes.

### Essential Flam Rudiments
The following are the foundational flam-based patterns used in the Musicians Institute curriculum:

1.  **Flam Accent**: A triplet-based pattern where the flam occurs on the first note of each group of three.
2.  **Flam Tap**: Alternating flams followed by a single tap in the same hand (lR R, rL L).
3.  **Flamacue**: A five-note grouping characterized by an accent on the second note (the first note after the flam).
4.  **Single Flam Paradiddle**: A standard [[Paradiddles|Paradiddle]] with a flam on the first note.
5.  **Single Flammed Mill**: An inverted paradiddle (R R L R) with a flam on the first note.
6.  **Flam Paradiddle-Diddle**: A six-note pattern (lR L R R L L).
7.  **Swiss Army Triplet**: A unique rudiment where the flam and the following note are played by the same hand (lR R L).

### Practice Focus
*   **Consistency**: Ensure the grace note height remains identical whether leading with the right or left hand.
*   **Up-Strokes**: Pay attention to the height of your up-strokes; they must be high enough to provide the necessary velocity for the subsequent accented flam.
*   **Sticking Accuracy**: Use a metronome to ensure the underlying rhythm (eighth notes, triplets, or 16th notes) remains perfectly steady despite the added complexity of the flams.
