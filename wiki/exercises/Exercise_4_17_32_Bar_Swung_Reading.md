# Exercise 4.17: 32-Bar Swung Reading
This exercise focuses on the independence required to maintain a [[Swung_16th_Notes]] feel while reading syncopated rhythms.

### The Hand Ostinato (Ex 4.16)
Before reading the 32-bar chart, establish the following hand pattern:
*   **Right Hand**: Constant 8th notes on the Hi-Hat (interpreted as swung).
*   **Left Hand**: Snare drum on beats 2 and 4.

### Execution Instructions
1.  **The Task**: Play the hand ostinato while reading the 32-bar rhythmic exercise (Ex 4.17) with the **Bass Drum**.
2.  **Method 1 (Straight)**: Play the entire exercise with straight 16th notes. Verbally count "1 e & ah."
3.  **Method 2 (Swung)**: Play the entire exercise with a swung 16th feel. Verbally count the triplet subdivision.
4.  **Tempo Range**: Start at 60 BPM; goal is 90 BPM.

### Technical Focus
*   **Open-Handed Playing**: Once comfortable, switch hands (Left Hand on Hi-Hat, Right Hand on Snare) to develop symmetry.
*   **Feel Distinction**: Ensure there is a clear, audible difference between the "Straight" and "Swung" versions of the same 32-bar phrase.
