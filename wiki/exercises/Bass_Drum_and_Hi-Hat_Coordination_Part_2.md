# Bass Drum and Hi-Hat Coordination Part 2
[[Bass_Drum_and_Hi-Hat_Coordination_Part_2]] is the second installment of exercises designed to further develop the independent and interdependent movement of the bass drum foot and the hi-hat foot/hand. It builds upon the foundational concepts introduced in "[[Bass_Drum_and_Hi-Hat_Coordination_Part_1]]". It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 7 of "[[Drum_Technique_1_(MI_Curriculum_Series)]]".

**Exercise Focus:**
*   **Increased Complexity:** Introducing more intricate rhythmic patterns for both the [[Bass_Drum]] and [[Hi-Hat]].
*   **Syncopation:** Exploring syncopated figures and off-beat placements for each limb.
*   **Dynamic Nuance:** Developing finer control over the dynamics of each limb independently.
*   **Groove Integration:** Applying these coordination skills to create more sophisticated and varied drum grooves.

**Typical Exercises Might Include:**
*   More complex [[16th_Notes]] patterns on the hi-hat hand while maintaining varied bass drum patterns.
*   Introducing open/closed [[Hi-Hat_Technique]] variations with foot coordination.
*   Playing more challenging unison or staggered patterns between the bass drum and hi-hat.

**Benefits:**
*   Enhances [[Limb Independence]] and overall [[Drum_Kit]] coordination.
*   Expands the drummer's rhythmic vocabulary for the lower half of the body.
*   Improves the ability to create and maintain diverse grooves.

**Pro-Tip:** As the exercises become more complex, maintaining relaxation is even more critical. If you feel tension, slow down significantly. Break down complex patterns into smaller segments, master each segment, and then gradually combine them. Counting out loud remains a valuable tool for rhythmic accuracy.
