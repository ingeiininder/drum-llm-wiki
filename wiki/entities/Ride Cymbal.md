# Ride Cymbal
The "Ride Cymbal" is a large, typically heavy cymbal on the [[Drum_Kit]], primarily used for time-keeping and creating a sustained, shimmering, or "riding" sound. It provides a different timbre and sustain compared to the [[Hi-Hat]] or [[Crash_Cymbal]].

The ride cymbal is often employed in [[Chorus (Song Section)]]s, [[Bridge (Song Section)]]s, or [[Coda (Song Section)]]s to add energy, fullness, or a sense of openness to the [[Groove]]. It can be played in various ways:
*   On the bow for a clear, defined "ping" sound.
*   On the bell for a louder, more cutting accent.
*   On the edge for a washier, more sustained sound.

In this curriculum, a [[Playing Surface Switch]] to the [[Ride_Cymbal]] is noted during the [[Bridge (Song Section)]] within the [[Coda (Song Section)]].

**Pro-Tip:** Develop control over different areas of the ride cymbal to access its full sonic palette. The choice of stick tip (wood vs. nylon) and the angle of attack will significantly impact the sound. Practice maintaining a consistent ride pattern while simultaneously playing [[Bass_Drum]] and [[Snare_Drum]] patterns, ensuring the ride remains clear and articulate.
