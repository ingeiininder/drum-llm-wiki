# Half Rest
A **Half Rest** is a fundamental [[Rhythmic_Values|rest value]] in music notation, indicating a period of silence.

## Characteristics
*   **Duration**: In a standard [[4-4_Time_Signature]], a half rest is equal to **two beats**, which is half the duration of a [[Whole_Rest]] and half the duration of a full [[Measure]].
*   **Notation**: It is indicated by a solid rectangular symbol that sits on top of the third line of the musical staff. (Often remembered as a "hat" sitting on the line, whereas a whole rest is a "hole" hanging from the line).
*   **Counting**: When counting aloud in 4/4 time, a half rest is typically counted as "1 - 2" or "3 - 4", with no sound produced for two beats.

## Importance for Drummers
*   **Rhythmic Space**: Half rests are crucial for creating rhythmic space within a [[Measure]], allowing for syncopation and dynamic phrasing.
*   **Precision**: Accurately observing half rests requires precise timing and a strong internal [[Beat]] to ensure the silence is exactly two beats long.

## Pro-Tip for Drummers
*   **Active Silence**: Treat a half rest as an "active silence." You are actively *not* playing for two beats, but you are still engaged with the rhythm and pulse.
*   **Subdivision Awareness**: Even during a half rest, maintain awareness of the underlying [[Subdivision]] (e.g., [[Eighth_Note]]s or [[Sixteenth_Note]]s) to ensure accurate re-entry.
