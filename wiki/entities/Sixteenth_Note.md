# Sixteenth Note
A fundamental [[Rhythmic_Values]] in music, the [[Sixteenth Note]] is equal to one-sixteenth of a whole note or one-sixteenth of a measure in [[4-4_Time_Signature]]. It is derived by dividing a [[Quarter Note]] into four equal parts, or an [[Eighth Note]] into two equal parts.

**Notation:**
A single [[Sixteenth Note]] visually resembles a [[Quarter Note]] but features two [[Flags (Musical Notation)]] attached to its [[Stem (Musical Notation)]]. When multiple [[Sixteenth Note]]s occur consecutively, these flags are replaced by [[Double Beam]]s for improved readability.

**Value Breakdown:**
*   In [[4-4_Time_Signature]], one [[Sixteenth Note]] equals one-fourth of a beat.
*   One [[Quarter Note]] = Four [[Sixteenth Note]]s.
*   One [[Eighth Note]] = Two [[Sixteenth Note]]s.

**Usage:**
[[Sixteenth Note]]s are essential for creating intricate and driving rhythms, commonly found in [[Funk]], [[Fusion (Music)]], and [[Metal (Music)]] styles. Mastering their subdivision is a cornerstone of modern drumming.

**Pro-Tip:**
Practice [[Sixteenth Note]]s slowly and precisely with a [[Metronome]] to ensure evenness. Focus on developing a consistent stroke quality for each note. As you increase tempo, maintain relaxation to avoid tension, which can hinder speed and accuracy.
