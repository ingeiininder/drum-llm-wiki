# Eighth Note
The [[Eighth_Note]] is a fundamental [[Rhythmic Duration]] in musical [[Notation]], representing a subdivision of the [[Quarter_Note]]. It is one of the most commonly used rhythmic values in virtually all genres of modern music.

**Visual Characteristics:**
An eighth note is visually distinguished by:
*   A filled-in oval [[Note_Head]].
*   A vertical [[Note Stem]] extending from the note head.
*   A single [[Note Flag]] attached to the end of the stem.

When two or more eighth notes occur consecutively, their flags are typically replaced by a single horizontal **beam** connecting their stems. This beaming helps to group notes visually and makes [[Rhythm_Reading]] easier.

**Rhythmic Value:**
*   **Duration**: An eighth note is equal to one-half (1/2) of a [[Beat]].
*   **Relationship to Quarter Note**: Two eighth notes are equivalent in duration to one [[Quarter_Note]].
*   **Relationship to Measure (4/4 time)**: In common time (4/4), where a [[Whole_Note]] fills an entire [[Measure]] (four beats), an eighth note occupies one-eighth of the measure's total duration. Therefore, a 4/4 measure can contain a maximum of eight eighth notes.

**Pronunciation/Counting:**
When [[Counting Eighth Notes]], the standard verbalization is "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &", where the numbers (1, 2, 3, 4) represent the [[Downbeat]]s (quarter notes) and the "ands" (&) represent the [[Upbeat]]s (the second eighth note of each beat).

**Pro-Tip:**
To truly internalize the feel of an eighth note, practice [[Subdivision]] by tapping your foot on the quarter notes while vocally counting "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &". This helps to establish a strong [[Eighth Note Pulse]] and ensures accurate placement of both downbeats and upbeats.
