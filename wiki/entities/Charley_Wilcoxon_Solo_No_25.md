# Charley Wilcoxon Solo No. 25
Solo No. 25 is a technical etude that introduces the complex transition of placing a [[Drag]] after a [[Flams]] and before a [[Roll]].

### Key Technical Challenge
The "Flam-Drag-Roll" combination (specifically leading into a [[5-Stroke Roll]]) appears in bars 8, 9, and 10. This requires precise metering of the grace notes to ensure the roll starts exactly on time.

### Structural Overview
- **Length**: 16 bars.
- **Time Signature**: 2/4.
- **Check Patterns**: Use Ex 7.1 through 7.3 to isolate the Flam/Drag/Roll transitions.
- **Notation**: Note that Ex 7.4 demonstrates different ways to write the same rhythmic measure, highlighting the importance of understanding [[Rudimental Notation]].

### Practice Suggestions
- Identify all recurring rudiments (Flams, Drags, Paradiddles) before playing.
- Focus on the "metering" of the rhythms—ensure the drag doesn't "eat" the space required for the subsequent roll.
