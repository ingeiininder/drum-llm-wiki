# Head (Music Term)

The term "head" is a common piece of [[Jazz Terminology|jazz]] and [[Blues Terminology|blues terminology]] that refers to the thematic melody within a song's form.

## Definition

The "head" is the main [[Melody]] of a song. It is typically played at the beginning of a song's performance (the "head in") and again at the end (the "head out"). In a [[12-Bar Blues Open Form|12-bar blues form]], if a [[Bandleader]] requests "one head," it means the band will play through 12 bars of the melody.

## Usage

*   **Beginning and End**: The head serves as the primary melodic statement, framing the performance.
*   **Structure**: It defines the core musical identity of the piece before and after [[Improvisation|improvised]] sections (like solos).
*   **Communication**: Jazz and blues musicians often use a non-verbal [[Cue (Music)|cue]] for the "head" or "top" by patting their hand on top of their head. This signal ensures that everyone in the band "goes back to the melody" the next time the "top of the form" comes around.

## Head and Chorus Combined Usage

The term "head" is frequently used in conjunction with "[[Chorus (Jazz/Blues Term)|chorus]]" to outline the structure of a performance. For example, a bandleader might say: "We're going to play one head, one chorus of guitar solo, and one head out." This translates to:
*   12 bars of the melody (head in)
*   12 bars (one time through the 12-bar form) of guitar solo
*   12 bars of the melody to the end (head out)

## Pro-Tips for Drummers

*   **Melody Awareness**: Even though you're playing drums, be intimately familiar with the melody of the head. This helps you support it appropriately.
*   **Dynamic Support**: Adjust your dynamics and [[Orchestration]] to support the melody during the head sections. Often, the head is played with a more understated or grooving feel to highlight the melody.
*   **Listen for Cues**: Always be attentive to the [[Bandleader]] or other musicians for the "head" cue, especially in [[Open Form (Music)|open form]] performances.
