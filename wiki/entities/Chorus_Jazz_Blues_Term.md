# Chorus (Jazz/Blues Term)

In [[Jazz Terminology|jazz]] and [[Blues Terminology|blues]] contexts, the term "chorus" has a specific meaning that differs significantly from its use in pop music.

## Definition

Unlike a pop music chorus, which refers to "the hook" or main repeating section of a song, a jazz or blues chorus is slang for "one time through the form." This means that one chorus represents a complete cycle of the underlying musical structure, typically the [[12-Bar Blues Open Form|12-bar blues]] progression.

## Usage

*   **Form Cycle**: If a [[Bandleader]] says they want to play "one chorus of guitar solo" in a 12-bar blues form, it means the guitarist will solo for one complete 12-bar blues progression.
*   **Solo Sections**: Choruses are commonly used to delineate solo sections, allowing musicians to improvise over the established harmonic progression for a specified number of cycles.
*   **Counting Form**: Musicians often count choruses to keep track of the song's structure during extended improvisations.

## Head and Chorus Combined Usage

The term "chorus" is frequently used in conjunction with "[[Head_Music_Term|head]]" to outline the structure of a performance. For example, a bandleader might say: "We're going to play one head, one chorus of guitar solo, and one head out." This translates to:
*   12 bars of the melody (head in)
*   12 bars (one time through the 12-bar form) of guitar solo
*   12 bars of the melody to the end (head out)

## Pro-Tips for Drummers

*   **Form Awareness**: As a drummer, it's crucial to always know where you are in the form, especially during solo choruses. This allows you to support the soloist effectively and anticipate transitions.
*   **Dynamic Support**: Adjust your dynamics and [[Orchestration]] to support the soloist during their choruses. You might increase intensity, vary your groove, or provide rhythmic accents.
*   **Listen for Cues**: Be attentive to the [[Bandleader]] or other musicians for cues regarding the number of choruses or transitions back to the head.
*   **Groove Consistency**: Maintain a solid and consistent [[Groove]] throughout the choruses, providing a stable foundation for the soloist.
