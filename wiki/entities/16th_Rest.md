# 16th Rest
A 16th Rest is a [[Musical Rest Values]] representing a period of silence equal in duration to a [[16th Note]].

*   **Duration**: In 4/4 time, a 16th Rest is worth 1/4 of a beat of silence.
*   **Appearance**: It looks like a small "7" shape with two curved tops.
*   **Placement**: Sixteen 16th Rests can be placed into a single measure of 4/4 time. They are often found on any of the "e", "&", or "a" subdivisions.

**Pro-Tip**: 16th Rests are vital for creating complex rhythmic patterns and syncopation. Mastering their precise placement is key to playing modern grooves and fills accurately. Practice counting "1 e & a" and ensuring the silence is exactly one-quarter of a beat. These rests are frequently involved in [[Economizing Notation]].
