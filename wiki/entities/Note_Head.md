# Note Head
The [[Note Head]] is the fundamental oval-shaped part of a musical note. In standard melodic notation, its vertical position on the staff indicates the pitch. For rhythmic notation, particularly in percussion, the [[Note Head]] primarily signifies the duration of the sound and often indicates the specific drum or cymbal to be played (e.g., by its position on a specific line or space, or by a unique head shape like an 'x' for a cymbal).

In the context of a [[Sixteenth Note]], the [[Note Head]] is the core component to which the [[Stem (Musical Notation)]] and either [[Flags (Musical Notation)]] or [[Beams (Musical Notation)]] are attached. Its appearance (filled-in or open) also contributes to indicating the note's duration (e.g., a filled-in head for [[Quarter Note]]s, [[Eighth Note]]s, and [[Sixteenth Note]]s, versus an open head for half notes and whole notes).
