# Coda Sign
The "Coda Sign" is a musical notation symbol (a circle with a crosshair) that serves two primary functions:
1.  It marks the point in the music where a jump to the [[Coda (Song Section)]] should occur, typically after a [[D. S. al Coda]] or similar instruction.
2.  It also marks the beginning of the actual [[Coda (Song Section)]] itself.

When following an instruction like [[D. S. al Coda]], the performer plays until they reach the first [[Coda Sign]], then immediately jumps to the section marked with the second [[Coda Sign]].

**Pro-Tip:** The [[Coda Sign]] is a critical visual cue for navigation. Drummers should be prepared for a sudden jump in the music and any corresponding changes in [[Groove]], tempo, or dynamics that might accompany the transition into the [[Coda (Song Section)]]. Practice these jumps to ensure a seamless transition.
