# Jazz Terminology
Understanding the "language of the bandstand" is essential for navigating jazz charts and live performances.

*   **Chorus**: In jazz, a "chorus" refers to one full pass through the song's form (e.g., one 12-bar blues cycle). It is not the "hook" of the song as in pop music.
*   **The Head**: The thematic melody of the song. Usually played at the beginning and the end of the performance.
*   **Top/Head Cue**: A physical signal (often patting the top of the head) given by the bandleader to indicate that the group will return to the melody on the next chorus.
*   **Open Form**: Indicates the song repeats the form for an undetermined number of soloists.
*   **Trading**: A practice where the drummer alternates solo sections (usually 4 or 8 bars) with other instrumentalists (e.g., "Trading Fours").
