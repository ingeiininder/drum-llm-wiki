# Flags (Musical Notation)
[[Flags_(Musical_Notation)]] are curved lines attached to the end of a [[Stem_(Musical_Notation)]] that indicate the rhythmic value of a note. They are a crucial component of [[Rhythmic Notation]] for notes shorter than a [[Quarter_Note]].

**Number of Flags and Value:**
*   One flag denotes an [[Eighth_Note]].
*   Two flags denote a [[Sixteenth_Note]].
*   Three flags denote a thirty-second note, and so on.

**Beaming vs. Flags:**
When notes of equal or related rhythmic value occur consecutively, [[Flags_(Musical_Notation)]] are typically replaced by [[Beams_(Musical_Notation)]] for improved readability. For example, a series of [[Sixteenth_Note]]s will be connected by a [[Double_Beam]] rather than each note having two individual flags. This visual grouping helps musicians quickly identify rhythmic patterns within a beat or measure.
