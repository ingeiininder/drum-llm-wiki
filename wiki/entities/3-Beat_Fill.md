# 3-Beat Fill
A [[3-Beat Fill]] is a [[Drum Fills]] that occupies three beats. In drum notation, it is indicated by three [[Fill Markers]] (time slashes). This length provides a good balance between brevity and rhythmic development.

Three-beat fills are versatile and can be used for:
*   **Building momentum**: Leading into a new section with a sense of anticipation.
*   **Highlighting musical phrases**: Emphasizing a three-beat melodic or harmonic idea.
*   **Creating rhythmic tension**: Using odd groupings or syncopation.

**Pro-Tip**: When constructing a 3-beat fill, consider how it will connect to the surrounding [[Groove]]. It's often effective to start the fill on beat 2 of a measure, leaving beat 1 for a strong downbeat, or to use it to set up a strong downbeat in the following measure. Experiment with different drum voicings and dynamics to make your 3-beat fills expressive and musical.
