# 6/8 Clave
The **6/8 Clave** is the rhythmic "key" (Spanish: *clave*) for [[Afro_Cuban_6_8]] music. It is a two-bar (or one-bar 6/8) pattern that serves as the foundation for all other instruments in the ensemble.

### The Pattern
The 6/8 Clave consists of five notes. In a 6/8 time signature, the notes typically fall on:
**1**, (the "ah" of 1), **2**, **3**, **4**, **6** (approximate 8th note positions).

### Relationship to the Bell Pattern
The [[6/8_Bell_Pattern]] is an extension of the 6/8 Clave. The bell pattern contains the clave notes plus one additional "linking" note in each half of the pattern to create a more driving, circular feel.

### Practice Method
1.  Set a metronome to a dotted-quarter note pulse.
2.  Play the pulse with your foot ([[Hi-Hat]]).
3.  Clap the 6/8 Clave until it becomes a "loop" in your mind.
4.  Once comfortable, move the pattern to the [[Cowbell]] or the rim of the snare.
