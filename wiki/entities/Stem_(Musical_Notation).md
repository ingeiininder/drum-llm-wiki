# Stem (Musical Notation)
The [[Stem (Musical Notation)]] is the vertical line extending from the [[Note Head]] of a musical note. Its primary function is to connect the [[Note Head]] to [[Flags (Musical Notation)]] or [[Beams (Musical Notation)]], thereby indicating the note's rhythmic value.

**Direction:**
The direction of the [[Stem (Musical Notation)]] (up or down) is typically determined by the note's position on the staff to ensure readability. Generally, if the [[Note Head]] is on or above the middle line of the staff, the stem points down. If the [[Note Head]] is below the middle line, the stem points up. For percussion notation, stems often consistently point in one direction for clarity.

**Connection:**
For [[Sixteenth Note]]s, the [[Stem (Musical Notation)]] is where the two [[Flags (Musical Notation)]] are attached when the note is standalone, or where the [[Double Beam]] connects when notes are grouped together.
