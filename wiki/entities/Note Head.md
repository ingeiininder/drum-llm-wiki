# Note Head
The [[Note Head]] is the primary oval-shaped part of a musical note in [[Notation]]. It is the most fundamental component from which other elements like the [[Note Stem]], [[Note Flag]], or [[Beam]]s may extend.

**Appearance and Function:**
*   **Filled (Black) Note Head**: Indicates a shorter [[Rhythmic Duration]], typically a [[Quarter Note]] or any shorter value (e.g., [[Eighth Note]], [[Sixteenth Note]]).
*   **Unfilled (White) Note Head**: Indicates a longer rhythmic duration, typically a [[Half Note]] or [[Whole Note]].

The vertical position of the note head on the [[Musical Staff]] indicates the pitch of the note. However, for drummers reading rhythmic notation, the position on the staff often indicates which drum or cymbal to play, while the shape of the note head (filled or unfilled) is crucial for determining its duration.

**Pro-Tip:**
When reading drum notation, quickly distinguishing between filled and unfilled note heads is the first step in identifying the note's duration. This visual cue, combined with the presence or absence of a stem and flags/beams, allows for rapid interpretation of rhythmic patterns.
