# Whole Note
A **Whole Note** is a fundamental [[Rhythmic Values|note value]] in music notation.

## Characteristics
*   **Duration**: In a standard [[4-4_Time_Signature]], a whole note is equal to **four beats**, meaning it sustains for the entire duration of one [[Measure]].
*   **Notation**: It is indicated by a hollow oval note head without a stem.
*   **Counting**: When counting aloud in 4/4 time, a whole note is typically counted as "1 - 2 - 3 - 4", with the sound sustained for all four beats.

## Practice Exercise (Ex 1.16)
*   **Objective**: To internalize the duration of a whole note.
*   **Method**: While counting every beat out loud (1-2-3-4), play a single stroke on [[Beat]] 1 of every measure and sustain the sound (or imagine sustaining it on a drum) for the full four beats.
*   **Example**:
    *   Measure 1: Play on 1, sustain through 2, 3, 4.
    *   Measure 2: Play on 1, sustain through 2, 3, 4.

## Pro-Tip for Drummers
*   **Sustain vs. Decay**: On a drum, a whole note represents the *intended* duration of the sound. While a drum hit naturally decays, the mental concept is to hold that sound for the full four beats. This is crucial for understanding how to phrase longer notes, even if the physical sound doesn't last that long.
*   **Foundation**: Whole notes are the foundation upon which all other rhythmic values are built. A solid understanding of their duration is key to accurate timing.
