# 4/4 Time Signature
The [[4-4_Time_Signature]] is the most common [[Time_Signature]] in Western music, often referred to as "common time." It indicates the rhythmic organization of a piece of music.

**Notation:**
It is typically represented by the fraction 4/4 at the beginning of a piece of music, or sometimes by a large 'C' symbol (which stands for common time).

**Interpretation:**
*   **Top Number (4):** Indicates that there are four beats in each measure.
*   **Bottom Number (4):** Indicates that the [[Quarter_Note]] is the beat unit, meaning a [[Quarter_Note]] receives one beat.

**Relevance:**
Most of the rhythmic examples and explanations of [[Note_Value_Breakdown]] for [[Eighth_Note]]s, [[Sixteenth_Note]]s, and their corresponding rests are implicitly based on [[4-4_Time_Signature]]. It provides the fundamental framework for understanding how these rhythmic values are counted and grouped.

**Pro-Tip:**
While 4/4 is ubiquitous, understanding its structure (four quarter-note beats per measure) is key to grasping how other [[Time_Signature]]s differ and how rhythmic values behave within them. Always feel the four main beats when playing in 4/4, even when subdividing into smaller values.
