# Triple Threat (Composition)

**Triple Threat** is a technical exercise/composition by **Steve Kilgallon**, featured in the curriculum via the book *Double Pedal Metal*. The piece is designed as a tribute to the double-bass drumming style popularized by bands like Metallica (specifically the song "One").

### Core Concepts
*   **Sixteenth-Note Triplets**: The primary rhythmic motif of the piece.
*   **Lead Foot Awareness**: A significant challenge in this piece is the shifting orientation of the left foot, which often lands on the upbeats of 1 and 3 during triplet sequences.
*   **Balance**: Maintaining a centered physical weight is crucial for the execution of the [[Double Pedal]] "Groove Builders" found in the track.

### Performance Notes
The "Road Map" (IMG_1570) indicates a structure alternating between "Groove A" and various "Guitar Riffs." The guitar riffs are heavily based on sixteenth-note triplets, and the drummer is encouraged to orchestrate [[Drum_Fill]] that complement these triplet figures.

### Pro-Tips
*   **Counting**: Because the left foot occupies unusual upbeat positions, vocalizing the count is essential to prevent "rushing" the triplets.
*   **Spring Tension**: Refer to [[Pedal_Spring_Tension]] for hardware optimization specific to this style of playing.
