# First Ending
A First Ending is a musical notation device used in conjunction with repeat signs. It designates a section of music that is played only during the *first* pass through a repeated section.

*   **Appearance**: It is typically indicated by a bracketed line above the staff, labeled with a "1."
*   **Function**: After playing the music under the First Ending, the performer usually repeats back to an earlier point (often the beginning of the section or a specific repeat sign).

When navigating with a [[D.S. al Coda]] directive, the treatment of First Endings can vary:
*   In [[D.S. al Coda (Repeats are Good)]], the First Ending is played on the return.
*   In [[D.S. al Coda (No Repeats)]], the First Ending is skipped on the return, and the performer proceeds directly to the [[Second Ending]].

**Pro-Tip**: Always be aware of repeat signs and endings. Missing an ending can disrupt the entire ensemble. Practice navigating these sections smoothly to maintain musical flow.
