# 16th Note
A 16th Note is a [[Musical Note Values]] representing one-sixteenth the duration of a [[Whole Note]], or half the duration of an [[Eighth Note]].

*   **Duration**: In 4/4 time, a 16th Note is worth 1/4 of a beat.
*   **Appearance**: It looks like a filled-in oval with a stem and two flags. When multiple 16th Notes are grouped, their flags are replaced by two beams.
*   **Placement**: Sixteen 16th Notes can be placed into a single measure of 4/4 time. They are often counted as "1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a 4 e & a".

**Pro-Tip**: 16th Notes are crucial for playing faster, more intricate rhythms and fills. Developing smooth [[Sticking Patterns]] like [[Assigned Sticking]] and [[Double Strokes]] is essential for executing continuous 16th Notes cleanly and evenly. Focus on consistent subdivision and evenness between strokes.
