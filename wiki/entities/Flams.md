# Flams
A [[Flams]] is a drum rudiment and a common rhythmic occurrence where two notes played by different hands (or limbs) are intended to sound simultaneously, but one note (the grace note) precedes the main note by a very slight margin. While a controlled flam is a specific rudiment, an *unintended* flam is a common pitfall indicating a lack of precision.

**As a Rudiment**:
*   A flam is a specific rudiment where a quiet "grace note" precedes a louder "main note" on a different drum or surface. This creates a thicker, broader sound.

**As a Common Pitfall**:
*   In the context of a [[Groove]] like the [[A1_12_Bar_Blues_Groove]], "no flams should be present" means that notes intended to be played *simultaneously* by different limbs (e.g., [[Snare Drum]] and [[Bass Drum]] on a downbeat, or [[Ride Cymbal]] and bass drum) must hit *exactly* at the same time.
*   An unintended flam indicates a lack of [[Timing]] and [[Coordination]].

**Pro-Tip**: To eliminate unintended flams, practice slowly with a [[Metronome]]. Focus on the precise vertical alignment of your sticks and feet. Record yourself and listen back carefully to identify any slight discrepancies in timing between your limbs. The goal is a unified, single attack when notes are meant to be simultaneous.
