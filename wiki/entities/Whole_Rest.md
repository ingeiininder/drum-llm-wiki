# Whole Rest
A **Whole Rest** is a fundamental [[Rhythmic Values|rest value]] in music notation, indicating a period of silence.

## Characteristics
*   **Duration**: In a standard [[4-4_Time_Signature]], a whole rest is equal to **four beats**, meaning it signifies silence for the entire duration of one [[Measure]].
*   **Notation**: It is indicated by a solid rectangular symbol that hangs down from the fourth line of the musical staff.
*   **Counting**: When counting aloud in 4/4 time, a whole rest is typically counted as "1 - 2 - 3 - 4", with no sound produced for all four beats.

## Importance for Drummers
*   **Full Measure Silence**: The whole rest is used when an entire measure of silence is required, allowing for clear phrasing and separation between musical sections.
*   **Maintaining Pulse**: Even during a whole rest, a drummer must maintain their internal [[Tempo]] and [[Beat]] count to re-enter the music accurately.

## Pro-Tip for Drummers
*   **Internal Counting**: During a whole rest, continue to count the beats internally or subtly tap your foot to maintain the pulse. This prevents you from getting lost.
*   **Breathing**: Think of a whole rest as a musical breath. It's a moment to reset and prepare for the next musical phrase.
