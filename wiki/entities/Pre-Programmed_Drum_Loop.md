# Pre-Programmed Drum Loop
A [[Pre-Programmed_Drum_Loop]] is a pre-recorded, repeating rhythmic pattern, often created using electronic drum machines, software, or sampled acoustic drums. In modern music, particularly in genres like [[Country-Pop_Genre]], these loops are frequently incorporated to add rhythmic texture, provide a consistent foundation, or create a specific sonic aesthetic.

In [[Unit 5]], for the [[Song_Intro]] of [[Ex_5.1_Country_Pop_Intro]], a pre-programmed drum loop runs throughout the entire tune, even during rests. Initially, the instructor manages the loop, but students will be responsible for its operation in later units.

**Pro-Tip:** When playing with a drum loop, your primary goal is to lock in perfectly with it. Treat the loop as another band member. Practice playing on top of, behind, and directly with the click of the loop to develop impeccable [[Timing]]. This skill is essential for modern [[Drum_Performance]] in studio and live settings.
