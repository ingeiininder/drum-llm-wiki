# Charley Wilcoxon: Solo No. 1

Solo No. 1 is the introductory piece from **Charley Wilcoxon’s** iconic book, *[[The All-American Drummer: 150 Rudimental Solos]]*. It serves as a practical application of individual rudiments within a musical context.

### Compositional Structure
- **Time Signature**: 2/4.
- **Length**: 16 Bars.
- **Rudimental Vocabulary**:
    - [[Flam Tap]] (8th and 16th notes)
    - [[Flam Paradiddle]]
    - [[Flamacue]]
    - [[Alternating Flams]]
    - [[Single_Paradiddle]]
    - [[Triple_Paradiddle]]

### Educational Philosophy
Wilcoxon's solos are intended to be treated as **music**, not just exercises. The curriculum emphasizes:
1. **Coordination First**: Master the mechanics and sticking before adding dynamics.
2. **Dynamic Expression**: Apply the written dynamics to give the solo "color and life."
3. **Musicality**: Avoid playing a "combination of rudiments"; strive for honest musical expression.
