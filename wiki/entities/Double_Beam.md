# Double Beam
A [[Double Beam]] is a specific type of [[Beams (Musical Notation)]] consisting of two parallel horizontal lines used to connect the [[Stem (Musical Notation)]]s of [[Sixteenth Note]]s.

**Purpose:**
The presence of a [[Double Beam]] immediately signifies that the connected notes are [[Sixteenth Note]]s. It is primarily used to group four [[Sixteenth Note]]s together within a single beat in [[4-4_Time_Signature]], enhancing [[Rhythmic Reading]] clarity. This visual grouping helps drummers quickly identify the subdivision and maintain [[Rhythmic Accuracy]] during [[Performance (Music)]].

**Application:**
When [[Beaming Sixteenth Notes]], the [[Double Beam]] is the standard notation. It can also extend over [[Sixteenth Rest]]s to maintain the visual grouping of four subdivisions per beat, even when some are silent, ensuring that the rhythmic structure of the beat remains clear.

**Pro-Tip:**
When practicing [[Sixteenth Note]] patterns, pay attention to how the [[Double Beam]]s delineate the beats. This visual cue is a powerful aid for keeping your place in the music and understanding the rhythmic flow.
