# Note Flag
A [[Note Flag]] is a curved or angled line attached to the end of a [[Note Stem]], extending from the [[Note Head]]. It is a crucial element in musical [[Notation]] that indicates a shorter [[Rhythmic Duration]] than a [[Quarter Note]].

**Appearance and Function:**
*   A single flag signifies an [[Eighth Note]].
*   Two flags signify a [[Sixteenth Note]].
*   Three flags signify a [[Thirty-Second Note]], and so on.
*   The direction of the flag (up or down) depends on the direction of the stem. If the stem goes up, the flag curves to the right. If the stem goes down, the flag curves to the left.

**Beaming:**
When multiple flagged notes of the same value appear consecutively, their flags are often replaced by horizontal **beams** connecting their stems. This practice, known as **beaming**, improves the readability of rhythmic patterns, especially in faster passages. For example, two eighth notes with flags would typically be beamed together with a single beam.

**Pro-Tip:**
For drummers, quickly recognizing the number of flags (or beams) is paramount for accurate [[Rhythm Reading]]. The more flags/beams, the faster the rhythmic value. Practice identifying these visually to improve your [[Sight-Reading]] speed and accuracy.
