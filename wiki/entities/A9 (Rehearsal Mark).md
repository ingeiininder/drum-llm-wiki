# A9 (Rehearsal Mark)
A9 is a specific [[Rehearsal Mark]] used to designate a particular section of a song. The convention of adding a measure number (e.g., '9') to a letter (e.g., 'A') helps pinpoint the exact starting point of a section, especially when the harmonic or rhythmic structure changes within a broader lettered section.

In this curriculum, [[A9 (Rehearsal Mark)]] specifically refers to a [[Pre-Chorus]] section that technically begins on what would be bar 9 of the overall 'A' section. This section incorporates a [[Syncopated Bass Drum (A9)]] pattern played under a [[Tight Hi-Hat]] groove.

**Pro-Tip:** When encountering numbered rehearsal marks, pay close attention to the musical context. These often signify subtle but important shifts in feel, dynamics, or instrumentation. For drummers, this might mean a change in [[Groove]], [[Playing Surface Switch|playing surface]], or a specific [[Fill]] that sets up the next section. Always confirm the exact measure count if possible to avoid confusion.
