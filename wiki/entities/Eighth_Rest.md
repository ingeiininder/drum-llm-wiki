# Eighth Rest
An Eighth Rest is a [[Musical Rest Values]] representing a period of silence equal in duration to an [[Eighth Note]].

*   **Duration**: In 4/4 time, an Eighth Rest is worth 1/2 of a beat of silence.
*   **Appearance**: It looks like a small "7" shape with a curved top.
*   **Placement**: Eight Eighth Rests can be placed into a single measure of 4/4 time. They are often found on the "ands" or "e's" of the beat.

**Pro-Tip**: Eighth Rests are frequently used to create syncopation and rhythmic interest. Accurately observing them is vital for a tight, precise groove. Practice counting "1 & 2 &" and ensuring the silence on the "&" is exactly half a beat long. This is also a common element in [[Economizing Notation]].
