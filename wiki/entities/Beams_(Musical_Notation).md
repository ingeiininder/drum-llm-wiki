# Beams (Musical Notation)
[[Beams (Musical Notation)]] are horizontal lines used in musical notation to connect the [[Stem (Musical Notation)]]s of multiple consecutive notes of equal or related rhythmic value (e.g., [[Eighth Note]]s, [[Sixteenth Note]]s, [[Thirty-Second Note]]s). They are a fundamental element of [[Rhythmic Notation]].

**Function:**
*   **Readability**: [[Beams (Musical Notation)]] replace individual [[Flags (Musical Notation)]] and serve to visually group notes, making rhythmic patterns much easier to read and comprehend, especially in fast or complex passages.
*   **Rhythmic Grouping**: They help to delineate rhythmic groupings within a beat or measure, clarifying the [[Beat Placement]] and subdivision.

**Types:**
The number of beams indicates the subdivision:
*   One beam for [[Eighth Note]]s.
*   Two beams for [[Sixteenth Note]]s (referred to as a [[Double Beam]]).
*   Three beams for thirty-second notes, and so on.

**Context:**
In the context of [[Beaming Sixteenth Notes]], beams are crucial for organizing groups of four [[Sixteenth Note]]s within a beat, often extending over [[Sixteenth Rest]]s to maintain visual consistency.

**Pro-Tip:**
When encountering beamed notes, mentally (or physically) emphasize the first note of each beamed group. This helps to reinforce the beat and subdivision, improving your [[Internal Clock (Drumming)]] and [[Rhythmic Accuracy]].
