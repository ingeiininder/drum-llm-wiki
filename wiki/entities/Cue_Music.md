# Cue (Music)

In musical performance, especially in [[Open Form (Music)|open forms]] like the [[12-Bar_Blues_Open_Form]], a "cue" is a critical element of [[Ensemble Performance|ensemble communication]].

## Definition

A cue is a notice or signal made from one band member to the rest of the band. Its purpose is to signal a specific event, such as a break, a rest, a change in section, or, most commonly, an ending.

## Usage in Charts

In a drum chart, you might see the text "on cue." This instruction tells the drummer to get the band's attention by playing a short [[Drum_Fill|fill]] or counting them into a specific section, often a [[Coda_Music|coda]] ending.

## Importance

Since open forms do not have a specific, pre-determined ending written into the structure, cues become essential for a cohesive performance. They ensure that all musicians transition or end together.

## Types of Cues

Cues can be:
*   **Verbal**: A count-off, a spoken instruction ("last time," "to the bridge").
*   **Visual**: A nod, a hand signal (e.g., patting the head for the [[Head_Music_Term|head]]), eye contact.
*   **Musical**: A specific [[Drum_Fill|fill]], a rhythmic figure, a melodic phrase played by a lead instrument.

## Pro-Tips for Drummers

*   **Be Attentive**: Always be aware of the [[Bandleader]] or other lead musicians for cues. Your role as the timekeeper often means you're responsible for executing or reinforcing cues.
*   **Clear Fills**: If you are responsible for giving a cue via a fill, make sure it's clear, concise, and leads directly into the next section or ending. Avoid overly complex or distracting fills.
*   **Count-Offs**: Practice giving clear and consistent count-offs when initiating a cue for an ending or new section.
*   **Anticipate**: In familiar forms, try to anticipate when a cue might be coming, allowing you to react promptly and smoothly.
