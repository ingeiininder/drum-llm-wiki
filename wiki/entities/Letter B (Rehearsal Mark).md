# Letter B (Rehearsal Mark)
"Letter B" is a common [[Rehearsal Mark]] used in musical notation to designate a specific section of a song. In this curriculum, [[Letter B (Rehearsal Mark)]] functions as the [[Chorus (Song Section)]] of the song, representing its main theme or "hook."

This section is structured as an [[Eight-Bar Phrase]] and incorporates [[First and Second Endings]] to facilitate repeats. Drummers will find that [[Letter B (Rehearsal Mark)]] features a [[Syncopated Snare Drum (B Section)]] hit on the "a" of 2, often accompanied by a [[Sloshy Hi-Hat]] sound to enhance the overall feel.

**Pro-Tip:** Rehearsal marks like Letter B are crucial for navigating charts efficiently. Always be aware of the musical context associated with each letter. For drummers, this means understanding how the groove, dynamics, and instrumentation should shift to define the character of the section. In a chorus, this often means a more open, driving feel compared to a verse or pre-chorus.
