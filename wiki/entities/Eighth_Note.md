# Eighth Note
An [[Eighth Note]] is a fundamental [[Rhythmic_Values]] in music. In [[4-4_Time_Signature]], an [[Eighth Note]] receives half a beat.

**Notation:**
A single [[Eighth Note]] consists of a filled-in [[Note Head]], a [[Stem (Musical Notation)]], and one [[Flags (Musical Notation)]]. When grouped with other eighth notes or shorter values, two or more [[Eighth Note]]s are connected by a single [[Beams (Musical Notation)]].

**Subdivision:**
An [[Eighth Note]] can be subdivided into two [[Sixteenth Note]]s.

**Importance:**
[[Eighth Note]]s are a very common subdivision, forming the basis for many grooves and rhythmic patterns across various musical genres. They are frequently used in conjunction with [[Eighth Rests]] to create rhythmic interest and syncopation. Understanding and accurately playing [[Eighth Note]]s is a core skill for any drummer.

**Pro-Tip:**
Practice [[Eighth Note]]s with a [[Metronome]], counting "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &." Ensure that each "and" falls precisely halfway between the numbered beats. This develops a solid internal sense of the half-beat subdivision, which is crucial for all faster rhythms.
