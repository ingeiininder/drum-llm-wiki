# Charley Wilcoxon Solo No. 2
Solo No. 2 is a foundational rudimental etude by [[Charley Wilcoxon]], focusing on the integration of [[Ruff]] and [[Roll]] rudiments within a 2/4 time signature.

### Structural Overview
- **Length**: 17 bars (including first and second endings).
- **Time Signature**: 2/4.
- **Key Rudiments**:
    - **Bar 1**: [[3-Stroke Ruff]] into a [[7-Stroke Ruff]].
    - **Bars 4-5**: [[Flam Tap]] (eighth and 16th note variations).
    - **Bar 7**: [[Flam Accent]].
    - **Bar 8**: [[7-Stroke Roll]] (starting on the "&" of beat 2).
    - **Bar 11**: [[Single Flam Paradiddle]].
    - **Bars 13-14**: [[Drag_Paradiddle_2]].
    - **Bars 16-17**: [[9-Stroke Roll]].

### Practice Strategy
1. **Check Patterns**: Utilize Ex 6.1 through 6.4 to isolate the [[Ruff]] and [[Roll]] articulations before attempting the full solo.
2. **Dynamics**: Initially practice the solo with no accents to master the rhythmic flow. Once comfortable, apply the written dynamic markings.
3. **Articulation**: Focus on the cleanliness of the "ruff" strokes to ensure they do not sound like "crushed" rolls.
