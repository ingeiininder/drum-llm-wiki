# Second Ending
A Second Ending is a musical notation device used in conjunction with repeat signs. It designates a section of music that is played only during the *second* (or subsequent) pass through a repeated section, after the [[First Ending]] has been skipped.

*   **Appearance**: It is typically indicated by a bracketed line above the staff, labeled with a "2."
*   **Function**: After playing the repeated section for the first time and taking the First Ending, the performer repeats back. On the second pass, they skip the First Ending and proceed directly to the music under the Second Ending. This usually leads to the next section of the piece or a [[Coda]].

When navigating with a [[D.S. al Coda]] directive, the Second Ending is often the destination after the return, especially in a [[D.S._al_Coda_No_Repeats]] scenario.

**Pro-Tip**: Be prepared to smoothly transition from the repeated section directly into the Second Ending. This requires anticipation and a clear understanding of the musical form. In a live setting, listening to the other musicians is key if the instructions are ambiguous.
