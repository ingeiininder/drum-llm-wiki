# Final Bar Line
The final bar line is a specific type of [[Bar Line]] used in [[Music Notation]] to unequivocally mark the conclusion of a piece of music. It is composed of two parallel vertical lines, with the second line being noticeably thicker than the first.

## Function
*   **Indicates Ending**: Its sole purpose is to confirm the absolute end of a musical composition.
*   **Visual Clarity**: The distinct appearance of the final bar line provides a clear visual signal to the performer that no further music is to be played.

It is the definitive symbol for the completion of a musical work, distinguishing it from [[Double Bar Line]]s which merely separate sections within a piece.
