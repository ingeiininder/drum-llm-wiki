# Syncopated Rhythms
**[[Syncopation|Syncopation]]** is a rhythmic device that involves placing [[Accents|accents]] or notes on normally weak beats or [[Upbeat]] (e.g., the "&s" and "ahs" between the main beats), rather than on the strong [[Downbeat]]. This creates rhythmic tension, interest, and a sense of "off-[[Beat|beat]]" feel.

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Emphasis on Weak Beats**: Notes or [[Accents|accents]] fall on beats that are typically unaccented.
*   **Anticipation or Delay**: Rhythms can anticipate a strong [[Beat|beat]] or delay a note that would normally fall on a strong [[Beat|beat]].
*   **Rhythmic Drive**: [[Syncopation|Syncopation]] often provides a driving, forward-moving feel to music.

**Example:**
Instead of a [[Quarter_Note]] falling squarely on [[Beat|beat]] 1, a syncopated rhythm might place an [[Eighth_Note]] on [[Beat|beat]] 1, followed by a quarter note on the "&" of 1, which then sustains over [[Beat|beat]] 2. This shifts the emphasis away from the expected downbeat.

**Pro-Tip for Drummers:** Developing a strong internal [[Metronome]] and a solid understanding of [[Subdivision]] is essential for playing syncopated rhythms accurately. Practice [[Counting_Out_Loud|counting out loud]], emphasizing the upbeats where the syncopated notes fall. This helps to internalize the "push and pull" of [[Syncopation|syncopation]] and execute it with precision and [[Groove|groove]]. [[Ties]] are frequently used to notate syncopated rhythms clearly.
