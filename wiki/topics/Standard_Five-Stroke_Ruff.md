# Standard [[Five-Stroke_Ruff|Five-Stroke Ruff]]
The [[Standard_Five-Stroke_Ruff]] is a foundational [[Drum_Rudiment]] where the first four alternating strokes are unaccented grace notes, and the fifth stroke is accented. This creates a distinct "ruff" sound leading into a strong [[Beat|beat]].

**Notation and Sticking:**
The basic sticking pattern is R L R L R or L R L R L, with the fifth stroke being the accent.
Example: `r l r l R` (where lowercase denotes unaccented, uppercase denotes accented).

**Practice Notes:**
*   **Note Values:** Practice this rudiment using various note values, such as [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] and [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]], to develop control at different tempos.
*   **Dynamic Contrast:** Emphasize the dynamic difference between the soft grace notes and the strong accent. The grace notes should be played very close to the drum head, almost brushing it, while the accent should come from a higher [[Stick_Height|stick height]].
*   **Evenness:** Ensure the four grace notes are evenly spaced and consistent in volume before the accent.

**Common Pitfalls:**
*   **Uneven Grace Notes:** The four unaccented strokes might not be even in volume or spacing.
*   **Weak Accent:** The fifth stroke might not be sufficiently accented, blurring the rudiment's characteristic sound.
*   **Lack of Flow:** The transition from grace notes to accent might feel stiff rather than fluid.

**Pro-Tip:**
Think of the grace notes as a quick "scramble" leading into a powerful "hit." Use a [[Metronome]] and start at slow tempos, gradually increasing speed while maintaining clarity and dynamic contrast. Experiment with different stick heights for the grace notes (very low) and the accent (higher) to exaggerate the dynamic spread.
