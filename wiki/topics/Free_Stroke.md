# Free Stroke
The [[Free Stroke]] is a fundamental drumming technique that is synonymous with the [[Gladstone Technique]]. Its core principle is to allow the [[Drumstick]] to naturally rebound after striking a drum or practice pad, returning to its original "up" position without any muscular effort to lift it.

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Natural Rebound:** The stick is "thrown" down, and the natural elasticity of the stick and the drumhead causes it to bounce back.
*   **Minimal Effort:** The drummer's hand and wrist facilitate this rebound rather than actively pulling the stick up.
*   **Efficiency:** By utilizing the natural physics of rebound, the [[Free Stroke]] conserves energy, allowing for greater speed, endurance, and fluidity.
*   **Foundation:** It serves as a foundational technique for developing [[Stick Control]], dynamic articulation, and is often contrasted with the [[Control Stroke]] or [[Down Stroke]].

**Practice Considerations:**
*   **Isolation:** Begin by practicing the stroke in isolation, allowing the stick to bounce freely while keeping fingers extended.
*   **Wrist Involvement:** Gradually incorporate the wrist, allowing it to follow the natural motion of the stick as it rebounds. The wrist should *not* be the primary force lifting the stick.
*   **Continuous Motion:** Practice continuous [[Free Stroke]]s, aiming for even, consistent rebound heights and dynamics.
*   **Dynamic Control:** The height of the rebound can be controlled to produce different dynamics and speeds. A higher rebound typically corresponds to a louder stroke and is used for slower rhythms (e.g., [[Eighth Notes]]), while a lower rebound is used for faster rhythms (e.g., [[Sixteenth Notes]]).

**Pro-Tip:** The feeling of a true [[Free Stroke]] is one of effortlessness. If you feel tension or are actively lifting the stick, you're likely fighting the natural rebound. Focus on relaxation and letting the stick do the work. This technique is crucial for developing a relaxed and powerful stroke.
