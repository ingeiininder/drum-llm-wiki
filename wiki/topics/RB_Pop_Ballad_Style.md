# R&B-Pop Ballad Style

The R&B-Pop Ballad is a genre defined by romantic narratives, slow tempos, and a specific rhythmic "pocket." This curriculum focuses on the **6/8 Ballad** style, popularized by artists such as Alicia Keys and John Mayer.

### Structural Form: Verse-[[Pre-Chorus|Pre-Chorus]]-Chorus
1.  **Verse (Section A)**: Focuses on the "storytelling" narrative. Usually features a [[Cross_Stick_Technique]] and a [[Tight_Hi-Hat]].
2.  **[[Pre-Chorus|Pre-Chorus]] (Section B)**: Also known as the "Rise" or "Climb." It builds tension leading into the hook.
3.  **Chorus (Section C)**: The "Hook" or most memorable line. [[Dynamics|Dynamics]] increase, often shifting to the [[Ride_Cymbal]] and a [[Center_Snare_Stroke]] (no rim shot).

### Key Rhythmic Element
The defining characteristic of this style is the [[Swung_16th_Feel]]. Even when the [[Chart|chart]] is written in straight [[16th_Notes|16th notes]], the performance must imply a triplet-based [[Subdivision|subdivision]] to achieve the correct "swung" feel.

---PRO-TIP---
In 6/8 ballads, the placement of the [[Bass_Drum]] on the "&" of 3 and the "&" of 6 is critical. If these notes are played "straight" rather than "swung," the [[Groove|groove]] will lose its soulful R&B character.
