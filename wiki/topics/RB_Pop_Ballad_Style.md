# R&B-Pop Ballad Style

The R&B-Pop Ballad is a genre defined by romantic narratives, slow tempos, and a specific rhythmic "pocket." This curriculum focuses on the **6/8 Ballad** style, popularized by artists such as Alicia Keys and John Mayer.

### Structural Form: Verse-Pre-Chorus-Chorus
1.  **Verse (Section A)**: Focuses on the "storytelling" narrative. Usually features a [[Cross-Stick Technique]] and a [[Tight Hi-Hat]].
2.  **Pre-Chorus (Section B)**: Also known as the "Rise" or "Climb." It builds tension leading into the hook.
3.  **Chorus (Section C)**: The "Hook" or most memorable line. Dynamics increase, often shifting to the [[Ride Cymbal]] and a [[Center Snare Stroke]] (no rim shot).

### Key Rhythmic Element
The defining characteristic of this style is the [[Swung 16th Feel]]. Even when the chart is written in straight 16th notes, the performance must imply a triplet-based subdivision to achieve the correct "swung" feel.

---PRO-TIP---
In 6/8 ballads, the placement of the [[Bass Drum]] on the "&" of 3 and the "&" of 6 is critical. If these notes are played "straight" rather than "swung," the groove will lose its soulful R&B character.
