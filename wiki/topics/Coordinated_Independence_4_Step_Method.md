# Coordinated Independence: 4-Step Method

This systematic approach is used to master complex rhythmic patterns between all four limbs. It distinguishes between **[[Jazz_Phrasing]]** (swung eighths) and **Even 16th-note [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]**.

### The 4-Step Practice Routine
For any given rhythmic phrase, practice in these four stages:
1.  **Step A**: [[Ride_Cymbal|Ride Cymbal]] pattern + [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] (2 & 4 for [[Jazz|Jazz]], 8ths for [[Funk|Funk]]) + Phrase played between **Snare and [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**.
2.  **Step B**: [[Ride_Cymbal|Ride Cymbal]] pattern + Phrase played between **Snare and [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] Foot**.
3.  **Step C**: No [[Ride_Cymbal|Ride Cymbal]]. Phrase played between **Snare and [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]** with [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] foot on a steady [[Pulse|pulse]].
4.  **Step D**: No [[Ride_Cymbal|Ride Cymbal]]. Phrase played between **Snare and [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] Foot**.

### [[Phrasing|Phrasing]] Styles
- **Swung Eighths**: The written [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] are interpreted with a "triplet feel" (long-short), typical of [[Jazz|Jazz]] and [[Blues|Blues]].
- **Even 16ths**: Notes are played with mathematical precision, typical of [[Rock|Rock]], [[Funk|Funk]], and Latin styles.

### Creative Application
Once the written exercises are mastered, students are encouraged to use these patterns as the basis for a **Drum Solo** or a unique **[[Groove|Groove]] Idea**.
