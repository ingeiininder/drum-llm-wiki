# Coordinated Independence: 4-Step Method

This systematic approach is used to master complex rhythmic patterns between all four limbs. It distinguishes between **[[Jazz Phrasing]]** (swung eighths) and **Even 16th-note Phrasing**.

### The 4-Step Practice Routine
For any given rhythmic phrase, practice in these four stages:
1.  **Step A**: Ride Cymbal pattern + Hi-Hat (2 & 4 for Jazz, 8ths for Funk) + Phrase played between **Snare and Bass Drum**.
2.  **Step B**: Ride Cymbal pattern + Phrase played between **Snare and Hi-Hat Foot**.
3.  **Step C**: No Ride Cymbal. Phrase played between **Snare and Bass Drum** with Hi-Hat foot on a steady pulse.
4.  **Step D**: No Ride Cymbal. Phrase played between **Snare and Hi-Hat Foot**.

### Phrasing Styles
- **Swung Eighths**: The written eighth notes are interpreted with a "triplet feel" (long-short), typical of Jazz and Blues.
- **Even 16ths**: Notes are played with mathematical precision, typical of Rock, Funk, and Latin styles.

### Creative Application
Once the written exercises are mastered, students are encouraged to use these patterns as the basis for a **Drum Solo** or a unique **Groove Idea**.
