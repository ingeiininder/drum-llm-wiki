# Syncopated [[Groove|Groove]] ([[Groove|Groove]] #2)
"Syncopated [[Groove|Groove]]" ([[Groove|Groove]] #2) refers to a `[[Drum_Groove]]` that emphasizes rhythms that fall *between* the main beats, creating a sense of rhythmic tension and release. This [[Groove|groove]], presented as part of Ex 7.12, likely features `[[Bass_Drum]]` and `[[Snare_Drum]]` patterns that intentionally deviate from a straightforward `[[Back-Beat]]` or `[[Quarter_Note]]` [[Pulse|pulse]].

`[[Syncopation]]` is a cornerstone of many modern styles, including `[[Funk]]`, `[[Jazz]]`, and `[[Modern_Rock]]`.

**Pro-Tip:** When playing syncopated grooves, `[[Subdividing]]` is crucial. Mentally (or vocally) counting the smallest `[[Note_Value]]` (e.g., `[[16th_Notes]]`) will help you place the off-[[Beat|beat]] rhythms accurately and maintain `[[Tempo]]`. Focus on the interplay between the different `[[Drum_Seat]]` voices, ensuring that the syncopated elements feel natural and contribute to the overall flow, rather than sounding disjointed.
