# ABC 24-Bar Form

The **ABC 24-Bar Form** is a specific structural template used in [[Pop-Rock Drumming]] to organize a song into three distinct sections, each defined by a particular rhythm, chord structure, and phrase length.

### Section Breakdown
1. **A Section (4 Bars)**: Often features a unique phrase or a variation of a [[Snare Drum Train Variations|Train Groove]].
2. **B Section (12 Bars)**: Typically a 6-bar phrase that is repeated. This section often introduces more syncopation and [[Ghost Notes]].
3. **C Section (8 Bars)**: Usually the "hook" or chorus section, often utilizing a [[Medium Hi-Hat]] or [[Ride Cymbal]] for a more open sound.

### Navigation
Charts using this form frequently employ "Road Map" symbols such as **D.S. al Coda** and specific rehearsal letters to guide the performer through the 24-bar cycle.
