# Double Strokes: Lead Method
The Double Strokes: Lead Method is an application of [[Double_Strokes]] where a specific hand consistently initiates the double stroke pattern. This method is crucial for developing independent strength and control in each hand.

*   **Concept**: When playing a continuous series of [[16th_Note]]s using double strokes, one hand (either right or left) is designated as the "lead" hand, always starting the double stroke sequence.
*   **Types**:
    *   **[[Right_Hand_Lead_Double_Strokes]]**: The pattern would be RR LL RR LL...
    *   **[[Left_Hand_Lead_Double_Strokes]]**: The pattern would be LL RR LL RR...

**Benefits**:
*   **Hand Strength and Endurance**: Practicing with a consistent lead hand helps build specific strength and endurance in that hand.
*   **Consistency**: Ensures a predictable [[Sticking_Patterns]] for specific rhythmic phrases.

**Pro-Tip**: It is essential to practice both the [[Right_Hand_Lead_Double_Strokes]] and [[Left_Hand_Lead_Double_Strokes]] variations equally. This ensures balanced development of both hands, preventing one hand from becoming significantly weaker or less controlled than the other. Focus on making the second stroke of each double as strong and clear as the first.
