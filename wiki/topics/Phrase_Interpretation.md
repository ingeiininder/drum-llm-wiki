# Phrase Interpretation
**Phrase Interpretation** (or [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]) is the art of deciding which notes in a sequence require a [[Set-up_Fill]] and which should be left alone. Musicality and common sense must supersede the technical ability to [[Fill|fill]] every gap.

### The "Less is More" Rule
A common mistake is setting up every single note in a group of consecutive [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]]. This "clouds" the [[Phrasing|phrasing]] and destroys the intended "staggered" effect of the rhythm.

### Guidelines for [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]
1.  **Identify the Lead-in**: In a string of upbeat notes, the first few notes often act as a lead-in to a larger "target" figure.
2.  **Groove Through**: Sometimes it is more effective to maintain the [[Groove]] through the first few notes of a phrase and only set up the final, most important note.
3.  **The "No [[Fill|Fill]]" Approach**: Occasionally, a composer wants the band to "kick in" together with no warning. In these cases, leaving the figure entirely alone creates the most dramatic impact.

### Pro-Tip
Look for "[[Fill|Fill]]" or "No [[Fill|Fill]]" indicators on the [[Chart|chart]]. If they aren't there, listen to the melody. If the melody is sparse, your fills can be more adventurous. If the melody is busy, keep your [[Phrasing|phrasing]] simple.
