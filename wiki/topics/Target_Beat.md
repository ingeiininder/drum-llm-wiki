# Target Beat
The **Target Beat** is the specific downbeat used to anchor and prepare an [[Ensemble Figures]] that occurs on an upbeat (the "&"). In professional chart reading, a drummer does not simply play the figure; they must "set it up" by producing a strong sound on the preceding downbeat to provide the ensemble with a rhythmic reference point.

### Common Target Beats
*   **The "&" of Beat 3**: The target beat is the **downbeat of 3**. A strong snare or bass drum hit on beat 3 serves as the [[Set-up Fill]] for the figure on the upbeat.
*   **The "&" of Beat 4**: The target beat is the **downbeat of 4**. 

### Pro-Tip
When executing a set-up, the volume of the target beat should be slightly lower than the [[Ensemble Figures]] itself to ensure the actual figure remains the primary focus. Think of the target beat as the "question" and the ensemble figure as the "answer."

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Rushing the Upbeat**: Drummers often rush the "&" after playing the target beat. Maintain strict [[Subdivision]] to ensure the ensemble can land together.
*   **Missing the Target**: Failing to play a strong downbeat before an upbeat figure can leave the band "floating" without a clear pulse to lock into.
