# Target [[Beat|Beat]]
The **Target [[Beat|Beat]]** is the specific downbeat used to anchor and prepare an [[Ensemble_Figures]] that occurs on an upbeat (the "&"). In professional [[Chart|chart]] reading, a drummer does not simply play the figure; they must "set it up" by producing a strong sound on the preceding downbeat to provide the ensemble with a rhythmic reference point.

### Common [[Target_Beats|Target Beats]]
*   **The "&" of [[Beat|Beat]] 3**: The target [[Beat|beat]] is the **downbeat of 3**. A strong snare or [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] hit on [[Beat|beat]] 3 serves as the [[Set-up_Fill]] for the figure on the upbeat.
*   **The "&" of [[Beat|Beat]] 4**: The target [[Beat|beat]] is the **downbeat of 4**. 

### Pro-Tip
When executing a set-up, the volume of the target [[Beat|beat]] should be slightly lower than the [[Ensemble_Figures]] itself to ensure the actual figure remains the primary focus. Think of the target [[Beat|beat]] as the "question" and the ensemble figure as the "answer."

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Rushing the Upbeat**: Drummers often rush the "&" after playing the target [[Beat|beat]]. Maintain strict [[Subdivision]] to ensure the ensemble can land together.
*   **Missing the Target**: Failing to play a strong downbeat before an upbeat figure can leave the band "floating" without a clear [[Pulse|pulse]] to lock into.
