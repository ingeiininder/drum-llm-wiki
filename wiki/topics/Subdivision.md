# Subdivision
"Subdivision" is the act of mentally or physically dividing a beat into smaller, equal parts. It is a fundamental concept in rhythm and crucial for accurate timing, especially when playing syncopated figures or complex rhythms. Common subdivisions include 8th notes, 16th notes, and triplets.

For example, in this curriculum, precise [[Subdivision]] of 16th notes is emphasized for properly placing [[Syncopated Bass Drum (A9)]] and [[Syncopated Snare Drum (B Section)]] hits on the "a" of 2.

**Pro-Tip:** Strong internal [[Subdivision]] is the bedrock of good time-keeping. Practice counting subdivisions out loud (e.g., "1-e-&-a, 2-e-&-a...") while playing a simple [[Groove]] or even just tapping your foot. This helps to internalize the rhythmic grid and ensures that all notes, especially those off the main beats, are placed accurately and consistently. A metronome set to subdivide can be an invaluable tool.
