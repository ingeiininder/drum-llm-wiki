# Standard Seven-Stroke Ruff
The [[Standard Seven-Stroke Ruff]] is a foundational [[Drum Rudiment]] where the first six alternating strokes are unaccented grace notes, and the seventh stroke is accented. This creates a longer, more sustained "ruff" sound leading into a strong beat compared to the [[Five-Stroke Ruff]].

**Notation and Sticking:**
The basic sticking pattern is R L R L R L R or L R L R L R L, with the seventh stroke being the accent.
Example: `r l r l r l R` (where lowercase denotes unaccented, uppercase denotes accented).

**Practice Notes:**
*   **Note Values:** Practice this rudiment using various note values, such as 16th-note triplets and quarter notes, to develop control at different tempos and rhythmic feels.
*   **Dynamic Contrast:** Emphasize the dynamic difference between the six soft grace notes and the strong accent. The grace notes should be played very close to the drum head, almost brushing it, while the accent should come from a higher stick height.
*   **Evenness:** Ensuring the six grace notes are evenly spaced and consistent in volume is paramount for a smooth "ruff" sound.

**Common Pitfalls:**
*   **Uneven Grace Notes:** The six unaccented strokes are prone to becoming uneven in volume or spacing, especially at faster tempos.
*   **Rushing:** Drummers often rush the grace notes to get to the accent.
*   **Weak Accent:** The seventh stroke might not be sufficiently accented, diminishing the rudiment's characteristic sound.

**Pro-Tip:**
Think of the grace notes as a mini [[Single Stroke Roll]] that smoothly transitions into the accent. Use a [[Metronome]] and start at very slow tempos, focusing on the individual articulation of each grace note before increasing speed. The goal is a seamless, flowing "roll" that resolves with a clear accent. This rudiment is excellent for developing endurance and precision in grace note execution.
