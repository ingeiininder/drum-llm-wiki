# Non-Descript Groove

A **Non-Descript Groove** is a conceptual approach to time-keeping where the drummer plays "time" without adhering to a repetitive rhythmic cycle or a standard "beat."

### Key Elements
- **Lack of Repetition**: Avoiding the standard 1-bar or 2-bar loops common in popular music.
- **Textural Focus**: Using tools like [[Buzz Rolls]], quiet mid-head strokes, and wash-oriented [[Hi-Hat]] openings to create a soundscape.
- **Improvised Structure**: The rhythm is constantly changing, reacting to the musical environment rather than driving it with a fixed pulse.

### Application in Fusion
In the track [[Angel_Track.md]], this technique is used during the first 16 bars to create an atmospheric intro. It requires high levels of [[Interdependence]] and a deep understanding of [[Linear Drumming]] to keep the pulse "felt" by the listener without it being explicitly stated.

**Pro-Tip**: To master this, practice playing over a click track while intentionally avoiding the "1" with your bass drum or snare for several measures at a time.
