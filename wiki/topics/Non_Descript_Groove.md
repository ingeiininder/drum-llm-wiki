# Non-Descript [[Groove|Groove]]

A **Non-Descript [[Groove|Groove]]** is a conceptual approach to time-keeping where the drummer plays "time" without adhering to a repetitive rhythmic cycle or a standard "[[Beat|beat]]."

### Key Elements
- **Lack of Repetition**: Avoiding the standard 1-bar or 2-bar loops common in popular music.
- **Textural Focus**: Using tools like [[Buzz_Rolls]], quiet mid-head strokes, and wash-oriented [[Hi-Hat]] openings to create a soundscape.
- **Improvised Structure**: The rhythm is constantly changing, reacting to the musical environment rather than driving it with a fixed [[Pulse|pulse]].

### Application in Fusion
In the track [[Angel_Track.md]], this technique is used during the first 16 bars to create an atmospheric intro. It requires high levels of [[Interdependence]] and a deep understanding of [[Linear_Drumming]] to keep the [[Pulse|pulse]] "felt" by the listener without it being explicitly stated.

**Pro-Tip**: To master this, practice playing over a click track while intentionally avoiding the "1" with your [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] or snare for several measures at a time.
