# Sixteenth Note Value
The [[Sixteenth Note Value]] refers to the duration of a [[Sixteenth Note]] relative to the [[Time Signature]] and other [[Rhythmic_Values]]s. Understanding this value is fundamental to comprehending [[Rhythmic Subdivision]].

**Key Equivalencies (in [[4-4_Time_Signature]]):**
*   A [[Sixteenth Note]] is equal to one-sixteenth of a whole note.
*   A [[Sixteenth Note]] is equal to one-fourth of a [[Quarter Note]] (which typically receives one beat).
*   A [[Sixteenth Note]] is equal to one-half of an [[Eighth Note]].

**Breakdown:**
*   One [[Quarter Note]] = Two [[Eighth Note]]s = Four [[Sixteenth Note]]s (all equal to one beat).
*   One [[Eighth Note]] = Two [[Sixteenth Note]]s.

**Concept:**
The term "sixteenth note" directly reflects its value: one-sixteenth of a whole note. Consequently, a maximum of sixteen [[Sixteenth Note]]s can fit into a single measure of [[4-4_Time_Signature]] (4 beats x 4 subdivisions per beat = 16).

**Pro-Tip:**
To truly internalize [[Sixteenth Note Value]], practice [[Note Value Breakdown]] exercises. Start with a slow [[Metronome]] and play a [[Quarter Note]], then two [[Eighth Note]]s, then four [[Sixteenth Note]]s, all within the same beat. This helps to feel the acceleration and subdivision accurately.
