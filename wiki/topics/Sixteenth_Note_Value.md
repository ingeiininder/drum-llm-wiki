# Sixteenth [[Note_Value|Note Value]]
The [[Sixteenth_Note_Value]] refers to the [[Duration|duration]] of a [[Sixteenth_Note]] relative to the [[Time_Signature]] and other [[Rhythmic_Values]]s. Understanding this value is fundamental to comprehending [[Rhythmic_Subdivision]].

**Key Equivalencies (in [[4-4_Time_Signature]]):**
*   A [[Sixteenth_Note]] is equal to one-sixteenth of a whole note.
*   A [[Sixteenth_Note]] is equal to one-fourth of a [[Quarter_Note]] (which typically receives one [[Beat|beat]]).
*   A [[Sixteenth_Note]] is equal to one-half of an [[Eighth_Note]].

**Breakdown:**
*   One [[Quarter_Note]] = Two [[Eighth_Note]]s = Four [[Sixteenth_Note]]s (all equal to one [[Beat|beat]]).
*   One [[Eighth_Note]] = Two [[Sixteenth_Note]]s.

**Concept:**
The term "sixteenth note" directly reflects its value: one-sixteenth of a whole note. Consequently, a maximum of sixteen [[Sixteenth_Note]]s can fit into a single [[Measure|measure]] of [[4-4_Time_Signature]] (4 beats x 4 subdivisions per [[Beat|beat]] = 16).

**Pro-Tip:**
To truly internalize [[Sixteenth_Note_Value]], practice [[Note_Value_Breakdown]] exercises. Start with a slow [[Metronome]] and play a [[Quarter_Note]], then two [[Eighth_Note]]s, then four [[Sixteenth_Note]]s, all within the same [[Beat|beat]]. This helps to feel the acceleration and [[Subdivision|subdivision]] accurately.
