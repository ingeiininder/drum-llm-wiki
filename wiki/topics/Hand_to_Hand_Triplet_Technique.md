# Hand-to-Hand Triplet Technique

The **Hand-to-Hand Triplet Technique** applies the principle of continuous alternation to an eighth-note triplet grid. Unlike 16th notes, triplets create a shifting lead between the hands.

### The Alternating Lead
Because there are three notes per beat, the lead hand alternates every quarter note:
- **Beats 1 and 3**: Lead with the **Right Hand**.
- **Beats 2 and 4**: Lead with the **Left Hand**.

### Drum Set Application
1. **Snare Foundation**: Maintain a continuous, quiet triplet flow on the snare drum.
2. **Orchestration**: Move the written rhythmic figures to the cymbals.
3. **Cymbal Distribution**: Play right-side figures on the right crash/ride and left-side figures on the left crash.
4. **Bass Drum Support**: Every cymbal hit must be reinforced by the [[Bass Drum]].
5. **Hi-Hat Foot**: Add quarter notes on the hi-hat with the left foot to anchor the pulse.

### Variations
- **Tom-Tom Orchestration**: Move the accents to the toms instead of cymbals.
- **Left-Hand Lead**: Reverse the entire pattern so that beats 1 and 3 lead with the left hand (LRL RLR).
