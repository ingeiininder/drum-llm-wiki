# Ruffs (Rudiment Family)
The [[Ruffs_Rudiment_Family]] is one of the five [[Rudiment Families]] and consists of rudiments characterized by a short single-stroke roll (or grace notes) that is generally played either before or after an [[Accents (Drumming)|accent]]. Ruffs add a subtle "drag" or "flurry" sound to a main note, enhancing its articulation and texture.

**Core Concept:**
A ruff typically involves two or more unaccented grace notes played quickly before or after a main accented note. The number of grace notes determines the specific ruff (e.g., 3-stroke, 4-stroke).

**Rudiments in this Family (Focus for this Unit):**
*   [[Three-Stroke Ruff]]
*   [[Four-Stroke Ruff]]
*   5-Stroke Ruff (not detailed in this OCR, but mentioned as part of the family)
*   7-Stroke Ruff (not detailed in this OCR, but mentioned as part of the family)

**Application:**
Ruffs are versatile and can be used to add embellishment, create rhythmic interest, or provide a sense of "push" or "pull" to a beat. They are common in marching band drumming, orchestral percussion, and various contemporary drumming styles.

**Pro-Tip:** The grace notes in a ruff should be played very softly and quickly, almost blending into the main accented note. The goal is to create a "brush" or "smear" effect rather than distinct individual notes for the grace notes. Focus on the timing and dynamic contrast between the grace notes and the accent.
