# Combined Subdivision [[Ensemble_Figures|Ensemble Figures]]

Advanced [[Drum_Reading]] involves navigating phrases that mix quarter, eighth, and [[16th_Notes|16th notes]], including dotted values. These "combined" figures require the drummer to shift between different [[Subdivision]] grids seamlessly.

### Complex Figure Examples
*   **Tied Figures**: A 16th note on the "ah" tied to an eighth note on the following downbeat. This creates a long [[Duration|duration]] that starts on a very short subdivision.
*   **Dotted Eighths on the "e"**: A common [[Syncopation|syncopation]] where the note starts on the second 16th note and lasts for three [[16th_Notes|16th notes]], ending on the "ah."

### Set-up Logic for Combined Figures
When multiple figures occur in one [[Measure|measure]], the drummer must provide "mini-fills" or "intermittent setups."
1.  **Identify the Gaps**: Look for the space between the figures.
2.  **[[Fill|Fill]] the Grid**: Use two [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] on the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] to bridge the gap and define the timing for the next figure.
3.  **Maintain the [[Pulse|Pulse]]**: Even when playing complex fills, the underlying quarter-note [[Pulse|pulse]] must remain audible or felt.

### Common Phrases in Modern Genres
1.  **The Double "e"**: Dotted [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] on the "e" of beats 3 and 4.
2.  **The "ah" Push**: [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] on the "ah" of beats 1 and 3 tied to [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]].
3.  **The [[Funk|Funk]] "ah"**: A dotted eighth on the "e" of [[Beat|beat]] 3 tied to another dotted eighth, followed by a 16th on the "ah" of [[Beat|beat]] 4.

**Pro-Tip**: Loop these common phrases and experiment with different [[Orchestration]] (snare vs. toms) to develop a "vocabulary" of setups.
