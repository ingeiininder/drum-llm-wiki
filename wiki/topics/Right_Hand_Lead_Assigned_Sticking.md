# Right Hand Lead (Assigned Sticking)
In [[Assigned Sticking]], the concept of Right Hand Lead dictates that when playing a continuous stream of [[16th Note]]s with [[Alternating_Hands_Sticking]], the right hand is "assigned" to play all the downbeats (1, 2, 3, 4) and the upbeats ("&"s).

*   **Pattern**: R L R L R L R L (for 1 e & a 2 e & a)
*   **Right Hand's Role**: The right hand strikes on the "1", "&", "2", "&", "3", "&", "4", "&" subdivisions.
*   **Visualization**: If you isolate just the right hand's part, it effectively plays a pattern of [[Eighth Note]]s. This can be a helpful mental tool for ensuring proper beat placement.

This consistent assignment helps drummers internalize the rhythm and develop [[Muscle_Memory_Drumming]] for accurate [[Drum Reading]] and performance.

**Pro-Tip**: Practice isolating the right-hand part to ensure it's strong and consistent. Even when the left hand is playing, the right hand should maintain its internal pulse on the assigned subdivisions. This creates a solid rhythmic foundation.
