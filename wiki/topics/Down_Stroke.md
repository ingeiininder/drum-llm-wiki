# Down Stroke
A [[Down_Stroke]] is a drumming technique that is synonymous with the [[Control_Stroke]]. It involves striking the drum and then immediately stopping the natural rebound of the [[Drumstick]] to leave it in a low resting position, typically 1-2 inches above the drumhead.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **[[Accents|Accents]]:** Often used to play accented notes, as the stroke starts from a higher position and delivers more force.
*   **Dynamic Control:** By stopping the rebound, the [[Down_Stroke]] allows for precise control over [[Dynamics|dynamics]], setting up subsequent softer notes (taps) from a low starting point.
*   **Transitions:** It serves as an effective transition from a [[Free_Stroke]] (where the stick rebounds fully) to a controlled, low position, preparing for the next phrase or dynamic shift.

**Mechanism:**
The stopping action is typically achieved by applying gentle pressure with the back two fingers (ring and pinky) immediately after the stick makes contact with the drumhead.

**Important Considerations:**
*   **Timing:** The stick should be caught *after* impact, not before. Catching it before impact ("choking the stick") will dampen the drum's sound and transfer unwanted energy to the wrist, potentially causing injury.
*   **Efficiency:** The goal is to use minimal effort. The stick's momentum carries it down, and the fingers provide a controlled stop.
*   **Relaxation:** Maintaining a relaxed grip and body is crucial to avoid tension and injury, especially when practicing transitions between [[Free_Stroke]]s and [[Down_Stroke]]s.

**Pro-Tip:** Practice the [[Down_Stroke]] in conjunction with [[Free_Stroke]]s to develop fluidity in dynamic changes. The ability to seamlessly execute both types of strokes is fundamental for expressive and technically proficient drumming.
