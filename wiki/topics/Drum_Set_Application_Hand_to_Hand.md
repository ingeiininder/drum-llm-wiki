# Drum Set Application: Hand-to-Hand
Once the [[Hand_to_Hand_Technique]] is mastered on a single surface, it is transferred to the full drum set to enhance mobility and creative [[Interpretation]].

### Orchestration Methods
1.  **Tom-Tom Distribution**: Play the continuous 16th-note flow on the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] (quietly). Move the accented [[Ensemble_Figures]] to the toms.
    *   **Right Hand [[Accents|Accents]]**: Floor Tom.
    *   **Left Hand [[Accents|Accents]]**: Rack Tom.
2.  **Cymbal/Bass Drum Support**: Place all written [[Accents|accents]] on the cymbals (Crash or Ride) while simultaneously playing the [[Bass_Drum]] to support the figure. The "filler" notes remain on the snare.
3.  **Flam Interpretation**: Play the written figures as [[Flams]] between the toms and [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] for a thicker texture.

### Foot Patterns
To solidify the independence, drummers should layer foot ostinatos under the hand-to-hand figures:
*   **Standard**: [[Quarter_Notes|Quarter notes]] on the Bass Drum or [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]].
*   **Advanced**: "2 and 4" on the [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] or "8th note" Bass Drum patterns.

### Mobility Benefits
This method eliminates "hitch" movements when moving between drums. Because the hands are already in a constant L-R-L-R motion, the drummer simply reaches for a different sound source on the assigned hand's turn.
