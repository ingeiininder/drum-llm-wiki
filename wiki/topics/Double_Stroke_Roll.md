# Double Stroke Roll
The [[Double Stroke Roll]] is a fundamental drumming rudiment consisting of two strokes played by each hand in succession (RRLL). It is essential for all drummers, forming the basis for many fills, solos, and orchestral passages.

## Core Goal
The primary objective in producing an open, clean, and evenly subdivided double stroke roll is to ensure both notes of each double sound equal in tone and dynamic. This is achieved through:
*   **Even Stick Heights**: Maintaining consistent stick heights for both strokes within each double.
*   **Relaxed Grip**: A relaxed [[Grip]] allows the sticks to "breathe" within the hand, applying just enough pressure in the [[Fulcrum]] to control the natural motion without micro-managing.

## Development Steps
1.  **Individual Stroke Mastery**: Develop control over individual strokes, including the [[Free Stroke]] (allowing natural rebound) and the [[Down Stroke]] (stopping rebound with back fingers).
2.  **Isolation**: Practice isolating each hand, focusing on the two individual strokes (e.g., RR or LL) using four-part counting for 16th-note partials (Ex 7.3).
3.  **Combination**: Put the isolated strokes together to create the full double stroke roll (Ex 7.4).
    *   **Crucial Point**: Do not accent the first note of each double. This is a common pitfall that leads to uneven rolls. Strive for equal dynamics.

## Accuracy
*   **Check Patterns**: Use [[Check Patterns]] (Ex 7.5) to ensure accuracy in note placement and evenness. These patterns often alternate between single strokes and double strokes to highlight any discrepancies.

### Pro-Tip
Think of the first stroke of a double as initiating the motion, and the second stroke as a controlled rebound or "bounce" that is guided by the fingers. The faster you play, the more the fingers take over the control of the second stroke. Practice with a metronome at various tempos, focusing on the quality and evenness of each stroke rather than just speed.
