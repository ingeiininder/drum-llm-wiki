# Center Snare Hit (Butt End)
The [[Center_Snare_Hit_Butt_End]] is a specific [[Snare_Drum_Articulation|snare drum articulation]] where the butt end (the thicker, heavier end) of the [[Drumstick|drumstick]] strikes the absolute center of the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] head. Crucially, this technique is executed *without* hitting the rim, distinguishing it from a [[Rimshot_Technique]].

This produces a dry, focused, and often lower-pitched sound with minimal overtones, offering a distinct contrast to other snare [[Articulations|articulations]].

**Pro-Tip:** This technique is excellent for achieving a deep, fundamental snare tone, often used in ballads, [[Jazz|jazz]], or when a less aggressive, more controlled sound is desired. It can also be used to create a "dead" or muted snare sound for specific rhythmic effects.
