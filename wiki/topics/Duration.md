# Duration

Duration refers to the specific length of time a note or rest is held. In musical notation, the choice of note value (e.g., quarter note, half note) symbolizes the relative duration of that sound. For drummers, understanding duration is critical for accurately matching the sustain and [[Phrasing|phrasing]] of other instruments in an ensemble.

## Core Concepts

- **Sustain**: Duration is closely linked to sustain, which is the period of time a sound remains audible before fading to silence. While wind and keyboard instruments have long sustain, drum components generally have shorter sustain.
- **Short vs. Long Notes**: 
    - **Short Notes**: Typically represented by [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] or [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] on "short-sounding" instruments like the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]].
    - **Long Notes**: Represented by half notes, whole notes, or tied notes. These are often orchestrated on instruments capable of duration, such as **Crash Cymbals**.

## Extending Duration

- **Ties**: A curved line that connects two notes of the same pitch to extend their sustain. For example, a whole note tied to another whole note results in an eight-[[Beat|beat]] duration.
- **Dots**: A dot placed after a note increases its value by one-half. It is an efficient way to display extended note values (e.g., a **[[Dotted_Quarter_Note|Dotted Quarter Note]]** lasts for 1.5 beats).

## Application on the Drum Set

- **Ensemble Figure Orchestration**: When a [[Chart|chart]] provides [[Ensemble_Figures|ensemble figures]] with long durations, the drummer should use "shouldered" crashes or other resonant surfaces to mimic the sustain of the band (e.g., matching a long guitar or brass chord).
- **[[Subdividing|Subdividing]] for Accuracy**: Since long notes last for over one [[Beat|beat]], drummers must subdivide mentally to ensure they "hold" the note for its full duration before moving to the next rhythmic figure.
- **[[Staccato|Staccato]] vs. [[Martellato|Martellato]]**: Articulations that modify duration. **[[Staccato|Staccato]]** indicates a note played as short as possible, while **[[Martellato|Martellato]]** indicates a strongly accented, short note.
