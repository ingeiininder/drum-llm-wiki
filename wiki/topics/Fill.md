# Fill
A `[[Fill]]` (or drum fill) is a short, often improvisational, rhythmic passage played by the drummer to transition between sections of a song, add excitement, or provide a momentary break from the main `[[Groove]]`. Fills are crucial for adding dynamic interest and helping to define the musical structure.

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Transitional**: Fills typically occur at the end of a phrase or section (e.g., at the end of an `[[Eight-Bar Phrase]]`) to lead into the next part of the song.
*   **Rhythmic Variety**: They often involve moving around the drum kit (snare, toms, cymbals) and employing different rhythmic subdivisions than the main groove.
*   **Dynamic Contrast**: Fills can range from subtle and understated to powerful and complex, depending on the musical context.

Examples of fills are indicated in `[[Ex 3.20]]` and `[[Ex 4.3]]`, demonstrating their role in both `[[One-Handed 16ths Play-Along]]` and `[[Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove]]` contexts.

**Pro-Tip**: A good fill serves the song. It should be well-timed, musically appropriate, and executed cleanly. Avoid overplaying; sometimes the simplest fill is the most effective. Practice a vocabulary of fills that you can adapt to different musical situations.
