# Repeat Sign
A repeat sign is a common symbol in [[Music Notation]] that indicates a section of music should be played again. It is a type of [[Bar Line]] characterized by two vertical dots placed to the left or right of the bar lines.

## Structure
*   **Beginning Repeat Sign**: Consists of a double bar line with two dots to its right. This marks the start of a section to be repeated. If no beginning repeat sign is present, the repetition is understood to start from the beginning of the piece.
*   **Ending Repeat Sign**: Consists of a double bar line with two dots to its left. This marks the end of a section to be repeated.

## Function
*   **Repetition**: When an ending repeat sign is encountered, the performer returns to the most recent beginning repeat sign (or the beginning of the piece if none is present) and plays the section again.
*   **Efficiency**: Repeat signs are a concise way to write out music that contains repeated sections, saving space and making scores easier to read by avoiding redundant notation.

## Related Concepts
*   [[First and Second Endings]]: Often used in conjunction with repeat signs to provide different endings for repeated sections.
*   [[D. C. al Fine]] and [[D. S. al Coda]]: Other navigational symbols that involve repetition or jumping to different sections.
