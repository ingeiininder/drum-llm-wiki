# Time Signature

A Time Signature is a visual indicator that dictates the [[Meter|meter]] of a piece of music and the corresponding note value assigned to each [[Beat|beat]]. It is placed to the right of the clef at the beginning of a [[Chart|chart]] and whenever the [[Meter|meter]] changes.

## Visual Structure

A time signature is displayed as two numbers stacked vertically:
- **Top Number**: Indicates how many beats are in each [[Measure|measure]].
- **Bottom Number**: Indicates the rhythmic value of each [[Beat|beat]] (e.g., **4** for a quarter note, **8** for an eighth note).

## Regular (Symmetrical) Meters

Most popular music is written in "common" or symmetrical time signatures, meaning individual measures can be evenly divided in half:

- **4/4 (Common Time)**: Four quarter-note beats per [[Measure|measure]]. Often indicated by the letter **C**.
- **2/4**: Two quarter-note beats per [[Measure|measure]]. Common in marches and samba.
- **3/4**: Three quarter-note beats. Though it has an "odd" top number, it is considered a symmetrical [[Meter|meter]]. In 3/4 time, drummers must be careful not to rush and ensure the downbeat of **1** is clearly defined.
- **6/8**: Six eighth-note beats per [[Measure|measure]]. It is divided into **two groups of three [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]]**. It is distinct from 3/4 (which divides six [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] into three groups of two).

## Odd (Asymmetrical) Meters

"Odd" meters refer to time signatures that cannot be evenly divided in half, often found in **Progressive Metal**, **Fusion**, and **Modern [[Jazz|Jazz]]**:

- **5/8**: Five eighth-note beats. Typically grouped as 2+3 or 3+2.
- **7/8**: Seven eighth-note beats. Common groupings include 2+2+3 or 3+2+2.
- **5/4 and 7/4**: Asymmetrical meters where the [[Beat|beat]] falls on the quarter note.

## Specialized Notations

- **[[Cut_Time|Cut Time]] (2/2)**: Indicated by the symbol **¢**. It is a 4/4 [[Measure|measure]] "cut" in half, meaning there are two beats per [[Measure|measure]] and the half-note gets the [[Beat|beat]]. This facilitates easier reading in fast tempos.
- **Mixed (Changing) Meters**: A [[Chart|chart]] with fluctuating time signatures (e.g., alternating between 4/4 and 3/4).
- **"12/8 Feel"**: Writing four groups of three eighth-note [[Triplets|triplets]] within a **4/4** time signature to achieve a 12/8 sound without changing the [[Meter|meter]].

## Drummer's Execution

Locking into an asymmetrical or [[Odd_Meter|odd meter]] [[Groove|groove]] requires precise physical coordination and mental counting. Professional drummers often use **Subdivision** (e.g., counting the eighth-note [[Pulse|pulse]] of a 7/8 [[Chart|chart]]) to ensure they can navigate in and out of [[Ensemble_Figures|ensemble figures]] with ease.
