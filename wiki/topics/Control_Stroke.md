# Control Stroke
A [[Control Stroke]], also known as a [[Down Stroke]], is a drumming technique where the natural rebound of the [[Drumstick]] is intentionally stopped or "denied" immediately after impact. This differs from the [[Free Stroke]] (or [[Gladstone Technique]]) where the stick is allowed to rebound fully.

**Key Characteristics:**
1.  **Denying Rebound:** After the stick strikes the drum, the back two fingers (typically the ring and pinky fingers) are used to gently stop the stick from rebounding fully.
2.  **Resting Position:** This action places the stick in a low resting position, typically 1-2 inches above the drumhead.
3.  **Purpose:** The [[Control Stroke]] is primarily used for playing accents or as a transition from a higher stroke to a lower, preparatory position for subsequent softer notes (taps). It allows for precise dynamic control and sets up future strokes efficiently.

**Common Pitfalls:**
*   **"Catching and Choking" Before Impact:** It's crucial to catch the stick *low and after the impact*. If you catch and choke the stick *before* it impacts the drum, it will not only stifle the drum's open sound but also transfer all the energy back into your wrist, leading to tension and potential injury.
*   **Excessive Force:** The stopping action should be gentle and controlled, not a forceful squeeze.

**General Rule:**
As a general rule, it's often preferred to keep your sticks in a "down position" when not actively using them. While an "up position" only allows for a downward stroke, a "down position" offers more options for subsequent strokes (e.g., an [[Up Stroke]] to prepare for an accent, or a [[Tap Stroke]] from the low position).

**Practice Considerations:**
*   Practice incorporating [[Control Stroke]]s as transitions within a series of [[Free Stroke]]s.
*   Focus on minimal effort and allowing the stick to do the work, with the fingers providing the gentle stop.
*   Maintain relaxation and breathe naturally. If tension arises, slow down or rest.

**Pro-Tip:** The [[Control Stroke]] is essential for dynamic contrast and setting up complex rhythmic patterns. Mastering the smooth transition between [[Free Stroke]]s and [[Control Stroke]]s is a hallmark of advanced [[Stick Control]].
