# Rests
While [[Note_Value|note heads]] indicate when to play a rhythm or produce a sound, **Rests** indicate when there should be silence. Rests are just as integral to musical expression and rhythm as notes, providing space, tension, and release. Similar to note heads, rests have specific symbols for varying durations.

## Main Rest Values (in 4/4 Time)
*   **[[Whole_Rest]]**: Equal to four beats (a whole [[Measure]] of silence).
*   **[[Half_Rest]]**: Equal to two beats (half a measure of silence).
*   **[[Quarter_Rest]]**: Equal to one beat (one beat of silence).
*   [[Eighth_Rest]]: Equal to half a beat of silence.
*   [[Sixteenth_Rest]]: Equal to a quarter of a beat of silence.

## Importance for Drummers
*   **[[Groove|Groove]] Definition**: Rests define the "space" in a [[Groove|groove]]. A drummer's ability to accurately place rests is crucial for creating a tight, musical, and impactful rhythm.
*   **[[Phrasing|Phrasing]] and [[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: Rests contribute significantly to musical [[Phrasing|phrasing]] and [[Dynamics|dynamics]]. A well-placed silence can be more powerful than a loud hit.
*   **Internal Clock**: Accurately observing rests requires a strong internal clock and precise timing, as the silence must be measured just as carefully as the sound.

## Pro-Tip for Drummers
*   **"Play" the Rests**: Think of rests not as "doing nothing," but as "playing silence." Maintain your internal [[Tempo]] and [[Subdivision]] even during rests.
*   **Body Awareness**: Sometimes, drummers will subtly move their foot or hand during a rest to maintain the internal [[Pulse|pulse]] without producing sound.
*   **Listen to the Space**: Pay attention to how rests are used in professional recordings. Notice how they create tension, anticipation, or a sense of release.
