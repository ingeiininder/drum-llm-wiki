# Coda Section (Unit 9)
The Coda section serves as the concluding part of the piece and is broken down into two distinct subsections, each presenting unique rhythmic and dynamic challenges.

**Structure and Challenges:**
*   **First Section (Starts on 3rd bar of Coda)**:
    *   This section features a two-bar pattern. It's important to remember that the first two bars of the coda are technically the last two bars of the preceding B section, so the "first section" of the coda proper begins after those.
    *   **Tricky Part**: A significant challenge in this section is the [[Snare/Crash Combination]] on beat 2 of the first measure of the pattern. This requires precise coordination and dynamic control to ensure both instruments speak clearly and simultaneously.
    *   **Counting**: It is crucial to "count in three" during this section. This implies a triple meter or a strong emphasis on a three-beat grouping, which might contrast with previous sections and requires careful internalizing of the pulse.
    *   **Instrumentation**: The [[Ride Cymbal]] is indicated, suggesting a lighter, more sustained texture compared to the hi-hat-driven sections.

**Performance Notes:**
*   **Coordination**: The snare/crash combination demands focused practice. Ensure your hands are perfectly synchronized for a unified sound.
*   **Meter Awareness**: Consciously counting in three will help you navigate the rhythmic feel and avoid getting lost, especially if the preceding sections were in a different meter or feel.
*   **Dynamic Control**: The ride cymbal should be played with sensitivity, allowing the snare/crash accents to cut through effectively.

This section often provides a sense of resolution or a final thematic statement, making its precise execution vital for the overall impact of the song.
