# Four-Stroke Ruff
The [[Four-Stroke Ruff]] is a drum rudiment that, like other ruffs, involves grace notes preceding a primary stroke. While less common than the [[Three-Stroke Ruff]] or [[Five-Stroke Ruff]], it is part of the broader family of rudiments. It is mentioned as a rudiment in Unit 4 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
rrLL (or LLrr for Left Hand Lead) - *Note: This is often interpreted as a [[Double Drag]] or a specific type of [[Four-Stroke Roll]] with an accent.*

**Breakdown:**
*   **rr:** Two quiet grace notes (drags) played with the right hand.
*   **LL:** Two quiet grace notes (drags) played with the left hand, often leading into an implied accent on the final stroke of a phrase or a subsequent note.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Embellishment:** Adds a specific texture and rhythmic feel, often used in marching or orchestral drumming.
*   **Coordination:** Helps develop control over multiple grace notes before a main beat.

**Pro-Tip:** The interpretation of the [[Four-Stroke Ruff]] can vary. Focus on making all four grace notes very even and quiet, creating a smooth "drag" sound. The challenge is to maintain control and evenness across the two hands for the grace notes.
