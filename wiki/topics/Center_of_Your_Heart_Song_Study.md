# Center of Your Heart: Song Study

"Center of Your Heart" is a ballad study featuring the playing of [[Todd_Sucherman]]. It focuses on emotional delivery, song shaping, and advanced [[Snare_Drum]] textures.

### Technical Nuances
*   **Playing the Click**: The tempo is deceptively difficult. Sucherman suggests playing slightly "behind the click" for most of the song to create a relaxed feel, then "burying the click" (aligning perfectly) during high points to add energy and urgency without actually rushing.
*   **Ghost Notes and Buzzes**: A signature element of this performance is the use of very quiet [[Ghost_Notes]] and light buzzes. Sucherman often lets the stick drop onto the head after a loud [[Back-Beat]], creating a nearly inaudible buzz that adds texture.
*   **Cymbal Work**: Use "oceanic, swiping crashes" on the second beats of the choruses to enhance the emotional content. Varying the [[Hi-Hat]] aperture (tightening and opening) between sections helps define the song's structure.

### Specific Figures
*   **Six-Stroke Roll Variation**: At bar 16, Sucherman utilizes a [[Six_Stroke_Roll]] that begins with a [[Flams]]. This is an unusual technical choice that provides a unique rhythmic weight to the fill.
