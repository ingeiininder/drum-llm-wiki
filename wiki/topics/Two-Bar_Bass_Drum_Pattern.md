# Two-Bar Bass Drum Pattern
A `[[Two-Bar Bass Drum Pattern]]` is a rhythmic sequence played on the `[[Bass Drum]]` that repeats every two measures. While shorter than a `[[Four-Bar Bass Drum Pattern]]`, it still offers significant rhythmic interest and is a common building block for many `[[Grooves]]`.

**Role in Drumming:**
*   **Repetition and Drive**: The shorter cycle creates a strong, often driving, repetitive feel that can be very effective in rock and pop music.
*   **Combination with Other Elements**: It is frequently combined with other rhythmic elements, such as `[[Hi-Hat Opening]]`, to create a distinct character for a section.
*   **Phrasing**: It helps define two-bar or `[[Eight-Bar Phrase]]` sections within a song structure.

`[[Ex 4.2]]` is an example of a 'B' section groove that incorporates a `[[Two-Bar Bass Drum Pattern]]` along with a `[[Hi-Hat Opening]]` within a `[[Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove]]`. This pattern is often used to create a contrasting feel to a preceding 'A' section.
