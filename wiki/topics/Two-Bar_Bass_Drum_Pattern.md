# Two-Bar Bass Drum Pattern
A `[[Two-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` is a rhythmic sequence played on the `[[Bass_Drum]]` that repeats every two measures. While shorter than a `[[Four-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]`, it still offers significant rhythmic interest and is a common building block for many `[[Grooves]]`.

**Role in Drumming:**
*   **Repetition and Drive**: The shorter cycle creates a strong, often driving, repetitive feel that can be very effective in [[Rock|rock]] and pop music.
*   **Combination with Other Elements**: It is frequently combined with other rhythmic elements, such as `[[Hi-Hat_Opening]]`, to create a distinct character for a section.
*   **[[Phrasing|Phrasing]]**: It helps define two-bar or `[[Eight-Bar_Phrase]]` sections within a song structure.

`[[Ex_4.2]]` is an example of a 'B' section [[Groove|groove]] that incorporates a `[[Two-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` along with a `[[Hi-Hat_Opening]]` within a `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]`. This pattern is often used to create a contrasting feel to a preceding 'A' section.
