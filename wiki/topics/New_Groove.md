# New [[Groove|Groove]]
A "New [[Groove|Groove]]" refers to a distinct rhythmic pattern or feel introduced at a specific point in a song, often to mark a change in [[Song_Structure]], mood, or energy. It provides a fresh rhythmic foundation that contrasts with previous sections.

In this curriculum, a new [[Groove|groove]] is mentioned as a characteristic of the [[Bridge_(Song_Section)]] within the [[Coda_(Song_Section)]], indicating a shift in the drum pattern and overall rhythmic feel.

**Pro-Tip:** Introducing a new [[Groove|groove]] is a powerful tool for drummers to shape the song's narrative. When a new [[Groove|groove]] is called for, consider how it should differ from the previous one in terms of [[Playing_Surface_Switch|instrumentation]], density, [[Dynamics|dynamics]], and rhythmic complexity. The goal is to create a clear and intentional shift that supports the song's arrangement.
