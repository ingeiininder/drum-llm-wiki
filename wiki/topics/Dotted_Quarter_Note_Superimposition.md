# Dotted Quarter Note Superimposition

A hallmark of [[Jazz]] phrasing is the use of dotted quarter notes to create a "three-over-four" feel. This involves playing a sequence of notes that each last 1.5 beats, causing the accents to shift relative to the downbeat.

### Phrasing Over the Bar Line
Because the dotted quarter note does not align with the 4/4 bar line every measure, the pattern "crosses the bar line." 
*   **Example**: A sequence starting on beat 1 will land on the "&" of 2, then beat 4, then the "&" of 1 in the following measure.
*   **Sticking**: Often practiced with the right hand on the [[Ride Cymbal]] (or hi-hat) and the left hand on the [[Snare Drum]] to emphasize the syncopation.

### Musical Effect
This creates a temporary sense of a different meter, adding tension and interest to a [[Drum Solo]] or a melodic accompaniment. It is a "sophisticated" rhythmic device common in [[Bossa Nova]] and modern Jazz.
