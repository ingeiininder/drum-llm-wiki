# [[Dotted_Quarter_Note|Dotted Quarter Note]] Superimposition

A hallmark of [[Jazz]] [[Phrasing|phrasing]] is the use of dotted [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] to create a "three-over-four" feel. This involves playing a sequence of notes that each last 1.5 beats, causing the [[Accents|accents]] to shift relative to the downbeat.

### [[Phrasing|Phrasing]] Over the [[Bar_Line|Bar Line]]
Because the [[Dotted_Quarter_Note|dotted quarter note]] does not align with the 4/4 [[Bar_Line|bar line]] every [[Measure|measure]], the pattern "crosses the [[Bar_Line|bar line]]." 
*   **Example**: A sequence starting on [[Beat|beat]] 1 will land on the "&" of 2, then [[Beat|beat]] 4, then the "&" of 1 in the following [[Measure|measure]].
*   **Sticking**: Often practiced with the right hand on the [[Ride_Cymbal]] (or [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]]) and the left hand on the [[Snare_Drum]] to emphasize the [[Syncopation|syncopation]].

### Musical Effect
This creates a temporary sense of a different [[Meter|meter]], adding tension and interest to a [[Drum_Solo]] or a melodic accompaniment. It is a "sophisticated" rhythmic device common in [[Bossa_Nova]] and modern Jazz.
