# [[Bossa_Nova|Bossa Nova]] [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]] Ostinato

The **[[Bossa_Nova|Bossa Nova]] [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]] Ostinato** is a syncopated Brazilian pattern used as a rhythmic anchor. In a pedagogical context, it serves as an excellent vehicle for developing high-level [[Independence]] and [[Coordination]].

### Pattern Variations
1. **Basic Eighth-Note Pattern**: A continuous flow of [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] with specific [[Accents|accents]] or a "double-tap" feel.
2. **Dotted-Eighth/Sixteenth Pattern**: A more authentic [[Syncopation|syncopation]] where the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] plays the dotted-eighth and sixteenth note figures (Ex 7.8), providing the characteristic "sway" of the [[Bossa_Nova|Bossa Nova]].

### Integration with [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]
To complete the foot ostinato, the [[Hi-Hat_Foot]] typically plays on beats 2 and 4 (the "chick" sound). In advanced variations, the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] may shift to all upbeats (the "&"s) to provide a counter-rhythm to the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]].

### Technical Focus
- **Non-Flamming**: Ensure the hands (reading the melody) do not flam against the constant foot pattern.
- **Dynamic Balance**: The [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] should be "feathered" or played with a consistent, controlled volume so it doesn't overpower the [[Snare_Drum]] or [[Ride_Cymbal]].
