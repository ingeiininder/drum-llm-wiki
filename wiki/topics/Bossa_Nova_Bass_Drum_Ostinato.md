# Bossa Nova Bass Drum Ostinato

The **Bossa Nova Bass Drum Ostinato** is a syncopated Brazilian pattern used as a rhythmic anchor. In a pedagogical context, it serves as an excellent vehicle for developing high-level [[Independence]] and [[Coordination]].

### Pattern Variations
1. **Basic Eighth-Note Pattern**: A continuous flow of eighth notes with specific accents or a "double-tap" feel.
2. **Dotted-Eighth/Sixteenth Pattern**: A more authentic syncopation where the bass drum plays the dotted-eighth and sixteenth note figures (Ex 7.8), providing the characteristic "sway" of the Bossa Nova.

### Integration with Hi-Hat
To complete the foot ostinato, the [[Hi-Hat Foot]] typically plays on beats 2 and 4 (the "chick" sound). In advanced variations, the hi-hat may shift to all upbeats (the "&"s) to provide a counter-rhythm to the bass drum.

### Technical Focus
- **Non-Flamming**: Ensure the hands (reading the melody) do not flam against the constant foot pattern.
- **Dynamic Balance**: The bass drum should be "feathered" or played with a consistent, controlled volume so it doesn't overpower the [[Snare Drum]] or [[Ride Cymbal]].
