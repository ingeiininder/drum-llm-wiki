# R&B-Pop Ballad
The **R&B-Pop Ballad** is a foundational style in contemporary drumming, characterized by a deep pocket and specific textural shifts between song sections. In this curriculum, the style is explored through the use of [[Swung_Sixteenth_Notes]] at a moderate [[Tempo|tempo]] (approximately 115 BPM).

### Performance Characteristics
*   **Feel**: The [[Groove|groove]] often utilizes a "swung" 16th-note feel, providing a loping, relaxed quality compared to straight 16ths.
*   **Instrumentation Transitions**:
    *   **Verses**: Typically utilize a [[Tight_Hi-Hat]] paired with either a [[Center_Snare]] hit or a [[Cross_Stick]] for a more intimate, percussive sound.
    *   **Choruses**: Often transition to the [[Ride_Cymbal]] and a full [[Center_Snare]] backbeat to increase the energy and "open up" the sound.
*   **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: Often involves a slight *ritardando* (slight rit.) at the conclusion of the arrangement to provide a professional finish.

### Pro-Tips
*   **Recording**: To develop a stylistically correct sound, record your practice sessions. Listen for the consistency of the "swing" in your [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] and the clarity of your [[Cross_Stick]] technique.
*   **[[Metronome|Metronome]] Training**: Practice these grooves at 85, 100, and 115 BPM to ensure the pocket remains stable across different subdivisions of the ballad [[Tempo|tempo]].
