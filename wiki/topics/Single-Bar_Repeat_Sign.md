# Single-Bar [[Repeat_Sign|Repeat Sign]]
A [[Single-Bar_Repeat_Sign]] is a common notation device used to indicate that the preceding [[Measure|measure]] should be repeated. It serves as a shorthand to create cleaner, more concise musical charts, especially in genres like [[Jazz|jazz]], pop, and [[Rock|rock]] where rhythmic patterns often repeat for extended periods.

**Symbol:**
The symbol for a single-bar repeat is a forward slash (/) with two dots, one above and one below the middle line of [[The_Staff|the staff]].

**Placement:**
This symbol sits directly in the middle of the [[Measure|measure]] it is replacing.

**Usage:**
Instead of writing out the same [[Measure|measure]] multiple times, a composer can write the initial [[Measure|measure]] and then use single-bar [[Repeat_Signs|repeat signs]] for subsequent repetitions. For example, if a [[Drum_Groove]] repeats for four measures, the first [[Measure|measure]] is written out, and the next three measures would contain a single-bar [[Repeat_Sign|repeat sign]].

**Professional Insight:**
While [[Single-Bar_Repeats|single-bar repeats]] simplify notation, drummers must remain vigilant. It's easy to lose track of how many times a [[Measure|measure]] needs to be repeated, especially in longer sections.
*   **Counting is Key:** Always count the number of measures you've played and how many repeats are indicated.
*   **Context Matters:** Pay attention to the musical context. If a repeated series carries over to a new line, the rhythm is often notated again at the beginning of the new line to reassure the player.
*   **Combining Repeats:** [[Single-Bar_Repeats|Single-bar repeats]] can also be used in conjunction with other [[Repeat_Signs]] like [[Start_Repeat_Sign]] and [[End_Repeat_Sign]] to create longer repeated sections, requiring careful tracking of the musical form.
