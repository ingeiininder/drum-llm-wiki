# [[Rock|Rock]] Shuffle
The [[Shuffle]] is a versatile rhythmic feel that can be applied across many musical styles. One particularly popular and enduring form is the **[[Rock|Rock]] Shuffle**.

**Characteristics and History:**
*   **Rhythmic Feel**: The [[Rock|rock]] shuffle is characterized by a triplet-based eighth-note feel, where the first and third parts of the triplet are played, creating a "long-short" swing feel. This gives it a distinctive "rumbling" quality.
*   **Historical Context**: This [[Groove|groove]] has been a staple in [[Rock|rock]] music since the 1960s, drawing heavily from [[Blues]] and [[R&B]] traditions.
*   **Modern Relevance**: Despite its long history, the [[Rock|rock]] shuffle remains highly relevant in contemporary music. Many modern [[Rock|rock]] bands, such as Smashing Pumpkins, Louis XIV, Muse, and Radiohead, continue to utilize this [[Groove|groove]] to great effect, demonstrating its timeless appeal and adaptability.

**Performance Notes:**
*   **Consistency**: The key to a great [[Rock|rock]] shuffle is consistency in the triplet feel. Each "long-short" pattern must be even and grooving.
*   **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: The shuffle can range from subtle and laid-back to powerful and driving. Pay attention to the dynamic requirements of the specific song.
*   **Instrumentation**: While often played on the [[Hi-Hat]] or [[Ride_Cymbal]], the [[Rock|rock]] shuffle can also be adapted to other surfaces, such as the [[Floor_Tom]], to create different textures and moods, as seen in the [[Intro_Rock_Shuffle_Unit_10]].

Mastering the [[Rock|rock]] shuffle provides a drummer with a powerful and expressive tool for a wide range of musical contexts.
