# Sloshy Hi-Hat

The **Sloshy Hi-Hat** (also known as the **[[Half-Open_Hi-Hat|Half-Open Hi-Hat]]**) is a specific articulation used to create a thick, aggressive, and sustained cymbal sound. It is a staple sound for choruses and high-energy sections in [[Rock|Rock]], Punk, and [[Funk|Funk]] genres.

## Execution and Sound Production
To achieve a "sloshy" sound, the drummer must find the right balance of foot pressure:
1.  **Foot Pressure**: Release the hi-hat pedal slightly so the top and bottom cymbals are barely touching and vibrating against each other.
2.  **Stick Placement**: Use the **shoulder** of the stick to strike the **edge** of both cymbals simultaneously.
3.  **Volume**: Typically performed at a high dynamic level (Fortissimo) to provide a broad, expansive feel to the [[Groove]].

## Musical Function
- **Energy Build**: Employed in more energetic sections like a [[Chorus_(Song_Section)]] to provide a "washy" wall of sound.
- **Dynamic Contrast**: Provides a stark contrast to a [[Tight_Hi-Hat_Sound]], which is used for more constrained sections like verses.

## Notation
In drum charts, this is often indicated by a **circle with a slash** (or a small 'x' with a circle) placed over the note.

---PITFALL---
**Do not use the tip of the stick** on the top of the cymbal when a "sloshy" sound is required. Striking with the tip results in a thin, clicking sound that lacks the necessary sustain and "wash" to support powerful sections like a [[Double_Time_Feel]].
