# Sloshy Hi-Hat

The **Sloshy Hi-Hat** (also known as Half-Open Hi-Hat) is an articulation used to create a thick, aggressive, and sustained cymbal sound.

### Execution
*   **Foot Pressure**: Release the hi-hat pedal slightly so the top and bottom cymbals are barely touching, allowing them to vibrate against each other.
*   **Stick Placement**: Use the **shoulder** of the stick to strike the edge of both cymbals simultaneously.
*   **Dynamics**: Typically performed at a **Fortissimo** (very loud) level.

### Notation
In drum charts, this is often indicated by a **circle with a slash (Ø)** placed over the first note of the section.

---PITFALL---
Do not play with the tip of the stick on the top of the cymbal when a "sloshy" sound is indicated. This will result in a "thin" sound that lacks the necessary energy to support a [[Double-Time Feel]].
