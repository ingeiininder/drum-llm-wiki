# Eighth-Note Triplets
[[Eighth-Note Triplets]] are the most common application of [[Triplets]] in drumming and are the rhythmic basis for [[Swing Feel]] and [[Shuffle Feel]]. They occur when a [[Quarter Note]] (or a single beat in common time) is divided into three equal [[Eighth Notes]].

**Concept in Detail:**
Normally, a [[Quarter Note]] is divided into two [[Eighth Notes]] (1-&). With an eighth-note triplet, the same quarter note duration is now filled by *three* eighth notes. This is indicated by a "3" placed above the beam connecting the three eighth notes.

*   **Quarter Note divided in two:** Two [[Eighth Notes]]
*   **Quarter Note divided in three:** Three [[Eighth-Note Triplets]]

In a 4/4 measure, there are twelve eighth-note triplets (3 triplets per beat x 4 beats = 12).

**Distinction from Eighth-Note Patterns:**
It's crucial to differentiate an eighth-note triplet from a simple grouping of three [[Eighth Notes]]. An eighth-note triplet *subdivides a single beat* into three equal parts. A pattern of three eighth notes might span a beat and a half, or be syncopated across beat divisions, but it doesn't necessarily imply a triplet feel unless notated as such. The "3" above the beam is the definitive indicator.

**Pro-Tip:** To truly internalize eighth-note triplets, practice them extensively with a [[Metronome]]. Try setting the metronome to click on each beat, then on each triplet partial. This helps develop the internal feel for the three-part division. Listen to a lot of [[Jazz]] and [[Blues]] music to hear how eighth-note triplets are used in context.
