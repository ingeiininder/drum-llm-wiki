# 90-Degree Stick Angle (German Grip)
In [[German Grip]], a key visual characteristic is the angle formed by the drumsticks. When both sticks are held with their tips positioned at the center of the drumhead, they should ideally meet at a 90-degree angle.

## Significance:
*   **Ergonomics**: This angle helps ensure that the hands and wrists are in an optimal position for the [[Wrist Technique (German Grip)]] to generate power efficiently.
*   **Consistency**: Maintaining this angle promotes symmetrical playing and consistent sound production between both hands.
*   **Indicator of Proper Form**: If the angle is significantly greater than 90 degrees, it often indicates that the elbows are flaring out too wide (a "chicken wing" posture), which can lead to tension and inefficiency. Conversely, an angle much less than 90 degrees might suggest the hands are too close together or the grip is too narrow.

## Pro-Tip:
Regularly check your stick angle using a mirror or video recording. Adjust your [[Elbow Position (German Grip)]] to ensure your elbows remain relatively close to your sides, facilitating the correct 90-degree angle and promoting relaxed, powerful strokes.
