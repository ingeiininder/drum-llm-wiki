# [[Accents|Accents]] (Drumming)
In drumming, an accent is a note played louder than the surrounding notes, causing it to stand out and contribute expression and color to a performance. [[Accents|Accents]] are a crucial part of a drummer's vocabulary, allowing for dynamic shaping and rhythmic emphasis.

## Purpose
*   **Expression**: [[Accents|Accents]] provide dynamic contrast, making musical phrases more engaging and expressive.
*   **Rhythmic Definition**: They can highlight specific beats or subdivisions, clarifying the rhythmic structure of a pattern.
*   **Color**: By varying the intensity of strokes, drummers can add different "colors" or textures to their playing.

## Execution
[[Accents|Accents]] are typically achieved by increasing the force of the stroke, often involving a larger [[Stick_Height|stick height]] or a more powerful motion (e.g., a full [[Down_Stroke]] or a strong [[Moeller_Technique]] stroke). The contrast between accented and unaccented notes (or [[Taps_(Drumming)]]) is what defines an accent.

## Relationship with Moeller Technique
One of the greatest advantages of learning the [[Moeller_Technique]] is its ability to offer drummers an effective way to play [[Accents|accents]] within a series of notes. The whipping motion of Moeller allows for powerful, controlled [[Accents|accents]] to be seamlessly integrated into continuous patterns, often preceded by a [[Moeller_Up_Stroke]] for efficiency.

## Pro-Tips
*   **Dynamic Range**: Practice creating a clear and consistent dynamic separation between accented and unaccented notes.
*   **Control**: Ensure that [[Accents|accents]] are controlled and precise, not merely loud. The goal is to make them stand out intentionally.
*   **Context**: The intensity of an accent should always be considered within the musical context. A "loud" accent in a soft passage will differ from a "loud" accent in a heavy [[Rock|rock]] [[Groove|groove]].
