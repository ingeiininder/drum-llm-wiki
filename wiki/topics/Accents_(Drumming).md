# Accents (Drumming)
In drumming, an accent is a note played louder than the surrounding notes, causing it to stand out and contribute expression and color to a performance. Accents are a crucial part of a drummer's vocabulary, allowing for dynamic shaping and rhythmic emphasis.

## Purpose
*   **Expression**: Accents provide dynamic contrast, making musical phrases more engaging and expressive.
*   **Rhythmic Definition**: They can highlight specific beats or subdivisions, clarifying the rhythmic structure of a pattern.
*   **Color**: By varying the intensity of strokes, drummers can add different "colors" or textures to their playing.

## Execution
Accents are typically achieved by increasing the force of the stroke, often involving a larger stick height or a more powerful motion (e.g., a full [[Down Stroke]] or a strong [[Moeller Technique]] stroke). The contrast between accented and unaccented notes (or [[Taps (Drumming)]]) is what defines an accent.

## Relationship with Moeller Technique
One of the greatest advantages of learning the [[Moeller Technique]] is its ability to offer drummers an effective way to play accents within a series of notes. The whipping motion of Moeller allows for powerful, controlled accents to be seamlessly integrated into continuous patterns, often preceded by a [[Moeller Up Stroke]] for efficiency.

## Pro-Tips
*   **Dynamic Range**: Practice creating a clear and consistent dynamic separation between accented and unaccented notes.
*   **Control**: Ensure that accents are controlled and precise, not merely loud. The goal is to make them stand out intentionally.
*   **Context**: The intensity of an accent should always be considered within the musical context. A "loud" accent in a soft passage will differ from a "loud" accent in a heavy rock groove.
