# Chunky Shouldered [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]

The **Chunky Shouldered [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]** is an articulation technique used to create a thick, forceful eighth-note [[Pulse|pulse]]. It is designed to make a [[Groove|groove]] feel "fatter" and give the illusion of a faster [[Tempo|tempo]] than the actual BPM.

### Execution
- **Grip**: Use a loose grip.
- **Stick Position**: Use the **shoulder** of the stick rather than the tip.
- **Contact**: Strike both the top and bottom [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] cymbals simultaneously.
- **Sound**: The goal is a "chunk-chunk" sound rather than a "chick-chick" sound.

### Common Pitfalls
- **Using the Tip**: Striking only the top of the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] with the tip can sound "corny" or "drum machine-like."
- **Shoulder/Tip Motion**: Do not use a rocking motion that [[Accents|accents]] the downbeats; this creates an "old-fashioned" feel. The "chunky" sound should be even across all [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]].
