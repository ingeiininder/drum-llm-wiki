# Four-Way Coordination: Double Permutations

This advanced study focuses on combining **"double" 16th-note snare accents** with **"double" 16th-note bass drum permutations**. This builds upon the foundational coordination established in previous units.

### Key Components
1.  **Snare Accents**: Moving pairs of 16th-note accents across the beat.
2.  **Bass Drum Permutations**: Specific double-stroke patterns on the kick drum that must align or syncopate against the snare accents.
3.  **Hi-Hat Ostinatos**: To develop true [[Four-Way Coordination]], apply eighth-note stepping hi-hat patterns (the "foot ostinato") throughout the exercises.

### Practice Methodology
*   **Four-Part Counting**: Use vocal counting (1-e-&-a) while playing to ensure mathematical accuracy of the permutations.
*   **Balance**: Focus on the physical balance on the throne. Stepping the hi-hat while playing double bass drum notes requires a stable core.

### Summary Exercises
The unit concludes with a summary exercise (Ex 3.18) that integrates multiple variations, testing the player's ability to switch between different accent and kick patterns seamlessly.
