# Four-Way Coordination: Double Permutations

This advanced study focuses on combining **"double" 16th-note snare [[Accents|accents]]** with **"double" 16th-note [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] permutations**. This builds upon the foundational coordination established in previous units.

### Key Components
1.  **Snare [[Accents|Accents]]**: Moving pairs of 16th-note [[Accents|accents]] across the [[Beat|beat]].
2.  **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]] Permutations**: Specific double-stroke patterns on the kick drum that must align or syncopate against the snare [[Accents|accents]].
3.  **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] Ostinatos**: To develop true [[Four-Way_Coordination]], apply eighth-note stepping [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] patterns (the "foot ostinato") throughout the exercises.

### Practice Methodology
*   **[[Four-Part_Counting|Four-Part Counting]]**: Use vocal counting (1-e-&-a) while playing to ensure mathematical accuracy of the permutations.
*   **Balance**: Focus on the physical balance on the throne. Stepping the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] while playing double [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] notes requires a stable core.

### Summary Exercises
The unit concludes with a summary exercise (Ex 3.18) that integrates multiple variations, testing the player's ability to switch between different accent and kick patterns seamlessly.
