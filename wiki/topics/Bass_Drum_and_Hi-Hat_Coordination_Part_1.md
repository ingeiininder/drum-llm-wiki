# Bass Drum and Hi-Hat Coordination Part 1
This chapter focuses on developing fundamental coordination between the [[Bass_Drum]] and [[Hi-Hat]] on the drumset. The exercises are designed to build a strong foundation for playing [[Linear_Rhythms]] and [[Layered_Rhythms]] with your feet.

## Core Concept
In typical drumset performance, the [[Hi-Hat]] often plays a steady [[Ostinato]] (a repeated rhythmic or melodic phrase) while various [[Bass_Drum]] rhythms are played against it. This creates a rhythmic interplay between the two feet.

## Essential Balance and Posture
As you engage both feet in these exercises, it's crucial to focus your balance on your seat. You can no longer rely on your feet for stability.
*   **Hands Position**: Allow your hands to rest at your sides, or hold your sticks in a resting position one to two inches above the [[Snare_Drum]] head.
*   **Avoid External Support**: Do not hold onto the drum or anything else to assist your balance. This practice defeats the purpose of developing proper independent balance, which is essential for advanced drumming.

## Hi-Hat Methods Introduced
This section introduces two primary methods for playing the hi-hat ostinato:

### 1. Heel-Toe Hi-Hat Method
This method is used to produce the hi-hat ostinato on beats 2 and 4, often creating a "chick" sound.
*   **Exercises**: Ex 6.22, Ex 6.23, Ex 6.24, Ex 6.25. (Specific notation for these exercises would be found in the curriculum.)

### 2. Heel-Up Hi-Hat Method
This method is used to produce an eighth-note hi-hat ostinato, providing a continuous rhythmic [[Pulse|pulse]].
*   **Exercises**: Ex 6.26, Ex 6.27, Ex 6.28, Ex 6.29. (Specific notation for these exercises would be found in the curriculum.)

### Pro-Tip
Developing strong core strength and maintaining a relaxed, upright posture will significantly aid in achieving independent foot control and balance. Think of your body as a stable anchor from which your limbs can move freely.
