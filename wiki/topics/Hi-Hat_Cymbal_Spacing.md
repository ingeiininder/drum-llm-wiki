# Hi-Hat Cymbal Spacing
[[Hi-Hat Cymbal Spacing]] refers to the distance between the top and bottom hi-hat cymbals when the pedal is in its open position. This spacing is a critical factor in achieving a tight, articulate "chick" sound, regardless of whether you are using the [[Heel/Toe Hi-Hat Method]] or the [[Heel-Up Hi-Hat Method]].

**Optimal Spacing:**
To generate a clear and resonant "chick" sound, the two hi-hat cymbals must have enough space to resonate and then close sharply. A minimum of **one inch** between the cymbals is necessary to produce this desired sound. (Refer to Ex. 4.19 in source material for an illustration of optimal spacing).

**Compromised Spacing:**
Anything less than one inch of spacing will compromise the sound quality. The "chick" may sound weak, choked, or "fluffy" because the cymbals do not have enough room to vibrate freely before closing. (Refer to Ex. 4.20 in source material for an illustration of compromised spacing).

**Adjusting Spacing:**
The hi-hat cymbal spacing is typically adjusted using the clutch that holds the top hi-hat cymbal. Loosening or tightening the clutch on the hi-hat rod allows you to raise or lower the top cymbal, thereby increasing or decreasing the gap.

**Pro-Tip:**
Always check your hi-hat spacing as part of your drum setup. Even a slight adjustment can significantly impact the quality of your hi-hat sound. Experiment with slightly more or less than one inch to find the sweet spot that best suits your cymbals and desired sound, but always ensure there's enough room for the cymbals to "breathe" and resonate. Proper spacing is fundamental to good [[Hi-Hat Technique]].
