# Inward (Inverted) Paradiddle
The [[Inward (Inverted) Paradiddle]] is one of the four basic [[Paradiddle]] variations, offering a distinct [[Sticking Patterns]] that challenges hand coordination and control. It is often referred to as the "Inverted Paradiddle" due to the placement of its double strokes.

**Sticking Pattern (Ex 8.2)**:
R L L R L R R L

**Pro-Tip**: Pay close attention to the transition between the double stroke and the subsequent single stroke. Ensure the double stroke maintains clarity and evenness, and that the following single stroke is executed with precision. This variation is excellent for developing control over [[Double Strokes]] in different contexts.
