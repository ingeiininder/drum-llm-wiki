# Pre-Chorus
The "Pre-Chorus" is a distinct section in a song's structure that serves as a transitional bridge, specifically designed to build anticipation and lead into the main [[Chorus_(Song_Section)]] (or "hook"). It often features a change in [[New_Harmonic_Structure]] or rhythmic intensity compared to the preceding [[Verse_(Song_Section)]], preparing the listener for the main theme.

In the context of this curriculum, a Pre-Chorus might be designated by a specific [[Rehearsal_Mark]] like [[A9_(Rehearsal_Mark)]], indicating its position within the song's form.

**Pro-Tip:** Drummers should approach the Pre-Chorus with an understanding of its transitional role. This is an excellent place to subtly increase energy, perhaps by altering [[Playing_Surface_Switch|playing surfaces]], introducing a slightly more active [[Bass_Drum]] pattern (like a [[Syncopated_Bass_Drum_(A9)]]), or varying [[Hi-Hat]] articulation, all while maintaining a clear connection to the upcoming chorus. The goal is to create a sense of forward motion without peaking too early.
