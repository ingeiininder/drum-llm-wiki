# Written-Out [[Drum_Fill|Drum Fill]]
A [[Written-Out_Drum_Fill]] occurs when a composer specifically writes out a [[Drum_Fill|drum fill]] in the musical [[Chart|chart]], indicating that a particular [[Fill|fill]] should be played at a specific time. This ensures the composer's exact musical intention is realized.

For example, in bar 16 of Letter B, a [[Fill|fill]] is written out between the [[Snare_Drum]]/[[floor_tom]] and the [[Bass_Drum]]. This [[Fill|fill]] is preceded by a [[Crash_Cymbal]] on [[Beat|beat]] one of [[Measure|measure]] 16, which serves as both an ending to the preceding [[Ensemble_Figures]] and the beginning of the [[Measure|measure]] 16 [[Fill|fill]].
