# Intro to Funk-Rock
[[Funk-Rock]] is a dynamic and influential subgenre that emerged from the evolution of [[Rock]] music. While early [[Rock 'n' Roll]] had strong roots in the [[Blues]], [[Blues-Rock]] as a distinct subgenre didn't fully develop until the late 1960s.

**Historical Context:**
*   **Blues-Rock Foundation:** [[Blues-Rock]] borrowed the concept of an [[Instrumental Combo]] and [[Loud Amplification]] from [[Rock 'n' Roll]]. This hybrid became immensely popular, leading to bands like Cream, Led Zeppelin, and ZZ Top, who were known for their louder sound and [[Riff]]-oriented compositions.
*   **Modern Tradition:** The curriculum highlights the modern tradition of [[Funk-Rock]] as exemplified by artists like the [[John Mayer Trio]] and [[Steve Jordan]], who are celebrated for their sophisticated grooves and stylistic versatility.

**Key Elements for Drummers:**
*   **Groove-Oriented:** [[Funk-Rock]] places a strong emphasis on the groove, often featuring intricate [[Bass Drum]] and [[Snare Drum]] patterns, and a solid [[Hi-Hat]] foundation.
*   **Syncopation:** Expect significant [[Syncopation]] in drum parts, drawing from both [[Funk]] and [[Rock]] influences.
*   **Dynamic Range:** The style often requires a broad dynamic range, from subtle [[Ghost Notes]] to powerful [[Rimshot]]s and [[Crash Cymbal]] accents.

**Pro-Tip:** To truly grasp [[Funk-Rock]], immerse yourself in the music of its pioneers and modern masters. Pay close attention to how drummers like [[Steve Jordan]] blend [[Funk]] rhythms with [[Rock]] power, focusing on feel, pocket, and the interaction with other instruments. [[Subdividing]] is essential for accurately executing the often complex rhythmic figures.
