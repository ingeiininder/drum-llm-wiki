# Quarter-Note Triplets
[[Quarter-Note Triplets]] occur when a [[Quarter Note]] occupies the duration of two [[Eighth-Note Triplet Partials]]. This means that within a triplet grouping, a quarter note will replace two consecutive eighth notes. This concept allows for greater rhythmic variety within triplet subdivisions, combining longer note values with the inherent triplet feel.

**Core Concept:**
Since two [[Eighth Notes]] equal one [[Quarter Note]], within a triplet bracket, two eighth-note triplets can be combined to form a quarter-note triplet. This is still contained within the "3" grouping, indicating its triplet context.

**Combinations with Eighth-Note Triplets and Rests:**
The text outlines several combinations:

1.  **Quarter-Note Triplet on First Partial (occupying 1st & 2nd), with Eighth-Note Triplet on Third Partial:**
    *   Counting: "1-trip-let" (where "1-trip" is the quarter note, and "let" is the eighth note).
2.  **Eighth-Note Triplet on First Partial, with Quarter-Note Triplet on Second Partial (occupying 2nd & 3rd):**
    *   Counting: "1-trip-let" (where "1" is the eighth note, and "trip-let" is the quarter note).
3.  **Quarter-Note Triplet on First Partial (occupying 1st & 2nd), with Eighth-Note Triplet Rest on Third Partial:**
    *   Counting: "1-trip-rest".
4.  **Eighth-Note Triplet Rest on First Partial, with Quarter-Note Triplet on Second Partial (occupying 2nd & 3rd):**
    *   Counting: "rest-trip-let".
5.  **Eighth-Note Triplet on First Partial, with Quarter-Note Triplet Rest on Second Partial (occupying 2nd & 3rd):**
    *   Counting: "1-rest-rest".
6.  **Quarter-Note Triplet Rest on First Partial (occupying 1st & 2nd), with Eighth-Note Triplet on Third Partial:**
    *   Counting: "rest-rest-let".

**Pro-Tip:** When playing instruments with no apparent note length (like [[Drums]]), the distinction between a quarter-note triplet and two consecutive eighth-note triplets might seem subtle. However, understanding the notation is crucial for accurate interpretation. Focus on the *duration* of the silence or sound. These combinations are vital for creating complex [[Groove]] and [[Fill]] in [[Swing]] and [[Shuffle]] contexts.
