# Reggae Balance of Sound

The "player's mix" for [[Reggae]] is a variation of the standard Pop-Rock balance, with specific adjustments for the snare and cross-stick.

### The Volume Hierarchy (Loudest to Softest)
1.  **Bass Drum & Snare Drum**: These are the primary drivers. The snare (or rimshot) should be very prominent.
2.  **Cross-Stick**: Unlike other genres where the cross-stick is a "soft" effect, in Reggae, it must be played with enough authority to match the snare drum's dynamic range.
3.  **Hi-Hat**: Placed significantly below the kick and snare in the mix, providing a crisp but secondary texture.

### Comparison to Pop-Rock
In Pop-Rock, the hi-hat is often quite loud to drive the eighth-note feel. In Reggae, the "weight" shifts almost entirely to the interaction between the kick and the snare/cross-stick on beat 3.
