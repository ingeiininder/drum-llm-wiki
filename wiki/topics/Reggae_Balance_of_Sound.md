# Reggae Balance of Sound

The "player's mix" for [[Reggae]] is a variation of the standard Pop-[[Rock|Rock]] balance, with specific adjustments for the snare and cross-stick.

### The Volume Hierarchy (Loudest to Softest)
1.  **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]] & [[Snare_Drum|Snare Drum]]**: These are the primary drivers. The snare (or [[Rimshot|rimshot]]) should be very prominent.
2.  **Cross-Stick**: Unlike other genres where the cross-stick is a "soft" effect, in Reggae, it must be played with enough authority to match the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]]'s dynamic range.
3.  **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: Placed significantly below the kick and snare in the mix, providing a crisp but secondary texture.

### Comparison to Pop-[[Rock|Rock]]
In Pop-[[Rock|Rock]], the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] is often quite loud to drive the eighth-note feel. In Reggae, the "weight" shifts almost entirely to the interaction between the kick and the snare/cross-stick on [[Beat|beat]] 3.
