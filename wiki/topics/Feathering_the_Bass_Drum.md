# Feathering the [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]
Feathering is the technique of playing the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] on straight [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] at an extremely quiet dynamic. It is designed to add "point" and "attack" to the upright bass's walking line without cluttering the frequency range.

### Execution
*   **Dynamic**: "Felt but not heard." If the listener can distinctly identify the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] as a separate sound, it is too loud.
*   **Beater**: Use a felt beater.
*   **Foot Position**: Choke up on the pedal (toes near the chain/strap).
*   **Stroke**: The beater should only travel 1 to 1.5 inches away from the head.

### Application
*   **When to Feather**: Typically during solo choruses to drive the band.
*   **When to Stop**: Often omitted during [[The_Head_(Jazz)]] or during very sparse drum breaks to allow the melody to breathe.

> [!CAUTION]
> Ensure the feathered [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] strikes exactly with the [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]] and [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]]. Any "flamming" between these limbs will destroy the [[Time_Feel|time feel]].
