# Feathering the Bass Drum
Feathering is the technique of playing the bass drum on straight quarter notes at an extremely quiet dynamic. It is designed to add "point" and "attack" to the upright bass's walking line without cluttering the frequency range.

### Execution
*   **Dynamic**: "Felt but not heard." If the listener can distinctly identify the bass drum as a separate sound, it is too loud.
*   **Beater**: Use a felt beater.
*   **Foot Position**: Choke up on the pedal (toes near the chain/strap).
*   **Stroke**: The beater should only travel 1 to 1.5 inches away from the head.

### Application
*   **When to Feather**: Typically during solo choruses to drive the band.
*   **When to Stop**: Often omitted during [[The Head (Jazz)]] or during very sparse drum breaks to allow the melody to breathe.

> [!CAUTION]
> Ensure the feathered bass drum strikes exactly with the ride cymbal and hi-hat. Any "flamming" between these limbs will destroy the time feel.
