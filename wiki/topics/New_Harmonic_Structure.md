# New Harmonic Structure
A "New Harmonic Structure" refers to a change in the underlying chord progression or key of a song at a specific point. This shift in harmony often accompanies changes in [[Song_Structure]], such as moving into a [[Bridge_(Song_Section)]] or [[Coda_(Song_Section)]], and contributes significantly to the song's emotional and musical development.

In this curriculum, a new harmonic structure is identified as a characteristic of the [[Bridge_(Song_Section)]] within the [[Coda_(Song_Section)]], indicating a fresh set of chords that provide contrast to the earlier parts of the song.

**Pro-Tip:** While drummers don't play chords, understanding harmonic changes is crucial. A new harmonic structure often implies a change in mood or intensity, which should inform your [[Groove]] and [[Dynamics|dynamics]]. Listen for these shifts and adjust your playing to support the new harmonic landscape, perhaps by altering [[Playing_Surface_Switch|playing surfaces]], [[Bass_Drum]] patterns, or overall density.
