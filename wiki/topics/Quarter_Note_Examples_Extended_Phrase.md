# Quarter Note Examples Extended Phrase
Similar to [[Half_Note_Examples_Extended_Phrase]], [[Dotted_Quarter_Note]]s can also form the basis of "over the bar" phrases. These are rhythmic patterns that extend across [[Measure|measure]] lines, creating a continuous flow and shifting rhythmic emphasis.

**How it Works:**
*   A [[Dotted_Quarter_Note]] is worth 1.5 [[Beat]]s (three [[Eighth_Note]]s).
*   When a pattern based on this 1.5-beat [[Duration|duration]] is repeated, it will naturally shift its starting point relative to the [[Downbeat]] of each [[Measure|measure]] in [[4-4_Time_Signature]].
*   This creates a sense of rhythmic displacement, where the pattern "travels" across the bar lines.

**Pro-Tip:** To effectively play these extended phrases, focus on the repeating dotted quarter note motif as a single rhythmic unit. Practice feeling the 1.5-beat [[Pulse|pulse]] and how it interacts with the 4/4 [[Meter|meter]]. This helps in maintaining the [[Groove|groove]] and preventing the rhythm from sounding rigid or fragmented at the bar lines.
