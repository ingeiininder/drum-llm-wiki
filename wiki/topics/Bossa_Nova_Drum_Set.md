# Bossa Nova (Drum Set)

The **Bossa Nova** is a Brazilian rhythm characterized by its sophisticated, understated elegance and straight-eighth subdivision. Unlike the aggressive nature of Samba, the Bossa Nova requires a lighter touch and a "cool" aesthetic.

### Core Components
1. **Ride Cymbal**: A light, even straight-eighth note pattern.
2. **Snare Drum**: Typically played as a [[Cross-Stick]] (side-stick) pattern. The pattern is often a two-bar phrase.
3. **Bass Drum**: Played with an "unburied" beater to produce a round, resonant "open" sound. The pattern usually emphasizes the "1" and the "(+) of 2."
4. **Hi-Hat**: Operated by the foot on beats 2 and 4.

### Performance Notes
- **Dynamic Balance**: The ride cymbal should be shimmering but not overpowering. The cross-stick must be consistent and "woody" in tone.
- **The "Unburied" Beater**: In Bossa Nova, allowing the bass drum beater to bounce off the head (rather than "burying" it) is essential for the authentic, warm Brazilian low-end.
