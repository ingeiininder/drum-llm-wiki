# Split Doubles

**Split Doubles** is a conceptual and technical approach to playing [[Double_Stroke_Roll]] patterns within [[Odd_Note_Groupings]] (such as [[Quintuplets]] or [[Septuplets]]). 

### Mechanics
In standard even groupings (like 16th notes), a double stroke (RR LL) usually aligns symmetrically with the beat. In a "Split Double" scenario:
- The metronome strike (the "downbeat") may fall on the *second* note of a double stroke rather than the first.
- This requires the player to maintain a perfectly even rhythmic grid while the sticking pattern "shifts" across the pulse.

### Exercise Context
In [[Wrist_Turn_Technique]] studies, split doubles are used to bridge the gap between [[Single_Stroke_Roll]] triplets and odd-numbered double stroke patterns.
- **Quintuplet Example**: RR LL R | L RR LL | R L RR L... (The double is "split" by the beat).
- **Septuplet Example**: Seven notes per beat using doubles requires a high degree of mental tracking to ensure the "split" doesn't cause a rhythmic "hiccup" or [[Flamming]].

### Professional Context
Mastering split doubles is critical for advanced [[Coordinated_Independence]] and contemporary jazz phrasing, where rudimental patterns are often superimposed over odd meters or polyrhythmic structures.
