# Shuffle

A Shuffle is a triplet-based [[Groove|groove]], typically written in 4/4 time, that serves as a cornerstone of Blues, Jazz, and Rock drumming. It is characterized by a "broken triplet feel" where the middle note of each eighth-note triplet is suppressed.

## Rhythmic Foundation

- **The Broken Triplet**: The basic shuffle pattern consists of the first and third notes of an eighth-note triplet. This creates a "long-short" loping rhythm.
- **Swing Connection**: The eighth-note triplet is the common rhythmic denominator for both swing and shuffle feels, providing the "inner clock" for the music.

## Major Shuffle Variations

- **Blues Shuffle**: The standard accompaniment for a **12-Bar Blues** (I-IV-V). It emphasizes a consistent, driving pocket and is often performed in an "open form" allowing for repeated choruses.
- **[[Rock_Shuffle|Rock Shuffle]]**: A "rumbling" [[Groove|groove]] used in rock music since the 1960s (e.g., **The Smashing Pumpkins**, **Muse**). 
    - **Orchestration**: Often uses the **Floor Tom** as a riding surface rather than the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] to create a darker, "ominous" vibe. 
    - **Technique**: Requires maintaining a medium volume on the floor tom while hitting the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] with a loud, aggressive backbeat on beats 2 and 4.
- **Half-Time Shuffle**: Developed by session legend **Bernard "Pretty" Purdie** (the "Purdie Shuffle"). 
    - In a **Standard Half-Time Shuffle**, the backbeat is placed only on **[[Beat|beat]] 3**, typically phrased with eighth-note triplets.
    - In a **Half-Time [[Shuffle_Feel|Shuffle Feel]]** (common in indie-pop), the backbeat stays on 2 and 4, but the underlying [[Pulse|pulse]] is phrased as **16th-note triplets**.
- **Jump Swing Shuffle**: An uptempo, energetic version often found in "Jump Swing" and New Orleans-style blues.
- **Shuffled [[Reggae|Reggae]]**: Fuses the Jamaican "one-drop" or "four-on-the-floor" rhythms with a [[Shuffle_Feel|shuffle feel]], often incorporating "anticipated" crash [[Accents|accents]] on the last triplet of [[Beat|beat]] 4.

## Technical Execution

- **Isolation**: Achieving a proper, steady shuffle is critical; if the drum shuffle is uneven, the entire ensemble's performance (especially the guitar) will suffer.
- **[[Ghost_Notes|Ghost Notes]]**: Advanced shuffles (like the Purdie Shuffle) rely heavily on [[Ghost_Notes|ghost notes]] played on the snare to [[Fill|fill]] in the "inner" triplet subdivisions.
- **Surface Switching**: Drummers often switch from [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] (during soft sections or the "Head") to the [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]] or floor tom (during solos or louder sections) to mark the form of a shuffle tune.
