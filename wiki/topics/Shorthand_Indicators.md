# Shorthand Indicators

**Shorthand indicators** are symbols or text instructions used in drum charts to keep the notation concise, particularly during "open" sections or solos. They allow the drummer more freedom to interact with the soloist without being tethered to specific rhythmic notation.

### Common Indicators
- **"Play 8"**: Instructs the drummer to play eight bars of time in the established feel.
- **"Open"**: Indicates a section of indeterminate length, usually continuing until a cue is given.
- **"On Cue"**: Signals that the next section will begin only when a physical or musical signal is provided by the bandleader or soloist.

### Performance Philosophy
Shorthand indicators allow the drummer to "relax" from the technical demands of [[Sight_Reading]] and focus on listening. A common pitfall for developing drummers is becoming fixated on the [[Chart|chart]] (the "eyes-down" trap) and failing to interact with the band. 

### Pro-Tip
When you see a shorthand indicator like "Guitar Solo - Play 8," use the first few bars to establish a solid foundation, then gradually increase your [[Interactivity]] based on the soloist's energy.
