# Five-Stroke Roll
The [[Five-Stroke Roll]] is a fundamental drum rudiment and one of the most common [[Double Stroke Roll]]s. It consists of two [[Double Stroke]]s followed by a single accented stroke, totaling five notes. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 7 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
RRLLR (or LLRRL for Left Hand Lead)

**Breakdown:**
*   **RR:** Two strokes with the right hand (a [[Double Stroke Technique]]).
*   **LL:** Two strokes with the left hand (a [[Double Stroke Technique]]).
*   **R:** A single, accented stroke with the right hand, which typically marks the end of the roll and leads into the next beat or phrase.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Roll Development:** Essential for developing control over the [[Double Stroke Technique]] and creating smooth, sustained sounds.
*   **Fills and Embellishments:** Widely used in drum fills, transitions, and to embellish rhythmic patterns across various musical styles.
*   **Dynamic Control:** Allows for dynamic shaping, with the initial double strokes often softer and the final single stroke accented.

**Pro-Tip:** The key to a good [[Five-Stroke Roll]] is to ensure that all four initial double strokes are even in volume and spacing, and that the final single stroke is clearly accented. Practice slowly, focusing on the rebound for the second stroke of each pair. Gradually increase tempo, aiming for a smooth, continuous sound rather than five distinct individual notes.
