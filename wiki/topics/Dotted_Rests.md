# Dotted Rests
Similar to [[Dotted_Notes]], [[Rests]] can also employ dots to extend their [[Duration]]. A **dotted rest** increases the [[Rest_Value]] by one-half of its original value. While [[Rests]] cannot utilize [[Ties]], dots provide an efficient way to represent longer periods of silence.

**Purpose of Dotted Rests:**
*   **Economize Notation**: Dotted rests can replace multiple shorter rests, simplifying the visual appearance of the music. For example, instead of an [[Eighth_Rest]] and a [[Sixteenth_Rest]] occupying the "1-e-&" of a [[Beat|beat]], a single [[Dotted_Eighth_Rest]] can be used.
*   **Extend Silence**: They allow for precise notation of silence that extends beyond standard rest values.

**How it Works:**
*   A dot adds 50% of the rest's original value.
*   *Example: A [[Half_Rest]] (2 beats) with a dot becomes a [[Dotted_Half_Rest]] (3 beats).*
*   *Example: A [[Quarter_Rest]] (1 [[Beat|beat]]) with a dot becomes a [[Dotted_Quarter_Rest]] (1.5 beats).*
*   *Example: An [[Eighth_Rest]] (0.5 beats) with a dot becomes a [[Dotted_Eighth_Rest]] (0.75 beats).*

**Pro-Tip:** For drummers, accurately observing dotted rests is just as important as playing dotted notes. The silence must be precisely timed and held for its full duration. Practice mentally [[Subdividing|subdividing]] through dotted rests to ensure you re-enter the rhythm at the exact correct moment. Using a [[Metronome]] is crucial for developing this precision.
