# Dotted Rests
Similar to [[Dotted Notes]], [[Rests]] can also employ dots to extend their [[Duration]]. A **dotted rest** increases the [[Rest Value]] by one-half of its original value. While [[Rests]] cannot utilize [[Ties]], dots provide an efficient way to represent longer periods of silence.

**Purpose of Dotted Rests:**
*   **Economize Notation**: Dotted rests can replace multiple shorter rests, simplifying the visual appearance of the music. For example, instead of an [[Eighth Rest]] and a [[Sixteenth Rest]] occupying the "1-e-&" of a beat, a single [[Dotted Eighth Rest]] can be used.
*   **Extend Silence**: They allow for precise notation of silence that extends beyond standard rest values.

**How it Works:**
*   A dot adds 50% of the rest's original value.
*   *Example: A [[Half Rest]] (2 beats) with a dot becomes a [[Dotted Half Rest]] (3 beats).*
*   *Example: A [[Quarter Rest]] (1 beat) with a dot becomes a [[Dotted Quarter Rest]] (1.5 beats).*
*   *Example: An [[Eighth Rest]] (0.5 beats) with a dot becomes a [[Dotted Eighth Rest]] (0.75 beats).*

**Pro-Tip:** For drummers, accurately observing dotted rests is just as important as playing dotted notes. The silence must be precisely timed and held for its full duration. Practice mentally subdividing through dotted rests to ensure you re-enter the rhythm at the exact correct moment. Using a [[Metronome]] is crucial for developing this precision.
