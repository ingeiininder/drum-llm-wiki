# Swung 16th [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]

**Swung 16th [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]** is a rhythmic feel where [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] are played with a "lilt" rather than as even divisions of the [[Beat|beat]]. While often notated as straight [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] for legibility, the performance follows a triplet-based grid.

### The Triplet Relationship
Mathematically, [[Swung_16th_Notes|swung 16th notes]] are derived from **sixteenth-note [[Triplets|triplets]]** (sextuplets). The "swung" feel is created by playing the first and third partials of each eighth-note triplet [[Subdivision|subdivision]], or more specifically, removing the second and fourth notes of a 16th-note triplet grouping.

### Practice Strategies
- **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]]**: Before counting in, mentally subdivide the swung 16ths to ensure the correct placement of syncopated notes, such as the "ah" of 4.
- **[[Metronome|Metronome]] Use**: Practice with the [[Metronome|metronome]] set to sixteenth-note [[Triplets|triplets]]. This prevents the [[Groove|groove]] from "straightening out" into standard [[16th_Notes|16th notes]], a common pitfall when tempos increase.
- **Counting**: Use the "1-trip-let-&-trip-let" vocalization to lock in the sextuplet [[Phrasing|phrasing]].

### Applications
This [[Phrasing|phrasing]] is foundational to [[Modern_Rock_Drumming]], [[Hip-Hop_Drumming]], and [[Soul-Funk_Drumming]].
