# Swung 16th Phrasing

**Swung 16th Phrasing** is a rhythmic feel where 16th notes are played with a "lilt" rather than as even divisions of the beat. While often notated as straight 16th notes for legibility, the performance follows a triplet-based grid.

### The Triplet Relationship
Mathematically, swung 16th notes are derived from **sixteenth-note triplets** (sextuplets). The "swung" feel is created by playing the first and third partials of each eighth-note triplet subdivision, or more specifically, removing the second and fourth notes of a 16th-note triplet grouping.

### Practice Strategies
- **Subdivision**: Before counting in, mentally subdivide the swung 16ths to ensure the correct placement of syncopated notes, such as the "ah" of 4.
- **Metronome Use**: Practice with the metronome set to sixteenth-note triplets. This prevents the groove from "straightening out" into standard 16th notes, a common pitfall when tempos increase.
- **Counting**: Use the "1-trip-let-&-trip-let" vocalization to lock in the sextuplet phrasing.

### Applications
This phrasing is foundational to [[Modern Rock Drumming]], [[Hip-Hop Drumming]], and [[Soul-Funk Drumming]].
