# Five-Stroke Roll [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]
In the context of [[New_Orleans_Second_Line_Drumming]] and [[Jazz|jazz]], the **Five-Stroke Roll** on [[Beat|beat]] 1 can be interpreted in two primary ways depending on the desired texture.

### 1. Open Rudimental Roll
- **Execution**: RRLL R or LL RR L.
- **Sound**: Each double stroke is distinct and measured. This provides a "marching" or "street" quality to the [[Groove|groove]].
- **Context**: Best used when a crisp, rhythmic articulation is required.

### 2. Closed (Buzz) Roll
- **Execution**: The initial four notes are pressed into the head to create a continuous "buzz" or "press" sound, resolving on the single stroke of [[Beat|beat]] 1.
- **Sound**: A smooth, sustained texture that bridges the gap between the previous [[Measure|measure]] and the new downbeat.
- **Context**: Often used in conjunction with a [[Flam_Buzz]] on the preceding [[Beat|beat]] to create a seamless flow of texture.

### Pro-Tip
When playing in a [[Swing_Eighth_Notes]] feel, ensure the roll doesn't "straighten out" the feel. The resolution of the roll must land exactly on the downbeat to anchor the ensemble.
