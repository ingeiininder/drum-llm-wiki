# Beaming [[Sixteenth_Notes|Sixteenth Notes]]
[[Beaming_Sixteenth_Notes]] is the practice of connecting consecutive [[Sixteenth_Note]]s (and sometimes [[Sixteenth_Rest]]s) with horizontal lines called [[Beams_(Musical_Notation)]] to improve readability and visually group notes within a [[Beat|beat]].

**Purpose:**
*   **Clarity**: Without beaming, a series of [[Sixteenth_Note]]s with individual [[Flags_(Musical_Notation)]] can appear cluttered and difficult to read quickly, especially in fast passages.
*   **Organization**: Beaming organizes notes into clear, digestible groups, typically groups of four [[Sixteenth_Note]]s within a single [[Beat|beat]] in [[4-4_Time_Signature]]. This visual grouping helps the performer instantly recognize the [[Subdivision|subdivision]] and [[Beat_Placement]].
*   **Performance Aid**: During [[Performance_(Music)]] or [[Sight_Reading]], beamed groups allow drummers to process rhythmic patterns more efficiently, contributing to greater [[Rhythmic_Accuracy]].

**Visual Aid:**
A group of four [[Sixteenth_Note]]s is connected by a [[Double_Beam]]. This visual cue immediately tells the musician that these are [[Sixteenth_Note]]s and that they belong to the same [[Beat|beat]]. Beams can also extend over [[Sixteenth_Rest]]s to maintain the visual grouping of four subdivisions per [[Beat|beat]], even when some are silent.

**Pro-Tip:**
When practicing, consciously look for the beamed groups rather than individual notes. This "chunking" of information is a key skill for efficient [[Rhythmic_Reading]] and helps in understanding the rhythmic flow within each [[Beat|beat]].
