# New Harmonic Structure
A "New Harmonic Structure" refers to a change in the underlying chord progression or key of a song at a specific point. This shift in harmony often accompanies changes in [[Song Structure]], such as moving into a [[Bridge (Song Section)]] or [[Coda (Song Section)]], and contributes significantly to the song's emotional and musical development.

In this curriculum, a new harmonic structure is identified as a characteristic of the [[Bridge (Song Section)]] within the [[Coda (Song Section)]], indicating a fresh set of chords that provide contrast to the earlier parts of the song.

**Pro-Tip:** While drummers don't play chords, understanding harmonic changes is crucial. A new harmonic structure often implies a change in mood or intensity, which should inform your [[Groove]] and dynamics. Listen for these shifts and adjust your playing to support the new harmonic landscape, perhaps by altering [[Playing Surface Switch|playing surfaces]], [[Bass Drum]] patterns, or overall density.
