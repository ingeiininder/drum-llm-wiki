# Rudiments

A Drum Rudiment is a fundamental rhythmic pattern or sticking sequence used to build physical coordination, speed, and musical vocabulary. Rudiments are the basic "building blocks" of drumming, serving the same function that scales serve for a pianist or guitarist.

## The Purpose of Rudiments

- **Technique Development**: Practicing rudiments is a highly effective way to gain the physical freedom necessary to express musical ideas effortlessly.
- **Rhythmic Organization**: Rudiments organize basic stickings and rhythms into standardized forms that every serious student should master.
- **Independence and Control**: They allow drummers to alternate leading hands and move around the drum set with precision.

## Key Rudimental Families

Drum rudiments are traditionally grouped into families. Major categories include:

### The Ruff Family
A **Ruff** is a short sequence of unaccented strokes leading to an accented note.
- **3-Stroke Ruff**: Two unaccented strokes followed by an accent.
- **4-, 5-, and 7-Stroke Ruffs**: Longer sequences that provide rhythmic density before an accent.
- **Inverted Ruffs**: A variation where the unaccented notes follow the initial accent.

### The [[Paradiddle|Paradiddle]] Family
The **[[Paradiddle|Paradiddle]]** is one of the most popular rudiments because it naturally alternates the leading hand while playing an even phrase.
- **[[Single_Paradiddle|Single Paradiddle]]**: **RLRR LRLL**.
- **[[Double_Paradiddle|Double Paradiddle]]**: **RLRLRR LRLRLL** (often played as eighth-note [[Triplets|triplets]]).
- **[[Paradiddle-diddle|Paradiddle-Diddle]]**: **RLRRLL** (common in modern [[Linear_Drumming|linear drumming]]).

### Rolls
- **[[Closed_Roll|Closed Roll]] (Buzz Roll)**: Continuous sound achieved by overlapping multiple-rebound strokes.
- **Open Roll ([[Double_Stroke_Roll|Double Stroke Roll]])**: Precise doubles (**RRLLRRLL**) used to sustain longer notes.

### Modern Rudiments
- **Hairta**: An alternating-stroke rudiment combining doubles and singles (**RLRR L** or **LRLL R**). It is a staple of contemporary fills and solo phrases.

## Professional Practice Methodology

- **[[Rudimental_Recall|Rudimental Recall]]**: Solidifying the brain-to-body signal is best achieved by **recalling** information after a break, rather than simple rote repetition.
- **The Performance Rule**: "Focus while you practice, but forget it when you perform." The rudiments should become a natural part of your vocabulary that surfaces without conscious thought during a song.
- **Tools**: Use **Subdivision** and a **[[Metronome|Metronome]]** while practicing to ensure every partial of the rudiment is even in timing and volume. Monitoring stick heights in a mirror helps maintain physical consistency.
