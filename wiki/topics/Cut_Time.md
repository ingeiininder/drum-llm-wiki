# Cut Time (2/2)

**Cut Time**, also known as *alla breve*, is a time signature represented by the symbol **₵** or **2/2**. It is a mathematical "cutting" of [[Common Time]] (4/4) in half.

### Key Characteristics
- **The Beat**: The **Half Note** receives the beat.
- **Count**: There are **two beats** per measure (Counted "1, 2" instead of "1, 2, 3, 4").
- **Feel**: It provides a "two-feel," which is essential for many up-tempo styles.

### Why Use Cut Time?
1. **Tempo Management**: At very fast tempos (e.g., 200 BPM), counting in 4/4 can cause a musician to tense up. By switching to Cut Time, the musician counts "1, 2" at 100 BPM, which encourages a more relaxed performance.
2. **Visual Clarity**: Cut Time minimizes the use of small notation. A passage that would require [[16th Notes]] in 2/4 or 4/4 can be written using [[8th Notes]] in Cut Time, making the chart easier to [[Sight Reading|sight-read]].

### Comparison: Cut Time vs. 4/4
A rhythm played in Cut Time at half-note = 100 BPM sounds identical to the same rhythm played in 4/4 at quarter-note = 200 BPM. The difference lies entirely in the **notation** and the **mental pulse** of the performer.

> [!PRO-TIP]
> When you see a "C" with a vertical line through it, immediately shift your internal pulse to the half-note. This is particularly common in [[Jazz]], [[Samba]], and [[Mambo]] charts.
