# Fill Development: Compression

**Compression** is a creative rhythmic device used to transform a "seed" idea into a more dense or complex vocabulary. By taking a standard rhythmic figure and "squeezing" it into a faster subdivision, a drummer can repackage familiar vocabulary to sound fresh and sophisticated.

### The Process of Transformation
1.  **The Seed Idea**: Start with a common 16th-note hand/foot combination (e.g., [[Ex 3.8]]).
2.  **Rhythmic Compression**: Take that same sequence and execute it as [[16th-Note Triplets]] or [[Sextuplets]].
3.  **Voice Isolation**: To ensure accuracy during compression, isolate the [[Bass Drum]] and [[Snare Drum]] voices. This reveals the underlying "skeleton" of the rhythm (e.g., alternating 8th notes in a triplet context).

### Pro-Tip: Maintaining Momentum
When transitioning between a 16th-note feel and a compressed triplet feel, focus on the placement of the snare drum on the downbeats. If the transition feels "sloppy," isolate the right-hand movement to verify it is maintaining a consistent [[Quarter-Note Triplet]] or 8th-note triplet pulse.

### Benefits
*   Enhances [[Soloing]] vocabulary without requiring "new" licks.
*   Improves rhythmic accuracy across different subdivisions.
*   Develops the ability to "repackage" ideas for the audience, maintaining interest through variation.
