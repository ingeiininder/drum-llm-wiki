# Tom Tom (Notation)
In [[Drum Set Notation Key]], [[Tom Tom]]s are notated on [[The Staff]] to represent their distinct pitches and roles in fills and rhythmic patterns.

## Standard Placement
*   Tom toms are represented by a standard solid black [[Note Head]].
*   Their placement varies depending on the number of toms in the setup and their relative pitches. Higher-pitched toms are placed higher on the staff, while lower-pitched toms are placed lower. For example:
    *   A high tom might be on the fourth space.
    *   A mid tom might be on the third line.
    *   A low tom might be on the second space.

## Function in Notation
Tom tom notation is primarily used for fills, melodic drumming, or to add rhythmic texture and emphasis within a groove. The ascending placement on the staff visually corresponds to the ascending pitches of the toms on the drum set.
