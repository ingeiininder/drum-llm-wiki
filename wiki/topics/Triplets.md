# Triplets
[[Triplets]] are a fundamental rhythmic concept where a note is divided into three equal parts, occupying the same rhythmic space normally taken by two notes of equal value. While triplets can be applied to any rhythmic value, the most common usage, especially in drumming, is with [[Eighth-Note Triplets]].

**Core Concept:**
Instead of a binary subdivision (dividing a beat into two, four, eight, etc. equal parts), triplets introduce a ternary subdivision (dividing a beat into three equal parts). This creates a distinct rhythmic feel that is crucial for styles like [[Swing Feel]] and [[Shuffle Feel]].

**Notation:**
Triplets are typically indicated by placing a number three (3) above or below the beam connecting the notes. Sometimes, a bracket is also used to clearly group the notes, especially when combining notes and rests within the triplet.

**Rhythmic Impact:**
Triplets introduce a "push-pull" or "lopsided" feel compared to straight subdivisions. This rhythmic tension and release are vital for creating musical interest and groove.

**Pro-Tip:** The key to playing triplets accurately is to *feel* the three equal divisions within the beat. Many drummers find it helpful to vocalize the subdivisions (e.g., "1-trip-let, 2-trip-let") or to practice with a [[Metronome]] set to click on the triplet subdivisions. Avoid rushing the last note of the triplet; ensure all three notes are perfectly even.
