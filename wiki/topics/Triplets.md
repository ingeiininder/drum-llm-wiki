# Triplets
[[Triplets]] are a fundamental rhythmic concept where a note is divided into three equal parts, occupying the same rhythmic space normally taken by two notes of equal value. While triplets can be applied to any rhythmic value, the most common usage, especially in drumming, is with [[Eighth_Note_Triplets]].

**Core Concept:**
Instead of a binary [[Subdivision|subdivision]] (dividing a [[Beat|beat]] into two, four, eight, etc. equal parts), triplets introduce a ternary [[Subdivision|subdivision]] (dividing a [[Beat|beat]] into three equal parts). This creates a distinct rhythmic feel that is crucial for styles like [[Swing_Feel]] and [[Shuffle_Feel]].

**Notation:**
Triplets are typically indicated by placing a number three (3) above or below the beam connecting the notes. Sometimes, a bracket is also used to clearly group the notes, especially when combining notes and [[Rests|rests]] within the triplet.

**Rhythmic Impact:**
Triplets introduce a "push-pull" or "lopsided" feel compared to straight subdivisions. This rhythmic tension and release are vital for creating musical interest and [[Groove|groove]].

**Pro-Tip:** The key to playing triplets accurately is to *feel* the three equal divisions within the [[Beat|beat]]. Many drummers find it helpful to vocalize the subdivisions (e.g., "1-trip-let, 2-trip-let") or to practice with a [[Metronome]] set to click on the triplet subdivisions. Avoid rushing the last note of the triplet; ensure all three notes are perfectly even.
