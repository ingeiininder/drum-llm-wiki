# Partial Beams

**Partial Beams** are a specific notation convention used in [[Sheet_Music]] to clearly indicate the [[Duration|duration]] of [[16th_Notes]] (or shorter values like 32nd notes) when they are not fully connected to other notes of the same value. They are particularly important when different note values are combined within a single [[Beat|beat]], especially in [[Shared_Beams]] groupings.

## Purpose

*   **Clarity**: Partial beams help to visually distinguish 16th notes from [[Eighth_Note]] when they are not part of a continuous group of 16th notes.
*   **Rhythmic Grouping**: They maintain the visual grouping of notes within a [[Beat|beat]], even when an [[Eighth_Note]] separates two 16th notes.
*   **Readability**: Correct use of partial beams significantly improves the readability of complex rhythmic figures.

## How They Work

*   **16th Notes**: A 16th note has two flags when written individually, or two beams when grouped. A partial beam is essentially one of these two beams.
*   **Direction**: Partial beams *always face inward* towards the center of the [[Beat|beat]] or the note they are connected to. They *never face outward*. This convention ensures consistency and clarity.

## Example (16th Note + Eighth Note + 16th Note)

A classic example where partial beams are essential is the [[1-e-a_Grouping_16th_Note_Eighth_Note_16th_Note]] (16th note, eighth note, 16th note within one [[Beat|beat]]).
*   The first 16th note will have a partial beam extending to the right, connecting to the main beam shared with the eighth note.
*   The final 16th note will have a partial beam extending to the left, connecting to the main beam shared with the eighth note.

This clearly shows that the first and last notes are 16th notes, while the middle note is an eighth note, all grouped within one [[Beat|beat]].

## Pro-Tip

When writing or reading rhythms with mixed subdivisions, pay close attention to partial beams. They are not just aesthetic; they are critical for correctly interpreting the [[Rhythmic_Values|rhythmic values]]. Incorrectly drawn partial beams (e.g., facing outward) can lead to misreading the rhythm.
