# [[Rehearsal_Letters|Rehearsal Letters]] II (Augmented [[Rehearsal_Letters|Rehearsal Letters]])
[[Rehearsal_Letters]] are standard notation tools used to mark different sections of a song, aiding in communication during rehearsals and performances. [[Rehearsal_Letters_II_Augmented_Rehearsal_Letters]] refers to the practice of augmenting these letters with a number (e.g., A2, B2, C2).

This augmentation denotes the reappearance of a previously established phrase or section (e.g., [[A_Section_Song_Structure]], [[B_Section_Song_Structure]]) but with a slight variation. These variations can occur in:
*   **Melody**: A change in the main melodic line.
*   **[[Groove|Groove]]**: A subtle alteration to the [[Drum_Set_Grooves]].
*   **[[Fill|Fill]] Length**: A change in the [[Duration|duration]] or complexity of a [[Drum_Fill]] (e.g., [[B_Section_Song_Structure]] might have a [[2-Beat_Fill]], while B2 has a [[4-Beat_Fill]] and additional fills).

**Pro-Tip**: When encountering augmented [[Rehearsal_Letters|rehearsal letters]], drummers must be particularly attentive to the specific changes indicated. These variations are often crucial to the song's dynamic and structural development. Always scan ahead in the [[Chart|chart]] to identify what makes A2 different from A, or B2 from B. This might involve changes in [[Articulations]], [[Dynamics]], or the placement and length of [[Drum_Fill]].
