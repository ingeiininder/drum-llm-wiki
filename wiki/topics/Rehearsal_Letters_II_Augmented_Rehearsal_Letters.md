# Rehearsal Letters II (Augmented Rehearsal Letters)
[[Rehearsal Letters]] are standard notation tools used to mark different sections of a song, aiding in communication during rehearsals and performances. [[Rehearsal Letters II (Augmented Rehearsal Letters)]] refers to the practice of augmenting these letters with a number (e.g., A2, B2, C2).

This augmentation denotes the reappearance of a previously established phrase or section (e.g., [[A Section (Song Structure)]], [[B Section (Song Structure)]]) but with a slight variation. These variations can occur in:
*   **Melody**: A change in the main melodic line.
*   **Groove**: A subtle alteration to the [[Drum Set Grooves]].
*   **Fill Length**: A change in the duration or complexity of a [[Drum Fills]] (e.g., [[B Section (Song Structure)]] might have a [[2-Beat Fill]], while B2 has a [[4-Beat Fill]] and additional fills).

**Pro-Tip**: When encountering augmented rehearsal letters, drummers must be particularly attentive to the specific changes indicated. These variations are often crucial to the song's dynamic and structural development. Always scan ahead in the chart to identify what makes A2 different from A, or B2 from B. This might involve changes in [[Articulation]], [[Dynamics]], or the placement and length of [[Drum Fills]].
