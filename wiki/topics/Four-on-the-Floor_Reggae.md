# Four-on-the-Floor [[Reggae|Reggae]]

In contrast to the [[One-Drop_Groove]], the **Four-on-the-Floor** [[Reggae|reggae]] [[Groove|groove]] features a [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] [[Pulse|pulse]] on every quarter note (1, 2, 3, 4).

### Characteristics
*   **Drive**: This pattern provides an insistent, steady drive often found in "steppers" style [[Reggae|reggae]] (e.g., Bob Marley's "Exodus").
*   **Snare Articulation**: While the cross-stick is still common, drummers often switch to a sharp **[[Rimshot|rimshot]]** on [[Beat|beat]] 3 to match the increased energy of the four-on-the-floor kick.
*   **Transitions**: Moving from a One-Drop to Four-on-the-Floor requires careful attention to the quarter-note [[Pulse|pulse]] to prevent [[Tempo|tempo]] shifts.

### Usage
This [[Groove|groove]] is frequently used in modern [[Reggae|reggae]]-[[Rock|rock]] (e.g., Sublime) and during high-energy solo sections or vamps.
