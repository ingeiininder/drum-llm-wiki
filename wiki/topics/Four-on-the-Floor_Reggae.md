# Four-on-the-Floor Reggae

In contrast to the [[One-Drop_Groove]], the **Four-on-the-Floor** reggae groove features a bass drum pulse on every quarter note (1, 2, 3, 4).

### Characteristics
*   **Drive**: This pattern provides an insistent, steady drive often found in "steppers" style reggae (e.g., Bob Marley's "Exodus").
*   **Snare Articulation**: While the cross-stick is still common, drummers often switch to a sharp **rimshot** on beat 3 to match the increased energy of the four-on-the-floor kick.
*   **Transitions**: Moving from a One-Drop to Four-on-the-Floor requires careful attention to the quarter-note pulse to prevent tempo shifts.

### Usage
This groove is frequently used in modern reggae-rock (e.g., Sublime) and during high-energy solo sections or vamps.
