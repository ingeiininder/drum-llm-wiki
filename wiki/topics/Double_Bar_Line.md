# Double Bar Line
A double bar line is a type of [[Bar Line]] used in [[Music Notation]] to visually separate distinct sections of music within a single piece. It consists of two parallel vertical lines.

## Function
*   **Section Separation**: The primary purpose of a double bar line is to indicate a change in musical form or structure. This could signify:
    *   A change in [[Tempo]].
    *   A change in [[Time Signature]].
    *   A change in [[Key Signature]].
    *   The end of a major section (e.g., verse, chorus, bridge).
*   **Visual Aid**: It serves as a clear visual cue for the performer, helping them to keep their place and anticipate upcoming changes or new musical ideas while reading the score.

Unlike a single bar line which simply divides [[Measure (Music Notation)|measures]], the double bar line implies a more significant structural division.
