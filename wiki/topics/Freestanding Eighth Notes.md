# Freestanding Eighth Notes
[[Freestanding Eighth Notes]] refer to [[Eighth Notes]] that are not connected by a [[Beams (Notation)]]. Instead, each individual eighth note is notated with its own [[Flags (Notation)]].

This notation is typically used when:
1.  A single eighth note appears by itself.
2.  Eighth notes are interspersed with [[Eighth Rests]] or other note values in a way that makes beaming impractical or less clear.
3.  The composer intends to emphasize the individual attack of each note rather than a continuous phrase.

**Contrast with Beamed Notes:**
While [[Beams (Notation)]] group notes for visual clarity and to indicate rhythmic phrasing, freestanding notes with flags highlight individual note placements. Both forms represent the same rhythmic value.

**Professional Insight:**
When encountering a mix of flagged and beamed eighth notes, it's crucial to maintain consistent [[Subdivision]]. The visual difference should not alter your internal counting or the rhythmic accuracy of your playing. Always count "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &" to ensure precise placement of both flagged and beamed notes.
