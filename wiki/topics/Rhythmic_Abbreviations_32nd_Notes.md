# Rhythmic Abbreviations (32nd Notes)

In professional drum charts and hand-written scores, composers often use **rhythmic abbreviations** to simplify the appearance of [[32nd Note Notation]]. This prevents the "clutter" of triple beams.

### The Slash System
Instead of writing out triple beams, a composer may write [[16th Note]] groupings and add a **single slash** to each individual stem. 
*   A 16th note (two beams) + one slash = 3 32nd-note beams.
*   If a note has three slashes on the stem, the performer must determine based on context if the composer intends for a [[Rolls|Roll]] (open or closed) or measured 32nd notes.

### Interpretation
These abbreviations sound identical to fully notated 32nd notes. As a performer, you must be familiar with these variances to interpret [[Ensemble Figures]] correctly.
