# Playing Surface Switch
A "Playing Surface Switch" refers to the act of changing the primary drum or cymbal being used for time-keeping or [[Groove|groove]] articulation within a song. This technique is a fundamental aspect of drum set orchestration, used to create dynamic contrast, define different song sections, and alter the overall texture of the drum part.

Common examples include switching from:
*   [[Hi-Hat]] to [[Ride_Cymbal]] (as seen in the [[Bridge_(Song_Section)]] within the [[Coda_(Song_Section)]] in this curriculum).
*   Hi-hat to [[Open_Hi-Hat]].
*   Ride cymbal to [[Floor_Tom]] or [[Snare_Drum]] rim.

This switch often coincides with changes in [[Song_Structure]] (e.g., moving from a [[Verse_(Song_Section)]] to a [[Chorus_(Song_Section)]] or entering a [[Bridge_(Song_Section)]]), [[New_Groove]]s, or shifts in [[New_Harmonic_Structure]].

**Pro-Tip:** Masterful drummers use playing surface switches intentionally to serve the song. Practice smooth transitions between surfaces, ensuring that the [[New_Groove|new groove]] starts cleanly and with the desired feel. Consider the dynamic and timbral impact of each surface and how it contributes to the emotional arc of the music.
