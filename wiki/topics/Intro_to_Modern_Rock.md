# Intro to Modern Rock
This chapter introduces drummers to the fundamentals of playing and reading in [[3/4 Time Signature]] within the context of [[Modern Rock]]. While [[3/4 Time Signature]] is common across many genres, its application in modern rock bands like Paramore, Muse, and Radiohead highlights its contemporary relevance.

**Key Concepts for Drummers:**
*   **Versatility:** From a drummer's perspective, playing in [[3/4 Time Signature]] is fundamentally similar to other time signatures, requiring the same core skills of [[Tempo]] maintenance and rhythmic accuracy.
*   **Common Pitfall: Rushing:** A common trap for young players is to rush the [[Tempo]] in [[3/4 Time Signature]]. This often stems from undervaluing the importance of beats 2 and 3, focusing too heavily on the strong downbeat of 1.
*   **Importance of Beats 2 and 3:** While beat 1 is strong, beats 2 and 3 are crucial for establishing the feel and preventing the groove from rushing. Drummers must give them equal rhythmic weight and attention.
*   **Metronome Practice:** Utilizing a [[Metronome]] is highly recommended for developing a solid [[3/4 Time Signature]] feel.

**Pro-Tip:** To combat rushing in [[3/4 Time Signature]], try practicing with a [[Metronome]] that accents beat 1, but consciously focus your internal count and physical feel on giving equal weight to beats 2 and 3. Experiment with different subdivisions (e.g., [[Eighth Notes]], [[16th Notes]]) to solidify your internal clock within this meter. Listening to songs in [[3/4 Time Signature]] from various genres can also help internalize the feel.
