# Heel Up Technique
The [[Heel Up Technique]] is a versatile [[Bass Drum Technique]] where the drummer's heel is slightly raised off the pedal board, typically one to two inches. This position offers more options in terms of dynamics and speed compared to the [[Heel Down Technique]].

**Execution:**
1.  **Position:** The heel is slightly raised off the pedal board.
2.  **Foot Placement:** The ball of the foot rests on the pedal, similar to [[Heel Down Technique]], but with the heel elevated.
3.  **Pivot Point:** The primary focus for generating the stroke remains the pivoting of the ankle, just as with the [[Heel Down Technique]].
4.  **Leg Engagement (Secondary):** While the ankle is the primary mover, raising the heel allows other leg muscles to share responsibility, reducing the isolation of the shin muscle. This position allows for some engagement of the upper leg for energy and power, but the stroke itself should still originate from the ankle pivot.

**Advantages:**
*   **Increased Dynamics and Speed:** Offers a greater range of dynamics and is less fatiguing for faster playing due to the distribution of muscular effort across more leg muscles.
*   **Reduced Fatigue:** By engaging more muscles in the leg, the shin muscle is no longer isolated, leading to greater endurance.
*   **Power Generation:** Allows for more power to be generated, making it suitable for louder musical passages and accents.

**Common Misconception & Pro-Tip:**
Think of [[Heel Up Technique]] as a "heel-down motion with a heel-up position." The pivoting of the ankle should be identical in both positions. A common pitfall is using too much upper leg, resulting in the knee "bobbing" up and down like a piston. While some sympathetic knee movement is natural, excessive knee movement indicates over-reliance on the upper leg. For general playing, the knee should only move sympathetically within the stroke. This will change when playing very loud accents or sections, where more leg involvement is necessary.

**Practice Suggestion:** When playing [[Heel Up Technique]], focus on your ankle being the primary motion generator, avoiding unnecessary knee-bobbing.
