# Hi-Hat Articulation (A Section)
The A section features a medium-loud, yet consistent, one-handed eighth-note [[hi-hat]] pattern with an accent on each quarter-note pulse (beats 1, 2, 3, and 4). Achieving the correct sound and dynamic control for both accented and un-accented notes is critical.

## Accented Note Technique
To achieve the accented sound, use the shoulder of the [[drum stick]], approximately a third of the way down from the tip. The primary motion for the loud, accented note should come from your [[Wrist]]. (See also: [[Accented Hi-Hat Note Technique]])

## Un-accented Note Technique
For the un-accented sound, use the shoulder of the [[drum stick]], just beneath the tip. Strike the *edge* (not the top) of the [[hi-hat]] cymbals with this part of the stick. This technique produces a very thick, yet dynamically controlled un-accented [[hi-hat]] tone, with a lower volume compared to the accented note. The motion for the quiet, un-accented note should primarily involve your [[Fingers]] and partial [[Wrist]]. (See also: [[Un-accented Hi-Hat Note Technique]])

## Important Note
**Do not use the tip of the stick on the top of the [[hi-hat]] cymbals.** This can result in a "corny" or "drum machine-like" sound, which is generally undesirable for this style.
