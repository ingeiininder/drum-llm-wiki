# Multi-Rests
`[[Multi-Rests]]` are a common `[[Musical_Notation]]` convention used to indicate multiple measures of `[[Rests]]` for a particular instrument. They are represented by a thick, black horizontal bar with a number placed above it. The black bar signifies a rest, and the number indicates the exact quantity of measures to be rested. For example, in this [[Chart|chart]], four bars of rest for the drummer are notated using a multi-rest, as shown in `[[Ex_9.3]]`.

While a simple concept, it's easy to lose track of your place within a multi-rest, especially in very long ones. A recommended method for `[[Counting_Through_a_Multi-Rest]]` is to add the `[[Measure_Number]]` as you count each [[Measure|measure]] (e.g., "1, 2, 3, 4" for the beats, then "[[Measure|measure]] 2," "[[Measure|measure]] 3," etc.), as illustrated in `[[Ex_9.4]]`.

**Pro-Tip:** When encountering multi-rests, especially in a live performance or recording session, actively count them out, even if silently. This maintains your `[[Internal_Pulse]]` and ensures a precise `[[Entrance]]` when your part resumes. Avoid the temptation to "zone out" during long rests; stay engaged with the music and the `[[Timekeeping]]` of the other musicians.
