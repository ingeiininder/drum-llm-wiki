# Guitar Introduction ([[Funk|Funk]]-[[Rock|Rock]])
In the context of a [[Funk_Rock]] [[Chart|chart]], a [[Guitar_Introduction]] is a common structural element where the guitar plays solo for the initial bars before the full band enters. This sets the mood, [[Tempo|tempo]], and harmonic framework for the piece.

**Key Details:**
*   **[[Duration|Duration]]:** The example describes a four-bar guitar introduction.
*   **Notation:** This is typically indicated by text on the [[Chart|chart]], such as "1st Time: Guitar Only."
*   **Drummer's Role:** During this section, the drummer (and bassist) remain silent, preparing for their entrance.
*   **Full Band Entry:** The full band's entry is often marked by text like "Full band in on D.C." or "1st Time: Bass & Drums in."

**Important Considerations for Drummers:**
*   **Counting In:** Even though you're not playing, it's crucial to count the [[Guitar_Introduction]] accurately to ensure a precise and confident entry.
*   **Listening:** Pay close attention to the guitar's [[Phrasing|phrasing]], [[Dynamics|dynamics]], and rhythmic feel to inform your subsequent drum part.
*   **D.C. al Coda Implications:** As noted, if a [[D.C._al_Coda_Funk-Rock_Intro]] is present, the drums and bass will perform through the intro section a second time, requiring careful [[Chart|chart]] navigation.

**Pro-Tip:** Treat the [[Guitar_Introduction]] as an integral part of the performance. Use this time to mentally prepare for your entrance, visualize your [[Groove|groove]], and lock into the guitarist's feel. A strong, confident entry after an intro can significantly impact the overall performance.
