# Shared Beams

**Shared Beams** refer to the practice of connecting different rhythmic values (typically [[Eighth Notes]] and [[16th Notes]]) within a single beat or measure using common beams. This notation helps to visually group notes according to their rhythmic pulse, making [[Drum Reading]] clearer and more intuitive.

## Purpose of Beaming

*   **Clarity**: Beaming helps to visually organize notes, making it easier to identify the beat and its subdivisions.
*   **Rhythmic Grouping**: Notes are typically beamed together to represent a single beat or a clear rhythmic phrase.
*   **Readability**: Consistent beaming improves the overall readability of [[Sheet Music]].

## Beaming Eighth Notes and 16th Notes

In [[4/4 Time Signature]], a beat can be subdivided into four [[16th Notes]] (1 e & a). When combining eighth notes and 16th notes within a single beat, shared beams are used to show their relationship:

*   **Eighth Notes**: Have a single beam.
*   **16th Notes**: Have two beams.

When an eighth note and 16th notes are combined in a beat, they will share the primary beam, and the 16th notes will have an additional, shorter beam (a [[Partial Beams|partial beam]]) to indicate their shorter duration.

## Common Shared Beam Groupings (within one beat)

The source material highlights three common groupings:

1.  **Eighth Note + Two 16th Notes (1-&-a)**:
    *   An [[Eighth Note]] occupies the first half of the beat (1-e).
    *   Two [[16th Notes]] occupy the second half of the beat (&-a).
    *   Notation: The eighth note and the first 16th note share a main beam. The two 16th notes are connected by a second, partial beam.

2.  **Two 16th Notes + Eighth Note (1-e-&)**:
    *   Two [[16th Notes]] occupy the first half of the beat (1-e).
    *   An [[Eighth Note]] occupies the second half of the beat (&-a).
    *   Notation: The two 16th notes are connected by two beams. The second 16th note and the eighth note share a main beam.

3.  **16th Note + Eighth Note + 16th Note (1-e-a)**:
    *   A [[16th Note]] on the "1".
    *   An [[Eighth Note]] on the "e" and "&".
    *   A [[16th Note]] on the "a".
    *   Notation: The first 16th note has a partial beam extending to the right. The eighth note is connected to the first 16th note by a main beam and to the final 16th note by another main beam. The final 16th note has a partial beam extending to the left. This is where [[Partial Beams]] are crucial for clarity.

## Pro-Tip

Understanding shared beams is not just about reading; it's about internalizing the rhythmic feel. When you see a shared beam, recognize it as a single rhythmic unit within the beat. Practice these groupings with a [[Metronome]] and vocalize the subdivisions ("1 e & a") to ensure precise execution.
