# Shared Beams

**Shared Beams** refer to the practice of connecting different [[Rhythmic_Values|rhythmic values]] (typically [[Eighth_Note]] and [[16th_Notes]]) within a single [[Beat|beat]] or [[Measure|measure]] using common beams. This notation helps to visually group notes according to their rhythmic [[Pulse|pulse]], making [[Drum_Reading]] clearer and more intuitive.

## Purpose of Beaming

*   **Clarity**: Beaming helps to visually organize notes, making it easier to identify the [[Beat|beat]] and its subdivisions.
*   **Rhythmic Grouping**: Notes are typically beamed together to represent a single [[Beat|beat]] or a clear rhythmic phrase.
*   **Readability**: Consistent beaming improves the overall readability of [[Sheet_Music]].

## Beaming [[Eighth_Notes|Eighth Notes]] and 16th Notes

In [[4-4_Time_Signature]], a [[Beat|beat]] can be subdivided into four [[16th_Notes]] (1 e & a). When combining [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] and 16th notes within a single [[Beat|beat]], shared beams are used to show their relationship:

*   **[[Eighth_Notes|Eighth Notes]]**: Have a single beam.
*   **16th Notes**: Have two beams.

When an eighth note and 16th notes are combined in a [[Beat|beat]], they will share the primary beam, and the 16th notes will have an additional, shorter beam (a [[Partial_Beams|partial beam]]) to indicate their shorter [[Duration|duration]].

## Common Shared Beam Groupings (within one [[Beat|beat]])

The source material highlights three common groupings:

1.  **Eighth Note + Two 16th Notes (1-&-a)**:
    *   An [[Eighth_Note]] occupies the first half of the [[Beat|beat]] (1-e).
    *   Two [[16th_Notes]] occupy the second half of the [[Beat|beat]] (&-a).
    *   Notation: The eighth note and the first 16th note share a main beam. The two 16th notes are connected by a second, partial beam.

2.  **Two 16th Notes + Eighth Note (1-e-&)**:
    *   Two [[16th_Notes]] occupy the first half of the [[Beat|beat]] (1-e).
    *   An [[Eighth_Note]] occupies the second half of the [[Beat|beat]] (&-a).
    *   Notation: The two 16th notes are connected by two beams. The second 16th note and the eighth note share a main beam.

3.  **16th Note + Eighth Note + 16th Note (1-e-a)**:
    *   A [[16th_Note]] on the "1".
    *   An [[Eighth_Note]] on the "e" and "&".
    *   A [[16th_Note]] on the "a".
    *   Notation: The first 16th note has a partial beam extending to the right. The eighth note is connected to the first 16th note by a main beam and to the final 16th note by another main beam. The final 16th note has a partial beam extending to the left. This is where [[Partial_Beams]] are crucial for clarity.

## Pro-Tip

Understanding shared beams is not just about reading; it's about internalizing the rhythmic feel. When you see a shared beam, recognize it as a single rhythmic unit within the [[Beat|beat]]. Practice these groupings with a [[Metronome]] and vocalize the subdivisions ("1 e & a") to ensure precise execution.
