# Slow Swing Subdivisions

Playing [[Slow_Swing]] (e.g., 60 BPM) is one of the most difficult tasks for a drummer. The wide space between [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] makes it easy to rush, drag, or "straighten out" the swing feel.

### The Rule of [[Subdividing|Subdividing]]
To maintain a consistent [[Legato]] feel on the [[Ride_Cymbal]], drummers must internally subdivide the [[Pulse|pulse]] into **Eighth-Note [[Triplets|Triplets]]**. 
*   **Counting**: Instead of counting "1, 2, 3, 4," count "1-trip-let, 2-trip-let, 3-trip-let, 4-trip-let."
*   **Application**: This [[Subdivision|subdivision]] should be applied to everything: the [[Four-on-the-Floor]] [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]], the [[Cross_Stick]] backbeat, and especially during [[Stop_Time]] figures.

### Practice Strategy
1.  Set the [[Metronome]] to eighth-note [[Triplets|triplets]] at 60 BPM.
2.  Play the [[Groove|groove]], ensuring every note aligns with the triplet grid.
3.  Switch the metronome back to [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] but continue counting the [[Triplets|triplets]] internally. This builds "internal clock" reliability.

### Common Pitfalls
At slow tempos, there is a tendency to play the "ah" of the swing [[Beat|beat]] too close to the following downbeat, making it sound like a 16th note. Constant triplet awareness prevents this "stiff" feel.
