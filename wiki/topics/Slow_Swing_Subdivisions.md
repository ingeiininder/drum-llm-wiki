# Slow Swing Subdivisions

Playing [[Slow Swing]] (e.g., 60 BPM) is one of the most difficult tasks for a drummer. The wide space between quarter notes makes it easy to rush, drag, or "straighten out" the swing feel.

### The Rule of Subdividing
To maintain a consistent [[Legato]] feel on the [[Ride Cymbal]], drummers must internally subdivide the pulse into **Eighth-Note Triplets**. 
*   **Counting**: Instead of counting "1, 2, 3, 4," count "1-trip-let, 2-trip-let, 3-trip-let, 4-trip-let."
*   **Application**: This subdivision should be applied to everything: the [[Four-on-the-Floor]] bass drum, the [[Cross-stick]] backbeat, and especially during [[Stop-Time]] figures.

### Practice Strategy
1.  Set the [[Metronome]] to eighth-note triplets at 60 BPM.
2.  Play the groove, ensuring every note aligns with the triplet grid.
3.  Switch the metronome back to quarter notes but continue counting the triplets internally. This builds "internal clock" reliability.

### Common Pitfalls
At slow tempos, there is a tendency to play the "ah" of the swing beat too close to the following downbeat, making it sound like a 16th note. Constant triplet awareness prevents this "stiff" feel.
