# Four on the Floor ([[Groove|Groove]] #1)
"Four on the Floor" ([[Groove|Groove]] #1) is a fundamental `[[Drum_Groove]]` characterized by the `[[Bass_Drum]]` playing on every `[[Quarter_Note]]` of the [[Measure|measure]]. This creates a strong, driving [[Pulse|pulse]] that is foundational in many styles, including `[[Rock]]`, `[[Disco]]`, `[[Dance_Music]]`, and `[[Blues]]` (especially in a driving context).

While the OCR only provides the name and implies a musical example (Ex 7.12), the essence of this [[Groove|groove]] is its unwavering `[[Downbeat]]` emphasis.

**Pro-Tip:** Even though the bass drum plays consistently, focus on maintaining a relaxed feel. Avoid tension, especially in your `[[Bass_Drum_Foot_Technique]]`. Experiment with different `[[Hi-Hat]]` or `[[Ride_Cymbal]]` patterns (e.g., [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]], [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]]) and `[[Snare_Drum]]` backbeats (e.g., on beats 2 and 4) to create variations while keeping the core "four on the floor" [[Pulse|pulse]]. This [[Groove|groove]] is excellent for developing `[[Timekeeping]]` and `[[Foot_Independence]]`.
