# Four on the Floor (Groove #1)
"Four on the Floor" (Groove #1) is a fundamental `[[Drum Groove]]` characterized by the `[[Bass Drum]]` playing on every `[[Quarter Note]]` of the measure. This creates a strong, driving pulse that is foundational in many styles, including `[[Rock]]`, `[[Disco]]`, `[[Dance Music]]`, and `[[Blues]]` (especially in a driving context).

While the OCR only provides the name and implies a musical example (Ex 7.12), the essence of this groove is its unwavering `[[Downbeat]]` emphasis.

**Pro-Tip:** Even though the bass drum plays consistently, focus on maintaining a relaxed feel. Avoid tension, especially in your `[[Bass Drum Foot Technique]]`. Experiment with different `[[Hi-Hat]]` or `[[Ride Cymbal]]` patterns (e.g., eighth notes, sixteenth notes) and `[[Snare Drum]]` backbeats (e.g., on beats 2 and 4) to create variations while keeping the core "four on the floor" pulse. This groove is excellent for developing `[[Timekeeping]]` and `[[Foot Independence]]`.
