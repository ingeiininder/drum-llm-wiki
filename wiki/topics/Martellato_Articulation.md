# Martellato Articulation

**Martellato** (Italian for "hammered") is an articulation mark indicating that a note should be played with significant force and a sharp, sudden attack.

### Application in Drumming
In a drum chart, martellato eighth notes (often indicated by a heavy accent or wedge symbol) require:
*   A strong, downward stroke.
*   Immediate muffling or a "staccato" feel, depending on the context.
*   High velocity to match the "hammered" description.

### Context
In [[Drum Reading 3]], martellato articulations are used to end phrases (such as the end of a 10-bar phrase) to provide a definitive, aggressive conclusion to the section.
