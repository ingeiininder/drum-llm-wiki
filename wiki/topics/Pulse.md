# Pulse

Pulse is the "heartbeat" of a piece of music. It represents the forward-moving momentum of a composition and is measured in beats. Maintaining a steady pulse is the primary responsibility of a drummer and serves as the foundation for all rhythmic subdivisions and grooves.

## Core Concepts

- **Momentum**: All music has momentum, which is the underlying pulse that listeners and musicians feel as a constant flow.
- **Pulse vs. Meter**: While the pulse is the constant [[Beat|beat]], **Meter** is the measurement of that pulse in specific groups (most commonly groups of two, three, or four beats).
- **Foundation of Feel**: A strong quarter-note pulse is essential for establishing a proper time feel. For instance, in [[Jazz|Jazz]], the Essence of the [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]] rhythm is a solid quarter-note pulse.

## Maintaining a Steady Pulse

- **Subdivision as a Tool**: At slow tempos, drummers often mentally or physically subdivide smaller note values (like eighth-note [[Triplets|triplets]] or [[16th_Notes|16th notes]]) to avoid rushing the main quarter-note pulse.
- **Coordination**: Every limb must line up with the underlying pulse to ensure a "clean" sound without unintentional flams.
- **Physical Anchors**: The [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] foot closing on all quarter-note pulses (1, 2, 3, and 4) often acts as a physical anchor for the drummer's timekeeping.

## Metric Modulation
**Metric Modulation** is a technique where the value of the pulse is redefined. For example, a [[Note_Value|note value]] from one section (such as a [[Dotted_Eighth_Note|dotted eighth note]]) can become the new eighth-note pulse in the following section, effectively changing the tempo while maintaining a mathematical relationship to the original pulse.
