# [[Marking_a_Song's_Form|Marking a Song's Form]]
[[Marking_a_Song's_Form]] is the practice of using sonic and dynamic changes on the drum set to highlight and differentiate between various sections of a song's structure (e.g., [[AABA_Form]]). This technique helps to simulate and draw attention to sectional differences, guiding the listener through the song's journey and adding musical interest.

This can be accomplished by:
*   Changing the [[Bass_Drum_Articulation]] pattern in each section.
*   Altering the [[Hi-Hat_Tone]].
*   Adding [[Hi-Hat_Opening_Technique]]s.

**Pro-Tip:** Effective [[Marking_a_Song's_Form]] is a hallmark of professional drumming. It demonstrates a deep understanding of musicality and arrangement, allowing the drummer to contribute significantly to the overall feel and impact of a performance. Listen to how other instruments are articulating the form and respond accordingly.
