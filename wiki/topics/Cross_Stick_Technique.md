# Cross-Stick Technique

The **Cross-Stick** (or Side-Stick) is a snare drum articulation that produces a "click" sound, frequently used in ballads, bossa nova, and R&B.

### Execution
1.  **Stick Orientation**: Turn the stick around and use the **butt end** for a fuller, warmer tone.
2.  **Positioning**: Place the tip (bead) of the stick on the head, roughly between the **8 and 9 o'clock** position. The butt end should extend over the rim between the **2 and 3 o'clock** position.
3.  **The Stroke**: Grip the stick normally, then "throw" the butt end into the rim. Upon impact, flatten your fingers to apply pressure, keeping the stick in contact with the head to muffle unwanted overtones.

### Finding the "Sweet Spot"
Every snare drum is unique. Experiment with the ratio of "on-drum" vs. "off-drum" stick length to find the most resonant "sweet spot" for that specific instrument.

---PRO-TIP---
For a consistent sound, try to hit the same spot on the rim every time. Some drummers mark their "sweet spot" on the rim with a small piece of tape or a pencil mark during recording sessions.
