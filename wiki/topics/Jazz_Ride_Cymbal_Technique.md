# [[Jazz|Jazz]] [[Ride_Cymbal_Technique|Ride Cymbal Technique]]
The [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]] is the foundation of [[Jazz|jazz]] time-keeping. A good "[[Time_Feel|time feel]]" starts with a solid quarter-note [[Pulse|pulse]], followed by the addition of the "skip note" (the 'a' of the triplet).

### Playing Areas
*   **Halfway (Edge to Bell)**: The standard playing area. Provides a balance of "definition" (the stick click) and "wash" (the shimmering overtones).
*   **Toward the Bell**: Increases definition and reduces wash. Useful for faster tempos.
*   **Toward the Edge**: Increases wash and reduces definition. Useful for lush, slow ballads.

### Shouldering the Ride
In [[Jazz|jazz]], [[Accents|accents]] are rarely played on a separate crash cymbal. Instead, drummers "shoulder" the ride. Use the shoulder of the stick to strike the top surface of the [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]]. This creates a full, washy accent while controlling overtones and maintaining the flow of the rhythm.

### Texture and Form
In [[Jazz|jazz]] performance, it is common to use different ride cymbals for different sections (e.g., Ride 1 for [[The_Head_(Jazz)]], Ride 2 for the guitar solo). This helps in [[Marking_the_Form_Jazz|Marking the Form]].
