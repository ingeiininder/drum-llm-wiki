# Jazz Ride Cymbal Technique
The ride cymbal is the foundation of jazz time-keeping. A good "time feel" starts with a solid quarter-note pulse, followed by the addition of the "skip note" (the 'a' of the triplet).

### Playing Areas
*   **Halfway (Edge to Bell)**: The standard playing area. Provides a balance of "definition" (the stick click) and "wash" (the shimmering overtones).
*   **Toward the Bell**: Increases definition and reduces wash. Useful for faster tempos.
*   **Toward the Edge**: Increases wash and reduces definition. Useful for lush, slow ballads.

### Shouldering the Ride
In jazz, accents are rarely played on a separate crash cymbal. Instead, drummers "shoulder" the ride. Use the shoulder of the stick to strike the top surface of the ride cymbal. This creates a full, washy accent while controlling overtones and maintaining the flow of the rhythm.

### Texture and Form
In jazz performance, it is common to use different ride cymbals for different sections (e.g., Ride 1 for [[The Head (Jazz)]], Ride 2 for the guitar solo). This helps in [[Marking_the_Form_Jazz|Marking the Form]].
