# Short vs Long Note Interpretation

In [[Ensemble Reading]], drummers must decide how to orchestrate rhythmic figures based on their duration. This is known as **Note Length Interpretation**. Assigning specific drum set voices to "short" and "long" notes helps the drummer support the horn section or melody more effectively.

### Orchestration Rules (Ex 9.5)
When reading a rhythmic staff, apply the following logic:

1. **Short Notes**: 
   - **Definition**: Any note equal to an eighth note or shorter (e.g., 8th notes, 16th notes).
   - **Voice**: Play on the **Snare Drum**.
   - **Character**: Staccato, crisp, and immediate.

2. **Long Notes**:
   - **Definition**: Any note longer than an eighth note (e.g., Quarter notes, Dotted Quarters, Half notes, Whole notes, or tied notes).
   - **Voice**: Play on the **Bass Drum** (often reinforced with a cymbal crash).
   - **Character**: Sustained, heavy, and resonant.

### The Hi-Hat/Ride Anchor
While interpreting these figures, maintain a consistent ostinato:
- **Ride Cymbal**: Continual eighth notes.
- **Hi-Hat Foot**: Quarter notes on all four beats.

### Application
This technique is the foundation for [[Big Band Drumming]] and modern chart reading. It allows the drummer to "color" the rhythm, making the drum part sound like a musical arrangement rather than just a series of hits.
