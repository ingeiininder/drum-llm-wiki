# Fulcrum
The fulcrum in drumming refers to the pivot point where the stick is held, allowing it to move freely while still being controlled by the hand. It is the balance point that enables the stick to rebound naturally and efficiently.

## Basic Principle:
The stick acts as a lever, and the fulcrum is the point where the hand applies pressure to control the stick's motion. A well-established fulcrum is crucial for:
*   **Rebound**: Allowing the stick to bounce freely off the playing surface.
*   **Control**: Guiding the stick's trajectory and dynamics.
*   **Efficiency**: Minimizing effort and tension for maximum speed and power.

## Common Fulcrum Types:
1.  **Thumb and Forefinger Fulcrum**: This is a very common and versatile fulcrum, where the stick rests between the thumb and the first crease of the forefinger. It's often associated with [[American Grip]] and [[French Grip]].
2.  **[[Second-Finger Fulcrum]]**: In this variation, the second finger (middle finger) takes the place of the forefinger, with the stick resting between the first and second crease of the second finger. The thumb remains positioned slightly between the forefinger and second finger. This fulcrum often coincides with [[Wrist Technique]] and can provide more power, especially for heavier back-beat drumming.

## Importance:
A proper understanding and application of different fulcrums are essential for a professional drummer. Depending on the desired dynamic, intensity, or speed, one fulcrum might be more advantageous than another, allowing for greater expression and helping to prevent [[Injury Prevention]]. Experimentation and practice with both types are highly recommended.
