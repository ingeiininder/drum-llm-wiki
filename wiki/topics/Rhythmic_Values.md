# Rhythmic Values
Rhythmic values define the duration of musical sounds (notes) and silences (rests). They are fundamental to understanding and performing music, dictating *when* to play and *for how long*. Within a standard [[4/4 Time Signature]] (four beats per [[Measure]]), there are several main note and rest values.

## Main Note Values (in 4/4 Time)
*   **[[Whole Note]]**: Equal to four beats (a whole measure).
*   **[[Half Note]]**: Equal to two beats (half of a measure).
*   **[[Quarter Note]]**: Equal to one beat (one quarter of a measure).
*   [[Eighth Note]]: Equal to half a beat.
*   [[Sixteenth Note]]: Equal to a quarter of a beat.

## Main Rest Values (in 4/4 Time)
*   **[[Whole Rest]]**: Equal to four beats (a whole measure of silence).
*   **[[Half Rest]]**: Equal to two beats (half a measure of silence).
*   **[[Quarter Rest]]**: Equal to one beat (one beat of silence).
*   [[Eighth Rest]]: Equal to half a beat of silence.
*   [[Sixteenth Rest]]: Equal to a quarter of a beat of silence.

## Rhythmic Value Tree
The relationship between these values can be visualized as a tree, where each longer note or rest value can be divided into shorter ones:
*   One [[Whole Note]] = Two [[Half Note]]s
*   One [[Half Note]] = Two [[Quarter Note]]s
*   One [[Quarter Note]] = Two [[Eighth Note]]s
*   One [[Eighth Note]] = Two [[Sixteenth Note]]s

The same proportional relationships apply to rests.

## Importance for Drummers
*   **Groove Construction**: Drummers use rhythmic values to construct grooves, determining the duration of each drum hit and the spaces between them.
*   **Fill Development**: Understanding how to combine different rhythmic values is essential for creating dynamic and musical drum fills.
*   **Reading Music**: Proficiency in rhythmic values is the cornerstone of [[Sight Reading]] drum notation.

## Pro-Tip for Drummers
*   **Feel the Subdivision**: Don't just count the main beats; internalize the subdivisions (e.g., the "ands" for eighth notes, the "e-and-as" for sixteenth notes). This creates a more precise and solid rhythmic foundation.
*   **Practice with Rests**: Rests are just as important as notes. Practice playing rhythms that incorporate rests to develop control over silence and space in your playing.
