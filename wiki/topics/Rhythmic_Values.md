# Rhythmic Values
Rhythmic values define the [[Duration|duration]] of musical sounds (notes) and silences ([[Rests|rests]]). They are fundamental to understanding and performing music, dictating *when* to play and *for how long*. Within a standard [[4-4_Time_Signature]] (four beats per [[Measure]]), there are several main note and rest values.

## Main Note Values (in 4/4 Time)
*   **[[Whole_Note]]**: Equal to four beats (a whole measure).
*   **[[Half_Note]]**: Equal to two beats (half of a measure).
*   **[[Quarter_Note]]**: Equal to one beat (one quarter of a measure).
*   [[Eighth_Note]]: Equal to half a beat.
*   [[Sixteenth_Note]]: Equal to a quarter of a beat.

## Main Rest Values (in 4/4 Time)
*   **[[Whole_Rest]]**: Equal to four beats (a whole measure of silence).
*   **[[Half_Rest]]**: Equal to two beats (half a measure of silence).
*   **[[Quarter_Rest]]**: Equal to one beat (one beat of silence).
*   [[Eighth_Rest]]: Equal to half a beat of silence.
*   [[Sixteenth_Rest]]: Equal to a quarter of a beat of silence.

## Rhythmic Value Tree
The relationship between these values can be visualized as a tree, where each longer note or rest value can be divided into shorter ones:
*   One [[Whole_Note]] = Two [[Half_Note]]s
*   One [[Half_Note]] = Two [[Quarter_Note]]s
*   One [[Quarter_Note]] = Two [[Eighth_Note]]s
*   One [[Eighth_Note]] = Two [[Sixteenth_Note]]s

The same proportional relationships apply to [[Rests|rests]].

## Importance for Drummers
*   **[[Groove|Groove]] Construction**: Drummers use rhythmic values to construct grooves, determining the [[Duration|duration]] of each drum hit and the spaces between them.
*   **[[Fill|Fill]] Development**: Understanding how to combine different rhythmic values is essential for creating dynamic and musical drum fills.
*   **Reading Music**: Proficiency in rhythmic values is the cornerstone of [[Sight_Reading]] drum notation.

## Pro-Tip for Drummers
*   **Feel the Subdivision**: Don't just count the main beats; internalize the subdivisions (e.g., the "ands" for [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]], the "e-and-as" for [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]]). This creates a more precise and solid rhythmic foundation.
*   **Practice with [[Rests|Rests]]**: [[Rests|Rests]] are just as important as notes. Practice playing rhythms that incorporate [[Rests|rests]] to develop control over silence and space in your playing.
