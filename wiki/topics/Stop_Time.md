# Stop-Time
**Stop-Time** is a performance directive indicating that the rhythm section should cease playing a continuous [[Groove]] or "time" and instead play only specific, punctuated rhythmic figures.

### Interpretation Guidelines
- **Literal Meaning**: "Stop playing time." The [[Pulse|pulse]] is maintained internally, but the audible output is limited to the written hits.
- **Orchestration**: While the lead sheet provides the rhythm (e.g., beats 2, 3, and the "&" of 3), the drummer is encouraged to use creative [[Orchestration]] across the kit.
- **Transitioning**: Stop-time sections are frequently used as transitions or "set-ups" for upcoming solos or [[Meter|meter]] changes.

### Pro-Tip
The space between the notes is as important as the notes themselves. Avoid the temptation to "[[Fill|fill]]" the gaps with [[Ghost_Notes]] unless specifically instructed, as this defeats the "stop" effect.
