# Staccato
**Staccato** (Italian for "detached") is an articulation marking that instructs the drummer to play a note as short as possible with no sustain.

### Notation
Represented by a small dot placed above or below the note head.

### Application on Drum Set
*   **Cymbals**: Requires an immediate [[Cymbal Choke]].
*   **Drums**: Often played in the center of the head for a dry, focused "crack" with minimal overtones.
*   **Hi-Hats**: Played with a very tight foot splash or a closed "tip" stroke.

### Why use Staccato?
It is often used to clean up notation. Instead of writing complex rhythms with many small rests (which can be hard to read at high speeds), a composer writes quarter notes with staccato dots to indicate the same "short" feel.
