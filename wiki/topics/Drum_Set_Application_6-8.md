# Drum Set Application in 6/8

Transitioning from snare-only reading to the full [[Drum_Seat]] in [[6/8_Time]] involves distributing rhythmic phrases across different voices ([[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]], Snare, and Cymbals).

### Phrasing and Orchestration
Exercises **3.16, 3.17, and 3.18** demonstrate how four-bar phrases are constructed in a drum set context. 
- **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: Often emphasizes the dotted quarter [[Pulse|pulse]] (1 and 4) or mirrors the syncopated reading rhythms.
- **[[Snare_Drum|Snare Drum]]**: Usually handles the backbeat or ghost note subdivisions.
- **Cymbals**: Provide the consistent eighth-note or dotted-quarter [[Pulse|pulse]].

### Performance Checklist
- **Dotted Quarter BPM**: 60, 70, 80, 90, 100.
- **Musicality**: Ensure that the four-bar phrases feel like a singular musical thought rather than four disconnected measures.
- **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: Pay attention to the balance between the kick drum and the snare; in 6/8, the "feel" can easily become heavy if the subdivisions aren't played with a light, dance-like quality.
