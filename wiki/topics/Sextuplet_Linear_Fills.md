# Sextuplet Linear Fills

Building upon basic [[Linear Drumming]] combinations, **Sextuplet Linear Fills** utilize modular hand/foot patterns to create high-velocity, melodic phrases around the drum set.

### Core Sticking Patterns
The foundation of these fills relies on two primary modular stickings:
1.  **RLL (Right-Left-Left)**: A versatile pattern where the accent typically falls on the first partial (the Right hand).
2.  **RRL (Right-Right-Left)**: Often used to shift the accent or create different melodic contours.
3.  **LLR (Left-Left-Right)**: The inversion of RRL, moving the accent to the third partial of the triplet group.

### Orchestration and Color
*   **Tom Movement**: Moving the accented "Right" hand between the snare, rack tom, and floor tom while the "Left-Left" remains as ghost notes on the snare.
*   **Cymbal Support**: Adding [[Bass Drum]] hits simultaneously with cymbal crashes on the accented notes to add "color" and power to the fill.

### Technical Goal
The objective is to make the accents "pop" out of a continuous string of sextuplets, creating a melodic line that floats over the ghosted notes.
