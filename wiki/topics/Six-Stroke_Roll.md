# Six-Stroke Roll
The [[Six-Stroke Roll]] is a versatile drum rudiment, often considered a variation of the [[Double Stroke Roll]] family, though it can also be played as a combination of double and single strokes. It consists of six notes, typically two [[Double Stroke]]s followed by two single strokes. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 7 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
RRLLRL (or LLRRLR for Left Hand Lead)

**Breakdown:**
*   **RR:** Two strokes with the right hand.
*   **LL:** Two strokes with the left hand.
*   **R:** A single stroke with the right hand.
*   **L:** A single stroke with the left hand, often accented.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Roll Development:** Develops control over both [[Double Stroke Technique]] and [[Single Stroke Roll]] concepts within a single rudiment.
*   **Fills and Grooves:** Extremely popular for drum fills, especially in rock, funk, and jazz, due to its natural rhythmic feel. It can also be integrated into grooves.
*   **Versatility:** Can be played in various subdivisions (e.g., [[Sixteenth Notes]], [[Eighth Note Triplets]]) to create different rhythmic effects.

**Pro-Tip:** The [[Six-Stroke Roll]] is often played as a [[Sixteenth Note]] triplet or a sextuplet. Focus on making the initial double strokes even and controlled, and then smoothly transition into the single strokes. The final two single strokes are often accented or played with more emphasis to provide a strong rhythmic resolution. Practice slowly with a metronome to ensure evenness and clarity between all six notes.
