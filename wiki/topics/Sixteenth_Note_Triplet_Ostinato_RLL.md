# 16th-Note Triplet Ostinato (RLL)

This advanced ostinato pattern is designed to develop [[Left_Hand]] ghost note execution and independence while reading [[Bass_Drum]] figures.

### The Pattern (RLL)
The core sticking is **R L L**:
*   **Right Hand (RH)**: Moves between the [[Hi-Hat]] (or [[Ride_Cymbal]]) and the [[Snare_Drum]].
*   **Left Hand (LH)**: Remains on the snare drum, playing strictly [[Ghost_Notes]].
*   **Backbeat**: The RH moves to the snare drum to play a strong accent on beats 2 and 4.

### Coordination Challenges
1.  **The "Ghost" Double**: The LH must play two quiet notes in a row (the "LL" of the RLL) consistently without [[Accents|accents]].
2.  **Bass Drum Independence**: While the hands maintain the RLL flow, the bass drum reads syncopated 16th-note triplet figures.
3.  **Ride Variation**: Once comfortable on the hi-hat, move the RH to the ride cymbal and add various [[Hi-Hat]] foot patterns (quarters, "and" counts, or 2 and 4).

### Pro-Tip
Focus on the height of your sticks. The RH should have a high stroke for the backbeat and a medium stroke for the hi-hat, while the LH should never leave the "ghost note" zone (approx. 1 inch from the drumhead).
