# Short Re-Intro
A "Short Re-Intro" is a brief, often two-bar, musical section that serves as a transition back to an earlier part of the song, typically the [[Verse (Song Section)]] or the main [[Intro]]. It's used after a significant section like a [[Chorus (Song Section)]] or a series of [[First and Second Endings]] to reset the listener's ear and prepare for the return of familiar material.

In this curriculum, a short re-intro is used after the second ending of [[Letter B (Rehearsal Mark)]] to set up a return to [[Letter A]] (the verse). It often features a [[Tight Hi-Hat]] groove, providing a clean and concise reset.

**Pro-Tip:** Drummers should treat a re-intro as a palate cleanser. It's an opportunity to pull back slightly in energy or complexity, creating a clear demarcation before the next section. Ensure the groove is solid and precise, setting a strong foundation for the upcoming verse.
