# Hi Hat Cymbal (Notation)
In [[Drum_Set_Notation_Key]], the [[Hi_Hat_Cymbal]] is a crucial element for rhythmic articulation and [[Groove|groove]] definition, and its notation reflects its various states (open, closed, foot pedal).

## Standard Placement
*   The [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] cymbal is notated with an **'X' [[Note_Head]]**.
*   Its typical position is on the **top space** of [[The_Staff]] or sometimes on the top line, often just below the [[Ride_Cymbal_Notation]] if both are present.

## Function in Notation
[[Hi-Hat|Hi-hat]] notation indicates when the hi-hats should be played. Additional symbols are frequently used to denote specific techniques:
*   **Closed [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: A standard 'X' note head.
*   **[[Open_Hi-Hat|Open Hi-Hat]]**: An 'X' note head with a small circle above it.
*   **Foot [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] (Chick)**: An 'X' note head below the staff, often on the first ledger line, indicating the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] pedal is pressed down to create a "chick" sound.

Understanding these variations is vital for accurately interpreting and performing [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] patterns.
