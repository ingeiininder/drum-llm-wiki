# Hi Hat Cymbal (Notation)
In [[Drum Set Notation Key]], the [[Hi Hat Cymbal]] is a crucial element for rhythmic articulation and groove definition, and its notation reflects its various states (open, closed, foot pedal).

## Standard Placement
*   The hi-hat cymbal is notated with an **'X' [[Note Head]]**.
*   Its typical position is on the **top space** of [[The Staff]] or sometimes on the top line, often just below the [[Ride Cymbal (Notation)]] if both are present.

## Function in Notation
Hi-hat notation indicates when the hi-hats should be played. Additional symbols are frequently used to denote specific techniques:
*   **Closed Hi-Hat**: A standard 'X' note head.
*   **Open Hi-Hat**: An 'X' note head with a small circle above it.
*   **Foot Hi-Hat (Chick)**: An 'X' note head below the staff, often on the first ledger line, indicating the hi-hat pedal is pressed down to create a "chick" sound.

Understanding these variations is vital for accurately interpreting and performing hi-hat patterns.
