# 16th Notes on Snare Drum with Quarter Note Bass Drum
This specific [[Groove]] pattern is described for the [[Verse_Section_Letter_A]] in a [[Country-Pop_Genre]] [[Chart|chart]]. It involves:
*   A repeating [[16th_Notes]] pattern played on the [[Snare_Drum]]. This often implies a ghost note pattern or a light, driving rhythm.
*   The [[Bass_Drum]] playing on all [[Quarter_Note]] underneath the snare drum pattern, providing a solid, consistent foundation.

This combination creates a driving yet supportive feel, common in many pop and country-pop songs, as demonstrated in [[Ex_5.2_Country_Pop_Verse_Groove]].

**Pro-Tip:** When playing 16th notes on the snare, focus on consistency of [[Dynamics|dynamics]]. If it's a ghost note pattern, ensure the primary backbeats (usually 2 and 4) pop out clearly. The quarter note bass drum needs to be [[Rock|rock]]-solid, locking in with the bass guitar to create a strong rhythmic anchor.
