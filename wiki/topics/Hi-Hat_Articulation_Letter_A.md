# Hi-Hat Articulation (Letter A)
For the 'A' section of a song, the hi-hat articulation is designed to provide a subtle yet consistent rhythmic foundation, often contrasting with the louder bass and snare drums. This involves:

*   **Pattern:** A two-handed alternating 16th-note hi-hat pattern.
*   **Evenness and Softness:** The pattern should be played evenly and softly.
*   **Dynamic:** A dynamic of piano (soft) is recommended.
*   **Stick Contact:** This is achieved by using the [[Hi-Hat_Shoulder_Strike_Edge]] – striking the *edge* (not the top) of the hi-hat cymbals with the shoulder of the stick, just beneath the tip.

This technique creates a very thick and dynamically controlled hi-hat tone.

**Pro-Tip:** By intentionally bringing the hi-hat volume lower, it creates a dynamic illusion, automatically making the [[Bass_Drum_Articulation_Letter_A]] and [[Snare_Drum_Articulation_Letter_A]] appear to sound louder and more prominent within the overall drum mix. This dynamic contrast is key to [[Marking a Song's Form]].
