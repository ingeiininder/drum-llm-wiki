# [[Chart|Chart]] Analysis: [[Shuffle_Feel|Shuffle Feel]]

Reading a [[Shuffle|shuffle]] [[Chart|chart]] (e.g., "Eight Bars to Go") requires an understanding of triplet-based [[Subdivision]] and specific roadmap markings.

### Key Characteristics
*   **Swung Eighths**: All written [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] are played as the first and third notes of an eighth-note triplet.
*   **Dynamic Range**: [[Shuffle|Shuffle]] charts often feature wide dynamic shifts, moving from *pianissimo* (pp) to *fortissimo* (ff) over short phrases.
*   **Silent Breaks**: Professional charts often include "stop-time" or full-[[Measure|measure]] [[Rests|rests]]. Drummers must count through these [[Rests|rests]] accurately to "hit" the next entrance (e.g., an entrance on the "&" of [[Beat|beat]] 1).

### Reading Roadmaps
*   **[[Rehearsal_Letters|Rehearsal Letters]]**: Used to navigate sections (Intro, A, B, C, etc.).
*   **[[Repeat_Signs|Repeat Signs]]**: Often used for 4-bar or 8-bar phrases. To keep the music interesting, vary your [[Set-up_Fill]] on the second pass of a repeat.
*   **Decrescendo/Crescendo**: Pay close attention to long hairpins that span multiple measures, as these dictate the intensity of your fills.

### Handling "Pushes" in [[Shuffle|Shuffle]]
In a [[Shuffle|shuffle]], [[Ensemble_Figures|ensemble figures]] often land on the "ah" of a triplet. These require a triplet-based set-up rather than the 16th-note set-up used in [[Rock|rock]] or [[Funk|funk]].
