# Chart Analysis: Shuffle Feel

Reading a shuffle chart (e.g., "Eight Bars to Go") requires an understanding of triplet-based [[Subdivision]] and specific roadmap markings.

### Key Characteristics
*   **Swung Eighths**: All written eighth notes are played as the first and third notes of an eighth-note triplet.
*   **Dynamic Range**: Shuffle charts often feature wide dynamic shifts, moving from *pianissimo* (pp) to *fortissimo* (ff) over short phrases.
*   **Silent Breaks**: Professional charts often include "stop-time" or full-measure rests. Drummers must count through these rests accurately to "hit" the next entrance (e.g., an entrance on the "&" of beat 1).

### Reading Roadmaps
*   **Rehearsal Letters**: Used to navigate sections (Intro, A, B, C, etc.).
*   **Repeat Signs**: Often used for 4-bar or 8-bar phrases. To keep the music interesting, vary your [[Set-up Fill]] on the second pass of a repeat.
*   **Decrescendo/Crescendo**: Pay close attention to long hairpins that span multiple measures, as these dictate the intensity of your fills.

### Handling "Pushes" in Shuffle
In a shuffle, ensemble figures often land on the "ah" of a triplet. These require a triplet-based set-up rather than the 16th-note set-up used in rock or funk.
