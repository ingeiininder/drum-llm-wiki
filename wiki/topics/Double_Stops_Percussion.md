# Double Stops (Percussion)

A **Double Stop** in drumming occurs when two different sound sources (typically the snare drum and a tom) are struck simultaneously to create a thicker, more powerful composite sound.

### Application in Hard Rock
In [[Hard Rock Drumming]], double stops between the **[[Snare Drum]]** and **[[Floor Tom]]** are used to reinforce heavy guitar or bass riffs. This creates a "tribal" or "industrial" texture that is more impactful than a single drum stroke.

### Technical Execution
*   **Placement**: For the best tone, strike the center of both the snare drum and the floor tom. Avoid [[Rimshot|rim shots]] during double stops to ensure a deep, open sound.
*   **Consistency**: A common pitfall is the "drifting" of the right hand. Drummers must visually monitor the floor tom to ensure the stick doesn't move toward the edge, which would result in a thinner sound and inconsistent volume compared to the snare.
*   **Balance**: The volume between the two drums and the [[Bass Drum]] must be perfectly matched to create a unified "wall of sound."

> [!TIP]
> **Pro-Tip**: Use your wrists to start the stroke and "grab" the stick with your back three fingers just before impact. This adds a crisp attack to the open tone.
