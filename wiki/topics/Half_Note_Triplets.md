# Half-Note Triplets

Half-note triplets represent three equal notes played over the duration of a whole note (four quarter notes). They are a further expansion of the triplet family.

### Mathematical Relationship
*   Two quarter-note triplets = One half-note triplet.
*   In a 4/4 measure, there are exactly three half-note triplets.

### Derivation from Eighth-Note Triplets
To find the placement of half-note triplets, one can play every **fourth** eighth-note triplet (Ex 2.7):
*   **Grid**: **1** - trip - let - 2 - **trip** - let - 3 - trip - **let** - 4 - trip - let
*   **Result**: The notes fall on **1**, the **trip of 2**, and the **let of 3**.

### Visual Hierarchy (Ex 2.8)
1.  **Eighth-Note Triplets**: 12 notes per bar.
2.  **Quarter-Note Triplets**: 6 notes per bar.
3.  **Half-Note Triplets**: 3 notes per bar.

### Pro-Tip
Half-note triplets are often used in jazz and contemporary drumming to create a "stretching" effect against the pulse. Practice these with a metronome clicking on beats 1 and 3 to ensure the three notes are perfectly spaced across the four beats.
