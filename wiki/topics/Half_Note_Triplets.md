# Half-Note [[Triplets|Triplets]]

Half-note [[Triplets|triplets]] represent three equal notes played over the [[Duration|duration]] of a whole note (four [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]]). They are a further expansion of the triplet family.

### Mathematical Relationship
*   Two quarter-note [[Triplets|triplets]] = One half-note triplet.
*   In a 4/4 [[Measure|measure]], there are exactly three half-note [[Triplets|triplets]].

### Derivation from Eighth-Note [[Triplets|Triplets]]
To find the placement of half-note [[Triplets|triplets]], one can play every **fourth** eighth-note triplet (Ex 2.7):
*   **Grid**: **1** - trip - let - 2 - **trip** - let - 3 - trip - **let** - 4 - trip - let
*   **Result**: The notes fall on **1**, the **trip of 2**, and the **let of 3**.

### Visual Hierarchy (Ex 2.8)
1.  **Eighth-Note [[Triplets|Triplets]]**: 12 notes per bar.
2.  **Quarter-Note [[Triplets|Triplets]]**: 6 notes per bar.
3.  **Half-Note [[Triplets|Triplets]]**: 3 notes per bar.

### Pro-Tip
Half-note [[Triplets|triplets]] are often used in [[Jazz|jazz]] and contemporary drumming to create a "stretching" effect against the [[Pulse|pulse]]. Practice these with a [[Metronome|metronome]] clicking on beats 1 and 3 to ensure the three notes are perfectly spaced across the four beats.
