# Feel Transitions

In professional drumming, the ability to seamlessly navigate transitions between different rhythmic subdivisions—specifically moving from a swung feel to a straight feel—is a critical skill. A successful transition ensures the ensemble remains cohesive and the musical flow is not interrupted.

### The Setup Fill
To avoid a new section appearing "out of nowhere," drummers utilize a **setup fill**. This is typically played one bar before the new section begins.
- **Subdivision Alignment**: The fill should utilize the subdivision of the *upcoming* section. For example, when moving from [[Jazz Swing]] (triplet-based) to [[Bossa Nova]] (straight-eighth based), the setup fill should consist of clear, precise straight-eighth notes.
- **Cues**: This serves as a rhythmic "cue" to the band, signaling the impending change in pulse and feel.

### Technical Management
Transitions often involve physical changes in equipment, such as switching from [[Brush Technique|brushes]] to sticks. 
- **The Two-Bar Hold**: Charts often provide a two-bar hold (extended whole notes) to facilitate this change.
- **Pro-Tip**: Stay relaxed during the switch. Rushing the physical movement of picking up sticks often leads to rushing the subsequent tempo.

### Common Transitions
1. **Jazz to Bossa**: Transitioning from a [[Jazz Ride Pattern]] (triplet feel) to a [[Bossa Nova]] (straight feel).
2. **Bossa to Jazz**: Transitioning back to swing requires a triplet-based fill in the final bar of the straight section to re-establish the swing pulse.
3. **Double-Time Transitions**: Often marked by [[Metric Modulation]], where the eighth note of the current section becomes the quarter note of the next.
