# [[Note_Value|Note Value]] Review
Chapter 1 of Unit 7 provides a systematic review of fundamental [[Note_Values]] and their symmetrical subdivisions, reinforcing concepts typically covered in Units 1-6. This review is crucial for establishing a strong rhythmic foundation before delving into more complex topics like [[Triplets]].

**Key Relationships Reviewed:**
*   **[[Whole_Note]]:** Equals two [[Half_Note]], four [[Quarter_Note]], eight [[Eighth_Note]], or sixteen [[16th_Notes]].
*   **[[Half_Note]]:** Equals two [[Quarter_Note]], four [[Eighth_Note]], or eight [[16th_Notes]].
*   **[[Quarter_Note]]:** Equals two [[Eighth_Note]] or four [[16th_Notes]].
*   **[[Eighth_Note]]:** Equals two [[16th_Notes]].

**Pro-Tip:** A solid understanding of these relationships is paramount for accurate [[Drum_Reading]] and developing a strong internal [[Metronome]]. Practice [[Subdividing|subdividing]] each [[Note_Value|note value]] mentally and physically (e.g., tapping your foot for [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]], counting "1-&-a" for [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]], "1-e-&-a" for [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]]) to internalize these divisions. This will make learning new rhythmic concepts much easier.
