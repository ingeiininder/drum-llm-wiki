# Swung Eighth-Note Triplet Placement
In a jazz or triplet-based setting, the rhythmic grid shifts from a binary (straight) feel to a ternary (swung) feel.

### The Triplet Grid
A triplet contains three eighth notes in the space of two. In swing:
1.  **The Downbeat**: Remains on the "1".
2.  **The Upbeat**: Moves to the **third partial** of the triplet.

### Freestanding Eighth Notes
When a chart shows a freestanding eighth note on an upbeat in a swing feel, it must be placed on the third partial of the triplet. This makes the upbeat closer to the *following* downbeat than the *preceding* one.

**Pro-Tip**: Think of the middle triplet partial as a "ghosted" or silent space that pushes the upbeat later in the beat, creating the "lilt" characteristic of [[Jazz Phrasing]].
