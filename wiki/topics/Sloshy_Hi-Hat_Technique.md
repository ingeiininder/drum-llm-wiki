# Sloshy Hi-Hat Technique

The "sloshy" hi-hat is a staple of rock and [[Rap-Metal Drumming]], used to create a wall of sound and generate musical excitement during high-energy sections like choruses.

### Execution
- **Pedal Pressure**: Release the hi-hat pedal slightly so the cymbals are touching but not clamped. They should "sizzle" when struck.
- **Stick Placement**: Use the **middle-shoulder** of the stick against the **edge** of the hi-hat cymbals.
- **Dynamics**: Usually played *fortissimo* (very loud).

### Common Pitfalls
- **Accenting Downbeats**: Avoid using a "shoulder/tip" motion (moeller-style) that over-accents the downbeats. In Rap-Metal, the eighth notes should be a consistent, driving wash of sound.
- **Over-Opening**: If the cymbals are too far apart, you lose the "slosh" and get a "clash" sound. Find the "sweet spot" where the cymbals vibrate against each other.
