# Ensemble Figures
[[Ensemble Figures]] are specific rhythms or musical phrases that are played simultaneously by the entire ensemble (band). In drum notation, these figures are often written in the middle of the staff, above the standard drum part, and take precedence over time slashes or other ongoing grooves. They indicate a collective hit, a rhythmic motif, or a stop that the whole band executes together.

**Pro-Tip:** Drummers must prioritize [[Ensemble Figures]] to lock in with the band. This often means momentarily adjusting or interrupting the established groove to reinforce the collective rhythm. Listening intently to the other musicians and reacting precisely is key to making these figures impactful.
