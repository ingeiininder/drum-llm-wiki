# Single-Stroke Roll Development

Performing a smooth **Single-Stroke Roll** at varying lengths and tempos is one of the most challenging skills in percussion. This curriculum focuses on incremental growth, adding one note to each successive roll stroke (from 5-stroke to 15-stroke rolls).

### Counting Strategies for Rolls
To maintain timing at high speeds, use "lead hand counting" rather than counting every individual note:
- **5-Stroke Roll**: Count "3" for the lead hand.
- **6-Stroke Roll**: Count "3 &".
- **7-Stroke Roll**: Count "4".
- **9-Stroke Roll**: Count "5".
- **13-Stroke Roll**: Count "7".

### Key Technical Principles
- **Wrist Motion**: The movement should come primarily from turning the wrists. Avoid "squeezing" the sticks, which causes tension.
- **Stroke Height**: Each roll stroke should begin and end approximately **one inch** above the practice pad or drumhead, parallel to the floor.
- **Relaxation**: If tension is felt in the forearms, neck, or shoulders, immediately lower the [[Metronome]] tempo. Relaxation is the key to velocity.
- **Precision**: Every roll must begin and end exactly with the metronome click.

### Common Pitfalls
- **The "Gallop"**: Uneven spacing between notes. Ensure the [[Subdivision]] is perfectly mathematical.
- **Pressure Spikes**: Increasing grip tension as the number of notes increases. Keep pressure constant.
