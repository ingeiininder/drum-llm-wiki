# Gladstone Technique
The [[Gladstone_Technique]], also widely known as the [[Free_Stroke]], is a foundational drumming technique centered on allowing the [[Drumstick]] to naturally rebound after striking a surface. It is considered vitally important as it forms the basis for many other advanced techniques.

**Main Objective:**
The primary goal is to allow the stick to bounce back to its starting position naturally, free from any restriction, much like bouncing a ball. This maximizes efficiency by utilizing the stick's natural rebound rather than forcing it back up.

**Core Principles and Exercises:**
1.  **Starting Position:** Begin from an "up position" (stick raised).
2.  **Throwing the Stick:** "Throw" the stick down, allowing it to strike the drum.
3.  **Natural Rebound:** Let the stick bounce back into your hand.
4.  **Isolation Exercise:** Initially, keep your fingers extended downward with a loose, open grip, allowing the stick to fully rebound to its starting position. This helps to appreciate the natural potential of the rebound.
5.  **Wrist Motion:** Once comfortable with the isolation, begin to move your wrist in sync with the stick's natural motion. After throwing the stick, bend the wrist back and follow the stick as it returns. Crucially, *do not lift the stick with your hand*; allow it to bounce back. Catch the stick with your hand, not just your fingers.
6.  **Continuous Strokes:** Practice continuous strokes with each hand individually, ensuring the stick remains in constant motion with even and consistent lateral movement (no [[Accents|accents]]). The wrist moves in sync with the rebounding stick but should not be the primary force.
7.  **Rebound Height and Speed:** The height of the rebound is directly related to the speed of the stroke.
    *   **Full Stroke Position:** For slower rhythms (e.g., [[Eighth_Note]]), the stick should bounce back to a full stroke position (high).
    *   **Half Stroke Position:** For faster rhythms (e.g., [[16th_Notes]]), the stick should bounce back to a half stroke position (lower).

**Relationship to Other Techniques:**
The [[Gladstone_Technique]] is often contrasted with the [[Control_Stroke]] or [[Down_Stroke]], where the natural rebound is intentionally stopped. Mastering the [[Gladstone_Technique]] is essential for developing fluid [[Stick_Control]], dynamic range, and preventing injury.

**Pro-Tip:** Focus on relaxation and letting gravity do the work. Any tension will inhibit the natural rebound. Daily practice of the continuous stroke exercise (Ex 1.10 in the source material) for five minutes per hand is highly recommended to internalize this fundamental motion.
