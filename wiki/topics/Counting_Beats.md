# Counting Beats
Once the [[Meter]] of a piece of music is identified, a systematic counting method is applied to represent the progression of each [[Measure]]. This system helps musicians internalize the rhythm and accurately perform musical passages.

## Standard Counting Systems
*   **Two-[[Beat|Beat]] Meter**: Measures are typically counted as "1 - 2 | 1 - 2 |".
*   **Three-[[Beat|Beat]] Meter**: Measures are typically counted as "1 - 2 - 3 | 1 - 2 - 3 |".
*   **Four-[[Beat|Beat]] Meter**: Measures are typically counted as "1 - 2 - 3 - 4 | 1 - 2 - 3 - 4 |".

The vertical bar `|` indicates the start of a new measure.

## Pro-Tip for Drummers
*   **Vocalization**: Always vocalize your counts, especially when learning new rhythms or exercises. This helps to internalize the [[Pulse|pulse]] and develop a strong internal clock.
*   **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]]**: As you advance, you'll learn to count subdivisions within each [[Beat|beat]] (e.g., "1-e-&-a"). This precision is vital for complex rhythms and fills.
*   **Metronome Practice**: Practice counting along with a [[Metronome]] to develop impeccable timing and a consistent [[Tempo]].
