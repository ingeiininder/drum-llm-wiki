# Tied Eighth Notes
[[Ties]] are commonly applied to [[Eighth Notes]] to extend their [[Duration]], often creating [[Syncopated Rhythms]] or allowing notes to sustain across [[Bar Line]]s. This is particularly important for rhythms that emphasize [[Upbeat]] or fall between the main beats.

**Common Scenarios:**
*   **Extending Across a Bar Line**: An eighth note on an upbeat (e.g., the "&" of beat 4) can be tied to a note in the next measure (e.g., a [[Whole Note]]), creating a sustained sound that carries over the bar line. For example, an eighth note (0.5 beats) tied to a whole note (4 beats) results in a 4.5-beat duration.
*   **Creating Syncopation**: Tying an eighth note to a subsequent note can shift the rhythmic emphasis, making a note sound on an upbeat and sustain over a [[Downbeat]].
*   **Clarity in Notation**: Sometimes, a [[Quarter Note]] on an upbeat might be visually split into two tied eighth notes to clearly show its relationship to the surrounding subdivisions and maintain readability, especially when adhering to rules like the [[2_3_Rule]].

**Pro-Tip:** When reading tied eighth notes, pay close attention to the exact subdivision where the first note is struck and how long its combined duration extends. Use a [[Metronome]] and practice counting the subdivisions (e.g., "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &") to ensure precise timing of the attack and the sustained value.
