# AABA Form
[[AABA_Form]] is a common 32-bar song structure prevalent in [[Jazz|jazz]] standards, pop, and [[Rock|rock]] music. It consists of four 8-bar sections:
*   **A Section:** The main melodic and harmonic theme is presented.
*   **A Section (Repeat):** The main theme is repeated, often with slight variations.
*   **B Section:** A contrasting section, often referred to as the "bridge," which introduces new melodic or harmonic material.
*   **A Section (Return):** The main theme returns, bringing the song back to its familiar foundation.

**Pro-Tip:** Understanding [[AABA_Form]] is crucial for drummers to effectively "[[Marking_a_Song's_Form|mark the form]]" with dynamic and sonic changes, guiding the listener through the song's journey. Each section often calls for distinct [[Letter_A_Section_Drum_Articulations]] and [[Letter_B_Section_Drum_Articulations]].
