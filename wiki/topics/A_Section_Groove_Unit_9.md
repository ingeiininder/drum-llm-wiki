# A Section Groove (Unit 9)
The A section features a distinct two-bar groove that forms the foundation of this part of the song.

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Bass Drum**: A syncopated pattern that provides rhythmic drive. It should be played strong, but with the beater *off* the head. This technique allows for maximum resonance and a full, round tone without choking the drum.
*   **Snare Drum**: A solid snare hit occurs on beat 1 of every other bar. This creates a strong, foundational pulse that anchors the groove. The sound should be a warm, center snare hit – avoid [[Rimshot]]s unless specifically indicated, as they produce a different timbre.
*   **Hi-Hat**: The hi-hat should be played closed and consistently throughout the section. There are no accented notes on the hi-hat, emphasizing a steady, driving eighth-note pulse.

**Performance Notes:**
*   **Consistency**: The closed hi-hat needs to be exceptionally consistent in volume and timing to provide a solid rhythmic bed.
*   **Bass Drum Control**: Mastering the "beater off the head" technique for the [[Bass Drum]] is crucial for achieving the desired strong yet resonant sound. Practice this motion to ensure the beater rebounds immediately after striking.
*   **Snare Tone**: Aim for a full, rich sound from the center of the [[Snare Drum]]. Experiment with stick placement to find the "sweet spot" for a warm tone.

This groove, while seemingly simple, requires precise execution of dynamics and articulation to achieve the intended feel.
