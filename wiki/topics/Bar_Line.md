# Bar Line
A bar line is a vertical line used in [[Music_Notation]] to divide [[The_Staff]] into segments called [[Measure_Music_Notation|measures]]. These lines are fundamental for organizing musical time and rhythm.

## Function
*   **[[Measure|Measure]] Division**: Single bar lines clearly delineate the boundaries of each [[Measure|measure]].
*   **[[Meter|Meter]] Reset**: Bar lines correspond with and effectively "reset" the [[Time_Signature|meter]] for each new [[Measure|measure]], ensuring that the correct number of beats or [[Rhythmic_Values|rhythmic values]] are contained within each segment.
*   **Readability**: They provide a visual structure that helps musicians keep their place while reading music and understand the rhythmic grouping.

## Types of Bar Lines
While the single bar line is the most common, other types serve specific structural purposes:
*   **[[Double_Bar_Line]]**: Used to separate larger sections of music within a single piece.
*   **[[Final_Bar_Line]]**: Placed at the very end of a piece of music to indicate its conclusion.
*   **[[Repeat_Sign]]**: A specialized bar line with two dots, indicating that a section of music should be repeated.
