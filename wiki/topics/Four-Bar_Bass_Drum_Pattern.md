# Four-Bar Bass Drum Pattern
A `[[Four-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` refers to a rhythmic sequence played on the `[[Bass_Drum]]` that repeats every four measures. This longer pattern provides significant rhythmic variation and interest compared to a simpler one or two-bar repetition, allowing for more complex and evolving `[[Grooves]]`.

**Role in Drumming:**
*   **[[Phrasing|Phrasing]]**: It helps define the `[[Eight-Bar_Phrase]]` or longer sections of a song, often aligning with `[[Guitar_Riffs]]` or `[[Chord_Structure]]`.
*   **Dynamic Build-up**: A longer pattern can build tension or release energy over its [[Duration|duration]].
*   **Complexity**: It adds a layer of rhythmic sophistication to a `[[Groove]]`, especially when combined with `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Grooves]]`.

`[[Ex_4.1]]` is an example of an 'A' section groove that incorporates a `[[Four-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` within a `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]`. Mastering these longer patterns requires strong coordination and a good sense of musical form.
