# Four-Way Coordination: Accented Snare and Bass Drum

This study focuses on establishing individual dynamic control and independence by pairing [[Snare Drum]] accents with specific [[Bass Drum]] patterns. The primary objective is to maintain a consistent [[RLRL Sticking]] (alternating) on the snare while the bass drum follows or deviates from the accented notes.

### Core Concepts
- **Dynamic Permutation**: The exercises challenge the player to execute accents both in unison with the bass drum and independently of it. This develops the ability to "decouple" the limbs.
- **Subdivision Focus**: Patterns typically move through 8th-note and 16th-note variations.
- **Hi-Hat Integration**: Once the snare and bass drum relationship is stable, a [[Hi-Hat Ostinato]] (typically 8th-note stepping) is added to complete the four-way coordination.

### Pro-Tips and Pitfalls
- **Mirror Work**: Use a mirror to monitor stick heights. Accents should have a clear height differential compared to unaccented notes (ghost notes).
- **Four-Part Counting**: Verbally count "1 e & a" while playing to ensure the rhythmic placement of the bass drum does not shift the snare's timing.
- **Common Pitfall**: Avoid "flamming" between the accented snare and the bass drum. They should land exactly together for a unified vertical alignment.
