# Soul-[[Funk|Funk]] Drumming

**Soul-[[Funk|Funk]]** is a genre that prioritizes memorable melodies, [[Funk|funk]] instrumentation, and a definitive, swinging backbeat. It blends the professional craft of songwriting with [[Jazz|jazz]] improvisation.

### Historical Context
The style is epitomized by the 1960s and 70s work of Stevie Wonder (notably *Innervisions* and *Songs in the Key of Life*), Sly and the Family Stone, and modern practitioners like Amy Winehouse and Sharon Jones & The Dap Kings.

### Technical Foundation
Soul-[[Funk|Funk]] often utilizes the [[Jazz_Ride_Pattern]] but "translates" it into a [[Swung_16th_Phrasing]] context. This creates a sophisticated, rolling feel that is more syncopated than standard [[Rock|rock]] but more "locked" than traditional [[Jazz|jazz]].

### Key Elements
- **[[Ghost_Notes|Ghost Notes]]**: Essential for the "bubbling" feel of the [[Groove|groove]].
- **Snare Articulation**: Heavy use of the [[Snare_Rim_Shot]] on the backbeat (usually [[Beat|beat]] 3 in half-time or 2 and 4).
- **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: Played with the shoulder of the stick on the edge for a "thick" tone.
