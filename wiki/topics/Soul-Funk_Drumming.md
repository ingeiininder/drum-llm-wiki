# Soul-Funk Drumming

**Soul-Funk** is a genre that prioritizes memorable melodies, funk instrumentation, and a definitive, swinging backbeat. It blends the professional craft of songwriting with jazz improvisation.

### Historical Context
The style is epitomized by the 1960s and 70s work of Stevie Wonder (notably *Innervisions* and *Songs in the Key of Life*), Sly and the Family Stone, and modern practitioners like Amy Winehouse and Sharon Jones & The Dap Kings.

### Technical Foundation
Soul-Funk often utilizes the [[Jazz Ride Pattern]] but "translates" it into a [[Swung 16th Phrasing]] context. This creates a sophisticated, rolling feel that is more syncopated than standard rock but more "locked" than traditional jazz.

### Key Elements
- **Ghost Notes**: Essential for the "bubbling" feel of the groove.
- **Snare Articulation**: Heavy use of the [[Snare Rim Shot]] on the backbeat (usually beat 3 in half-time or 2 and 4).
- **Hi-Hat**: Played with the shoulder of the stick on the edge for a "thick" tone.
