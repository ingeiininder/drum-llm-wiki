# Ghost Notes (Snare Drum)

**Ghost Notes** (also known as notes at a *mezzo-piano* or *pianissimo* level) are low-volume notes played on the snare drum to fill out the rhythmic grid.

### The "Drop" Stroke
To achieve the proper ghost note sound in [[Soul-Funk Drumming]]:
1. **Loosen the fingers**: Allow the stick to drop naturally toward the head.
2. **Avoid Wrist Motion**: Unlike a standard stroke, the ghost note is a "drop" rather than a powered "hit."
3. **Placement**: Strike the center of the snare drum head with the tip of the stick.

### Musical Purpose
Ghost notes should be **"felt rather than heard."** They provide a rhythmic "undercurrent" that makes a groove feel more fluid and "funky."

### Common Pitfalls
- **Flamming**: Accidentally hitting the ghost note at the same time as the bass drum (unintentional unison).
- **Volume**: Playing them too loudly, which clutters the mix and obscures the backbeat.
