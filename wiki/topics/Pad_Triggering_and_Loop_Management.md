# Pad Triggering and Loop Management

Modern performance often requires the integration of electronic elements, such as starting and stopping loops or triggering samples via a multi-pad (e.g., Yamaha DTX-Multi12).

### Execution Guidelines
1.  **Precision Timing**: Triggers must be struck perfectly in sync with the [[Metronome]] or the internal clock of the loop. Even a slight flam between the stick and the click will cause the loop to drift from the band.
2.  **Hand Management**: Transitions between acoustic surfaces and electronic pads must be choreographed. For example, moving the left hand from the [[Hi-Hat]] to a pad to play syncopated rhythms while the right hand maintains the upbeat pulse.
3.  **Stopping Loops**: Dedicated pads are often assigned to "Stop" or "Mute" functions. In a [[Drum Break]], the first motion is often striking the "Stop" pad to silence the loop before executing acoustic fills.

### Professional Context
When using pads, ensure the "Sensitivity" and "Threshold" settings are adjusted to prevent "crosstalk" (where hitting one pad or an acoustic drum accidentally triggers another sound).
