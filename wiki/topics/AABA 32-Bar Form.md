# AABA 32-Bar Form
A classic musical structure often employed in R&B/pop charts (e.g., in the tradition of Maroon 5 and John Mayer). This form is comprised of two different sections, A and B, each assigned a rehearsal letter. Both sections represent three key musical attributes:
1.  A different rhythm (or groove).
2.  An eight-bar phrase.
3.  A particular riff and chord structure.

The A section occurs twice in a row, totaling 16 bars, and is often notated as two eight-bar phrases utilizing [[First and Second Endings]]. The form is typically navigated with repeats and can include a [[D.C. al Fine]] instruction.
