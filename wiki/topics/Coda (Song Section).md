# Coda (Song Section)
The "Coda" (Italian for "tail") is a concluding section of a musical piece, often serving as an outro or a final statement. Its function can vary: it might introduce new material, act as a [[Bridge (Song Section)]], or present a condensed version of earlier sections.

In this curriculum, the [[Coda (Song Section)]] initially functions as a [[Bridge (Song Section)]], introducing a [[New Groove]] and a [[New Harmonic Structure]]. Drummers will experience a [[Playing Surface Switch]] from the [[Hi-Hat]] to the [[Ride Cymbal]] during this part. The bridge section within the coda lasts for five measures and includes an [[Extended Ensemble Figure]].

Additionally, a coda can act as a "mini version" of the entire tune, incorporating elements from previous sections. For example, after the initial bridge, the coda might move into an [[Eight-Bar Phrase]] version of the [[Letter B (Rehearsal Mark)]] section and a shortened version of the [[Letter A]] section, often featuring a [[Sloshy Hi-Hat]] or [[Ride Cymbal]] groove.

**Pro-Tip:** The coda is often where a drummer can make a final impactful statement. Be prepared for shifts in [[Groove]], dynamics, and instrumentation. If the coda revisits earlier themes, consider how to interpret them with a sense of finality or resolution. Pay close attention to ensemble figures and ensure your playing supports the overall conclusion of the song.
