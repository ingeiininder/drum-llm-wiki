# AABA 32-Bar Form
The `[[AABA 32-Bar Form]]` is a classic and widely used song structure, particularly prevalent in jazz standards and pop music. It consists of four eight-bar sections: two identical 'A' sections, followed by a contrasting 'B' section (often called the bridge), and then a return to the 'A' section.

This form provides a familiar framework for listeners, offering repetition with a moment of departure before returning to the main theme. It is often used as a point of comparison when introducing other structures, such as the `[[ABAB 32-Bar Form]]`, highlighting how different arrangements of sections can create varied musical journeys within a similar overall length.
