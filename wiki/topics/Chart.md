# Chart

A Drum Chart (or Drum Part) is a technical document that serves as a musical roadmap for a performance. It provides the essential information a drummer needs to execute a song correctly, including grooves, navigation markers, dynamic changes, and ensemble figures.

## Roadmap Navigation
Professional charts use standardized Italian terminology and symbols to manage the song structure and save space on the page:

- **[[Rehearsal_Letters|Rehearsal Letters]] (A, B, C)**: Labels used to identify major song sections (e.g., Letter A is often the "Verse," and Letter B is the "Chorus").
- **Double Bar Lines**: Indicate the end of one section and the beginning of another.
- **[[Repeat_Signs|Repeat Signs]]**: Enclose a musical phrase that is to be played again.
- **Single and Multi-Bar Repeats**: A symbol telling the drummer to play the previous 1, 2, or 4 measures again. This keeps the chart "clean" and easy to scan.
- **Navigation Shortcuts**:
    - **D.C. (Da Capo)**: Go back to the very beginning.
    - **D.S. (Dal Segno)**: Go back to "The Sign" (Segno).
    - **Coda**: A specific, separate ending section.
    - **Fine**: Italian for "The End."

## Song Forms
Common musical structures found in charts include:
- **AABA (32-Bar Form)**: A classic structure with two similar sections (AA), a contrasting bridge (B), and a return to the main theme (A). 
- **12-Bar [[Blues|Blues]]**: An **Open Form** that repeats in 12-bar increments (I-IV-V chord progression). Drummers often signal the transition between "Head" and "Solo" sections within this form.

## Interpretation Tactics
Many drum charts are "Lead Sheets," meaning they only provide the basic skeleton of the song. The drummer must apply their own **Musicality**:
- **Don't Stare at the Chart**: Professional drummers use the chart as a reference, "checking in" every 4 to 8 bars while focusing their main attention on the **Time**, **Sound**, and the rest of the band.
- **Filling the Gaps**: Unless a fill is specifically "Written Out," the drummer is expected to choose a fill that stylistically fits the transition.
- **Marking the Form**: The chart guides the drummer on when to switch surfaces (e.g., from **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]** to **[[Ride_Cymbal|Ride Cymbal]]**) to help the band and audience feel the section changes.

## Specialized Notation
- **Common Time (C)**: A shorthand for the 4/4 time signature.
- **[[Multi-Rests|Multi-Rests]]**: A thick horizontal bar with a number above it, indicating multiple measures of silence for the drummer.
- **On Cue**: An instruction to wait for a physical signal from another band member before proceeding to the next section or ending.
