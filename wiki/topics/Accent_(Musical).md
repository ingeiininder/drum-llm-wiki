# Accent (Musical)
An [[Accent_(Musical)]] is an emphasis or stress placed on a particular note or [[Beat|beat]], making it stand out from the surrounding notes. In drumming, [[Accents|accents]] are fundamental for creating [[Groove_(Music)]], shaping [[Phrasing_(Music)]], and adding [[Dynamics_(Music)]] and expression to a performance.

**Notation:**
[[Accents|Accents]] are typically indicated by a horizontal wedge symbol (>) placed above or below the [[Note_Head]]. Other accent marks exist, such as the marcato (^) for a stronger, shorter accent, or [[Tenuto|tenuto]] (-) for a sustained emphasis.

**Execution on Drums:**
On the drums, an [[Accent_(Musical)]] is usually played with greater force or from a higher [[Stick_Height|stick height]] (e.g., a [[Full_Stroke]] or [[Down_Stroke]] in the [[Moeller_Technique]]), resulting in a louder sound. The contrast between accented and unaccented notes (often played as [[Ghost_Notes]] or tap strokes) is what gives rhythm its definition and feel.

**Communication:**
Drummers often refer to specific accent placements using [[Counting_Sixteenth_Notes]] syllables, such as "the accent occurs on the 'e' of 3," which precisely indicates the rhythmic location within a [[Beat|beat]].

**Pro-Tip:**
Practice [[Accents|accents]] by contrasting them sharply with unaccented notes. This develops control over your stick heights and [[Dynamics|dynamics]], allowing you to create a more musical and expressive performance. Start with simple [[Accent_Patterns|accent patterns]] and gradually incorporate them into more complex rhythms.
