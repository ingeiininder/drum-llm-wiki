# Accent (Musical)
An [[Accent (Musical)]] is an emphasis or stress placed on a particular note or beat, making it stand out from the surrounding notes. In drumming, accents are fundamental for creating [[Groove (Music)]], shaping [[Phrasing (Music)]], and adding [[Dynamics (Music)]] and expression to a performance.

**Notation:**
Accents are typically indicated by a horizontal wedge symbol (>) placed above or below the [[Note Head]]. Other accent marks exist, such as the marcato (^) for a stronger, shorter accent, or tenuto (-) for a sustained emphasis.

**Execution on Drums:**
On the drums, an [[Accent (Musical)]] is usually played with greater force or from a higher stick height (e.g., a [[Full Stroke]] or [[Down Stroke]] in the [[Moeller Technique]]), resulting in a louder sound. The contrast between accented and unaccented notes (often played as [[Ghost Notes]] or tap strokes) is what gives rhythm its definition and feel.

**Communication:**
Drummers often refer to specific accent placements using [[Counting Sixteenth Notes]] syllables, such as "the accent occurs on the 'e' of 3," which precisely indicates the rhythmic location within a beat.

**Pro-Tip:**
Practice accents by contrasting them sharply with unaccented notes. This develops control over your stick heights and dynamics, allowing you to create a more musical and expressive performance. Start with simple accent patterns and gradually incorporate them into more complex rhythms.
