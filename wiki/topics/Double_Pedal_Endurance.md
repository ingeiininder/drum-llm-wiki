# Double Pedal Endurance

Developing speed and longevity with [[Double_Bass_Drumming]] requires a combination of technical facility and physical conditioning. In high-intensity genres like [[Metal]], endurance is as critical as raw speed.

### Physical Conditioning
According to Steve Kilgallon (*Double Pedal Metal*), off-kit exercises can significantly improve drumming performance:
1. **Running**: Builds cardiovascular health and leg stamina.
2. **Calf-Raising Exercises**: Strengthens the gastrocnemius and soleus muscles, which are vital for the "heel-up" or "swivel" techniques.
3. **Jumping Rope**: Improves fast-twitch muscle response and overall coordination.

### Technical Application: "Heavying Up"
A common technique to increase the perceived weight of a [[Groove|groove]] is playing **unison hits**.
- **Unison Snare and Kick**: Hitting the snare and [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]](s) at the exact same time creates a massive, "heavy" sound.
- **Empty Space**: Utilizing "air" or space between rapid double-kick bursts can make the hits that *do* land feel more impactful.

### Common Pitfalls
- **Tension**: The "chainsaw" sound in metal requires relaxation. Excessive tension in the hip flexors or ankles will lead to early fatigue and uneven timing.
- **Unbalanced Unisons**: If the snare and kick are not perfectly aligned, the [[Groove|groove]] loses its "heavy" edge and sounds "flammed."
