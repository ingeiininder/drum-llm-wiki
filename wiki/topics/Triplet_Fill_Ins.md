# Triplet Fill-Ins
Triplet fill-ins are used to maintain momentum and "time feel" between [[Ensemble Figures]] in a swung or jazz context.

### Technical Approaches
1.  **Right-Hand Lead**: The right hand stays on the ride cymbal (often "shouldering" the edge) while the left hand fills in the remaining triplet partials on the snare. This is highly effective in **Jazz Combo** settings.
2.  **Hand-to-Hand (Alternating)**: Continuous `R L R L R L` triplets. This provides a denser, more driving sound suitable for **Rock Shuffle** or **Blues** settings.

### Practice Rotation
To master these, practice a figure using three distinct passes:
*   **Pass 1**: Alternating triplets on the snare, accenting the figure.
*   **Pass 2**: Alternating triplets on the snare, playing the figure on crashes with bass drum.
*   **Pass 3**: Right-hand lead technique on the ride cymbal.

**Pro-Tip**: Use these fills to "bridge the gap" between two-bar phrases, ensuring the energy doesn't drop during the rests.
