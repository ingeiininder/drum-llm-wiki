# Timekeeping Figures

**Timekeeping figures** are rhythmic notations that indicate what other instrumentalists in the ensemble are playing. Unlike [[Ensemble_Figures]], which typically require a rhythmic "hit" or orchestration from the entire drum kit, timekeeping figures are intended to help the drummer play a "musical" and complimentary [[Time_Feel]] without interrupting the flow of the [[Groove|groove]].

### Notation and Placement
- **Staff Position**: Written above [[The_Staff|the staff]].
- **Support**: Accompanied by [[Time_Slashes]] below them within [[The_Staff|the staff]].
- **Execution**: The general drum pattern must continue. The drummer "catches" or acknowledges the rhythm within the context of the existing feel.

### Stylistic Application
1.  **[[Jazz|Jazz]]**: Most commonly used in [[Jazz|jazz]] charts to indicate horn lines or piano comps. The drummer might lightly "feather" these rhythms on the [[Ride_Cymbal]] or [[Snare_Drum]] while maintaining the [[Swing_Feel]].
2.  **[[Rock|Rock]]/Pop**: Often used to show bass line rhythms. A common technique is for the [[Bass_Drum]] to "catch" the timekeeping figure while the [[Snare_Drum]] maintains a consistent [[Back-Beat]] on 2 and 4.

### Pro-Tips
- **Do Not Overplay**: The primary goal is to maintain the [[Groove|groove]]. If a timekeeping figure is too complex, prioritize the [[Timekeeping]] over the figure.
- **Sound Production**: Pay close attention to the balance. These figures should be integrated into the texture, not played as loud [[Accents|accents]] unless specified.
