# Open Non-Buried [[Bass_Drum_Technique|Bass Drum Technique]]
The [[Open_Non-Buried_Bass_Drum_Technique]] involves striking the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] head with the beater and immediately allowing the beater to rebound away from the head. This permits the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] head to resonate freely, producing a full, open, and sustained tone.

Typically, the felt side of the beater is used to achieve a warmer, more resonant sound. A common pitfall to avoid is "felt-flutter," where the beater bounces multiple times off the head, creating an undesirable buzzing sound.

**Pro-Tip:** This technique is ideal for achieving a full, resonant [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] sound, often preferred in [[Jazz|jazz]], lighter [[Rock|rock]], or when a less aggressive attack is needed. It provides a stark contrast to [[Burying_the_Bass_Drum_Beater]], which produces a shorter, more [[Staccato|staccato]] sound. Mastering both techniques allows for a wider dynamic and timbral palette.
