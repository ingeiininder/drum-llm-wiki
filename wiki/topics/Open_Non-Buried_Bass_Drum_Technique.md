# Open Non-Buried Bass Drum Technique
The [[Open Non-Buried Bass Drum Technique]] involves striking the bass drum head with the beater and immediately allowing the beater to rebound away from the head. This permits the bass drum head to resonate freely, producing a full, open, and sustained tone.

Typically, the felt side of the beater is used to achieve a warmer, more resonant sound. A common pitfall to avoid is "felt-flutter," where the beater bounces multiple times off the head, creating an undesirable buzzing sound.

**Pro-Tip:** This technique is ideal for achieving a full, resonant bass drum sound, often preferred in jazz, lighter rock, or when a less aggressive attack is needed. It provides a stark contrast to [[Burying the Bass Drum Beater]], which produces a shorter, more staccato sound. Mastering both techniques allows for a wider dynamic and timbral palette.
