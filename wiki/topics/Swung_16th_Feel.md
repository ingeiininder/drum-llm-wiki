# Swung 16th Feel

The **Swung 16th Feel** is a rhythmic interpretation where 16th notes are performed with a triplet-based lilt rather than as even divisions of the beat.

### Notation vs. Performance
*   **Notation**: Often written as standard 16th notes for legibility.
*   **Performance**: Performed as **16th-note triplets** (sextuplets).
*   **Indicators**: Charts will use the text "Swung 16ths" or a specific equivalence symbol (two 16ths = a triplet bracketed 8th and 16th).

### Application in 6/8 Time
In a 6/8 [[RB_Pop_Ballad_Style]], the drummer must subdivide the measure as:
`1 - & - 2 - & - 3 - & - 4 - & - 5 - & - 6 - &`
Where each "&" is the third part of a triplet grouping.

---PITFALL---
Be wary of "rhythmic drift" where the feel straightens out during fills. Always maintain the internal triplet subdivision to keep the groove consistent with the rest of the ensemble.
