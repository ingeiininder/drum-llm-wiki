# Drop-Outs

**Drop-outs** are short, abrupt interruptions of a [[Groove|groove]], typically lasting one or two beats. 

### Origin
This concept originated from DJs and producers using mixing consoles or MPCs. A producer would hit the "mute" button on the drum track for a moment. Crucially, when the mute is released, the [[Groove|groove]] does not restart; it continues as if it had been playing silently in the background.

### Execution
To perform a drop-out, simply omit all notes (bass, snare, and [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]]) for the specified [[Duration|duration]]. You must maintain the internal [[Pulse|pulse]] perfectly so that when you "re-enter," the [[Groove|groove]] is exactly where it should be.

### Context
Commonly used in [[Hip-Hop_Drumming]] to create space for a vocal line or to add rhythmic surprise.
