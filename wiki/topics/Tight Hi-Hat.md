# Tight Hi-Hat
"Tight Hi-Hat" describes a specific articulation and sound produced on the [[Hi-Hat]] cymbals. It generally implies playing with the hi-hats pressed firmly together, resulting in a crisp, short, and often dry sound. This articulation is typically achieved by applying consistent pressure to the hi-hat pedal, keeping the cymbals closed.

In the context of [[A9 (Rehearsal Mark)]], a tight hi-hat provides a solid rhythmic foundation for the [[Syncopated Bass Drum (A9)]] pattern, offering clarity and precision to the groove.

**Pro-Tip:** The "tightness" of the hi-hat can be varied by the amount of pressure applied to the pedal. A truly "tight" sound is very closed and precise, often used for funk, pop, or rock grooves where clarity is paramount. Experiment with different pedal pressures to find the sweet spot for the desired sound. Ensure your foot technique is consistent to maintain an even sound throughout the phrase.
