# Flam Rudiment Variations ([[Rudimental_Recall|Rudimental Recall]])

This section focuses on expanding the [[Flams]] vocabulary by introducing hybrid and advanced rudimental patterns. Mastery of these requires precise control over "grace note" placement and stick heights.

### Key [[Rudiments|Rudiments]]
*   **Pataflafla**: A four-note pattern with flams on the first and last notes (`lR L R rL`).
*   **Inverted Flam Tap**: A variation of the flam tap where the sticking is inverted, challenging the up-stroke/down-stroke transition.
*   **Flam Drag**: Combines a flam with a drag (double) on the following note.
*   **Double/Triple Flam [[Paradiddle|Paradiddle]]**: Incorporating multiple flams into [[Paradiddle|paradiddle]] structures.
*   **Same-Hand Flam [[Accents|Accents]]**: A coordination-heavy variation where the flams and [[Accents|accents]] do not alternate hands in the traditional manner.

### Technical Focus
*   **Stick Heights**: The grace note should be played very low (approx. 1 inch from the head), while the primary stroke height depends on the desired volume/accent.
*   **Sound Quality**: Avoid "flat flams" (where both sticks hit simultaneously). There should be a slight, consistent gap between the grace note and the primary stroke.
