# Sixteenth Notes in 6/8

In [[6/8 Time]], the introduction of [[Sixteenth Notes]] doubles the density of the standard eighth-note pulse. While in 4/4 time a quarter note is divided into four sixteenths ("1 e & a"), the structure of 6/8 changes the relationship between the downbeat and the subdivision.

### Mathematical Breakdown
- There are **six eighth notes** in one measure of 6/8.
- Each eighth note (downbeat) is divided into **two sixteenth notes**.
- This results in a total of **twelve sixteenth notes** per measure, typically organized into two groups of six.

### Counting System
The standard counting method for sixteenth notes in 6/8 is:
**1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 &**

In this system:
- The numbers (1-6) land on the [[Eighth Note]] pulses.
- The "&" (ands) represent the second half of each eighth note.

### Common Phrasing (Ex 4.4)
Common rhythmic figures in this meter often involve combinations of eighths and sixteenths, such as:
- The "gallop" (one eighth followed by two sixteenths—though in 6/8, this is more accurately an eighth and its subdivision).
- Constant sixteenth-note streams used in high-energy [[Groove]] patterns or linear fills.

> [!WARNING]
> A common pitfall is treating 6/8 sixteenths like 4/4 triplets. Ensure the "&" is exactly halfway between the numbered pulses to maintain a "straight" duple subdivision within the triple meter.
