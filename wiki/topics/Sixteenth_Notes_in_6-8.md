# [[Sixteenth_Notes|Sixteenth Notes]] in 6/8

In [[6/8_Time]], the introduction of [[16th_Notes]] doubles the density of the standard eighth-note [[Pulse|pulse]]. While in 4/4 time a quarter note is divided into four sixteenths ("1 e & a"), the structure of 6/8 changes the relationship between the downbeat and the [[Subdivision|subdivision]].

### Mathematical Breakdown
- There are **six [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]]** in one [[Measure|measure]] of 6/8.
- Each eighth note (downbeat) is divided into **two [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]]**.
- This results in a total of **twelve [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]]** per [[Measure|measure]], typically organized into two groups of six.

### Counting System
The standard counting method for [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]] in 6/8 is:
**1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 &**

In this system:
- The numbers (1-6) land on the [[Eighth_Note]] pulses.
- The "&" (ands) represent the second half of each eighth note.

### Common [[Phrasing|Phrasing]] (Ex 4.4)
Common rhythmic figures in this [[Meter|meter]] often involve combinations of eighths and sixteenths, such as:
- The "gallop" (one eighth followed by two sixteenths—though in 6/8, this is more accurately an eighth and its [[Subdivision|subdivision]]).
- Constant sixteenth-note streams used in high-energy [[Groove]] patterns or linear fills.

> [!WARNING]
> A common pitfall is treating 6/8 sixteenths like 4/4 [[Triplets|triplets]]. Ensure the "&" is exactly halfway between the numbered pulses to maintain a "straight" duple [[Subdivision|subdivision]] within the triple [[Meter|meter]].
