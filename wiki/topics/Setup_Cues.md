# Setup Cues

In an ensemble or big band setting, "Setup Cues" (or fundamental setups) are short drum figures used to signal upcoming [[Ensemble_Figures]] to the rest of the band.

### Function
Setups act as an audible "count-in" for syncopated hits, especially those occurring on the "and" (&) of a [[Beat|beat]]. Without a setup, the band may struggle to enter together, particularly at slow or very fast tempos.

### Execution
*   **Single-Note Setups**: A simple snare or [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] hit immediately preceding the ensemble figure.
*   **Placement**: If the band has a hit on the "&" of 2, the drummer might play a setup on the downbeat of 2.
*   **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: The setup should be loud enough for the band to hear but should not overshadow the actual ensemble hit.

### Pro-Tip
Always look at the horn players or the conductor when delivering a setup. Your physical movement (body language) is just as much a cue as the sound of the drum.
