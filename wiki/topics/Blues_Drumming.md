# [[Blues|Blues]] Drumming

**[[Blues|Blues]]** is a foundational American roots genre that evolved from African-American work songs and spirituals. It is characterized by the **[[12-Bar_Blues]]** structure and a heavy emphasis on **triplet-based feels**. [[Blues|Blues]] drumming is less about complexity and more about **feel, sound, and ensemble communication**.

## The Heart of the [[Blues|Blues]]: Triplet [[Subdivision|Subdivision]]
Unlike the "straight" [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] of Pop or [[Rock|Rock]], [[Blues|Blues]] is built on groups of three.
- **[[12-8_Feel]] (Slow [[Blues|Blues]])**: Every [[Beat|beat]] consists of three equal eighth-note [[Triplets|triplets]]. The Ride cymbal typically plays a continuous triplet [[Pulse|pulse]].
- **[[Shuffle_Feel]] ([[Shuffle|Shuffle]])**: A "broken" triplet feel where the middle note of each triplet is omitted. It creates a "skipping" or "bouncing" momentum.

## Key [[Articulations|Articulations]] & Sounds

### 1. The Cross-Stick (Side Stick)
Commonly used in slow [[Blues|blues]] verses and ballads to provide a warm, percussive backbeat.
- **[[Cross-Stick|The Sweet Spot]]**: Striking the rim with the butt end of the stick while the tip [[Rests|rests]] "off-center" on the head (typically between 8 and 9 o'clock).
- **Physical Execution**: Turning the stick around to use the thicker end for a deeper, fuller sound.

### 2. The Ride Cymbal [[Pulse|Pulse]]
The ride cymbal acts as the "blanket of sound" for the entire band.
- **[[Ride_Cymbal_Articulation|Body vs. Bell]]**: Playing halfway between the edge and bell for a balanced mix of definition and wash.
- **[[Eighth-Note_Triplets]]**: Maintaining the [[Pulse|pulse]] without "straightening out" into [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]].

### 3. The [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]: Open & Mellow
- **[[Bass_Drum_Technique|Open "Off the Head" Stroke]]**: In many [[Blues|blues]] settings, the beater is allowed to bounce off the head to allow the drum to resonate.
- **[[Feathering]]**: Playing the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] so quietly that it is "felt but not heard," locking in perfectly with the double bass.

## Professional Communication: Open Form
[[Blues|Blues]] is often performed as an **[[Open_Form]]**, meaning the 12-bar structure repeats until a signal is given.
- **[[The_Head]]**: The main melody of the tune. Drummers typically play the **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]** during the head.
- **[[A_Chorus]]**: One full time through the form (often for a solo). Drummers typically switch to the **[[Ride_Cymbal]]** to "lift" the energy.
- **[[Cue]]**: A visual signal, such as patting the top of the head, to notify the band to return to the melody or head out.
