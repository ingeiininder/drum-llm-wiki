# Practice Suggestions (Unit 6 - Chapter 3)
This section outlines homework assignments and general practice suggestions to reinforce the concepts covered in [[Unit 6 - Chapter 3: Bass Drum and Hi-Hat Coordination Part 1]] and other foundational techniques.

## Homework Assignments
*   **Ex 6.1 thru Ex 6.11**: Be able to play these exercises with the specified sticking variations (refer to page 40 of the original curriculum).
*   **Master Studies**: Be able to play exercises 1-12 from pages 13 and 14 of "Master Studies" (likely referring to the book by Joe Morello).
*   **Bass Drum & Hi-Hat Coordination**: Practice the coordination exercises without holding onto anything for balance. This reinforces the importance of [[Balance on the Drumset]].

## Practice Suggestions
*   **Lesson Review**: Read the entire lesson at least twice to fully grasp the concepts.
*   **Metronome Use**: Utilize an eighth-note triplet click, especially when working with [[Chapter 1: Double Stroke Technique]] (from Unit 7, but mentioned here for general practice).
*   **Daily Exercise Focus**:
    *   Play Ex 6.1 thru Ex 6.11 for two minutes each, every day.
    *   Focus on creating a significant [[Dynamic Spread]] between accented and unaccented notes.
*   **Master Studies Rotation**: Every day, play four of the twelve "Master Studies" exercises for two minutes each. Choose a different set of four exercises each day to ensure comprehensive coverage.
*   **Ruff Combinations**: Choose two [[Ruff Combinations]] exercises (e.g., from [[Five-Stroke Ruff Combinations]] or [[Seven-Stroke Ruff Combinations]]) and play them both for three minutes every day.
*   **Bass Drum & Hi-Hat Coordination**: As you work through these exercises, consciously focus on your balance resting on your seat. This will enable you to produce a "chippy" [[Hi-Hat]] sound and an authoritative [[Bass Drum]] performance.
*   **Review**: Regularly review areas from previous units that require further attention or refinement.

### Pro-Tip
Consistency is key in drumming practice. Even short, focused sessions daily are more effective than infrequent, long ones. Use a practice journal to track your progress, note areas for improvement, and plan your daily routine. This structured approach will accelerate your development.
