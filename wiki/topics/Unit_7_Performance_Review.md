# Unit 7 Performance Review

This review summarizes the key elements for performing the [[12-Bar Blues Open Form]] in Unit 7, emphasizing the integration of [[Groove_Assignment_Unit_7|groove]], [[Form_Assignment_Unit_7|form]], and [[Form_Orchestration_12-Bar_Blues|orchestration]].

## Key Elements Reviewed

1.  **Groove Assignment**: Your teacher will assign you a specific [[Drum Grooves|groove]] for both the [[Head_Music_Term|head]] sections and the [[Chorus_Jazz_Blues_Term|choruses]] of solo.
    *   *Example*: If [[Groove #1: Four on the Floor]] is assigned for the head and [[Groove #2: Syncopated]] for the choruses.
    *   *Orchestration*: The head (Groove 1) would be played on the [[Hi-Hat]], and the choruses (Groove 2) on the [[Ride Cymbal]].
2.  **Form Assignment**: You will also be given a specific form to follow.
    *   *Example*: Playing one head, two choruses of solo, and one head out, taking the [[Coda_Music|coda]] on the [[Cue (Music)|cue]].
    *   *Translation*: This means 12 bars of melody (Groove 1 on the hi-hat), followed by 24 bars of solo choruses (Groove 2 on the ride cymbal), and then one 12-bar head out (Groove 1 on the hi-hat), transitioning to the coda sign for the ending.

## Integrated Performance Example

Combining the example assignments above, a performance would look like this:

*   **Head In**: 12 bars, playing Groove 1 on the [[Hi-Hat]].
*   **Solo Choruses**: 24 bars (two 12-bar choruses), playing Groove 2 on the [[Ride Cymbal]] (with [[Hi-Hat Foot]] moving).
*   **Head Out**: 12 bars, playing Groove 1 on the [[Hi-Hat]], leading to the [[Coda_Music|coda]] on cue.

## Pro-Tips for Review

*   **Holistic Understanding**: Ensure you understand how all these elements (groove, form, orchestration) work together seamlessly.
*   **Practice Transitions**: Focus heavily on the transitions between sections (e.g., hi-hat to ride, Groove 1 to Groove 2).
*   **Internalize the Form**: Develop a strong internal sense of the 12-bar cycle to navigate the form without constant chart reading.
*   **Listen and Adapt**: Be prepared to listen to the band and adapt your playing to support the melody during heads and the soloist during choruses.
