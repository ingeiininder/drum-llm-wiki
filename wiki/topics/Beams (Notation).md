# Beams (Notation)
Beams are thick horizontal lines used in musical notation to visually group multiple [[Eighth Notes]] (or smaller subdivisions like [[Sixteenth Notes]]) together. Their primary purpose is to replace individual [[Flags (Notation)]] on notes, enhancing the readability and clarity of rhythmic patterns, especially in faster tempos.

Instead of each eighth note having its own flag, a beam connects them, typically in groups of two or four within a [[4-4_Time_Signature]]. This grouping helps musicians quickly identify the beat and the rhythmic pulse.

**Example Groupings:**
*   **Groups of Two:** Often used to clearly delineate each beat (e.g., "1 &", "2 &").
*   **Groups of Four:** Commonly used to group a full measure or half-measure of eighth notes, emphasizing the overall flow.

**Professional Insight:**
While beams simplify reading, it's crucial to remember that the underlying rhythmic value of each note remains the same. Drummers should practice counting these beamed groups out loud (e.g., "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &") while maintaining a steady [[Foot Tap]] on the [[Downbeat]] to internalize the rhythm accurately. Misinterpreting beam groupings can lead to rhythmic inaccuracies, so always subdivide mentally or verbally.
