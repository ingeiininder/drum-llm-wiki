# Eighth Note Notation and Values
The [[Eighth Note]] is a fundamental [[Rhythmic Duration]] in modern music and a cornerstone for understanding more complex rhythms. It represents a division of the [[Quarter Note]] into two equal parts, effectively doubling the rhythmic density within a given [[Beat]].

**Visual Representation:**
An eighth note visually resembles a [[Quarter Note]] but with an added [[Note Flag]] extending from its [[Note Stem]]. When multiple eighth notes appear consecutively, their flags are often connected by a horizontal beam for easier reading.

**Rhythmic Value:**
*   An eighth note is equal to **one-half of a [[Beat]]**.
*   In common time (4/4), where a [[Quarter Note]] receives one beat, an eighth note receives half a beat.
*   There are two eighth notes for every one quarter note.
*   In a 4/4 [[Measure]], there can be a maximum of eight eighth notes, hence the name "eighth note" (it takes up one-eighth of the measure's total duration).

**Importance:**
Mastering the eighth note is crucial for drummers as it:
*   **Expands Rhythmic Vocabulary**: Allows for more intricate and grooving patterns beyond quarter notes.
*   **Foundation for Subdivision**: It is the first step in understanding and internalizing [[Subdivision]], which is critical for rhythmic accuracy and feel.
*   **Prepares for Faster Rhythms**: Provides the basis for understanding [[Sixteenth Note]]s and other smaller rhythmic values.

**Pro-Tip:**
When learning eighth notes, focus on developing a strong internal [[Eighth Note Pulse]]. This means feeling the "and" between each numbered beat. Practice [[Counting Eighth Notes]] out loud ("1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &") while playing or tapping your foot to solidify this internal rhythm.
