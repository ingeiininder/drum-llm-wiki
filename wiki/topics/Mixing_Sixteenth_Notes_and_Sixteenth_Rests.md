# Mixing [[Sixteenth_Notes|Sixteenth Notes]] and Sixteenth [[Rests|Rests]]
[[Mixing_Sixteenth_Notes_and_Sixteenth_Rests]] is the practice of combining [[Sixteenth_Note]]s and [[Sixteenth_Rest]]s within a [[Beat|beat]] or [[Measure|measure]] to create a vast array of [[Rhythmic_Permutations]] and patterns. This is a core skill for developing complex [[Groove_(Music)]]s, fills, and [[Syncopated_Rhythms|syncopated rhythms]].

**Notation Clarity:**
To maintain readability, a [[Double_Beam]] is typically used to group four [[Sixteenth_Note]]s, [[Sixteenth_Rest]]s, or a combination thereof within every [[Beat|beat]]. These beams may extend over [[Rests|rests]] in some cases to visually maintain the grouping of four subdivisions per [[Beat|beat]], even when some are silent.

**Study Aids:**
*   **[[Imaginary_Bar_Line_(Study_Aid)]]**: Visualizing a [[Bar_Line|bar line]] between every [[Beat|beat]] helps to isolate and understand the rhythmic grouping within each individual [[Beat|beat]].
*   **Systematic Practice**: The text illustrates various "clichés" or common groupings, such as [[Groups_of_Three_Sixteenth_Notes_and_One_Sixteenth_Rest]] and [[Groups_of_Two_Sixteenth_Notes_and_Two_Sixteenth_Rests]]. Practicing these systematically helps internalize the feel of different rest placements.

**Pro-Tip:**
When practicing these mixed patterns, always use [[Verbal_Counting_(Rhythm)]] (1-e-&-a) to ensure precise [[Beat_Placement]] of both notes and [[Rests|rests]]. Focus on the *feel* of the silence as much as the sound of the notes. This active approach to [[Rests|rests]] is crucial for developing a tight and accurate [[Internal_Clock_(Drumming)]].
