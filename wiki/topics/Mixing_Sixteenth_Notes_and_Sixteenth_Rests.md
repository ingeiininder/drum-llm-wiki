# Mixing Sixteenth Notes and Sixteenth Rests
[[Mixing Sixteenth Notes and Sixteenth Rests]] is the practice of combining [[Sixteenth Note]]s and [[Sixteenth Rest]]s within a beat or measure to create a vast array of [[Rhythmic Permutations]] and patterns. This is a core skill for developing complex [[Groove (Music)]]s, fills, and syncopated rhythms.

**Notation Clarity:**
To maintain readability, a [[Double Beam]] is typically used to group four [[Sixteenth Note]]s, [[Sixteenth Rest]]s, or a combination thereof within every beat. These beams may extend over rests in some cases to visually maintain the grouping of four subdivisions per beat, even when some are silent.

**Study Aids:**
*   **[[Imaginary Bar Line (Study Aid)]]**: Visualizing a bar line between every beat helps to isolate and understand the rhythmic grouping within each individual beat.
*   **Systematic Practice**: The text illustrates various "clichés" or common groupings, such as [[Groups of Three Sixteenth Notes and One Sixteenth Rest]] and [[Groups of Two Sixteenth Notes and Two Sixteenth Rests]]. Practicing these systematically helps internalize the feel of different rest placements.

**Pro-Tip:**
When practicing these mixed patterns, always use [[Verbal Counting (Rhythm)]] (1-e-&-a) to ensure precise [[Beat Placement]] of both notes and rests. Focus on the *feel* of the silence as much as the sound of the notes. This active approach to rests is crucial for developing a tight and accurate [[Internal Clock (Drumming)]].
