# Funk-Rock

Funk-Rock is a hybrid subgenre that emerged in the late 1960s, blending the instrumental combo and [[Loud_Amplification|loud amplification]] of [[Rock_'n'_Roll|Rock 'n' Roll]] with the syncopated, [[Groove|groove]]-heavy foundations of Funk. It evolved from earlier [[Blues-Rock|Blues-Rock]] traditions into a more riff-oriented and aggressive style.

## Historical Context

- **Origins**: Developing out of the late-1960s [[Blues-Rock|blues-rock]] explosion, Funk-Rock bands took the raw energy of rock and infused it with complex rhythmic displacement.
- **Pioneering Bands**: Early influential groups include **Cream**, **Led Zeppelin**, and **ZZ Top**, who were known for louder, riff-heavy compositions.
- **Modern Evolution**: Contemporary Funk-Rock is exemplified by artists like the **John Mayer Trio**, featuring drummers like **Steve Jordan** who master high-tension, syncopated grooves.

## Musical Characteristics

- **Riff-Orientation**: Unlike standard pop-rock, Funk-Rock often centers around a repetitive, highly syncopated musical riff played in unison by the guitar and bass.
- **Syncopated Ghost-Notes**: Funk-Rock grooves frequently utilize advanced [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] **Ghost-Notes** and complex [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] patterns to create a "pushed" feel.
- **Advanced Techniques**: The genre often incorporates:
    - [[Metric_Modulation]]
    - [[Extended_Fills]]
    - [[Syncopated_Figures]]
