# Inverted Three-Stroke Ruff
The [[Inverted_Three-Stroke_Ruff]] is a more challenging variation of the [[Three-Stroke_Ruff]]. Unlike the [[Standard_Three-Stroke_Ruff]] where grace notes precede the accent, in the inverted version, the unaccented notes *follow* the one accented note.

**Execution:**
The basic sticking pattern for an [[Inverted_Three-Stroke_Ruff]] is typically:
*   **R L L** (first loud, next two soft)
*   **L R R** (first loud, next two soft)

**Practice Variations:**
1.  **Slower Tempos:** Due to its increased challenge, begin practicing this rudiment at slower tempos to ensure precision and control.
    *   *Example (Ex 4.3):* R L L R L R L L R L R R L L R L R L (Accent on the first note of each group, followed by two soft notes)
2.  **[[Accents|Accents]] on Upbeats:** Practice placing the accented notes on the "ands" or upbeat [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]]. This requires strong internal time and [[Subdivision|subdivision]].
    *   *Example (Ex 4.4):* (Accent on the 'and' of 1, 'and' of 2, etc.)
3.  **Individual Hand Development:** Just as with the standard version, practice the [[Inverted_Three-Stroke_Ruff]] with each individual hand for focused development.
    *   *Example (based on Ex 4.3):* R R R R R R R R (Accent on the first note of each group, followed by two soft notes, repeating with only the right hand)

**Pro-Tip:** The challenge here is to make the accented note strong and then immediately follow it with two soft, controlled notes from the *same* hand (or alternating hands, depending on the specific sticking). Maintain a loose grip and utilize rebound for the unaccented notes. Ensure you are confident with the quarter note [[Pulse|pulse]] to maintain even subdivisions, especially when [[Accents|accents]] fall on upbeats.
