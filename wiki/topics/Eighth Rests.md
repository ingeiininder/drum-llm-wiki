# Eighth Rests
An [[Eighth Rest]] is a fundamental symbol in musical notation that represents a period of silence equal to the duration of an [[Eighth Note]]. In common time signatures like [[4-4_Time_Signature]], an eighth rest is equivalent to half of a beat.

**Symbol and Placement:**
The symbol for an eighth rest resembles a small number seven (7) and is typically placed in the middle of the musical staff.

**Counting:**
Eighth rests are counted in the same way as eighth notes, using the "and" (&) count. For example, in a measure of 4/4, they would be counted as "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &", with the rest falling on either the number or the "and" depending on its placement.

**Common Usage:**
Eighth rests are frequently used to create rhythmic interest and syncopation by:
*   **Silencing Downbeats:** Placing an eighth rest on a downbeat (e.g., "1", "2") can create a feeling of anticipation or a "push" to the subsequent upbeat.
*   **Exposing Upbeats:** When a downbeat is silent, the following upbeat (e.g., "&" of 1) becomes more prominent, contributing to a groovier feel.

**Professional Insight:**
Mastering rests is as vital as mastering notes. Drummers often rush through rests or fail to observe their full duration, which can significantly impact the feel and accuracy of a groove. Practice internalizing the silence of an eighth rest, ensuring it occupies its full half-beat value. Use a [[Metronome]] to develop precise timing for both played notes and observed rests. When reading [[Freestanding Eighth Notes]] and eighth rests, pay close attention to each individual beat to avoid getting "lost" in consecutive rests.
