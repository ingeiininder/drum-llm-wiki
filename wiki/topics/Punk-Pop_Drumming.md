# Punk-Pop Drumming

**Punk-Pop** (or Pop-Punk) is a post-grunge sub-style of alternative [[Rock|rock]]. It blends the melodic sensibilities and chord structures of popular music with the high-velocity tempos and aggressive delivery of punk [[Rock|rock]].

### Historical Context
The style was popularized in the 1990s by bands such as:
*   **Green Day** (featuring drummer Tre Cool)
*   **Weezer** (featuring drummer Patrick Wilson)

Modern practitioners include bands like **Fall Out Boy**, **Franz Ferdinand**, and **Panic! At The Disco**.

### Key Rhythmic Characteristics
1.  **Straight-Eighth Foundations**: Grooves often rely on driving [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] on the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] or [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]].
2.  **Four-on-the-Floor**: The use of quarter-note [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] patterns (beats 1, 2, 3, and 4) is common to provide a "driving" feel.
3.  **[[Open_Hi-Hat|Open Hi-Hat]] [[Accents|Accents]]**: Frequently placing an [[Open_Hi-Hat|open hi-hat]] on the "&" of beats to add lift and energy.
4.  **[[Double_Time_Feel]]**: A hallmark of the genre, where the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] moves to the "off-beats" (the "&" of every [[Beat|beat]]) to create a sense of doubled speed without changing the actual BPM.

### Common Structure
Punk-pop songs frequently utilize the **[[ABAB_Form]]**, consisting of two main sections (Verse/Chorus) that alternate, usually in eight-bar phrases.
