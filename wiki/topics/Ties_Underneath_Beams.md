# Ties Underneath Beams
**Ties underneath beams** refers to a notational practice where [[Ties]] are placed below [[Beamed_Notes]], typically [[Eighth_Note]] or [[16th_Notes]]. While sometimes encountered, this method can occasionally reduce readability compared to tying individual notes.

**Common Scenarios:**
*   **Grouping Notes**: A tie might encompass all notes under a single beam, indicating a continuous sound across that group.
*   **Alternative Notation**: It's an alternative way to show a sustained sound within a beamed group, though often less common than tying each note to the next.

**Preferred Practice for Readability:**
For maximum clarity, especially in complex rhythmic passages, it is generally more practical and easier to read if each note is tied individually to the next note in the sequence, rather than having one long tie spanning multiple beamed notes. This clearly shows the start and end points of each tied segment.

**Pro-Tip:** As a [[Sight-Reader]], you must be versatile enough to interpret various notational methods. However, when given the choice (e.g., if you are transcribing or arranging), always prioritize the clearest possible notation. Tying individual notes under beams usually provides better visual cues for the performer, making the rhythm easier to grasp quickly.
