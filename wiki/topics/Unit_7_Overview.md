# Unit 7 Overview: Reading, Technique, Performance
Unit 7 marks a significant progression in rhythmic studies, bridging foundational [[Drum Reading]] with advanced [[Technique]] and [[Performance]] concepts. It begins with a comprehensive [[Note Value Review]] to solidify understanding of basic subdivisions before introducing the crucial concept of [[Triplets]], particularly [[Eighth-Note Triplets]].

**Key Chapters in Unit 7:**
*   **Chapter 1: Note Value Review:** Re-examines the symmetrical division of note values, from [[Whole Notes]] down to [[Sixteenth Notes]], reinforcing the fundamental building blocks of rhythm.
*   **Chapter 2: Eighth-Note Triplets:** Introduces the concept of dividing a beat into three equal parts, detailing the notation, counting, and various combinations of eighth-note triplets with rests and other note values. This chapter is foundational for understanding [[Swing Feel]] and [[Shuffle Feel]].
*   **Chapter 3: Exercises:** Provides practical application of the concepts learned, with a focus on [[Eighth-Note Triplets]] and [[Eighth-Note Triplet Rests]] in one-bar and two-bar phrases.

**Pro-Tip:** Unit 7 introduces a new rhythmic feel. Approach the [[Triplets]] section with an open mind and focus on *feeling* the three-part division of the beat, not just counting it. This will be crucial for developing an authentic [[Swing Feel]].
