# Anticipated Crash

An **Anticipated Crash** is an accent played slightly before the downbeat ([[Beat|Beat]] 1) of a new phrase or section.

### [[Reggae|Reggae]] Application
In a **shuffled [[Reggae|reggae]]** style, the anticipated crash typically falls on the **last triplet of [[Beat|beat]] 4**. 

### Purpose
*   **Energy**: It creates a sense of forward motion and "pushes" the band into the next section.
*   **Stylistic Marker**: It is a common vocabulary choice in Jamaican music, often used to signal transitions between the [[One-Drop_Groove]] and [[Four-on-the-Floor_Reggae]].

### Execution
The drummer plays a [[Fill|fill]] or a lead-in that culminates in a crash on the "ah" (the third triplet) of [[Beat|beat]] 4, leaving [[Beat|beat]] 1 of the next [[Measure|measure]] open or landing on a kick drum.
