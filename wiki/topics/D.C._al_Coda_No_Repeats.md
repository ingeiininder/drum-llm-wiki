# D.C. al Coda (No Repeats)
The instruction "[[D.S._al_Coda]] (No Repeats)" is a specific musical directive that modifies the standard [[D.S._al_Coda]] instruction. It means "go back to the beginning (Da Capo), play until you reach the Coda sign, then jump to the Coda section, *but ignore any [[Repeat_Signs|repeat signs]]* during this second pass."

**Key Differences from Standard D.C. al Coda:**
*   **No Repeats on Second Pass:** Unlike a standard [[D.S._al_Coda]], where repeats are typically observed on the second pass, this instruction explicitly overrides all [[Repeat_Signs|repeat signs]] (e.g., [[First_and_Second_Endings]]) from the beginning of the piece up to the Coda sign.
*   **Streamlined Form:** This creates a more direct and often shorter second section, leading directly to the [[Coda]].

**Implications for Drummers:**
*   **[[Chart|Chart]] Reading:** Requires careful attention to the text instruction. A quick glance might miss the "No Repeats" clause, leading to errors in form.
*   **Section Length:** Sections that were repeated on the first pass (e.g., an 8-bar "Letter A" section with a repeat) will become their full length (e.g., 8 bars) without the repeat on the second pass. This can lead to an [[Extended_Phrase_Funk-Rock]].
*   **Transitions:** Be prepared for potentially longer sections and direct transitions that might have been broken up by repeats on the first pass.

**Pro-Tip:**
*   **Pre-Read the [[Chart|Chart]]:** Always read through the entire [[Chart|chart]], including all instructions, before playing. Mark your [[Chart|chart]] if necessary to highlight "No Repeats" sections.
*   **Mental Rehearsal:** Mentally walk through the form, especially the second pass, to internalize the flow and identify where repeats are skipped.
*   **Listen for Cues:** While relying on the [[Chart|chart]], also listen for the band's cues to ensure you're navigating the form correctly, especially in a live setting.
