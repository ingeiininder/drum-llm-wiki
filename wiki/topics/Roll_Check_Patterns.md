# Roll [[Check_Patterns|Check Patterns]]

Roll [[Check_Patterns|Check Patterns]] are diagnostic exercises used to ensure that [[Double_Stroke_Roll]]s are perfectly metered and rhythmically accurate. They function by alternating between a "check" (single strokes) and the "roll" ([[Double_Strokes|double strokes]]) within the same rhythmic grid.

### 16th-Note Based Checks (Unit 8)
Used for solos in 2/4 or 4/4 time.
- **5-Stroke Roll Check**: Alternates between [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] and 32nd-note doubles.
- **6-Stroke Roll Check**: Focuses on the triplet-like feel of the six-stroke roll within a duple [[Meter|meter]].
- **10-Stroke Roll Check**: Ensures the long roll is metered correctly across the [[Beat|beat]].

### 6/8 Time Checks (Unit 9)
Used for compound meters where the eighth note is the [[Pulse|pulse]].
- These patterns help the player avoid the "triplet trap"—ensuring that 32nd-note doubles in 6/8 don't lose their relationship to the eighth-note [[Pulse|pulse]].
- **Ex 9.5**: Highlights different ways to read/write the 10-stroke roll in 6/8 to improve sight-reading.

### Why Use [[Check_Patterns|Check Patterns]]?
- **Consistency**: They reveal if your [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]] are faster or slower than the underlying 16th or 32nd notes.
- **Velocity**: They help develop the "snap" required to transition from a single stroke to a double stroke at high speeds.
- **Metering**: They provide a mathematical framework for [[Rudiments|rudiments]] that are often played "by feel."

> **Pro-Tip**: If the roll sounds "mushy" compared to the check, focus on the height of the second note of each double stroke. It should be equal in volume and clarity to the first.
