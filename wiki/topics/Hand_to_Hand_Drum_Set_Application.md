# Hand-to-Hand Drum Set Application

Once the [[Hand-to-Hand 16th Triplet Reading]] is mastered on a single surface, the technique is expanded to the full drum set. This adds melodic and timbral variety to the rhythmic figures.

### Method 1: Tom-Tom Orchestration
Maintain the continuous triplet flow on the [[Snare Drum]] at a ghost-note volume. Move the accented notes to the toms:
*   **Right Hand Accents**: Played on the Floor Tom.
*   **Left Hand Accents**: Played on Rack Tom #1.
*   **Foot Pattern**: Add a consistent [[Bass Drum]] or [[Hi-Hat]] pattern underneath to ground the groove (see [[Exercise 10.7]]).

### Method 2: Cymbal and Bass Drum Orchestration
This method mimics a "big band" setup style:
*   **Accents**: All written figures are played on cymbals (Crash or Ride).
*   **Support**: The [[Bass Drum]] must strike simultaneously with every cymbal accent.
*   **Snare Fill-ins**: Add unaccented snare drum notes (ghost notes) during the rests in the ensemble figure to maintain the triplet motion.

### Pro-Tip
When using Method 2, use the snare drum to "set up" the figures. Playing a slightly louder ghost note or a small crescendo leading into a cymbal crash helps the band anticipate the ensemble hit.
