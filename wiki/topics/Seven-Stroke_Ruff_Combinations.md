# [[Seven-Stroke_Ruff|Seven-Stroke Ruff]] Combinations
The [[Seven-Stroke_Ruff|seven-stroke ruff]] is an extended version of the [[Ruff_Rudiment|ruff rudiment]], typically involving seven notes played in quick succession, often with grace notes leading into a primary stroke. Similar to the [[Five-Stroke_Ruff_Combinations]], these exercises focus on developing control and rhythmic integration of this longer ruff.

### Pro-Tip
For longer ruffs like the seven-stroke, maintaining consistency in the grace notes is even more crucial. Practice slowly to ensure each note is present and even, then gradually increase speed. The goal is a smooth, continuous sound that builds into the accented note, rather than a choppy series of individual strokes. Pay attention to the rebound and use minimal effort for the grace notes.
