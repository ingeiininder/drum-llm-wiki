# Opening and Closing the [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]

This technique involves using the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] foot pedal to create a "bark" or sustained wash on specific subdivisions, typically the upbeats ("&").

### Professional Execution
- **Foot Control**: When opening the hats, lift the foot only slightly—just enough to "let some air in." 
- **Common Pitfall**: Lifting the foot completely off the pedal. This causes the cymbals to open too wide, resulting in a weak, sloppy, and uncontrolled sound.
- **Consistency**: The "bark" should be uniform in [[Duration|duration]] and volume throughout the section.

### Sound Profile
In [[Modern_Rock_Drumming]], this is often paired with "[[Burying_the_Beater|burying the beater]]" in the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] to create a heavy, aggressive "slosh" that complements distorted guitars.
