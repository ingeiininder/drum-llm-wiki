# Note Subdivisions in 3/4 Time
Understanding how different [[Note_Values]] subdivide within a [[Measure]] of [[3_4_Time_Signature]] is crucial for accurate reading and performance. In 3/4, a [[Quarter_Note]] receives one [[Beat]].

**Common Subdivisions in 3/4:**
*   **[[Dotted_Half_Note]]**: Worth three beats. In 3/4, a dotted half note fills an entire measure.
*   **[[Quarter_Note]]**: Worth one beat. Three [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] fit into one measure.
*   **[[Eighth_Note]]**: Worth half a beat. Six [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] fit into one measure (1 & 2 & 3 &).
*   **[[Eighth_Note_Triplet]]**: Worth one-third of a beat. Nine [[Eighth_Note_Triplets|eighth note triplets]] fit into one measure (1 trip-let 2 trip-let 3 trip-let).
*   **[[Sixteenth_Note]]**: Worth one-fourth of a beat. Twelve [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]] fit into one measure (1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a).

**Pro-Tip:** When learning a new [[Time_Signature|time signature]], it's helpful to practice counting and clapping each [[Subdivision|subdivision]]. This reinforces the relationship between the beat and its smaller components, building a strong internal [[Metronome]] for 3/4 time. Pay special attention to how the [[Dotted_Half_Note]] now occupies the entire measure.
