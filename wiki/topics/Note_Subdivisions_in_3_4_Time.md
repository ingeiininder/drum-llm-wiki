# Note Subdivisions in 3/4 Time
Understanding how different [[Note Values]] subdivide within a [[Measure]] of [[3_4_Time_Signature]] is crucial for accurate reading and performance. In 3/4, a [[Quarter Note]] receives one [[Beat]].

**Common Subdivisions in 3/4:**
*   **[[Dotted Half Note]]**: Worth three beats. In 3/4, a dotted half note fills an entire measure.
*   **[[Quarter Note]]**: Worth one beat. Three quarter notes fit into one measure.
*   **[[Eighth Note]]**: Worth half a beat. Six eighth notes fit into one measure (1 & 2 & 3 &).
*   **[[Eighth Note Triplet]]**: Worth one-third of a beat. Nine eighth note triplets fit into one measure (1 trip-let 2 trip-let 3 trip-let).
*   **[[Sixteenth Note]]**: Worth one-fourth of a beat. Twelve sixteenth notes fit into one measure (1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a).

**Pro-Tip:** When learning a new time signature, it's helpful to practice counting and clapping each subdivision. This reinforces the relationship between the beat and its smaller components, building a strong internal [[Metronome]] for 3/4 time. Pay special attention to how the [[Dotted Half Note]] now occupies the entire measure.
