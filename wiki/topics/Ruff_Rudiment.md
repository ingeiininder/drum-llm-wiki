# Ruff (Rudiment)
A **Ruff** (often referred to as a single-stroke roll variation) consists of a series of short, rapid notes leading into a primary stroke. Unlike the [[Drag]], which uses [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]], the ruff is typically played as alternating single strokes.

### Variations
- **[[3-Stroke_Ruff]]**: Two grace notes followed by a primary stroke (rlR or lrL).
- **[[4-Stroke_Ruff]]**: Three grace notes followed by a primary stroke (rlrL or lrlR).
- **[[7-Stroke_Ruff]]**: Six grace notes leading into an accented downbeat.

### [[Drumset_Mobility|Drumset Mobility]]
When moving ruffs around the [[Drum_Seat]], the inherent "break" in rhythmic flow must be addressed. 
- **Orchestration**: Practice moving the primary stroke to the toms while keeping the grace notes on the snare.
- **Rhythmic Integrity**: Use a [[Metronome]] and count the basic stickings out loud to internalize the subdivisions.

### Pro-Tip
The "Hairta" (or [[Herta]]) is a popular variation of the ruff used extensively in [[Rock|rock]] and metal for high-speed fills.
