# Let's Play One: Song Study

"Let's Play One" (by Peter Retzlaff and Jim Rupp) focuses on [[Brush_Technique]] within a standard [[12_Bar_Blues]] form. While the track is designed for brushes to match the relaxed mood, it can also be practiced with sticks to explore different textures.

### Form and Structure
*   **12-Bar [[Blues|Blues]]**: It is vital to track the form accurately. A common technique to outline the structure is playing a light [[Fill|fill]] at the end of bar 12.
*   **Drum Trades**: During "fours" or "eights," the 12-bar form continues. The drummer must maintain the internal [[Pulse|pulse]] and formal awareness while soloing.
*   **Texture Changes**: During the bass solo, consider moving the [[Ride_Pattern]] to the closed [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] or a cymbal using brushes to create a distinct sonic space.

### [[Chart|Chart]] Literacy
The curriculum introduces three types of notation for this tune:
1.  **Lead Sheet**: Melodic line and chords.
2.  **Abbreviated Drum [[Chart|Chart]]**: Roadmap with essential cues.
3.  **Full Drum [[Chart|Chart]]**: Detailed notation of the performance.
Professional drummers must be proficient in navigating all three formats.
