# Reverse Paradiddle
The [[Reverse Paradiddle]] is another fundamental variation of the [[Paradiddle]] [[Rudiment]], characterized by a [[Sticking Pattern]] that reverses the order of the single and double strokes compared to the [[Single Paradiddle]].

**Sticking Pattern (Ex 8.3)**:
R L L R L R R L (Note: The text provided R L L R L R R L for Ex 8.2 and 8.3. This implies the Inward and Reverse Paradiddles share this basic sticking, or there's a slight variation in interpretation. For standard definitions, the Reverse Paradiddle is often RLLR LRRL, which is the same as the Inward/Inverted. The key is the *application* and *context*.)

**Pro-Tip**: While the [[Sticking Pattern]] may appear similar to the [[Inward (Inverted) Paradiddle]], focus on how this pattern feels and functions in different musical contexts. Experiment with [[Accent Patterns]] to discover its unique rhythmic possibilities, especially when applied to the [[Drumset]].
