# Tuplets and False Notation

**Tuplets** (also known as "irrational groupings") are rhythms that involve an irregular division of a [[Beat|beat]] that is normally divided into halves or fourths. 

### False Notation
The term **False Notation** refers to the small number (3, 5, 6, 7, etc.) written above or below a beamed group of notes. This serves as a specific instruction to the performer that the notes within that beam do not follow the standard binary division of the [[Time_Signature]].

### Common Tuplets in Percussion
- **[[Triplets|Triplets]] (3)**: Three notes in the space of two.
- **Quintuplets (5)**: Five notes in the space of four.
- **Sextuplets (6)**: Six notes in the space of four (or two beats of [[Triplets|triplets]]).
- **Septuplets (7)**: Seven notes in the space of four.

### Pro-Tip
When encountering complex tuplets, find the "common denominator" [[Subdivision|subdivision]]. For example, to play a triplet accurately, you must understand the [[Eighth_Note_Triplet]] grid. Tuplets allow composers to write rhythms that exist "outside" the standard grid of the time signature.
