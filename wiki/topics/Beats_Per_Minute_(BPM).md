# Beats Per Minute (BPM)
**Beats Per Minute (BPM)** is the standard unit of measurement for [[Tempo]] in music. It quantifies the pace of a piece by indicating the number of [[Beat]]s that occur within a 60-second interval (one minute).

## Indication in Music
BPM is commonly indicated at the top of a piece of music, often above the first [[Measure|measure]]. It can be notated in two primary ways:

1.  **As a numerical value**: e.g., "120 bpm".
2.  **With a [[Note_Value]] equaling the BPM**: e.g., a [[Quarter_Note]] symbol followed by "= 120". This explicitly states which note value receives the beat at that tempo.

## Importance for Drummers
*   **Consistency**: BPM provides a precise target for maintaining a consistent tempo throughout a performance.
*   **Practice Tool**: A [[Metronome]] is an essential tool for practicing at specific BPMs, helping drummers develop a strong internal clock and improve their timing.
*   **Communication**: BPM is a universal language for musicians to communicate the desired speed of a song.

## Pro-Tip for Drummers
*   **Internalize Tempo**: Don't just rely on the metronome; strive to internalize different tempos. Practice counting and feeling various BPMs without external aid.
*   **Gradual Increase**: When learning a new exercise or piece, start at a slow BPM (e.g., 60-80 BPM) and gradually increase the tempo as your comfort and accuracy improve.
*   **Tempo Fluctuations**: While a consistent BPM is often desired, understanding how to intentionally manipulate tempo (e.g., [[Rubato]], [[Accelerando]], [[Ritardando]]) is also a crucial musical skill.
