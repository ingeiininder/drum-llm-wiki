# Rhythmic Illusion

A **Rhythmic Illusion** occurs when a drummer superimposes a rhythmic grouping over an existing template to create the auditory effect of the tempo speeding up or slowing down, without actually changing the underlying BPM.

### Technical Application
- **Grouping Subdivisions**: A primary method involves grouping [[Eighth-Note Triplets]] in sets of four or two. This creates a cross-rhythm that sounds like a different tempo.
- **Soloing vs. Modulation**: While rhythmic illusions are excellent for [[Drum Soloing]] ideas, they differ from [[Metric Modulation]] in that the intention is usually to return to the original rhythmic template rather than establishing a permanent new tempo.

### Performance Pitfalls
The most common mistake is losing the original "home" pulse. Drummers must maintain a strong internal reference to the original quarter note to ensure the illusion resolves correctly back into the primary groove.
