# Ten-Stroke Roll
The [[Ten-Stroke Roll]] is a drum rudiment belonging to the [[Double Stroke Roll]] family. It consists of five [[Double_Strokes]]s, typically ending with an accent on the final stroke. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 7 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
RRLLRRLLR L (or LLRRLLRRLL R for Left Hand Lead)

**Breakdown:**
*   **RRLLRRLLR:** Five pairs of [[Double Stroke Technique]] (RR, LL, RR, LL, RR).
*   **L:** A single, accented stroke with the left hand, which typically marks the end of the roll.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Roll Development:** Develops advanced control over the [[Double Stroke Technique]] and the ability to sustain a roll for an extended duration.
*   **Fills and Embellishments:** Used for longer, more sustained drum fills and to create a continuous roll sound.
*   **Endurance:** Helps build endurance in the hands for continuous double strokes.

**Pro-Tip:** The key to a successful [[Ten-Stroke Roll]] is maintaining absolute evenness in volume and spacing across all ten strokes. Focus on a relaxed grip and efficient rebound for each double stroke. Practice slowly with a metronome, ensuring that the roll sounds smooth and consistent before the final accented note. This rudiment is excellent for developing both technical control and musicality in rolls.
