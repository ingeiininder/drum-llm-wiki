# Paradiddle
The [[Paradiddle]] is one of the most fundamental and widely recognized drum [[Rudiments|rudiments]], belonging to the [[Diddles_Rudiment_Family]]. Its name is onomatopoeic, reflecting its sticking pattern: **Pa-ra-did-dle**.

**Basic Sticking Pattern:**
The core sticking for a single [[Paradiddle]] is:
*   **R L R R**
*   **L R L L**

This pattern consists of four notes: two single strokes followed by a double stroke (diddle). The alternating lead hand (R L R R, then L R L L) is a key feature, making it incredibly versatile for moving around the drum set.

**Variations:**
The [[Paradiddle]] is the basis for many other [[Rudiments|rudiments]], including:
*   [[Double_Paradiddle|Double Paradiddle]] (R L R L R R, L R L R L L)
*   [[Triple_Paradiddle|Triple Paradiddle]]
*   [[Paradiddle-diddle|Paradiddle-Diddle]]

**Application:**
*   **Grooves:** The [[Paradiddle]] can be easily orchestrated around the drum set to create countless grooves.
*   **Fills:** It forms the basis for many drum fills, allowing for smooth transitions and rhythmic interest.
*   **Soloing:** Essential for developing hand-to-hand coordination and creating complex solo patterns.
*   **Stick Control:** Excellent for developing evenness between single and [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]].

**Pro-Tip:** Focus on making the [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]] (the "diddle" part) sound as even and controlled as the single strokes. The second note of the diddle often relies on rebound, so ensure your grip is relaxed enough to allow the stick to bounce naturally. Practice with a [[Metronome|metronome]] and experiment with different [[Dynamics|dynamics]] and [[Accents|accents]] to unlock its full potential.
