# Letter B [[Groove|Groove]] ([[Funk|Funk]]-[[Rock|Rock]])
The `[[Letter_B_Groove_Funk-Rock]]` is a two-bar `[[Musical_Phrase]]` within the `[[Funk_Rock]]` [[Chart|chart]], characterized by its highly `[[Syncopated]]` nature. It features an `[[Eighth_Note]]` `[[Hi-Hat]]` pattern combined with `[[Sixteenth_Note]]`-based `[[Bass_Drum]]` and `[[Snare_Drum]]` patterns.

Similar to the `[[Letter_A_Groove_Funk-Rock]]`, the `[[Bass_Drum]]` and `[[Snare_Drum]]` rhythms do not always align with the `[[Open_Hi-Hat]]`, requiring significant `[[Limb_Independence]]` and precise `[[Rhythmic_Placement]]`. Examples `[[Ex_8.6]]` and `[[Ex_8.7]]` illustrate this [[Groove|groove]].

**Important:** To properly execute the `[[Sixteenth_Note]]` `[[Bass_Drum]]` and `[[Snare_Drum]]` patterns, `[[Subdividing]]` is again emphasized as crucial during both practice and performance.

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing a `[[Two-Bar_Phrase]]`, ensure you can play the entire phrase smoothly and consistently before looping it. Focus on the transition between the first and second bars, and how the phrase resolves or leads into the next repetition. The "Medium; Thick Hi-Hat" instruction suggests a specific `[[Hi-Hat_Technique]]` for a fuller, more sustained sound, often achieved by playing closer to the bell or with a slightly looser grip.
