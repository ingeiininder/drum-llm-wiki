# Snare, Floor Tom, Bass Drum Accent (Beat 4)
This technique describes a specific accent or mini-fill used to punctuate the end of a phrase, particularly on beat four. It involves a "solid hit" orchestrated across three drums: the [[Snare Drum]], [[Floor Tom]], and [[Bass Drum]]. This creates a powerful, full-sounding accent that can serve as a [[Drum Fill]] or a strong rhythmic marker.

In the context of the [[Verse Section (Letter A)]] in [[Country-Pop Genre]], this accent helps to emphasize the end of the phrase within the [[16th Notes on Snare Drum with Quarter Note Bass Drum]] groove.

**Pro-Tip:** The key to this accent is precision and simultaneous execution. Practice hitting all three drums exactly at the same time to get a unified, impactful sound. Experiment with dynamics – it should be a "solid hit," but still fit within the overall dynamic range of the song. This orchestration adds weight and interest to a groove.
