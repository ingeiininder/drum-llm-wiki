# Snare, Floor Tom, Bass Drum Accent ([[Beat|Beat]] 4)
This technique describes a specific accent or mini-[[Fill|fill]] used to punctuate the end of a phrase, particularly on [[Beat|beat]] four. It involves a "solid hit" orchestrated across three drums: the [[Snare_Drum]], [[Floor_Tom]], and [[Bass_Drum]]. This creates a powerful, full-sounding accent that can serve as a [[Drum_Fill]] or a strong rhythmic marker.

In the context of the [[Verse_Section_Letter_A]] in [[Country-Pop_Genre]], this accent helps to emphasize the end of the phrase within the [[16th_Notes_on_Snare_Drum_with_Quarter_Note_Bass_Drum]] [[Groove|groove]].

**Pro-Tip:** The key to this accent is precision and simultaneous execution. Practice hitting all three drums exactly at the same time to get a unified, impactful sound. Experiment with [[Dynamics|dynamics]] – it should be a "solid hit," but still fit within the overall dynamic range of the song. This orchestration adds weight and interest to a [[Groove|groove]].
