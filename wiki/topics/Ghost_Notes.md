# Ghost Notes

**Ghost Notes** (also known as "notes in parentheses" or "soft notes") are low-volume notes played on the snare drum to add texture, depth, and "flow" to a groove. In [[Pop-Rock Drumming]], these notes should be "felt rather than heard."

### Execution: The Drop Stroke
To achieve the correct mezzo-piano (mp) dynamic, drummers use a **[[Drop Stroke]]**. This involves:
- Loosening the fingers to let the stick drop and lightly tap the head.
- Avoiding a full wrist-motioned downstroke.
- Playing the tip of the stick in the center of the snare drum.

### Placement in Pop-Rock
A common pattern involves placing ghost notes on the "e" of beats 1 and 3, and the "ah" of beats 2 and 4. This creates a subtle 16th-note subdivision underneath the primary eighth-note hi-hat pattern.

### Pro-Tip
Avoid "flamming" ghost notes with the bass drum. To ensure perfect unisons, practice the pattern slowly with a 16th-note click, isolating the bass drum notes against the ghost note pattern (IMG_1104).
