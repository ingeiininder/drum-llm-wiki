# Ghost Notes

**Ghost Notes** (also known as "notes in parentheses" or "soft notes") are low-volume notes played on the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] to add texture, depth, and "flow" to a [[Groove|groove]]. In [[Pop-Rock_Drumming]], these notes should be "felt rather than heard."

### Execution: The Drop Stroke
To achieve the correct mezzo-piano (mp) dynamic, drummers use a **[[Drop_Stroke]]**. This involves:
- Loosening the fingers to let the stick drop and lightly tap the head.
- Avoiding a full wrist-motioned downstroke.
- Playing the tip of the stick in the center of the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]].

### Placement in Pop-[[Rock|Rock]]
A common pattern involves placing ghost notes on the "e" of beats 1 and 3, and the "ah" of beats 2 and 4. This creates a subtle 16th-note [[Subdivision|subdivision]] underneath the primary eighth-note [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] pattern.

### Pro-Tip
Avoid "flamming" ghost notes with the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]]. To ensure perfect unisons, practice the pattern slowly with a 16th-note click, isolating the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] notes against the ghost note pattern (IMG_1104).
