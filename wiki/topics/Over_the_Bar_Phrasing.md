# Over-the-Bar Phrasing

Over-the-bar phrasing occurs when a rhythmic motif or grouping (such as a [[Odd Groupings|grouping of three]]) does not reset at the bar line, but continues across it, temporarily obscuring the "1."

### Analytical Approach
The Musicians Institute curriculum emphasizes a specific mental approach to these figures:
1. **Count the Pulse, Not the Figure**: You must count (and feel) where beat 1 lies within every bar. 
2. **Avoid Repetition Counting**: A common mistake is counting "how many times" a figure is played (e.g., "I play this 3-note group 5 times"). This leads to errors because the drummer loses track of the underlying [[Timekeeping|meter]].
3. **Displacement**: Patterns are often displaced by a single 16th note in subsequent bars (Ex 10.10) to increase rhythmic tension.

### Complex Hand/Foot Fills
Fills in this style often utilize the same over-the-bar logic, using 32nd-note or 16th-note groupings of three distributed between the hands and feet.

| Grouping Type | Subdivision | Application |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Grouping of 3 | 16th Notes | Standard syncopation |
| Grouping of 3 | 32nd Notes | High-density fills (Ex 10.12) |
