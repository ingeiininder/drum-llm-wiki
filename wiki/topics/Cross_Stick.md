# Cross-Stick
The [[Cross_Stick]] (also known as "rim click" or "side stick") is a [[Snare Drum]] articulation produced by laying the stick across the drumhead and striking the rim with the shaft of the stick. This creates a dry, woody, and percussive sound, distinct from a standard snare hit or [[Rimshot]].

**Execution**:
*   Place the palm of your hand on the drumhead near the rim, holding the stick.
*   Allow the stick to rest across the drumhead, with the tip pointing towards the center.
*   Pivot your wrist to strike the rim with the shaft of the stick, typically about halfway down the stick.

**Characteristics**:
*   **Sound**: Dry, clicky, woody, with minimal sustain.
*   **Dynamics**: Generally softer than a rimshot or full snare hit, making it suitable for lower-volume settings.
*   **Application**: Widely used in [[Jazz]], [[Blues]], [[Country-Pop Style]], [[Latin]], and acoustic settings where a subtle backbeat is desired.
*   Featured in the [[A2_12_Bar_Blues_Groove]].

**Pro-Tip**: Consistency in sound is key for a good cross-stick. Experiment with the placement of your hand on the head and where the stick strikes the rim to find the optimal resonance. Ensure the stick doesn't slip, and the sound is clear and defined, not muffled.
