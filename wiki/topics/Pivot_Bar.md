# Pivot Bar

A **Pivot Bar** is a structural device in music where a single [[Measure|measure]] serves two functional roles simultaneously within the [[Phrasing|phrasing]] of a song.

### Example: Unit 8 [[Reggae|Reggae]] [[Chart|Chart]]
In the Unit 8 [[Chart|chart]] (Letter B), there is a 7-bar section that feels natural despite being an odd number of bars. This is due to the pivot bar at **Bar 4**:
*   It acts as the **last bar** of the first 4-bar phrase.
*   It simultaneously acts as the **first bar** of the second 4-bar phrase.

### Performance Impact
Recognizing a pivot bar helps a drummer maintain the "flow" of a [[Groove|groove]]. Instead of feeling like a "cut" or "short" phrase, the pivot bar allows the music to transition seamlessly between sections without disrupting the listener's sense of the four-bar [[Pulse|pulse]].
