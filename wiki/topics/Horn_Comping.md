# Horn Comping

Horn Comping is the art of accompanying horn sections by reinforcing their rhythmic figures and "setting up" their entrances.

### Techniques
1.  **Set-ups**: A short [[Drum_Fill]] or rhythmic lead-in that prepares the listener (and the band) for a horn entrance. For example, a two-beat fill at the end of a section to catch a downbeat horn shot.
2.  **Reinforcement**: Playing the exact rhythm of the horn section on the [[Snare_Drum]], [[Kick_Drum]], or cymbals.
3.  **Staccato Accents**: For short, sharp horn "stabs," use a combination of snare and kick, or a tight [[Hi-Hat]]/[[Splash_Cymbal]] hit.
4.  **Dynamic Alignment**: Matching the crescendos and decrescendos of the horn section to ensure the ensemble sounds like a single unit.

**Pro-Tip**: When comping, don't overplay. The goal is to make the horn figures "pop," not to overshadow them. In tunes like [[Bad_to_the_Bone_Song_Study]], the interaction between the bass and horns dictates where the drummer should place their accents.
