# Assigned Sticking
Assigned Sticking is a fundamental [[Sticking_Patterns]] method that provides a natural and intuitive way to interpret and play a steady flow of notes, particularly [[16th_Note]]s. It involves consistently assigning specific rhythmic subdivisions (or "partials") to either the right or left hand.

The core principle of Assigned Sticking, especially for continuous [[16th_Notes|16th notes]], is based on [[Alternating_Hands_Sticking]] (R L R L). This leads to:
*   **[[Right_Hand_Lead_Assigned_Sticking]]**: The right hand typically plays all downbeats (1, 2, 3, 4) and upbeats ("&"s).
*   **[[Left_Hand_Lead_Assigned_Sticking]]**: The left hand typically plays all "e's" and "ah's," filling in the spaces between the right-hand strokes.

This method is highly effective for:
*   **Internalizing Rhythms**: By visualizing the continuous alternating [[16th_Notes|16th notes]], drummers can ensure proper [[Beat_Placement|beat placement]] even when playing complex [[Rhythmic_Groupings]] with [[Rests|rests]].
*   **Developing [[Muscle_Memory_Drumming]]**: Consistent application of assigned sticking builds procedural memory, making rhythmic execution more fluid and unconscious.
*   **Improving [[Drum_Reading]]**: It provides a clear physical manifestation for written notation, speeding up the interpretation process.

**Applications**:
Assigned Sticking can be applied to various note groupings per [[Beat|beat]]:
*   [[Assigned_Sticking_One_Note_per_Beat]] (e.g., [[Quarter_Notes|Quarter Notes]])
*   [[Assigned_Sticking_Two_Notes_per_Beat]] (e.g., [[Eighth_Notes|Eighth Notes]])
*   [[Assigned_Sticking_Three_Notes_per_Beat]] (e.g., 16th note [[Triplets|triplets]] or specific 16th note/rest combinations)
*   [[Assigned_Sticking_Four_Notes_per_Beat]] (e.g., continuous [[16th_Notes|16th Notes]])

**Pro-Tip**: Practice assigned sticking diligently with a [[Metronome|metronome]]. The goal is to make the hand movements automatic, allowing your brain to focus on the musicality rather than individual stickings. This technique is a cornerstone for developing speed, control, and accuracy.
