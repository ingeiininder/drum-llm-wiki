# Assigned Sticking
Assigned Sticking is a fundamental [[Sticking Patterns]] method that provides a natural and intuitive way to interpret and play a steady flow of notes, particularly [[16th Note]]s. It involves consistently assigning specific rhythmic subdivisions (or "partials") to either the right or left hand.

The core principle of Assigned Sticking, especially for continuous 16th notes, is based on [[Alternating Hands (Sticking)]] (R L R L). This leads to:
*   **[[Right Hand Lead (Assigned Sticking)]]**: The right hand typically plays all downbeats (1, 2, 3, 4) and upbeats ("&"s).
*   **[[Left Hand Lead (Assigned Sticking)]]**: The left hand typically plays all "e's" and "ah's," filling in the spaces between the right-hand strokes.

This method is highly effective for:
*   **Internalizing Rhythms**: By visualizing the continuous alternating 16th notes, drummers can ensure proper beat placement even when playing complex [[Rhythmic Groupings]] with rests.
*   **Developing [[Muscle Memory (Drumming)]]**: Consistent application of assigned sticking builds procedural memory, making rhythmic execution more fluid and unconscious.
*   **Improving [[Drum Reading]]**: It provides a clear physical manifestation for written notation, speeding up the interpretation process.

**Applications**:
Assigned Sticking can be applied to various note groupings per beat:
*   [[Assigned Sticking: One Note per Beat]] (e.g., Quarter Notes)
*   [[Assigned Sticking: Two Notes per Beat]] (e.g., Eighth Notes)
*   [[Assigned Sticking: Three Notes per Beat]] (e.g., 16th note triplets or specific 16th note/rest combinations)
*   [[Assigned Sticking: Four Notes per Beat]] (e.g., continuous 16th Notes)

**Pro-Tip**: Practice assigned sticking diligently with a metronome. The goal is to make the hand movements automatic, allowing your brain to focus on the musicality rather than individual stickings. This technique is a cornerstone for developing speed, control, and accuracy.
