# Note Value

A Note Value symbolizes the relative duration of a note. It is the primary building block of musical notation, indicating how many beats or fractions of a [[Beat|beat]] a sound should occupy within a [[Measure|measure]].

## The Hierarchy of Note Values

Within a standard **4/4 time signature** (four beats per [[Measure|measure]]), note values are divided systematically:

- **Whole Note**: Equal to four [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]]; occupies one full [[Measure|measure]].
- **Half Note**: Equal to half a whole note (2 beats).
- **Quarter Note**: Equal to 1/4 of a [[Measure|measure]] (1 [[Beat|beat]]).
- **Eighth Note**: Equal to 1/8 of a [[Measure|measure]] (1/2 [[Beat|beat]]).
- **Sixteenth Note**: Equal to 1/16 of a [[Measure|measure]] (1/4 [[Beat|beat]]).

### Mathematical Relationships
The breakdown of values can be visualized as:
- **1 [[Measure|Measure]]** = 4 Quarters = 8 Eighths = 16 Sixteenths.
- **1 Quarter** = 2 Eighths = 4 Sixteenths.

## Modifying Note Values

- **Ties**: A curved line connecting two note heads, enabling a note to sustain past its standalone value or across a [[Bar_Line|bar line]].
- **Dotted Notes**: A dot increases a note's value by half of its original duration (e.g., a **[[Dotted_Half_Note|Dotted Half Note]]** equals 3 beats). Alternatively, a dotted note equals three of the subsequent (smaller) note value.
- **[[Fermata|Fermata]]**: A notation indicating that a note should be sustained for longer than its literal value, at the drummer's discretion.

## Notational Anatomy

- **Stem**: The vertical line extending from a [[Note_Head|note head]].
- **Flag**: A stroke attached to a stem to indicate subdivisions (one flag for 8th, two for 16th).
- **Beams**: Horizontal lines used to connect groups of notes (e.g., a group of four [[16th_Notes|16th notes]]). Beamed groups make complex rhythms easier to read at a glance.

## Professional Application

- **[[Subdividing|Subdividing]]**: At slow tempos, professional drummers "subdivide" (mentally count smaller values) to stay in time and maintain a consistent sound production. For example, counting eighth-note [[Triplets|triplets]] during a slow swing [[Chart|chart]].
- **Metric Modulation**: A change in [[Tempo|tempo]] where a note value from one section is made equivalent to a note value in a new section (e.g., the [[Dotted_Eighth_Note|dotted eighth note]] becoming the new eighth-note [[Pulse|pulse]]).
