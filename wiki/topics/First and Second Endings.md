# First and Second Endings
A common musical notation technique used to indicate repeated sections without writing them out multiple times, particularly in forms like the [[AABA 32-Bar Form]]. To navigate this structure:
1.  The first time playing through the section, follow the repeat under the first ending sign and return to the beginning of the section.
2.  Upon repeating the previous part, skip the first ending and play the second ending.

When a [[D.C. al Fine]] instruction includes "repeats are good," it signifies that these first and second endings should be observed during the repeat.
