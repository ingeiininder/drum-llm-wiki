# Check Patterns
[[Check Patterns]] are specific rhythmic exercises or sequences designed to ensure accuracy in note placement, timing, and dynamic consistency when practicing drumming techniques, particularly rudiments like the [[Double Stroke Roll]].

## Purpose
The primary purpose of check patterns is to provide a clear comparison or a structured way to verify that a particular technique is being executed correctly and evenly. They help drummers identify inconsistencies in their playing.

## Application for Double Strokes
In the context of [[Double Stroke Technique]], a basic check pattern for doubles (Ex 7.5) might involve:
*   Playing a sequence of alternating single strokes (e.g., `R L R L R L R L`).
*   Immediately following with a sequence of double strokes (e.g., `R R L L R R L L`).
*   By comparing the feel, sound, and timing of the single strokes to the double strokes, a drummer can assess the evenness and accuracy of their doubles. If the doubles sound rushed, uneven, or have an accented first note, the check pattern will highlight this.

### Pro-Tip
When using check patterns, pay close attention to the transition between the different sections. Any hesitation, change in tempo, or shift in dynamic indicates an area that needs more practice. Record yourself playing check patterns and listen back critically; what you hear can often reveal more than what you feel while playing.
