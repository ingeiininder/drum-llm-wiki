# Dotted Eighth Note
A **dotted eighth note** is an [[Eighth Note]] with a dot placed immediately to its right. This dot increases the [[Duration]] of the eighth note by one-half of its original value.

**Value Calculation:**
*   An [[Eighth Note]] is typically worth 0.5 [[Beat]]s (in [[4/4 Time Signature]]).
*   Half of an eighth note's value is 0.25 beats (equivalent to a [[Sixteenth Note]]).
*   Therefore, a dotted eighth note is worth 0.5 + 0.25 = **0.75 beats** (or three sixteenth notes).

**Implication for Rhythmic Placement:**
If a dotted eighth note starts on beat 1, it will sustain through beat 1, the "e" of 1, and the "&" of 1. The next available place for a note or rest would then be on the "ah" of beat 1. This creates very precise [[Syncopated Rhythms]] at the sixteenth-note level.

**Pro-Tip:** Dotted eighth notes are fundamental for creating intricate grooves and fills, especially in styles like funk, fusion, and contemporary rock. Mastering their precise 0.75-beat duration requires a strong internal [[Subdivision]] of [[Sixteenth Notes]] ("1 e & a"). Practice counting "1 e &" for the dotted eighth, then "a" for the next subdivision.
