# Half-Time [[Shuffle_Feel|Shuffle Feel]]
The **Half-Time [[Shuffle_Feel|Shuffle Feel]]** is a variation of the [[Shuffle|shuffle]] rhythm that places the [[Back-Beat]] on beats 2 and 4, distinct from the standard half-time [[Shuffle|shuffle]].

### Comparison of Shuffles
| Feature | Standard Half-Time [[Shuffle|Shuffle]] | Half-Time [[Shuffle_Feel|Shuffle Feel]] |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Backbeat Placement** | [[Beat|Beat]] 3 | Beats 2 and 4 |
| **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]]** | Eighth-note [[Triplets|Triplets]] | 16th-note [[Triplets|Triplets]] |
| **[[Pulse|Pulse]] Density** | Lower | Higher / More syncopated |

### Technical Requirements
- **Ghost Notes**: The [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] [[Ghost_Notes]] pattern must be played lightly within the 16th-note triplet grid.
- **Unisons**: Achieving perfect unisons between the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] and the ghosted snare notes is critical to prevent "flamming" the [[Groove|groove]].
- **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: Typically performed at a *mezzo-piano* (mp) dynamic to maintain the "sonically sweet" nature of [[Indie_Pop_Drumming]].

### Historical Context
The half-time [[Shuffle|shuffle]] finds its origins in the work of session legend [[Bernard_Purdie]] (the "Purdie [[Shuffle|Shuffle]]"). Modern interpretations, such as those by [[Jason_McGerr]], adapt these subdivisions for the Indie-Pop genre.
