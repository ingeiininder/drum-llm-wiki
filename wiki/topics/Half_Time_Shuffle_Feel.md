# Half-Time Shuffle Feel
The **Half-Time Shuffle Feel** is a variation of the shuffle rhythm that places the [[Back-Beat]] on beats 2 and 4, distinct from the standard half-time shuffle.

### Comparison of Shuffles
| Feature | Standard Half-Time Shuffle | Half-Time Shuffle Feel |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Backbeat Placement** | Beat 3 | Beats 2 and 4 |
| **Subdivision** | Eighth-note Triplets | 16th-note Triplets |
| **Pulse Density** | Lower | Higher / More syncopated |

### Technical Requirements
- **Ghost Notes**: The snare drum [[Ghost_Notes]] pattern must be played lightly within the 16th-note triplet grid.
- **Unisons**: Achieving perfect unisons between the bass drum and the ghosted snare notes is critical to prevent "flamming" the groove.
- **Dynamics**: Typically performed at a *mezzo-piano* (mp) dynamic to maintain the "sonically sweet" nature of [[Indie_Pop_Drumming]].

### Historical Context
The half-time shuffle finds its origins in the work of session legend [[Bernard Purdie]] (the "Purdie Shuffle"). Modern interpretations, such as those by [[Jason McGerr]], adapt these subdivisions for the Indie-Pop genre.
