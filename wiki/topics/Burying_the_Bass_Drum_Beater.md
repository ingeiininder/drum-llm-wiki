# Burying the Bass Drum Beater
[[Burying the Bass Drum Beater]] is a bass drum technique where, after striking the bass drum head, the beater is allowed to remain pressed against the head. This action effectively mutes the drumhead's resonance, resulting in a short, punchy, and staccato sound.

This technique is often contrasted with the [[Open Non-Buried Bass Drum Technique]], where the beater rebounds immediately after striking, allowing for full resonance.

**Pro-Tip:** [[Burying the Bass Drum Beater]] is excellent for tight, articulate bass drum patterns, especially in faster tempos or when a very defined, non-ringing attack is desired. It's a fundamental technique for achieving a powerful, controlled, and rhythmically precise bass drum sound in many contemporary styles.
