# Burying the Bass Drum Beater

**Burying the Bass Drum Beater** (also known as "[[Burying_the_Beater|Burying the Beater]]") is a [[Bass_Drum_Technique|bass drum technique]] where, immediately after striking the drum head, the beater is kept pressed firmly against the head rather than allowed to rebound.

## Sound and Characteristics
- **Short Sustain**: The beater acts as a muffle, resulting in a short, punchy, and [[Staccato|staccato]] sound.
- **Defined Attack**: This technique provides a very defined, non-ringing attack, perfect for contemporary styles that require a "dry" and controlled low-end.
- **Authoritative [[Pulse|Pulse]]**: It allows for a powerful and rhythmically precise sound, especially effective in faster tempos or high-energy [[Rock|rock]] grooves.

## Execution
- After the downstroke, your foot maintains pressure on the pedal, keeping the beater buried in the **Bass Drum Head**.
- Care must be taken to ensure the beater remains motionless against the head to avoid any unwanted "buzz" or double-hit sounds caused by the beater vibrating against the surface.

## Contrast with Open Technique
This technique is often contrasted with the **[[Off_the_Head_Bass_Drum_Technique]]** (or Open Technique), where the beater rebounds immediately after striking, allowing the drum to resonate fully.
