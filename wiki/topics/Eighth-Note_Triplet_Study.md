# Eighth-Note Triplet Study
The [[Eighth-Note Triplet Study]] delves into the various stickings and accent patterns available within this fundamental rhythm. While briefly touched upon with the [[Four-Stroke Ruff]] study, this chapter provides a comprehensive exploration.

**[[Eighth-Note Triplet Feel]]**:
*   **"Rounder" Feel:** Triplets have a distinct "rounder" or "softer, flowing" feel compared to straight eighth notes or 16th notes. This is due to the three evenly divided notes per beat.
*   **Maintaining Flow:** Even when practicing seemingly "square and rigid" stickings and accents, the core idea is to maintain this flowing, round feel.

**Benefits of Hand-to-Hand Triplets:**
*   **Natural Alternating Sticking:** Hand-to-hand triplets naturally result in alternating sticking on every beat (e.g., RLR LRL RLR LRL).
*   **Balanced Development:** This ensures that both hands receive the same amount of attention, leading to a very balanced study and symmetrical [[Drum Technique]].
*   **Even though not all exercises are strictly hand-to-hand, each hand will be focused on equally.**

**Key Areas of Focus:**
*   **Various Stickings:** Exploring different combinations of right and left hand strokes within the triplet grouping.
*   **Accent Patterns:** Applying accents to different notes within the triplet to create varied rhythmic emphasis.
*   **Maintaining Flow:** The overarching goal is to execute these patterns while preserving the inherent "round" and "flowing" feel of triplets.

**Pro-Tip:**
When practicing triplets, use a [[Metronome]] and subdivide the beat into three. Focus on making each of the three notes within the triplet equally spaced and dynamically consistent (unless an accent is specified). The "round" feel comes from a relaxed, fluid motion, avoiding any stiffness or choppiness. This study is crucial for developing rhythmic versatility and a strong internal clock.
