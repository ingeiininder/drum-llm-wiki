# Jazz Balance of Sound
The "player's mix" in jazz is the inverse of pop and rock. While pop-rock emphasizes the [[Bass Drum]] and [[Snare Drum]], jazz prioritizes the cymbals.

### The Hierarchy (Loudest to Softest)
1.  **[[Jazz Ride Cymbal Technique|Ride Cymbal]]**: The primary timekeeping voice and the loudest element.
2.  **[[Jazz Hi-Hat Technique|Hi-Hat]]**: The "backbeat" of jazz, played on 2 and 4.
3.  **Snare Drum**: Used for low-volume [[Comping (Jazz)]].
4.  **Bass Drum**: The softest voice, often "feathered" so it is felt rather than heard.

| Voice | Pop-Rock Priority | Jazz Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Ride/Hi-Hat | Low/Medium | **High** |
| Snare Drum | **High** | Medium/Low |
| Bass Drum | **High** | **Lowest** |
