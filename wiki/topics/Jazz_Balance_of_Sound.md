# [[Jazz|Jazz]] Balance of Sound
The "player's mix" in [[Jazz|jazz]] is the inverse of pop and [[Rock|rock]]. While pop-[[Rock|rock]] emphasizes the [[Bass_Drum]] and [[Snare_Drum]], [[Jazz|jazz]] prioritizes the cymbals.

### The Hierarchy (Loudest to Softest)
1.  **[[Jazz_Ride_Cymbal_Technique|Ride Cymbal]]**: The primary timekeeping voice and the loudest element.
2.  **[[Jazz_Hi-Hat_Technique|Hi-Hat]]**: The "backbeat" of [[Jazz|jazz]], played on 2 and 4.
3.  **Snare Drum**: Used for low-volume [[Comping_Jazz]].
4.  **Bass Drum**: The softest voice, often "feathered" so it is felt rather than heard.

| Voice | Pop-[[Rock|Rock]] Priority | [[Jazz|Jazz]] Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Ride/[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] | Low/Medium | **High** |
| Snare Drum | **High** | Medium/Low |
| Bass Drum | **High** | **Lowest** |
