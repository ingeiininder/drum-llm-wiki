# Dotted Quarter Note
A **dotted quarter note** is a [[Quarter_Note]] with a dot placed immediately to its right. This dot increases the [[Duration]] of the quarter note by one-half of its original value.

**Value Calculation:**
*   A [[Quarter_Note]] is typically worth 1 [[Beat]] (in [[4-4_Time_Signature]]).
*   Half of a quarter note's value is 0.5 beats (equivalent to an [[Eighth_Note]]).
*   Therefore, a dotted quarter note is worth 1 + 0.5 = **1.5 beats**.

**Implication for Rhythmic Placement:**
If a dotted quarter note starts on beat 1, it will sustain through beat 1 and the "&" of beat 1. The next available place for a note or rest would then be on the "&" of beat 2. This often creates [[Syncopated_Rhythms]] as it naturally extends across a [[Subdivision|subdivision]].

**Pro-Tip:** Dotted [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] are extremely common in drumming, especially for creating grooves and fills with a "push" or "pull" feel. Practice feeling the 1.5-beat duration by [[Subdividing|subdividing]] into [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] ("1 & 2 &"). This helps in accurately placing the next attack after the dotted quarter note.
