# Intro to [[Two-Handed_16ths|Two-Handed 16ths]]
The "Intro to `[[Two-Handed_16ths]]`" explores a prevalent drumming style that emerged from the fusion of aggressive `[[Heavy_Metal]]` elements with the rhythmic drive of `[[Synth-Pop]]` during the past decade. This approach incorporates `[[Syncopated_Beats]]` and complex `[[Rhythmic_Textures]]` into `[[Alternative_Rock]]` and `[[Metal]]` settings, making it extremely popular.

This style is characterized by:
*   **Continuous 16th-Note Feel**: Often achieved by alternating hands between the `[[Hi-Hat]]` or `[[Ride_Cymbal]]` and the `[[Snare_Drum]]` or `[[Tom-Toms]]`, creating a dense, driving rhythm.
*   **[[Syncopation|Syncopation]]**: Strategic placement of [[Accents|accents]] and `[[Bass_Drum]]` patterns off the main beats to create tension and excitement.
*   **Dynamic Textures**: Varying the intensity and articulation to build energy throughout a song.

Influential bands that popularized this sound include `[[Nine_Inch_Nails]]`, `[[The_Killers]]`, and `[[Linkin_Park]]`. Mastering `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]` is essential for drummers playing in contemporary [[Rock|rock]] and metal genres.
