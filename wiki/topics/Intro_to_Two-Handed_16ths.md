# Intro to Two-Handed 16ths
The "Intro to `[[Two-Handed 16ths]]`" explores a prevalent drumming style that emerged from the fusion of aggressive `[[Heavy Metal]]` elements with the rhythmic drive of `[[Synth-Pop]]` during the past decade. This approach incorporates `[[Syncopated Beats]]` and complex `[[Rhythmic Textures]]` into `[[Alternative Rock]]` and `[[Metal]]` settings, making it extremely popular.

This style is characterized by:
*   **Continuous 16th-Note Feel**: Often achieved by alternating hands between the `[[Hi-Hat]]` or `[[Ride Cymbal]]` and the `[[Snare Drum]]` or `[[Tom-Toms]]`, creating a dense, driving rhythm.
*   **Syncopation**: Strategic placement of accents and `[[Bass Drum]]` patterns off the main beats to create tension and excitement.
*   **Dynamic Textures**: Varying the intensity and articulation to build energy throughout a song.

Influential bands that popularized this sound include `[[Nine Inch Nails]]`, `[[The Killers]]`, and `[[Linkin Park]]`. Mastering `[[Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove]]` is essential for drummers playing in contemporary rock and metal genres.
