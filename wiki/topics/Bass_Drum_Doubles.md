# [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]] Doubles

**[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]] Doubles** refer to playing two consecutive notes on the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]], often at high speeds or within syncopated 16th-note patterns.

### Execution and Articulation
- **Consistency**: Both notes of the double must have the same volume, articulation, and "weight."
- **Avoid "Muddy" Sound**: If the first note is choked or the second note is weak, the [[Groove|groove]] will lack conviction and sound "sloppy."
- **Unison Placement**: In [[Funk|Funk]]-[[Rock|Rock]], [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] doubles are frequently played in unison with the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] or ghost notes.

### Technical Pro-Tips
- **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]]**: Practice these doubles while mentally (or vocally) [[Subdividing|subdividing]] [[16th_Notes|16th notes]]. This ensures the second note doesn't "slide" or rush.
- **Technique**: Utilize techniques such as the "Slide Technique" or "Heel-Toe" to achieve rapid doubles with minimal effort.
- **Even Placement**: Focus on the "space" between the two notes to ensure they are mathematically even within the 16th-note grid.
