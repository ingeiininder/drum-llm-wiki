# Snare Drum Loud on 2 and 4 (Rock Shuffle)
A key dynamic instruction for the Unit 10 rock shuffle is to play the [[Snare Drum]] loudly on beats 2 and 4. This creates a strong backbeat that is characteristic of rock music, even within a shuffle feel.

**The Challenge:**
*   This instruction presents a specific coordination and dynamic challenge, especially when combined with the [[Floor_Tom_as_Ride_Surface_Rock_Shuffle]] technique. You must maintain a medium, controlled volume on the floor tom for the ride pattern while simultaneously executing powerful, accented snare hits on the backbeats.

**Performance Notes:**
*   **Dynamic Contrast**: The goal is a clear and impactful dynamic contrast between the relatively softer, rumbling floor tom and the loud, cutting snare drum.
*   **Independence**: This requires significant hand independence and dynamic control. Practice isolating the snare hits, ensuring they are consistently loud and sharp without affecting the volume or consistency of your ride pattern.
*   **Stick Height**: To achieve the loud snare hits, utilize higher stick heights for beats 2 and 4, allowing gravity to assist in generating power. For the floor tom, maintain a lower, more controlled stick height.
*   **Body Mechanics**: Ensure your body mechanics are efficient, allowing for powerful snare strokes without tension, while maintaining a relaxed approach to the floor tom.

Mastering this dynamic interplay is essential for delivering the authentic feel and energy of this particular rock shuffle.
