# Eighth-Note Triplets

Eighth-note triplets are a primary [[Subdivision]] where a single beat is divided into three equal parts. In a standard 4/4 time signature, there are twelve eighth-note triplets per measure.

### Counting and Execution
The standard vocalization for this subdivision is **"1-trip-let, 2-trip-let, 3-trip-let, 4-trip-let."** 

### Technical Application
*   **Alternating Sticking**: Typically practiced using R-L-R L-R-L patterns to ensure evenness.
*   **Tempo Development**: Mastery involves maintaining rhythmic integrity across a range of 60 BPM to 120 BPM.
*   **Reading Exercises**:
    *   **Ex 1.5**: Basic triplet reading.
    *   **Ex 1.7**: Eight-bar phrasing using triplets.
    *   **Ex 1.9**: Sixteen-bar extended triplet phrasing.

### Pro-Tip
When playing triplets, ensure the "let" (the third partial) does not "swing" or sit too close to the following downbeat. It must be mathematically equidistant between the "trip" and the next beat. Use a metronome to check the alignment of the downbeats.
