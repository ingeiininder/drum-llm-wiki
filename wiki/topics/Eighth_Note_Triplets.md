# Eighth-Note Triplets

Eighth-note triplets are a primary [[Subdivision]] where a single [[Beat|beat]] is divided into three equal parts. They are the rhythmic basis for [[Swing_Feel]] and [[Shuffle_Feel]]. They occur when a [[Quarter_Note]] (or a single [[Beat|beat]] in common time) is divided into three equal [[Eighth_Note]].

## Derivation and Concept
Normally, a [[Quarter_Note]] is divided into two [[Eighth_Note]] (1-&). With an eighth-note triplet, the same quarter note [[Duration|duration]] is now filled by **three** [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]]. This is indicated by a "3" placed above the beam connecting the three [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]].

In a 4/4 [[Measure|measure]], there are twelve eighth-note triplets (3 triplets per [[Beat|beat]] x 4 beats = 12).

## Counting and Execution
The standard vocalization for this subdivision is:
**"1-trip-let, 2-trip-let, 3-trip-let, 4-trip-let"**

### Technical Application
- **Alternating Sticking**: Typically practiced using **R-L-R L-R-L** patterns to ensure evenness.
- **Equality of Placement**: Every note in the triplet must be mathematically equidistant. A common mistake is "swinging" the third partial (the "let") or sitting it too close to the following downbeat.
- **[[Tempo|Tempo]] Development**: Practice maintaining rhythmic integrity across a wide range (e.g., 60 BPM to 120 BPM).

## Reading and [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]
Developing mastery over eighth-note triplets involves:
- **Ex 1.5**: Basic triplet reading.
- **Ex 1.7**: Eight-bar [[Phrasing|phrasing]] using triplets.

## Pro-Tip
To truly internalize eighth-note triplets, practice with a [[Metronome]] set to click on each triplet partial, then gradually move to only clicking on the quarter-note downbeats. This helps develop your internal "triplet grid."
