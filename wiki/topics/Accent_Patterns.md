# Accent Patterns
[[Accent_Patterns]] refer to the deliberate emphasis placed on specific notes within a rhythmic phrase or [[Sticking_Patterns]]. [[Accents|Accents]] are crucial for adding musicality, shaping phrases, and creating dynamic contrast in drumming.

In the context of [[Rudiment]]s like the [[Paradiddle]], varying the [[Accent_Patterns]] can dramatically change the feel and sound of the same [[Sticking_Patterns]]. For example, while a [[Single_Paradiddle]] (RLRR LRLL) typically [[Accents|accents]] the first stroke of each four-note grouping, shifting the accent to other notes can create entirely new rhythmic figures.

**Role of [[Accent_Patterns]]**:
*   **[[Phrasing|Phrasing]]**: Defines the beginning and end of musical phrases.
*   **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: Creates contrast between loud (accented) and soft (unaccented) notes.
*   **Rhythmic Variation**: Generates new rhythmic interpretations from existing [[Sticking_Patterns]].
*   **Musical Expression**: Adds emotion and character to drumming.

**Pro-Tip**: Practice [[Rudiment]]s both with their traditional [[Accent_Patterns]] and with all notes played evenly (unaccented). Then, experiment with shifting [[Accents|accents]] to different notes within the pattern. This will significantly improve your dynamic control and rhythmic versatility, making your drumming more expressive and engaging.
