# Double Stroke Technique
The [[Double Stroke Technique]] is a fundamental drumming technique that involves playing two consecutive strokes with a single hand. It is essential for executing various [[Drum Roll]] rudiments and for achieving speed and fluidity in drumming. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 7 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Core Principle:**
The technique relies on a combination of muscular effort for the first stroke and utilizing the natural [[Rebound Stroke]] of the [[Drumstick]] for the second stroke.

**Execution:**
1.  **First Stroke:** The hand initiates a stroke, striking the drum.
2.  **Second Stroke (Rebound):** Immediately after the first stroke, the stick is allowed to rebound naturally. The fingers (and sometimes a slight wrist flick) guide and control this rebound to produce a second, distinct stroke.

**Benefits:**
*   **Speed:** Allows for playing notes twice as fast as single strokes with the same hand.
*   **Rolls:** Forms the basis for all [[Double Stroke Roll]] rudiments (e.g., [[Five-Stroke Roll]], [[Six-Stroke Roll]], [[Seven-Stroke Roll]], [[Ten-Stroke Roll]]).
*   **Efficiency:** Maximizes the use of natural rebound, reducing muscular effort for rapid passages.
*   **Fluidity:** Contributes to a smoother, more connected sound compared to single strokes at high tempos.

**Key Considerations:**
*   **Evenness:** The two strokes must be equally spaced and have consistent dynamics. This is often the biggest challenge.
*   **Control:** The second stroke, being a rebound, requires careful control from the fingers and wrist to prevent it from being too weak or uncontrolled.
*   **Relaxation:** Tension will inhibit the natural rebound. A relaxed grip and wrist are crucial.

**Pro-Tip:** Practice the [[Double Stroke Technique]] slowly, focusing on making the two strokes sound identical in volume and spacing. Start by dropping the stick and letting it bounce twice, then gradually add a slight push from the fingers for the second stroke. Use a metronome and practice leading with both hands (RR LL RR LL).
