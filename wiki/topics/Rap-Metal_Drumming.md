# Rap-Metal Drumming

Rap-Metal (or Nu-Metal) is a fusion style that emerged prominently in the 1990s and 2000s, combining the aggressive textures of Heavy Metal with the syncopated, groove-heavy foundations of Hip-Hop. Key artists include Korn, Limp Bizkit, and Linkin Park.

### Stylistic Pillars
1. **Syncopation**: Unlike traditional straight-ahead metal, Rap-Metal relies heavily on syncopated [[Bass Drum]] patterns that lock in with rhythmic guitar riffs.
2. **Dynamic Contrast**: The style often alternates between "tight" verses and "explosive" choruses.
3. **Sonic Textures**: Uses a mix of [[Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove]]s and wide-open, "sloshy" [[Hi-Hat]] patterns.

### Sectional Articulation
- **The Verse (Section A)**: Characterized by a "tight" sound. Use a [[Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove]] on the hi-hat played softly (*piano*) to make the [[Rim Shot]] snare and staccato bass drum pop.
- **The Chorus (Section B)**: Characterized by a "loose" sound. Switch to a [[One-Handed Eighth-Note Groove]] with a partially open hi-hat played *fortissimo*.

### Pro-Tip: The Dynamic Illusion
By playing the hi-hats softly during the 16th-note sections, you create a "dynamic illusion." This makes the kick and snare appear much louder and more authoritative in the mix without requiring the drummer to over-play.
