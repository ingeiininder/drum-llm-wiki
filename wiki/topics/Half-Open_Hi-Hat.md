# Half-[[Open_Hi-Hat|Open Hi-Hat]]

The **Half-[[Open_Hi-Hat|Open Hi-Hat]]** (notated with the symbol **Ø**) instructs the drummer to play the hi-hats with slight pressure, creating a "sloshy" or "washy" sound.

### Application in R&B/Hip-Hop
In [[Hip-Hop_Drumming]], the half-open sound is often used to "build" into a section. 
- **Open-Handed Technique**: A common stylistic choice is to play the half-[[Open_Hi-Hat|open hi-hat]] with the left hand while the right hand moves across the toms. 
- **Sonic Result**: The "sheen" of the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] wash paired with the "rumbling undercurrent" of the toms creates a dense, powerful texture.

### Comparison
- **Closed (+)**: Tight, articulate.
- **Open (o)**: Fully sustained, ringing.
- **Half-Open (Ø)**: Controlled sizzle.
