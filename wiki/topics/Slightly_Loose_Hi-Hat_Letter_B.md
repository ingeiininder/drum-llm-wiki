# Slightly Loose Hi-Hat (Letter B)
For the 'B' section of a song, the hi-hat articulation shifts to a [[Slightly Loose Hi-Hat (Letter B)]] pattern, designed to create a more energetic and driving feel compared to the 'A' section. This involves:

*   **Pattern:** A two-handed 16th-note hi-hat pattern.
*   **Evenness and Forcefulness:** The pattern should be played evenly and forcefully.
*   **Dynamic:** A dynamic of mezzo forte (medium loud) is recommended.
*   **Stick Contact:** This sound is achieved by using the middle/shoulder of the stick.

This technique, combined with a subtle looseness (often implying slight openings), gives the illusion that the 'B' section speeds up slightly, thereby generating a large amount of musical excitement and forward momentum.

**Pro-Tip:** The "slightly loose" feel often comes from a subtle, controlled opening of the hi-hats on certain notes, adding a shimmering, driving quality that propels the music forward. This is a key component of the [[Letter B Section Drum Articulations]].
