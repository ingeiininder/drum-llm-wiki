# Blues-Rock
[[Blues-Rock]] is a subgenre of [[Rock]] music that emerged in the late 1960s, building upon the foundations of early [[Rock_'n'_Roll]] which itself was heavily influenced by the [[Blues]].

**Characteristics:**
*   **Blues Foundation:** Retains the harmonic and melodic structures of traditional [[Blues]], often utilizing the [[12-Bar_Blues]] progression and [[Blues_Scale]].
*   **Rock Elements:** Incorporates the energy, [[Loud_Amplification]], and [[Instrumental_Combo]] format of [[Rock_'n'_Roll]].
*   **Riff-Oriented:** Often features prominent guitar [[Riff]]s and extended instrumental sections.
*   **Pioneering Bands:** Key bands that defined the [[Blues-Rock]] sound include Cream, Led Zeppelin, and ZZ Top, known for their powerful, often louder and more aggressive approach compared to earlier [[Blues]] artists.

**Drumming in Blues-Rock:**
Drummers in [[Blues-Rock]] often combine the shuffle and swing feels of [[Blues]] with the driving backbeats and power of [[Rock]]. This can involve:
*   Strong [[Back-Beat]]s on beats 2 and 4.
*   Use of [[Shuffle]] and [[Swing]] rhythms, especially in slower tempos.
*   Dynamic playing, supporting both raw, powerful sections and more nuanced, bluesy passages.
*   Emphasis on [[Groove|groove]] and supporting the guitar [[Riff]]s.

**Pro-Tip:** Listen to the drumming of John Bonham (Led Zeppelin) or Ginger Baker (Cream) to understand the blend of [[Blues]] sensitivity and [[Rock]] power. Focus on developing a strong [[Back-Beat]] and the ability to switch between straight and [[Swing]] feels seamlessly.
