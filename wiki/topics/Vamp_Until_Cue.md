# Vamp Until Cue

"Vamp until cue" is a performance instruction directing the musician to repeat a short, looped section (usually 2 or 4 bars) until a visual or musical signal is given to move to the next section.

### Key Rules
1.  **Visual Connection**: The drummer must maintain eye contact with the bandleader or soloist (e.g., the guitar player in a [[Bossa_Nova]] setting).
2.  **[[Phrasing|Phrasing]]**: Even though the section is a loop, transitions should always occur at the end of a full phrase (e.g., if it's a 4-bar vamp, the total [[Duration|duration]] will be a multiple of 4: 8, 12, 16, etc.).
3.  **The Cue**: Usually a head nod, a specific musical lick, or "big eyes."
4.  **The Transition**: Once the cue is received, complete the current iteration of the vamp before moving to the next letter (e.g., Letter D).

### Common Usage
*   **Introductions**: Establishing the [[Groove|groove]] before the melody starts.
*   **Solo Sections**: Allowing the soloist to determine the length of their improvisation.
