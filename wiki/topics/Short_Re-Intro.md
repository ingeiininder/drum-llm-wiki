# Short Re-Intro
A "Short Re-Intro" is a brief, often two-bar, musical section that serves as a transition back to an earlier part of the song, typically the [[Verse_(Song_Section)]] or the main [[Intro]]. It's used after a significant section like a [[Chorus_(Song_Section)]] or a series of [[First_and_Second_Endings]] to reset the listener's ear and prepare for the return of familiar material.

In this curriculum, a short re-intro is used after the second ending of [[Letter_B_(Rehearsal_Mark)]] to set up a return to [[Letter_A]] (the verse). It often features a [[Tight_Hi-Hat]] [[Groove|groove]], providing a clean and concise reset.

**Pro-Tip:** Drummers should treat a re-intro as a palate cleanser. It's an opportunity to pull back slightly in energy or complexity, creating a clear demarcation before the next section. Ensure the [[Groove|groove]] is solid and precise, setting a strong foundation for the upcoming verse.
