# Roll Variations
[[Drum_Rolls]] are fundamental [[Drum_Rudiments]] that create a sustained sound on a drum. [[Roll_Variations]] refer to the different methods of executing these rolls, often distinguished by the number of strokes per hand or the [[Duration|duration]] of the roll. Mastery involves understanding and executing variations such as:

*   **Single Stroke Rolls**: Each hand plays a single stroke.
*   **Double Stroke Rolls**: Each hand plays two strokes.
*   **Multiple Bounce Rolls (Press Rolls)**: Strokes are pressed into the drumhead to create multiple bounces.
*   **Five-Stroke Roll**, [[Seven-Stroke_Roll]], [[Nine_Stroke_Roll]], etc.

Proficiency in these variations is essential for [[Dynamic_Control]], musical [[Phrasing|phrasing]], and seamless transitions within drumming.

**Pro-Tip**: Practice rolls slowly with a [[Metronome]] to ensure evenness between hands and consistent bounce. Gradually increase [[Tempo|tempo]] only when precision is maintained.
**Common Pitfall**: Unevenness between hands, leading to a "lumpy" sound rather than a smooth, sustained roll. This often stems from insufficient attention to [[Stick_Height]] and rebound control.
