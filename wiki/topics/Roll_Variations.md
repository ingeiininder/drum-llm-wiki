# Roll Variations
[[Drum Rolls]] are fundamental [[Drum Rudiments]] that create a sustained sound on a drum. [[Roll Variations]] refer to the different methods of executing these rolls, often distinguished by the number of strokes per hand or the duration of the roll. Mastery involves understanding and executing variations such as:

*   **Single Stroke Rolls**: Each hand plays a single stroke.
*   **Double Stroke Rolls**: Each hand plays two strokes.
*   **Multiple Bounce Rolls (Press Rolls)**: Strokes are pressed into the drumhead to create multiple bounces.
*   **Five-Stroke Roll**, [[Seven-Stroke Roll]], [[Nine_Stroke_Roll]], etc.

Proficiency in these variations is essential for [[Dynamic Control]], musical phrasing, and seamless transitions within drumming.

**Pro-Tip**: Practice rolls slowly with a [[Metronome]] to ensure evenness between hands and consistent bounce. Gradually increase tempo only when precision is maintained.
**Common Pitfall**: Unevenness between hands, leading to a "lumpy" sound rather than a smooth, sustained roll. This often stems from insufficient attention to [[Stick_Height]] and rebound control.
