# Meter
Meter can be understood as the fundamental [[Pulse|pulse]] or "heartbeat" of a piece of music, providing its forward momentum. It's the underlying framework that organizes beats into regular, recurring groups.

## Characteristics
*   **[[Pulse|Pulse]]**: All music possesses a forward-moving [[Pulse|pulse]], counted in individual [[Beat]]s.
*   **Grouping**: Meter is measured in consistent groups of beats. The most common groupings are:
    *   **Two Beats**: Often found in genres like Country, Marches, and Polka.
    *   **Three Beats**: Characteristic of Waltzes.
    *   **Four Beats**: Prevalent in Pop, [[Rock|Rock]], and R&B.

## Importance for Drummers
Understanding meter is crucial for drummers as it dictates the rhythmic foundation and feel of a song. It informs how grooves are constructed, how fills are placed, and how to interact with other musicians. A drummer's primary role is often to establish and maintain a clear sense of meter.
