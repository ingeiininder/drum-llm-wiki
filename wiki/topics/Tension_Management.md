# Tension Management

**Tension Management** is the practice of maintaining physical relaxation while playing, which is critical for both [[Injury_Prevention|injury prevention]] and sound quality.

### Identifying Tension
- **Visual Cue**: Look at the gap between the thumb and index finger. If there is **no space**, you are likely gripping the stick too tightly.
- **Sonic Cue**: A tight grip produces a "choked," "pingy," or "[[Staccato|staccato]]" sound. A relaxed grip (imagine holding an egg) allows the instrument to vibrate, producing a "bright," "vibrant," and "wide" sound.

### Risks of Tension
- **Physical**: Playing with tension is dangerous and can lead to long-term repetitive strain injuries.
- **Musical**: Tension causes the drummer to rush and prevents the "pocket" from developing, especially in [[Swung_16th_Phrasing]].

> [!PRO-TIP]
> Periodically check your [[Fulcrum|fulcrum]] during long 16th-note [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] passages. If the hand feels "locked," stop, breathe, and reset with a looser grip.
