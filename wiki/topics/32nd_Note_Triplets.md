# 32nd-Note [[Triplets|Triplets]]

A **32nd-note triplet** is a rhythmic grouping where three notes occupy the space normally taken by two [[32nd_Notes]]. This represents a further subdivision of the [[16th-Note_Triplet]].

### Mathematical Breakdown
In a standard 4/4 [[Time_Signature]]:
- **Two 32nd notes** = One 16th note.
- **Three 32nd-note [[Triplets|triplets]]** = Two 32nd notes (or one 16th note).
- **Six 32nd-note [[Triplets|triplets]]** = One 8th note.
- **Twelve 32nd-note [[Triplets|triplets]]** = One Quarter note (one full [[Beat|beat]]).

### Placement and Feel
Developing a "feel" for these notes requires extreme precision in [[Subdivision]]. 
- **[[Metronome|Metronome]] Strategy**: When practicing at slow tempos (e.g., 50 BPM), set the [[Metronome|metronome]] to click on [[8th_Notes]] (100 BPM). This provides a closer reference point for the downbeat and upbeat, making it easier to slot the twelve notes per [[Beat|beat]] accurately.

### Common Combinations
32nd-note [[Triplets|triplets]] are rarely played in isolation for long periods. They are typically mixed with:
- [[16th_Notes]]
- Standard [[32nd_Notes]]
- [[16th-Note_Triplets]]

### Technical Execution
On the drum set, these are often played as "singles" (RLRLRL) or as part of [[Rudiment]] like the [[Six_Stroke_Roll]] or [[Double_Stroke_Roll]] variations.

> [!PRO-TIP]
> Avoid "crushing" the [[Triplets|triplets]]. Because they are so fast, there is a tendency to play them as a buzz or a blurred roll. Focus on "hearing" the individual notes within the grouping to ensure rhythmic integrity.
