# Finger Control vs. Wrist Control

In high-level technical drumming, particularly within [[Jazz-Fusion]] and [[Drum and Bass]], the ability to switch between different mechanical sources of motion is essential for maintaining both speed and power.

### Finger Control
- **Mechanism**: Utilizing the fingers (primarily index, middle, and ring) to bounce the stick.
- **Usage**: Ideal for fast groups of three 16th-notes or rapid-fire [[Ghost Notes]].
- **Benefit**: Maximum speed with minimum effort; allows for high-velocity subdivisions.

### Wrist Control (Locked Grip)
- **Mechanism**: The wrist provides the primary fulcrum and power source, often with a more "locked" finger position.
- **Usage**: Used for powerful, straight 8th-notes or heavy [[Back-Beat]] accents.
- **Benefit**: Provides the necessary "weight" and volume for backbeats and unison ensemble hits.

### The "Angel" Application
In the track [[Angel_Track.md]], the drummer must employ these techniques in quick succession. The right hand often switches back and forth within a single measure to accommodate the intricate hi-hat patterns of a "human" drum and bass feel.

**Common Pitfall**: Many students try to play fast finger-control passages using their wrists, leading to tension and "choking" the stick, which ruins the fluid feel of the groove.
