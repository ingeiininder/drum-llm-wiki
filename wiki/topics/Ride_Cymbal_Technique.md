# Ride Cymbal Technique
The [[Ride Cymbal]] is used to control the "sonic space" of a song, particularly during choruses and bridges.

### Tip vs. Shoulder
*   **Tip of the Stick**: Used for standard time-keeping. It produces a clear, defined "ping" sound.
*   **Shoulder of the Stick**: Occasionally "shouldering" the ride (striking with the neck of the stick) helps spread the sound. This fills a larger amount of sonic space, allowing for a more relaxed, "washy" feel.

### The Fermata Roll
At the conclusion of a piece (marked by a [[Fermata]]), a common technique is to perform a very quiet **double-stroke roll** on the ride or crash cymbal.
*   **Placement**: Play a few inches from the bell.
*   **Dynamic**: Usually starts at *piano* (p) and decrescendos to *pianissimo* (pp).
