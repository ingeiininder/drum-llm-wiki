# Ride Cymbal Technique
The [[Ride_Cymbal]] is the primary time-keeping cymbal used to control the "sonic space" and "energy" of a song. Unlike the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]], which is often used for tighter, more [[Rhythmic_Textures|rhythmic textures]], the ride provides a sustained wash of sound.

## [[Articulations|Articulations]] & Execution

### 1. Tip vs. Shoulder
- **Tip of the Stick**: Strike the **body** of the cymbal for a clear, defined "ping" with minimal wash. Used for standard time-keeping in Pop, [[Funk|Funk]], and [[Jazz|Jazz]].
- **Shoulder of the Stick**: Striking the **bell** or **edge** with the neck/shoulder of the stick (the thickest part of the taper). 

### 2. The Bell Strike
- **Technique**: Use the shoulder of the stick about **one-third down from the tip**. Strike the bell firmly for a piercing, bell-like tone.
- **Application**: Crucial for **[[Hard_Rock_Drumming]]** and marking section changes (Letter B).

### 3. The Fermata Roll
At the conclusion of a piece or during a dramatic pause (marked by a [[Fermata]]), perform a delicate **double-stroke roll** on the ride or crash cymbal.
- **Placement**: Play a few inches from the bell for a controlled, shimmering sound.
- **Dynamic**: Start at *piano* (p) and decrescendo to *pianissimo* (pp).

## Stylistic Applications

### Pop-[[Rock|Rock]] & Hard [[Rock|Rock]]
- **Sectional Lifting**: The ride is typically introduced in the **Chorus** or **Bridge** to "open up" the sound.
- **Hard [[Rock|Rock]] Specifics**: Maintain **quarter-note [[Accents|accents]]** on the bell. These [[Accents|accents]] (counts 1, 2, 3, 4) act as a rhythmic anchor for the band during loud sections.

### [[Blues|Blues]] & Slow 12/8
- **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: Play halfway between the edge and the bell. This provides a balance of definition (ping) and "underlying wash" that fills the frequency gap in a [[Blues|blues]] trio.
- **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]]**: Maintain a continuous **[[12-8_Feel]]** or **[[Eighth-Note_Triplets]]** [[Pulse|pulse]].

### [[Jazz|Jazz]]
- **The Swing Pattern**: The ride is the lead voice in [[Jazz|jazz]], playing the "spang-a-lang" swing rhythm.
- **Balance**: The ride cymbal is often the loudest element in a [[Jazz|jazz]] drummer's mix, providing the [[Pulse|pulse]] while the snare and [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] provide interactive "comping."

### Modern [[Rock|Rock]] & Indie
- **Wall of Sound**: Crashing on the ride (striking the edge with the shoulder) is common in modern alternative [[Rock|rock]] to create a massive, saturated texture.
