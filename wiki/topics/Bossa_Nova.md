# Bossa Nova

**Bossa Nova** (Portuguese for "new trend") is a sophisticated style of Brazilian music that emerged between 1958 and 1963. Popularized by legendary figures such as **Antonio Carlos Jobim**, **Vinicius de Moraes**, and **Joao Gilberto**, it significantly influenced the standard [[Jazz|jazz]] repertoire.

### Musical Characteristics
In a drumset context, Bossa Nova is defined by its understated elegance and specific [[Ostinato]] patterns. Unlike the high-energy Samba, Bossa Nova is typically performed at a lower dynamic level, often around *mezzo-piano* (mp).

### Core Components
1.  **Cymbal Pattern**: Continuous [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] played on the [[Hi-Hat]] or [[Ride_Cymbal]].
2.  **Cross-Stick Pattern**: A signature two-bar phrase (often referred to as a variation of the [[Clave]]).
3.  **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: A consistent dotted-quarter/eighth note rhythm (1 and the "&" of 3) played with a soft, "feathered" touch.

### Performance Context
Bossa Nova charts often utilize an [[ABAB_Form]], which is frequently "open" to allow for solo choruses. Drummers must be adept at transitioning between the "Head" (melody) and solo sections by changing textures, such as moving from the hi-hat to the ride cymbal.

> [!TIP]
> **Pro-Tip**: Authenticity in Bossa Nova comes from the "blend." The [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] should be felt more than heard, sitting just below the [[Cross_Stick]] in the mix. Avoid the common pitfall of accenting the second note of the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] pattern.
