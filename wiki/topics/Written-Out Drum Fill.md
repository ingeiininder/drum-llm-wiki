# Written-Out Drum Fill
A [[Written-Out Drum Fill]] occurs when a composer specifically writes out a drum fill in the musical chart, indicating that a particular fill should be played at a specific time. This ensures the composer's exact musical intention is realized.

For example, in bar 16 of Letter B, a fill is written out between the [[snare drum]]/[[floor tom]] and the [[bass drum]]. This fill is preceded by a [[crash cymbal]] on beat one of measure 16, which serves as both an ending to the preceding [[Ensemble Figures]] and the beginning of the measure 16 fill.
