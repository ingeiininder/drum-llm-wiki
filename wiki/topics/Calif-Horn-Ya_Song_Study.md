# Calif-Horn-Ya: Song Study

"Calif-Horn-Ya" (written by Jeff Salem, Josh Harris, and Dave DiMarco) is a technical exercise in maintaining a **laid-back feel**. The primary challenge is resisting the urge to rush, especially when the instrumentation becomes more complex.

### The "Coastal Drive" Concept
To achieve the correct [[Pocket]], visualize a leisurely, scenic drive. Rushing the tempo ("formula racing") will "crash" the groove and destroy the song's aesthetic.

### Critical Sections
*   **Section B & A2**: The bass player increases activity here. Avoid the common pitfall of matching that busyness; instead, maintain a steady [[Groove]] to anchor the rhythmic tension between the bass and horns.
*   **Horn Shots**: In sections A1 and A2, the horn shots are *not* anticipated—they land squarely on beat 1. 
*   **Dynamics**: Play "around" the horn crescendos, using your volume to support the ensemble's arc.

### Technical Requirements
*   **Fills**: Keep fills simple. Their primary purpose here is to set up horn figures, such as the shot in the third bar of section A.
*   **Groove Transitions**: Note the specific groove changes in the last two bars of the A sections, which often lead into a [[Drum_Fill]].
