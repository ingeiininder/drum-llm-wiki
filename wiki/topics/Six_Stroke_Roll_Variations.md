# Six-Stroke [[Roll_Variations|Roll Variations]]

The **Six-Stroke Roll** is a cornerstone rudiment for modern drumset playing, offering a unique blend of doubles and accented singles. In the Musicians Institute curriculum, these variations are primarily practiced as **16th-note [[Triplets|triplets]]** to facilitate fluid movement across the kit.

### Core [[Sticking_Patterns|Sticking Patterns]]
1.  **Standard Six-Stroke Roll**: `R L L R R L` (typically accented on the first and last notes).
2.  **Inverted Six-Stroke Roll**: `L R L L R R` (shifting the placement of the [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]]).
3.  **Sticking Variation (1)**: A modified sequence designed for specific rhythmic emphasis.
4.  **Sticking Variation (2)**: Further permutations to challenge [[Drumset_Mobility]].

### Technical Execution
*   **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]]**: Ensure the 16th-note [[Triplets|triplets]] are even. A common pitfall is "galloping" the doubles; they must occupy the same rhythmic space as the singles.
*   **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: The power of the six-stroke roll comes from the contrast between the accented singles and the "ghosted" or lower-dynamic doubles.

### Pro-Tip
When transitioning these to the drumset, keep the doubles on the [[Snare_Drum]] while moving the accented singles to the [[Toms]] or [[Cymbals]]. This creates a "melodic" shell around a consistent snare ghost-note center.
