# Flams (Rudiment Family)
The [[Flams (Rudiment Family)]] is one of the five [[Rudiment Families]] and is defined by rudiments that incorporate a "flam." A flam consists of two notes played almost simultaneously, where one note (the grace note) precedes the main note by a very small margin. This creates a fatter, broader sound than a single stroke.

**Core Concept:**
A flam is not a true simultaneous hit but rather a very close "drag" or "brush" of one stick (the grace note) just before the main stroke. The grace note is typically played softer and from a lower height than the main note.

**Examples (as mentioned in OCR):**
*   Flam Accent
*   Flam Tap
*   Swiss Triplet

**Application:**
Flams add a distinct character and emphasis to notes, making them sound fuller and more impactful. They are widely used in marching percussion, jazz, rock, and orchestral drumming to add color, articulation, and rhythmic drive.

**Pro-Tip:** The key to a good flam is the precise timing and dynamic contrast between the grace note and the main note. The grace note should be light and quick, almost "kissing" the drumhead, while the main note provides the primary impact. Practice making your flams sound consistent, regardless of which hand plays the grace note.
