# Ostinato Reading Technique

**Ostinato Reading** is a foundational coordination method where a student maintains a repetitive rhythmic pattern (the ostinato) in one or more limbs while reading independent rhythmic figures with the remaining limbs. This technique bridges the gap between pure technical exercises and real-world performance applications like [[Jazz]] or [[Latin_Drumming]].

### Core Methodology
In the Musicians Institute curriculum, this is approached by assigning specific limbs to the ostinato and others to the reading material:
- **Hand Ostinatos**: Maintaining a pattern on the [[Ride_Cymbal]] or [[Hi-Hat]] while reading rhythms with the [[Bass_Drum]].
- **[[Snare_Drum|Snare Drum]] Ostinatos**: Using the left hand to play a fixed rhythm while the right hand or feet execute the reading text.

### Swung Feel Application
When reading eighth-note exercises in a [[Swung_Feel]], the notation is interpreted through a triplet grid. Any note written on the "&" (upbeat) is shifted to the "let" (the third partial) of the [[Triplets]]. 

### Pro-Tips
- **Vocalize**: Always count the underlying [[Subdivision|subdivision]] (e.g., "1-ta-la-2-ta-la") out loud to ensure the ostinato does not drift when the reading figures become complex.
- **Limb Independence**: If a figure causes the ostinato to falter, isolate the specific "limb pairing" where the conflict occurs.
- **Common Pitfall**: Avoid "flaming" between the ostinato and the reading limb. They should strike simultaneously with perfect alignment.
