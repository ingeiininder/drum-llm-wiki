# Note Head
A note head is a primary symbol in [[Music Notation]] that is written on [[The Staff]] (either on a line or in a space). Its position indicates *where* a particular pitch or drum component occurs, and its appearance often contributes to indicating *when* (its [[Rhythmic Values|rhythmic value]]).

## Appearance and Design
Note heads can vary in appearance, with different designs conveying different rhythmic durations or specific instructions. Common variations include:
*   **Solid Dots**: Typically used for shorter durations like [[Quarter Note]]s, [[Eighth Note]]s, and [[Sixteenth Note]]s.
*   **Open Dots (Hollow)**: Often used for longer durations like [[Whole Note]]s and [[Half Note]]s.
*   **Triangles**: Can be used for specific percussion instruments or effects.
*   **Xs**: Commonly used in [[Drum Set Notation Key]] to represent [[Cymbal]]s (e.g., [[Hi Hat Cymbal (Notation)]], [[Ride Cymbal (Notation)]], [[Crash Cymbal (Notation)]]).
*   **Ovals**
*   **Diamonds**

## Placement
The placement of a note head on a specific line or in a specific space on [[The Staff]] determines:
*   **Pitch**: For melodic instruments, this indicates the specific musical pitch to be played.
*   **Drum Component**: For [[Drum Set Notation Key]], this indicates which drum or cymbal is to be struck.

The note head is often connected to a [[Stem (Music Notation)]] to further define its rhythmic value.
