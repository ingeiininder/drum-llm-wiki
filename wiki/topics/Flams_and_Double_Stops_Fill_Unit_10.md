# Flams and Double Stops [[Fill|Fill]] (Unit 10)
This is a recurring [[Fill|fill]] in the Unit 10 [[Rock_Shuffle|rock shuffle]], appearing three times (bars 8, 16, and 25). It's a complex [[Fill|fill]] combining [[Flams]] and [[Double_Stops]] over a consistent bass drum pattern.

**Structure of the [[Fill|Fill]]:**
*   **Bass Drum**: The [[Bass_Drum]] maintains a steady "four-on-the-floor" pattern throughout the [[Fill|fill]], providing a solid rhythmic anchor.
*   **Hands (Toms/Snare/Cymbals)**: The hands are responsible for filling in the upbeats on various drum kit components.
*   **Flams**: Technically, the [[Fill|fill]] features flams on the [[Toms]] on the "&" of beats 1, 2, and 3.
    *   **Sticking**: These should be played as *right-hand flams*, meaning the right hand is the primary stroke, and the left hand plays the grace note.
    *   **Sound**: The flams should be "very wide and open," indicating a clear separation between the grace note and the primary stroke, creating a full, broad sound rather than a tight, choked flam.
*   **Double Stops**: On [[Beat|beat]] 4, the flams come together to form double stops.
    *   **Instrumentation**: This double stop is played between the [[Snare_Drum]] and [[Crash_Cymbal]].
    *   **Variation**: In [[Measure|measure]] 25, this part is specifically played between the [[Floor_Tom]] and [[Rack_Tom]], offering a different timbre for the double stop.

**Performance Notes:**
*   **Coordination**: This [[Fill|fill]] demands excellent four-way coordination (bass drum, flamming hands, and double stop). Practice each element separately before combining them.
*   **[[Flam_Technique|Flam Technique]]**: Focus on achieving wide, open flams. The grace note should precede the primary stroke just enough to create a "splat" sound, not two distinct notes.
*   **Dynamic Balance**: Ensure the flams and double stops are played with appropriate [[Dynamics|dynamics]] to cut through, while the bass drum remains consistent.
*   **Context**: As a recurring [[Fill|fill]], ensure its execution is consistent each time it appears, while also allowing for subtle dynamic variations if the musical context demands it.
