# Snare Drum Rim Shot Articulations

A **Rim Shot** is executed by striking the center of the drumhead and the rim simultaneously. In modern drumming, the placement of the stick tip on the head significantly alters the drum's harmonic profile and "dryness."

### 1. Off-Center Rim Shot (Mezzo-Forte)
- **Placement**: Strike the drumhead just off-center (tip in the lower half of the center dot).
- **Sound**: Provides a crisp attack with a balanced amount of ring.
- **Execution**: Start the stroke with the wrist and "grab" the stick with the back three fingers just before impact—similar to a "high-five" slap.

### 2. "Just Past Center" Rim Shot (Forte)
- **Placement**: Aim for the area just past the absolute center of the drumhead.
- **Sound**: This produces a **drier** tone with fewer overtones and increased projection.
- **Context**: Used in higher-intensity sections (like a B section or Bridge) to differentiate from the main verse groove.

### Pro-Tip: The "Finger Snap"
To achieve the most authoritative rim shot, the stroke should be initiated by the wrist but "finished" by the fingers. This adds the necessary velocity for the stick to "crack" against the rim and head simultaneously.
