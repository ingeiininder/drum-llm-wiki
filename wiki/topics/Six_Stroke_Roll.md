# Six-Stroke Roll
The **Six-Stroke Roll** is a fundamental [[Rudiment]] consisting of four double strokes and two single strokes. In a traditional right-hand lead, the sticking is typically **R L L R R L**.

### Traditional Application
Traditionally, the Six-Stroke Roll is played with accents on the two single strokes (the first and last notes of the grouping). 
- **Sticking**: **R** L L R R **L**
- **Rhythmic Value**: Often interpreted as sextuplets or as a 16th-note based pattern (e.g., two 16ths and a triplet, or within a 4/4 framework).

### Drum Set Application
The single strokes of the Six-Stroke Roll are easily moved around the kit to create melodic fills and movements:
1. **Tom Movement**: Move the first "R" to the floor tom and the last "L" to the high tom.
2. **Cymbal Accents**: Play the single strokes on cymbals reinforced by the [[Bass Drum]].
3. **Linear Phrasing**: Integrating the single strokes into a [[Linear Drumming]] pattern by replacing one of the singles with a kick drum hit.

### Pro-Tip
To master the Six-Stroke Roll, focus on the transition between the single stroke and the start of the double. The "throw" of the single stroke should provide the energy for the subsequent [[Double Stroke Roll|Double Strokes]].
