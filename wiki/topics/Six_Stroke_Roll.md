# Six-Stroke Roll

The **Six-Stroke Roll** is a versatile and fundamental [[Rudiments|Rudiment]] consisting of six notes. It is primarily categorized under the roll family and is highly favored for its natural rhythmic flow in fills and grooves across [[Rock|Rock]], [[Funk|Funk]], and [[Jazz|Jazz]].

## Sticking Variations
The Six-Stroke Roll can be articulated in two primary ways:

1.  **Standard Sticking**: **R R L L R L** (or L L R R L R)
    - Two [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]] followed by two single strokes.
2.  **Traditional/Inverted Sticking**: **R L L R R L** (or L R R L L R)
    - Two single strokes (the first and last notes) flanking two sets of [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]]. This is the version most commonly used when [[Accents|accents]] are required on the single strokes.

## Rhythmic Interpretation
It is most frequently interpreted as **Sextuplets** or as a **Sixteenth-note triplet** pattern within a 4/4 framework.

## Drum Set Applications
The single strokes of the Six-Stroke Roll are often moved around the drum set to create melodic movement:
- **Tom Movement**: Move the first single "R" to the floor tom and the last "L" to the high tom for a sweeping melodic [[Fill|fill]].
- **Cymbal [[Accents|Accents]]**: Strike a cymbal along with the [[Bass_Drum]] on the accented single strokes.
- **Linear [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]**: Integrate the pattern into a [[Linear_Drumming]] sequence by replacing one of the single strokes with a kick drum hit.

## Pro-Tip
To master the Six-Stroke Roll, focus on the **"throw"** of the single strokes. The energy from the single stroke should provide the rebound necessary for the following double. Practice slowly with a **[[Metronome]]** to ensure the doubles are as clear and loud as the singles.
