# [[Rehearsal_Letters|Rehearsal Letters]] in 12-Bar [[Blues|Blues]]
In musical charts, [[Rehearsal_Letters]] (or numbers) are used to mark specific sections, aiding in navigation and communication during practice and performance. In the context of the [[12_Bar_Blues_Structure]], a specific convention is often used.

**Convention**:
*   Similar to 32-bar forms (like AABA or ABAB), the 12-bar [[Blues|blues]] structure is often repeated in its phrase length of 12 bars.
*   In this [[Chart|chart]], each complete 12-bar section is labeled as an "A section."
*   Each subsequent 12-bar repetition receives a numeric suffix (e.g., A1, A2, A3, A4).

**Purpose**:
*   Provides clear reference points for musicians.
*   Helps to quickly jump to specific sections during rehearsal.

**Pro-Tip**: Always pay close attention to [[Rehearsal_Letters|rehearsal letters]] and numbers. They are critical for understanding the form of the song and ensuring you are playing the correct part at the correct time, especially when dealing with repeats and variations between sections.
