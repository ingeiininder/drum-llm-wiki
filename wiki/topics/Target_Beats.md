# Target Beats

In the context of [[Ensemble_Figure_Interpretation|Ensemble Reading]], a **Target Beat** is the exact rhythmic location where a drummer begins or focuses their [[Set-up Fills|Set-up Fill]]. 

### Identifying the Target
The target beat is determined by the placement of the ensemble figure. To provide a clear signal to the band, the drummer usually targets the beat (or beats) immediately preceding the figure.

| Ensemble Figure Location | Typical Target Beat |
| :--- | :--- |
| Downbeat of 1 | Beat 4 (of the previous measure) |
| Downbeat of 2 | Beat 1 |
| Downbeat of 3 | Beat 2 |
| Downbeat of 4 | Beat 3 |

### Importance in Sight-Reading
Great sight-readers identify the target beat "on the fly." By recognizing the target, the drummer can process the notated music and execute a fill that fits the musical context without hesitation.

### Pro-Tips
- **The "One-Beat Rule"**: For most standard quarter-note figures, targeting the single beat prior is the most effective way to keep the band together without cluttering the arrangement.
- **Upbeat Targets**: When setting up an [[Upbeat Ensemble Figures|Upbeat Figure]] (e.g., the "&" of 2), the target is often the downbeat of that same beat (Beat 2).
