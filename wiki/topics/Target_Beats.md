# Target Beats

In the context of [[Ensemble_Figure_Interpretation|Ensemble Reading]], a **[[Target_Beat|Target Beat]]** is the exact rhythmic location where a drummer begins or focuses their [[Set-up_Fill|Set-up Fill]]. 

### Identifying the Target
The [[Target_Beat|target beat]] is determined by the placement of the ensemble figure. To provide a clear signal to the band, the drummer usually targets the [[Beat|beat]] (or beats) immediately preceding the figure.

| Ensemble Figure Location | Typical [[Target_Beat|Target Beat]] |
| :--- | :--- |
| Downbeat of 1 | [[Beat|Beat]] 4 (of the previous [[Measure|measure]]) |
| Downbeat of 2 | [[Beat|Beat]] 1 |
| Downbeat of 3 | [[Beat|Beat]] 2 |
| Downbeat of 4 | [[Beat|Beat]] 3 |

### Importance in Sight-Reading
Great sight-readers identify the [[Target_Beat|target beat]] "on the fly." By recognizing the target, the drummer can process the notated music and execute a [[Fill|fill]] that fits the musical context without hesitation.

### Pro-Tips
- **The "One-[[Beat|Beat]] Rule"**: For most standard quarter-note figures, targeting the single [[Beat|beat]] prior is the most effective way to keep the band together without cluttering the arrangement.
- **Upbeat Targets**: When setting up an [[Upbeat_Ensemble_Figures|Upbeat Figure]] (e.g., the "&" of 2), the target is often the downbeat of that same [[Beat|beat]] ([[Beat|Beat]] 2).
