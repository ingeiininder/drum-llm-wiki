# Swiss Army Triplet Variations

The **Swiss Army Triplet** is a foundational [[Flams]] rudiment that is exceptionally useful for drum set application due to its "offset" sticking, which allows for seamless mobility between different drums and cymbals.

### Basic Structure and Inversions
The standard Swiss Army Triplet (Right-Handed: `^R R L`) can be displaced to create new melodic shapes:
*   **First Inversion**: Displacing the sticking by one eighth-note triplet.
*   **Second Inversion**: Further displacement to shift the flam to the final partial of the group.

### 16th Note Conversion
A powerful modern application is converting the Swiss Army Triplet into a **16th-note pattern**. 
*   **Asymmetrical Grouping**: Because the rudiment is based on a grouping of three, playing it as [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] creates a polyrhythmic effect that "crosses the [[Bar_Line|bar line]]."
*   **Genre Application**: This conversion is a staple in [[Rock]] and [[Funk]] drumming, providing a syncopated, driving feel to fills.

### Technical Advantage
The design of the Swiss Army Triplet (RR L or LL R) means the hands are never "tangled." One hand stays relatively stationary while the other is free to move to a different surface, making it ideal for high-speed [[Linear_Drumming]] around the kit.
