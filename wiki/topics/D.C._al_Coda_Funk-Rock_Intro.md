# D.C. al Coda ([[Funk|Funk]]-[[Rock|Rock]] Intro)
The instruction "[[D.S._al_Coda]]" (Da Capo al Coda) is a common musical directive meaning "go back to the beginning (Da Capo) and play until you reach the Coda sign, then jump to the Coda section." In the context of a [[Funk_Rock]] [[Chart|chart]]'s introduction, this instruction has specific implications for the drummer.

**Specific Application in [[Funk|Funk]]-[[Rock|Rock]] Intro:**
*   **Initial Play-Through:** During the first pass, the [[Chart|chart]] might specify "1st Time: Guitar Only" for the introduction.
*   **Upon D.C. al Coda:** When the [[D.S._al_Coda]] instruction is encountered later in the [[Chart|chart]], the band returns to the beginning. Crucially, the text "Full Band in on D.C." indicates that the drums (and bass) *will* play through the introduction section the second time, unlike the first pass.
*   **Notated [[Groove|Groove]]:** The drum [[Groove|groove]] for this second pass through the intro is typically notated (e.g., Ex 8.1), confirming the full band's participation.

**Pro-Tip for Drummers:** Always read the entire [[Chart|chart]] carefully, paying close attention to all textual instructions and [[Repeat_Signs|repeat signs]]. The [[D.S._al_Coda]] can significantly alter the form and your part. Mentally rehearse the navigation, especially when an intro section that was initially solo becomes a full band section on the repeat. This requires precise [[Chart_Reading]] and awareness of the overall [[Form]].
