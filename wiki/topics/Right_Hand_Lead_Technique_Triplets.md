# Right-Hand Lead Technique ([[Triplets|Triplets]])

The **Right-Hand Lead (RHL) Triplet Technique** is a method for interpreting triplet-based rhythms (or swung [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]]) where the right hand plays the primary figures and the left hand fills the internal subdivisions.

### Execution
- **The Grid**: The underlying [[Pulse|pulse]] is an eighth-note triplet (1-trip-let, 2-trip-let).
- **The Lead**: The right hand plays the written figures on the [[Ride_Cymbal]] with [[Bass_Drum]] support.
- **The [[Fill|Fill]]**: The left hand fills in the "missing" parts of the triplet on the [[Snare_Drum]] as [[Ghost_Notes]].

### Swung Eighth Note Interpretation
In many musical contexts, eighth-note exercises are read with a **Swung Feel**. 
- Any note written on the "&" is interpreted as the "let" (the third part) of the triplet.
- This technique is foundational for [[Jazz_Drumming]] and [[Blues_Shuffle]] patterns.

### Practice Progression
1. Play the pattern on a single surface (Snare Drum) to master the sticking.
2. Move the Right Hand to the Ride Cymbal and add the Bass Drum.
3. Incorporate the Left Foot [[Hi-Hat]] on [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] to stabilize the "swing" feel.
