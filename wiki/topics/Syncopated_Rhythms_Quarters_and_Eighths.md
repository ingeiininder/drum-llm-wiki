# Syncopated Rhythms: Quarters and Eighths
When creating [[Syncopated_Rhythms]] with [[Quarter_Note]] and [[Eighth_Note]], the emphasis shifts from the strong [[Downbeat]] to the weaker beats or [[Upbeat]] (the "&s"). This often involves notes starting on an upbeat and sustaining across a downbeat.

**Key Applications:**
*   **[[Quarter_Notes|Quarter Notes]] on Upbeats**: A quarter note can be placed on the "&" of a [[Beat|beat]], sustaining for its full [[Duration|duration]] and crossing the subsequent downbeat. For example, an eighth note on [[Beat|beat]] 1 followed by a quarter note on the "&" of 1, which sustains over [[Beat|beat]] 2.
*   **Clarity with Ties**: To ensure readability and adhere to rules like the [[2_3_Rule]], [[Ties]] are often preferred. A quarter note on the "&" of 1, for instance, can be visually split into two [[Tied_Eighth_Notes]] (one on "&" of 1, tied to one on [[Beat|beat]] 2) to clearly show the downbeat of [[Beat|beat]] 2.
*   **Avoiding Obscured Downbeats**: It is generally considered incorrect to place a quarter note on the "&" of 2, as this would obscure the downbeat of [[Beat|beat]] 3. In such cases, using tied [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] (eighth on "&" of 2 tied to eighth on [[Beat|beat]] 3) makes the notation clear.

**Pro-Tip:** When interpreting syncopated rhythms, always look for the underlying [[Pulse|pulse]] and [[Subdivision|subdivision]]. Mentally "[[Fill|fill]] in" the missing downbeats or upbeats to understand where the notes *would* fall if they weren't syncopated. This helps in accurately placing the actual attacks and feeling the rhythmic tension.
