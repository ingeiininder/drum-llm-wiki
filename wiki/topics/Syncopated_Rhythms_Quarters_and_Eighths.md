# Syncopated Rhythms: Quarters and Eighths
When creating [[Syncopated Rhythms]] with [[Quarter Notes]] and [[Eighth Notes]], the emphasis shifts from the strong [[Downbeats]] to the weaker beats or [[Upbeats]] (the "&s"). This often involves notes starting on an upbeat and sustaining across a downbeat.

**Key Applications:**
*   **Quarter Notes on Upbeats**: A quarter note can be placed on the "&" of a beat, sustaining for its full duration and crossing the subsequent downbeat. For example, an eighth note on beat 1 followed by a quarter note on the "&" of 1, which sustains over beat 2.
*   **Clarity with Ties**: To ensure readability and adhere to rules like the [[2/3 Rule]], [[Ties]] are often preferred. A quarter note on the "&" of 1, for instance, can be visually split into two [[Tied Eighth Notes]] (one on "&" of 1, tied to one on beat 2) to clearly show the downbeat of beat 2.
*   **Avoiding Obscured Downbeats**: It is generally considered incorrect to place a quarter note on the "&" of 2, as this would obscure the downbeat of beat 3. In such cases, using tied eighth notes (eighth on "&" of 2 tied to eighth on beat 3) makes the notation clear.

**Pro-Tip:** When interpreting syncopated rhythms, always look for the underlying pulse and subdivision. Mentally "fill in" the missing downbeats or upbeats to understand where the notes *would* fall if they weren't syncopated. This helps in accurately placing the actual attacks and feeling the rhythmic tension.
