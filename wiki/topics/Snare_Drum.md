# Snare Drum

The Snare Drum is the primary rhythmic anchor of the drum kit. Known for its crisp, cutting sound—produced by the tension of metal snares against the bottom head—it defines the "backbeat" in modern music and the "conversation" in jazz.

## [[Articulations|Articulations]] and Strike Techniques

Developing a wide palette of sounds on the snare drum is essential for professional playing:

- **[[Rimshot|Rimshot]]**: Striking the center of the drumhead and the metal rim simultaneously. This produces a loud, powerful, and sharp accent used to drive Choruses or set up [[Ensemble_Figures|ensemble figures]].
- **Center Stroke (Non-[[Rimshot|rimshot]])**: Hitting only the center of the drumhead with the butt-end (or tip) of the stick. This results in a warmer, deeper, and more "open" sound.
- **Cross-Stick**: Placing the tip of the stick on the drumhead and striking the rim with the shoulder of the stick. This creates a distinct "wood click" or percussion-like sound common in Ballads and [[Bossa_Nova|Bossa Nova]].
- **[[Stick_Shot|Stick Shot]]**: Pressing the bead of one stick into the head and striking it with the other stick to produce a high-pitched, wood-tone accent (frequent in jazz).

## Role Across Genres

- **Rock and Pop**: The snare drum provides the unwavering **Backbeat** on beats 2 and 4. In aggressive styles, rimshots are used to cut through loud guitars.
- **Jazz**: Used for **Comping**—accompanying the soloist with subtle, reactive rhythmic phrases. It is generally played at a lower dynamic than the [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]].
- **Country-Pop**: Often utilizes repeating 16th-note snare patterns (the "Verse" [[Groove|groove]]) or syncopated backbeats (e.g., on the "a" of [[Beat|beat]] 2).
- **Soul-[[Funk|Funk]]**: Relies on light rimshots and **Ghost Notes** (very soft notes played between the main beats) to create complex, syncopated textures.

## Professional Indicators

- **Consistency**: A drummer's skill is often judged by the consistency of their snare drum sound—ensuring every backbeat has the same character and volume.
- **Marking the Form**: Changing snare [[Articulations|articulations]] (e.g., switching from cross-stick in the Verse to rimshots in the Chorus) is a key way to signal a change in song sections.
- **Set-Up Fills**: The snare is the primary surface for executing transition fills that "set up" upcoming [[Ensemble_Figures|ensemble figures]] or section changes.
