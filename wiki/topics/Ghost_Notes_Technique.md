# Ghost Notes
Ghost notes (also known as "notes in parentheses" or "grace notes") are low-volume notes played on the [[Snare Drum]] that provide rhythmic texture and "fill" the space between primary [[Back-Beat|backbeats]].

### Execution and Mechanics
To properly execute ghost notes alongside loud backbeats (on beats 2 and 4), the drummer must maintain a high degree of relaxation in the left hand (for right-handed players). 

*   **Dynamics**: Ghost notes should be played at a significantly lower dynamic level than the primary notes—often just an inch or two off the drumhead.
*   **The "Moeller" Connection**: Utilizing a controlled [[Moeller Technique]] or a "tap" stroke following a "down" stroke is essential for playing these notes efficiently at high speeds.
*   **Independence**: A common pitfall is allowing the hi-hat volume to fluctuate in sympathy with the snare ghost notes. The goal is a "steady-state" hi-hat against a "multi-level" snare dynamic.

### Professional Context
In [[RB_Funk_Drumming|R&B Funk]], ghost notes are not merely decorative; they define the "flow" of the groove. They should be felt more than heard by the audience, providing a subdivision "motor" for the band.
