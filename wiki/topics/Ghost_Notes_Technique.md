# [[Ghost_Notes|Ghost Notes]]
[[Ghost_Notes|Ghost notes]] (also known as "notes in parentheses" or "grace notes") are low-volume notes played on the [[Snare_Drum]] that provide rhythmic texture and "[[Fill|fill]]" the space between primary [[Back-Beat|backbeats]].

### Execution and Mechanics
To properly execute [[Ghost_Notes|ghost notes]] alongside loud backbeats (on beats 2 and 4), the drummer must maintain a high degree of relaxation in the left hand (for right-handed players). 

*   **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: [[Ghost_Notes|Ghost notes]] should be played at a significantly lower dynamic level than the primary notes—often just an inch or two off the drumhead.
*   **The "Moeller" Connection**: Utilizing a controlled [[Moeller_Technique]] or a "tap" stroke following a "down" stroke is essential for playing these notes efficiently at high speeds.
*   **Independence**: A common pitfall is allowing the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] volume to fluctuate in sympathy with the snare [[Ghost_Notes|ghost notes]]. The goal is a "steady-state" [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] against a "multi-level" snare dynamic.

### Professional Context
In [[RB_Funk_Drumming|R&B Funk]], [[Ghost_Notes|ghost notes]] are not merely decorative; they define the "flow" of the [[Groove|groove]]. They should be felt more than heard by the audience, providing a [[Subdivision|subdivision]] "motor" for the band.
