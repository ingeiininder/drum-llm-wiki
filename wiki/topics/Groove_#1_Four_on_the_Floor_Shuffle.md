# [[Groove|Groove]] #1 Four on the Floor (Shuffle)
This [[Groove|groove]], designated as "[[Groove|Groove]] #1," is a "[[Four_on_the_Floor]]" variation adapted for a [[Shuffle]] context. In a traditional [[Four_on_the_Floor]] pattern, the [[Bass_Drum]] plays on every quarter note. When applied to a [[Shuffle]] or [[Swing]] feel, this means the bass drum maintains a steady [[Pulse|pulse]], often with a slight emphasis on the "one" and "three" while the [[Hi-Hat]] and [[Snare_Drum]] articulate the [[Shuffle]] rhythm.

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Bass Drum:** Plays on all four beats of the [[Measure|measure]], providing a strong, driving foundation.
*   **[[Shuffle_Feel|Shuffle Feel]]:** The overall feel is a [[Swing]] eighth-note or triplet-based [[Shuffle]], which means the straight quarter-note bass drum interacts with a swung [[Hi-Hat]] and [[Snare_Drum]] pattern.

**Pro-Tip:** The challenge with a [[Four_on_the_Floor]] [[Shuffle]] is to maintain the [[Shuffle]] feel in the [[Hi-Hat]] and [[Snare_Drum]] without making the [[Bass_Drum]] feel too stiff or straight. Think of the bass drum as a solid anchor, allowing the top half of the kit to "dance" with the [[Swing]] rhythm. Ensure even [[Dynamics|dynamics]] and consistent timing, especially when integrating this into a [[Shuffle_12-Bar_Blues_Play-Along_Unit_7_Ex_7.11]].
