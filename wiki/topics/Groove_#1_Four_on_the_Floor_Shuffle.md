# Groove #1 Four on the Floor (Shuffle)
This groove, designated as "Groove #1," is a "[[Four on the Floor]]" variation adapted for a [[Shuffle]] context. In a traditional [[Four on the Floor]] pattern, the [[Bass Drum]] plays on every quarter note. When applied to a [[Shuffle]] or [[Swing]] feel, this means the bass drum maintains a steady pulse, often with a slight emphasis on the "one" and "three" while the [[Hi-Hat]] and [[Snare Drum]] articulate the [[Shuffle]] rhythm.

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Bass Drum:** Plays on all four beats of the measure, providing a strong, driving foundation.
*   **Shuffle Feel:** The overall feel is a [[Swing]] eighth-note or triplet-based [[Shuffle]], which means the straight quarter-note bass drum interacts with a swung [[Hi-Hat]] and [[Snare Drum]] pattern.

**Pro-Tip:** The challenge with a [[Four on the Floor]] [[Shuffle]] is to maintain the [[Shuffle]] feel in the [[Hi-Hat]] and [[Snare Drum]] without making the [[Bass Drum]] feel too stiff or straight. Think of the bass drum as a solid anchor, allowing the top half of the kit to "dance" with the [[Swing]] rhythm. Ensure even dynamics and consistent timing, especially when integrating this into a [[Shuffle_12-Bar_Blues_Play-Along_Unit_7_Ex_7.11]].
