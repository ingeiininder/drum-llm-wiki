# Stylistically Correct Notation

Notation is not just a mathematical representation of sound; it is a set of conventions that dictate the "feel" and "soul" of a [[Groove]]. Using incorrect notation—even if mathematically accurate—can lead to stiff or unmusical performances.

### [[Jazz|Jazz]] Conventions
*   **Standard**: [[Jazz_Swing]] is written in 4/4 with the [[Hi-Hat]] on beats 2 and 4.
*   **Incorrect**: Writing [[Jazz|Jazz]] in a way that places the hi-hat on every "upbeat" (e.g., in a slow 2/4) makes the ride pattern look overly dense and complex, often causing the drummer to play too "vertically" rather than with a "horizontal" flow.

### Latin and Up-[[Tempo|Tempo]] Styles
*   **Standard**: Up-[[Tempo|tempo]] Latin charts (like [[Mambo]]) are preferred in [[Cut_Time]].
*   **Reasoning**: Most Latin grooves are two-bar patterns felt in "2." Cut Time allows for the use of larger note values (quarters and eighths), which are easier to read at high speeds than a sea of [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]] in 4/4.
*   **Impact**: Correct notation allows the performer to see the "big picture" of the two-bar phrase rather than getting bogged down in individual beats.

> [!TIP] **Pro-Tip**
> When writing your own charts, always choose the [[Time_Signature|time signature]] that results in the fewest "beams" and "flags." The cleaner the page, the more relaxed the performance.
