# Setting Up the "e" of a [[Beat|Beat]]

In [[Ensemble_Figures]] reading, notes landing on the "e" of a [[Beat|beat]] (the second 16th note) require a clear **[[Target_Beat|target beat]]** to ensure the entire ensemble executes the rhythm with precision. 

### The [[Target_Beat|Target Beat]] Principle
When an ensemble figure occurs on the "e," the [[Target_Beat|target beat]] is the **downbeat** immediately preceding it. A clearly played downbeat provides the necessary anchor for the ensemble. Without this anchor, performers have a natural tendency to rush the "e," as they are often reading ahead and anticipating the [[Syncopation|syncopation]].

### Execution Strategies
1. **The Downbeat Anchor**: Playing a strong note on the downbeat (1, 2, 3, or 4) occupies the proper amount of space, forcing the ensemble to wait the correct [[Duration|duration]] before striking the "e."
2. **Subdivision Fills**: Utilizing a 16th-note-based [[Set-up_Fill]] helps the ensemble internalize the [[Subdivision]], assisting in perfect [[Beat_Placement|beat placement]].

### [[Target_Beats|Target Beats]] by Position
*   **[[Beat|Beat]] 1**: Target is the downbeat of 1. Common set-up: A [[Fill|fill]] on the "&" of [[Beat|beat]] 4.
*   **[[Beat|Beat]] 2**: Target is the downbeat of 2. In a standard [[Groove|groove]], the [[Back-Beat]] on the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] often handles this [[Target_Beat|target beat]] naturally.
*   **[[Beat|Beat]] 3**: Target is the downbeat of 3. Common set-up: A [[Fill|fill]] starting on [[Beat|beat]] 2.
*   **[[Beat|Beat]] 4**: Target is the downbeat of 4. Like [[Beat|beat]] 2, this is typically handled by the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] backbeat.

**Pro-Tip**: When expanding a [[Fill|fill]] (e.g., a two-[[Beat|beat]] set-up), ensure the final note of the [[Fill|fill]] lands firmly on the target downbeat to "launch" the ensemble figure.
