# Setting Up the "e" of a Beat

In [[Ensemble Figures]] reading, notes landing on the "e" of a beat (the second 16th note) require a clear **target beat** to ensure the entire ensemble executes the rhythm with precision. 

### The Target Beat Principle
When an ensemble figure occurs on the "e," the target beat is the **downbeat** immediately preceding it. A clearly played downbeat provides the necessary anchor for the ensemble. Without this anchor, performers have a natural tendency to rush the "e," as they are often reading ahead and anticipating the syncopation.

### Execution Strategies
1. **The Downbeat Anchor**: Playing a strong note on the downbeat (1, 2, 3, or 4) occupies the proper amount of space, forcing the ensemble to wait the correct duration before striking the "e."
2. **Subdivision Fills**: Utilizing a 16th-note-based [[Set-up Fill]] helps the ensemble internalize the [[Subdivision]], assisting in perfect beat placement.

### Target Beats by Position
*   **Beat 1**: Target is the downbeat of 1. Common set-up: A fill on the "&" of beat 4.
*   **Beat 2**: Target is the downbeat of 2. In a standard groove, the [[Back-Beat]] on the snare drum often handles this target beat naturally.
*   **Beat 3**: Target is the downbeat of 3. Common set-up: A fill starting on beat 2.
*   **Beat 4**: Target is the downbeat of 4. Like beat 2, this is typically handled by the snare drum backbeat.

**Pro-Tip**: When expanding a fill (e.g., a two-beat set-up), ensure the final note of the fill lands firmly on the target downbeat to "launch" the ensemble figure.
