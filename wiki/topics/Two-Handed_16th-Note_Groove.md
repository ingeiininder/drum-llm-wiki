# Two-Handed 16th-Note [[Groove|Groove]]
A `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]` is a fundamental drumming technique where `[[16th_Notes]]` are played continuously, typically on the `[[Hi-Hat]]` or `[[Ride_Cymbal]]`, but with both hands contributing to the pattern. This creates a dense, driving, and often `[[Syncopated_Beat]]` that is characteristic of `[[Rock_Music_Styles]]`, `[[Metal]]`, and `[[Alternative_Rock]]`.

**Common Characteristics:**
*   **Alternating Hands**: One hand (often the dominant hand) plays the primary `[[Hi-Hat]]` pattern, while the other hand (often the non-dominant hand) fills in the remaining 16th notes, sometimes on the hi-hat, snare, or a combination.
*   **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]] Patterns**: These grooves are frequently combined with intricate `[[Bass_Drum_Patterns]]`, such as a `[[Four-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` (e.g., `[[Ex_4.1]]`) or a `[[Two-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` (e.g., `[[Ex_4.2]]`), to create rhythmic complexity and drive.
*   **Hi-Hat Openings**: The inclusion of `[[Hi-Hat_Opening]]` adds texture and [[Accents|accents]], enhancing the `[[Rhythmic_Textures]]` of the [[Groove|groove]].

**Pro-Tip**: Focus on achieving an even sound and consistent [[Dynamics|dynamics]] between both hands. Practice slowly with a `[[Metronome]]` to ensure precision before building speed. This technique is crucial for developing endurance and fluidity in modern drumming.
