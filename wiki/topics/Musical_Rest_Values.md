# Musical Rest Values
Musical rest values represent periods of silence within a piece of music. While silent, [[Rests|rests]] are just as important as [[Musical_Note_Values]] because they define the rhythmic space and [[Phrasing|phrasing]]. Each rest has a specific [[Duration|duration]] equal to its note counterpart.

It is critical to remember that [[Rests|rests]] still "consume" or "occupy" space in time, meaning their [[Duration|duration]] must be precisely observed.

The common rest values include:
*   [[Whole_Rest]] (4 beats in 4/4 time)
*   [[Half_Rest]] (2 beats in 4/4 time)
*   [[Quarter_Rest]] (1 [[Beat|beat]] in 4/4 time)
*   [[Eighth_Rest]] (1/2 [[Beat|beat]] in 4/4 time)
*   [[16th_Rest]] (1/4 [[Beat|beat]] in 4/4 time)

Understanding and accurately observing rest values is essential for rhythmic precision and creating musical "breathing room" in your drumming.

**Pro-Tip**: When practicing, count [[Rests|rests]] silently or verbally to ensure their full [[Duration|duration]] is observed. Don't rush into the next note; let the silence speak. This is especially important when dealing with [[Economizing_Notation]] where [[Rests|rests]] might be combined.
