# Asymmetrical Groupings

**Asymmetrical Groupings** are the building blocks of **[[Odd Meter]]**. They define the internal pulse and "feel" of a measure by organizing beats into small clusters of 2 and 3.

### Common Grouping Units
*   **"2" Group**: A duple pulse (e.g., two eighth notes). Felt as a "short" beat.
*   **"3" Group**: A triple pulse (e.g., three eighth notes). Felt as a "long" beat.

### Application in 5/8
*   **2 + 3**: Short-Long.
*   **3 + 2**: Long-Short.

### Application in 7/8
*   **2 + 2 + 3**: Short-Short-Long.
*   **3 + 2 + 2**: Long-Short-Short.
*   **2 + 3 + 2**: Short-Long-Short.

### Why Groupings Matter
1.  **Ensemble Unity**: The entire band must agree on the grouping to play tight rhythms.
2.  **Groove Construction**: The bass drum often mirrors the start of each grouping, while the snare might provide a backbeat on one of the secondary pulses.
3.  **Soloing**: Understanding these groupings allows a drummer to phrase across the bar line while staying grounded in the meter.
