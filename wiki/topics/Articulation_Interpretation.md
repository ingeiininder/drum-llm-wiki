# Articulation Interpretation
In drum set performance, written [[Ensemble Figures]] often include articulation markings that dictate the duration, weight, and orchestration of the notes. While a standard chart shows the rhythmic figure, the drummer must interpret these markings to match the musical context.

### Core Articulations
1.  **[[Staccato Interpretation|Staccato]]**: Indicated by a dot above or below the note. These are played as short, detached notes.
2.  **[[Tenuto Interpretation|Tenuto]]**: Indicated by a horizontal line. These represent "long" notes, requiring sounds with more sustain.
3.  **[[Martellato Interpretation|Martellato]]**: Indicated by a wedge or "rooftop" accent. These are "short and fat" notes, played with significant weight and immediate decay.

### Mixed Articulations
Most professional charts utilize a mixture of these markings. The drummer's role is to create a cohesive "interpretation" that balances the [[Set-up Fills]] with the specific length of the notated figure.

**Pro-Tip**: When you see mixed articulations, use the orchestration to differentiate them. Play short notes on the snare or hi-hat and long notes on crash cymbals with bass drum reinforcement.
