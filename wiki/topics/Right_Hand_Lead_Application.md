# Right-Hand Lead Application

**Right-Hand Lead** is a sticking system where the right hand remains the primary driver of the rhythm, particularly useful for maintaining consistency in [[Drum Set Orchestration]] and [[Fill]] execution. In this system, specific sticking patterns are assigned to rhythmic groupings to ensure the right hand always lands on the primary beats or the start of new phrases.

### Sticking Logic
To maintain a consistent right-hand lead, the following assignments are used:
- **Eighth Notes**: Played as **R-L**.
- **16th Notes**: Played as **R-L-R-L**.
- **Triplets**: Played as **R-L-L**. 

### The Triplet Exception
In standard [[Hand-to-Hand Sticking]], odd-numbered groupings like triplets cause the lead hand to flip (R-L-R, then L-R-L). To maintain a permanent right-hand lead, the second note of the triplet is doubled with the left hand (**R-L-L**). This ensures that the next rhythmic grouping or beat always begins with the right hand.

### Pro-Tips
- **Consistency**: Using right-hand lead helps in developing a "strong side" for navigating the kit, especially when moving from snare to toms.
- **Pitfall**: Be careful not to let the doubled left hand in the triplet (**R-L-L**) become "mushy." Both left-hand notes must be rhythmically distinct and match the volume of the right hand.
