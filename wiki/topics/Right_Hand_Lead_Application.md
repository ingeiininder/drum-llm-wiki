# Right-Hand Lead Application

**Right-Hand Lead** is a sticking system where the right hand remains the primary driver of the rhythm, particularly useful for maintaining consistency in [[Drum_Set_Orchestration]] and [[Fill]] execution. In this system, specific [[Sticking_Patterns|sticking patterns]] are assigned to rhythmic groupings to ensure the right hand always lands on the primary beats or the start of new phrases.

### Sticking Logic
To maintain a consistent right-hand lead, the following assignments are used:
- **[[Eighth_Notes|Eighth Notes]]**: Played as **R-L**.
- **[[16th_Notes|16th Notes]]**: Played as **R-L-R-L**.
- **[[Triplets|Triplets]]**: Played as **R-L-L**. 

### The Triplet Exception
In standard [[Hand-to-Hand_Sticking]], odd-numbered groupings like [[Triplets|triplets]] cause the lead hand to flip (R-L-R, then L-R-L). To maintain a permanent right-hand lead, the second note of the triplet is doubled with the left hand (**R-L-L**). This ensures that the next rhythmic grouping or [[Beat|beat]] always begins with the right hand.

### Pro-Tips
- **Consistency**: Using right-hand lead helps in developing a "strong side" for navigating the kit, especially when moving from snare to toms.
- **Pitfall**: Be careful not to let the doubled left hand in the triplet (**R-L-L**) become "mushy." Both left-hand notes must be rhythmically distinct and match the volume of the right hand.
