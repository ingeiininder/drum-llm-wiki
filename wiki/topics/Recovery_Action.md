# Recovery Action

**Recovery Action** refers to the technical and mental process of returning to a stable [[Groove]] immediately after executing a syncopated [[Ensemble Figures|Ensemble Figure]]. 

### Challenges with 16th-Note Partials
When playing a figure on the **"e"** or **"ah"** of a beat, the drummer's physical momentum is shifted away from the downbeat. This often results in a "stumble" or a late arrival at the next beat.

### Technical Solutions
- **The "Wait"**: When playing on the "e" of beat 1, you must focus on landing precisely on beat 2. 
- **Hi-Hat Omission**: In [[One-Handed 16th-Note Hi-Hat Technique]], it is often necessary to omit the hi-hat note immediately following a crash/accent to allow the arm time to return to the hi-hat.
- **Snare Isolation**: Often, the [[Snare Drum]] backbeat (on 2 or 4) is played alone immediately following a figure to ensure the "pocket" is re-established before the full hi-hat pattern resumes.

### Common Pitfalls
- **Rushing the Downbeat**: The syncopation of the "ah" often creates the illusion of a rushed downbeat (see [[The Push]]).
- **Losing the Subdivision**: Stopping the internal 16th-note count during the "hit," leading to an uneven return to the time feel.
