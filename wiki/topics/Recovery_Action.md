# Recovery Action

**Recovery Action** refers to the technical and mental process of returning to a stable [[Groove]] immediately after executing a syncopated [[Ensemble_Figures|Ensemble Figure]]. 

### Challenges with 16th-Note Partials
When playing a figure on the **"e"** or **"ah"** of a [[Beat|beat]], the drummer's physical momentum is shifted away from the downbeat. This often results in a "stumble" or a late arrival at the next [[Beat|beat]].

### Technical Solutions
- **The "Wait"**: When playing on the "e" of [[Beat|beat]] 1, you must focus on landing precisely on [[Beat|beat]] 2. 
- **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] Omission**: In [[One-Handed_16th-Note_Hi-Hat_Technique]], it is often necessary to omit the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] note immediately following a crash/accent to allow the arm time to return to the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]].
- **Snare Isolation**: Often, the [[Snare_Drum]] backbeat (on 2 or 4) is played alone immediately following a figure to ensure the "pocket" is re-established before the full [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] pattern resumes.

### Common Pitfalls
- **Rushing the Downbeat**: The [[Syncopation|syncopation]] of the "ah" often creates the illusion of a rushed downbeat (see [[The_Push]]).
- **Losing the [[Subdivision|Subdivision]]**: Stopping the internal 16th-note count during the "hit," leading to an uneven return to the [[Time_Feel|time feel]].
