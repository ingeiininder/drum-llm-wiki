# Five-Stroke Ruff
The [[Five-Stroke_Ruff]] is a drum rudiment belonging to the "Drag" or "Ruff" family. It consists of four grace notes (drags) preceding a primary stroke. It is mentioned as a rudiment in Unit 4 of "[[Drum_Technique_1_(MI_Curriculum_Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
rrLLR (or LLrrL for Left Hand Lead)

**Breakdown:**
*   **rrLL:** Four quiet, quick grace notes (drags) played as two [[Double_Strokes]]s (rr then LL).
*   **R:** A louder, primary stroke played with the right hand.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Embellishment:** Similar to other ruffs, it embellishes a single stroke, creating a fuller, more sustained "drag" sound.
*   **Dynamic Contrast:** The grace notes are soft, while the primary note is accented, providing dynamic shape.
*   **Roll Development:** Helps develop control over multiple bounces and transitions into single strokes, foundational for [[Drum_Roll]]s.

**Pro-Tip:** The key to a good [[Five-Stroke_Ruff]] is to make the four grace notes very even, quiet, and tightly spaced, creating a smooth "buzz" or "drag" that leads directly into the accented final stroke. Practice slowly to ensure the grace notes are consistent and then gradually increase [[Tempo|tempo]], maintaining the dynamic contrast.
