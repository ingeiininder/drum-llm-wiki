# Sixteenth [[Rests|Rests]] in 6/8

[[Sixteenth_Rest]] in [[6/8_Time]] carry the same rhythmic value as [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]] but require specific notation standards to maintain readability.

### Notation and Beaming
To assist the performer in "visualizing" the [[Meter|meter]], sixteenth [[Rests|rests]] are typically housed under **double beams**. 
- Rhythms are grouped into two clusters of six sixteenth-note units.
- This grouping highlights the primary [[Dotted_Quarter_Note]] pulses ([[Beat|Beat]] 1 and [[Beat|Beat]] 4).

### Reading Strategy
When encountering complex phrases involving sixteenth [[Rests|rests]] (as seen in **Ex 4.6**):
1. **Identify the "Strong" Beats**: Look for the start of the first and second beam groupings.
2. **Ghosting**: Lightly tap the sixteenth-note [[Subdivision|subdivision]] on your leg or [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] to "[[Fill|fill]] in" the [[Rests|rests]] physically.
3. **Vocalization**: Use the "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 &" counting method, whispering the counts that land on [[Rests|rests]].

### Professional Context
In session work, 6/8 charts with heavy sixteenth-note rest usage often imply a "[[Staccato|staccato]]" or "tight" feel. Precision in exactly when the note *stops* is just as important as when it starts.
