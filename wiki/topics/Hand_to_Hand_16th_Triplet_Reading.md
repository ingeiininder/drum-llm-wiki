# Hand-to-Hand 16th Triplet Reading

This technique involves playing a continuous stream of [[16th-Note Triplets]] (sextuplets) while accenting specific notes to match a written [[Ensemble Figure]]. This approach is essential for developing "stagnant" figures into musical phrases with motion.

### Technical Execution
*   **Sticking**: Use a strict hand-to-hand (R L R L...) pattern.
*   **Dynamics**: This concept relies heavily on the contrast between [[Ghost Notes]] and accents. Unaccented notes should be played very quietly (1-2 inches from the head), while accented notes should be played at a full volume.
*   **Interpretation**: 16th-note ensemble figures are often interpreted as **swung 16th notes** when applied to a triplet grid.

### Application
In [[Exercise 10.1]], the drummer maintains the triplet flow. If the ensemble figure lands on beat 1, the "e" of 3, or beat 4, the Right Hand (RH) typically handles the accent. If it lands on the "and" of 2, the Left Hand (LH) takes the accent.

### Pro-Tip
To master this, first practice the continuous triplets without accents to ensure the "inner pulse" is even. Then, slowly introduce the accents from the reading text. This develops the ability to "see" rhythms within a subdivision grid.
