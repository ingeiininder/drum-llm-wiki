# Hand-to-Hand 16th Triplet Reading

This technique involves playing a continuous stream of [[16th-Note_Triplets]] (sextuplets) while accenting specific notes to match a written [[Ensemble_Figures]]. This approach is essential for developing "stagnant" figures into musical phrases with motion.

### Technical Execution
*   **Sticking**: Use a strict hand-to-hand (R L R L...) pattern.
*   **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: This concept relies heavily on the contrast between [[Ghost_Notes]] and [[Accents|accents]]. Unaccented notes should be played very quietly (1-2 inches from the head), while accented notes should be played at a full volume.
*   **Interpretation**: 16th-note ensemble figures are often interpreted as **[[Swung_16th_Notes|swung 16th notes]]** when applied to a triplet grid.

### Application
In [[Exercise_10.1]], the drummer maintains the triplet flow. If the ensemble figure lands on [[Beat|beat]] 1, the "e" of 3, or [[Beat|beat]] 4, the Right Hand (RH) typically handles the accent. If it lands on the "and" of 2, the Left Hand (LH) takes the accent.

### Pro-Tip
To master this, first practice the continuous [[Triplets|triplets]] without [[Accents|accents]] to ensure the "inner [[Pulse|pulse]]" is even. Then, slowly introduce the [[Accents|accents]] from the reading text. This develops the ability to "see" rhythms within a [[Subdivision|subdivision]] grid.
