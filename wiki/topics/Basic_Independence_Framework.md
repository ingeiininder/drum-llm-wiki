# Basic Independence Framework

Coordinated independence is the ability to maintain a steady [[Ostinato]] in one or more limbs while playing varying figures in others. This framework uses a standard [[Ride_Cymbal]] and [[Hi-Hat]] pattern as the foundation.

### The Foundation Pattern
- **Ride Cymbal**: [[Quarter_Notes|Quarter notes]] on beats 1 and 3; two [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] on beats 2 and 4.
- **Hi-Hat (Foot)**: Played on beats 2 and 4.
- **Stylistic Versatility**: When played with [[Straight_Eighth_Notes]], this applies to [[Rock|Rock]], [[Funk|Funk]], and Latin. When played with [[Swing_Eighth_Notes]], it serves as a foundation for [[Jazz|Jazz]] and Hip-Hop.

### The Four Rules of Pairing
To master basic independence, follow these alignment rules for the [[Snare_Drum]] and [[Bass_Drum]]:
1. **Downbeats**: Notes on the downbeat always pair with the [[Ride_Cymbal]].
2. **Backbeats (2 & 4)**: Notes on beats 2 and 4 pair with both the [[Ride_Cymbal]] and the [[Hi-Hat]] foot.
3. **Upbeats (1& and 3&)**: Notes on the "&" of 1 or 3 stand alone (no cymbal/hat pairing).
4. **Upbeats (2& and 4&)**: Notes on the "&" of 2 or 4 pair with the [[Ride_Cymbal]].

### Pro-Tips
- **Slow Practice**: There is no substitute for practicing slowly. Use a [[Metronome]] and ensure every "unison" hit (where limbs land together) is perfectly aligned with no [[Flamming]].
- **Mental Mapping**: Visualize the grid of [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] and identify exactly which limbs are active on every "e" and "ah" or "&".
