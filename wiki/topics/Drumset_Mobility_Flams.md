# Drumset Mobility: Flams
[[Flams]] rudiments are highly effective for enhancing specific notes within a groove or fill. On the drumset, flams can be "split" between different sound sources.

### Orchestration Rules
- **Split Flams**: When splitting a flam between a tom and the snare, the standard MI approach is to play the **Tom first**, then the **Snare**. This articulates the relationship of the flam most clearly.
- **Dynamics**: In these mobility exercises, the dynamic relationship between the grace note and the primary note is subjective. The focus should be on **rhythmic relationship** rather than volume.

### Key Exercises
- **Non-Alternating Flams (Ex 7.5)**: Moving the same-hand flam across the kit.
- **Flam Accents (Ex 7.7)**: Separating the flam from the subsequent inner notes across toms and snare.
- **[[Flamacue]] (Ex 7.8)**: Practicing the movement without the traditional accent to focus on the physical shift between drums.
- **Same-Hand Flam Accents**: Developing speed and control by keeping the flam lead on one hand while the other hand navigates the kit.
