# Inverted Four-Stroke Ruff
The [[Inverted_Four-Stroke_Ruff]] is a more challenging variation of the [[Four-Stroke_Ruff]]. In this version, the unaccented notes *follow* the one accented note, similar to the [[Inverted_Three-Stroke_Ruff]].

**Execution:**
The basic sticking pattern for an [[Inverted_Four-Stroke_Ruff]] is typically:
*   **R L L L** (first loud, next three soft)
*   **L R R R** (first loud, next three soft)

**Practice Variations:**
1.  **Slower Tempos:** These are more challenging, so begin practicing at slower tempos to ensure precision and control over the four strokes.
    *   *Example (Ex 4.7):* R L L L R L L L R L L L R L L L (Accent on the first note of each group, followed by three soft notes)
2.  **[[Accents|Accents]] on Beats 2 and 4:** Practice placing the accented notes on specific beats (e.g., beats 2 and 4) to develop rhythmic placement and internal clock.
    *   *Example (Ex 4.8):* (Accent on 2, then 4)
3.  **Individual Hand Development:** Just as with the standard version, practice the [[Inverted_Four-Stroke_Ruff]] with each individual hand for focused development.
    *   *Example (based on Ex 4.7):* R R R R R R R R (Accent on the first note of each group, followed by three soft notes, repeating with only the right hand)

**Pro-Tip:** The difficulty lies in executing a strong accent and then immediately controlling three soft, even notes that follow. This requires excellent rebound control and a very relaxed grip. Pay close attention to the consistency of the unaccented notes; they should not "die out" but maintain a clear, soft presence. Practice with a [[Metronome|metronome]] to ensure rhythmic accuracy.
