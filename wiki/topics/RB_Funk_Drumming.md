# R&B 16th-Note Funk
R&B Funk is a sub-style of R&B that rose to prominence between the mid-1960s and mid-1970s. It is characterized by highly [[Syncopation|syncopated]] bass drum patterns layered beneath intricate [[Hi-Hat]] and [[Snare Drum]] figures.

### Historical Context
The style was pioneered and popularized by artists and bands such as:
*   **James Brown** (The "Godfather of Soul")
*   **Tower of Power** (Known for David Garibaldi's linear style)
*   **Sly and the Family Stone**
*   **Cold Blood**

Modern artists like Justin Timberlake and Christina Aguilera continue to utilize these classic funk elements in contemporary pop production.

### Technical Characteristics
1.  **16th-Note Subdivisions**: The groove is felt in a straight 16th-note grid.
2.  **Ghost Notes**: Extensive use of [[Ghost Notes]] on the snare drum to create a "rolling" internal rhythm between backbeats.
3.  **Linear Influence**: Often features patterns where no two limbs hit at the same time, though traditional layered funk is the foundation here.
4.  **The "Ah" of 4**: A common [[Ensemble Figures|ensemble figure]] in this genre occurs on the last 16th note of the measure (the "ah" of beat 4), often leading back into the downbeat.

### Performance Pro-Tips
*   **Tempo Consistency**: When starting a tune where a guitar player begins alone, provide the tempo via [[Stick Clicks]] or hi-hat taps to ensure the band enters at the correct BPM (typically around 107 BPM for mid-tempo funk).
*   **Dynamic Balance**: Keep the hi-hat volume steady and uninfluenced by the complex snare drum activity. The [[Back-Beat]] on 2 and 4 should remain dominant over the ghost notes.
