# Eighth [[Rests|Rests]]
An [[Eighth_Rest]] is a fundamental symbol in musical notation that represents a period of silence equal to the [[Duration|duration]] of an [[Eighth_Note]]. In common time signatures like [[4-4_Time_Signature]], an eighth rest is equivalent to half of a [[Beat|beat]].

**Symbol and Placement:**
The symbol for an eighth rest resembles a small number seven (7) and is typically placed in the middle of the musical staff.

**Counting:**
Eighth [[Rests|rests]] are counted in the same way as [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]], using the "and" (&) count. For example, in a [[Measure|measure]] of 4/4, they would be counted as "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &", with the rest falling on either the number or the "and" depending on its placement.

**Common Usage:**
Eighth [[Rests|rests]] are frequently used to create rhythmic interest and [[Syncopation|syncopation]] by:
*   **Silencing Downbeats:** Placing an eighth rest on a downbeat (e.g., "1", "2") can create a feeling of anticipation or a "push" to the subsequent upbeat.
*   **Exposing Upbeats:** When a downbeat is silent, the following upbeat (e.g., "&" of 1) becomes more prominent, contributing to a groovier feel.

**Professional Insight:**
Mastering [[Rests|rests]] is as vital as mastering notes. Drummers often rush through [[Rests|rests]] or fail to observe their full [[Duration|duration]], which can significantly impact the feel and accuracy of a [[Groove|groove]]. Practice internalizing the silence of an eighth rest, ensuring it occupies its full half-[[Beat|beat]] value. Use a [[Metronome]] to develop precise timing for both played notes and observed [[Rests|rests]]. When reading [[Freestanding_Eighth_Notes]] and eighth [[Rests|rests]], pay close attention to each individual [[Beat|beat]] to avoid getting "lost" in consecutive [[Rests|rests]].
