# Indie-Pop Drumming
**Indie-Pop** is a melodic, angst-free counterpart to indie-[[Rock|rock]], characterized by tightly constructed grooves and a "warm, analog-esque" production style.

### Stylistic Characteristics
- **Efficiency**: No wasted space or extraneous notes. Every [[Fill|fill]] and ghost note must serve the melody.
- **Precision**: The grooves are often based on complex subdivisions like [[16th-Note_Triplets]] but must feel relaxed and "sweet."
- **Sonic Profile**: Warm drum tones, often utilizing vintage-style tuning or dampening to achieve a studio-ready sound.

### Key Artists and Influences
- **Steely Dan**: For precision and sophisticated [[Shuffle|shuffle]] feels.
- **Death Cab for Cutie**: Specifically the drumming of [[Jason_McGerr]].
- **Wilco** and **Elliot Smith**: For the "analog" aesthetic.

### Performance Strategy
When playing Indie-Pop charts, focus on the **Form and Figures**. Fills should be used to lead the band into new sections (e.g., a two-[[Beat|beat]] pickup into Letter A) rather than for technical display.
