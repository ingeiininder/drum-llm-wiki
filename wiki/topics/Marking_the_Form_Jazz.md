# Marking the Form ([[Jazz|Jazz]])
A drummer's primary duty beyond keeping time is to outline the structure of the song for the listener and the band. In [[Jazz|jazz]], this is often done through **sonic/textural changes** rather than just volume.

### Methods of Marking Form
*   **Cymbal Changes**: Switch from a dry [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]] during [[The_Head_(Jazz)]] to a washier, larger ride during the solo sections.
*   **Texture Changes**: Moving from [[Cross_Stick_Technique]] on the snare during a piano solo to a full snare sound for a saxophone solo.
*   **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: Using a "build-up" [[Fill|fill]] in the final two bars of a chorus to signal the start of a new soloist.
*   **Articulation**: Introducing [[Feathering_the_Bass_Drum]] only during specific choruses to increase the "drive" of the rhythm section.
