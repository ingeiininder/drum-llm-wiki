# Comping Horn Figures

**Comping** (short for accompanying or complementing) horn figures is the art of reinforcing the rhythmic punches of a brass section while maintaining the underlying [[Groove|groove]].

### Key Techniques
- **Unison [[Accents|Accents]]**: Striking the snare and/or cymbals at the exact moment the horns play a "shot."
- **Filling the Gaps**: Playing short rhythmic responses in the "holes" left by the horn section's [[Phrasing|phrasing]].
- **Dynamic Matching**: If the horns play a **[[Staccato|staccato]]** (short) note, the drummer should use a choked cymbal or a tight snare hit. If the horns play a **legato** (long) note, the drummer might use a crashing cymbal with more sustain.

### Professional Context
In tracks like [[Saturday_Night_in_Memphis]], comping is essential for "lifting" the band during solos and transitions. The drummer must be able to read a [[Drum_Chart]] that indicates "Horn Cues" and decide which figures to catch and which to play through.

> [!WARNING]
> **Common Pitfall**: Over-comping. If a drummer catches every single horn note, the [[Groove|groove]] can become disjointed. The goal is to support the *key* figures that define the phrase.
