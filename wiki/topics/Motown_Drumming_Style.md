# Motown Drumming Style
The drumming style associated with [[Motown]] (and often [[Stax]]) is characterized by specific [[Groove|groove]] constructions designed to drive a song's "hook."

### The "Four-on-the-Snare" [[Groove|Groove]]
A signature element of many Motown choruses is the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] playing on **every downbeat** (1, 2, 3, and 4). 
*   **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: Usually plays even [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]], often opening on the "&" of [[Beat|beat]] 4 every other bar to create a two-bar phrase.
*   **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: Typically employs a two-bar 16th-note pattern to provide [[Syncopation|syncopation]] against the steady snare.

### Sectional Contrasts
*   **Verses (A Sections)**: Often use a more traditional [[Back-Beat]] (snare on 2 and 4) or a [[Side-stick]] (cross-stick) to create space for the vocals.
*   **Bridges (C Sections)**: Frequently involve a transition from the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] to the [[Ride_Cymbal]] to match new chord changes, while maintaining the quarter-note snare feel to keep the energy high.
