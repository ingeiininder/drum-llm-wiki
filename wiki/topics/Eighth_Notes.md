# Eighth Notes

An **Eighth Note** (or quaver) is a musical note played for one eighth the [[Duration|duration]] of a [[Whole_Note]]. 

## Characteristics
- **Notation**: Drawn with an oval, filled-in [[Note_Head|note head]] and a straight note stem with one flag. Multiple consecutive eighth notes are typically connected with a single beam.
- **Value**: Two eighth notes equal one [[Quarter_Note]]. In a standard [[4-4_Time_Signature]], there are eight eighth notes per [[Measure]].

## Counting
Eighth notes are counted evenly within a [[Beat|beat]]: "1 & 2 & 3 & 4 &".

## Application
Eighth notes are the most common [[Subdivision|subdivision]] for [[Hi-Hat]] and [[Ride_Cymbal]] patterns in standard [[Rock|Rock]] and Pop [[Groove]]s.
