# Fill Markers
[[Fill Markers]] are a common notation convention used in drum charts to indicate the presence and duration of a [[Drum Fills]]. Just as [[Time Slashes]] are used to denote [[Drum Set Grooves]], they can also be used to represent beats within a fill.

Each individual time slash typically represents one beat of the fill. This provides a clear visual cue for the drummer to execute a fill of a specific length.

**Examples**:
*   [[1-Beat Fill]]: One time slash.
*   [[2-Beat Fill]]: Two time slashes.
*   [[3-Beat Fill]]: Three time slashes.
*   [[4-Beat Fill]]: Four time slashes.

**Pro-Tip**: While fill markers indicate the *length* of a fill, they don't always specify the exact notes. This gives the drummer creative freedom to improvise within the given duration, making each performance unique. However, in professional settings, specific fills might be written out if they are critical to the arrangement. Always be prepared to execute a fill that fits the musical context and transitions smoothly into the next section.
