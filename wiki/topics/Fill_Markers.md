# [[Fill|Fill]] Markers

**[[Fill|Fill]] Markers** are a common notation convention used in drum charts to indicate the presence and [[Duration|duration]] of a [[Drum_Fill]]. Just as [[Time_Slashes]] are used to denote the [[Duration|duration]] of a [[Groove|groove]], [[Fill|fill]] markers specify how many beats a drummer should [[Fill|fill]] before transitioning into the next section.

## Notation and Interpretation
Each individual time slash typically represents one [[Beat|beat]] of the [[Fill|fill]]. This provides a clear visual cue for the drummer to execute a [[Fill|fill]] of a specific [[Duration|duration]]:
- **1-[[Beat|Beat]] [[Fill|Fill]]**: One time slash.
- **2-[[Beat|Beat]] [[Fill|Fill]]**: Two [[Time_Slashes|time slashes]].
- **3-[[Beat|Beat]] [[Fill|Fill]]**: Three [[Time_Slashes|time slashes]].
- **4-[[Beat|Beat]] [[Fill|Fill]]**: Four [[Time_Slashes|time slashes]].

## Strategic Placement
In professional charts, [[Fill|fill]] markers are strategically placed at key transition points. For example:
- **Section Endings**: Often found in the last bar of an 'A' section leading into a chorus.
- **Volta Brackets**: Frequently placed in the second ending of a repeated section (e.g., Letter A).
- **Metric Landmarks**: Bar 8 of an [[Eight-Bar_Phrase|eight-bar phrase]] (Letter B).

## Pro-Tip
While [[Fill|fill]] markers indicate the **length** of a [[Fill|fill]], they rarely specify the exact notes. This gives the drummer creative freedom to improvise within the given [[Duration|duration]]. However, always ensure the [[Fill|fill]] fits the musical context and leads the band effectively into the next landmark (e.g., a crash on the downbeat of the next section).
