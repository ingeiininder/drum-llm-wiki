# Chorus (Song Section)
The "Chorus" is a fundamental section in most popular song structures, often referred to as the "hook" or main theme. It is typically the most memorable and melodically distinct part of a song, designed to be catchy and easily repeatable. While the term "chorus" can have varied meanings depending on musical context, in genres like country-pop, it consistently refers to this central, recurring musical idea.

The [[Chorus (Song Section)]] usually follows a [[Verse (Song Section)]] or [[Pre-Chorus]] and often features a fuller instrumentation, increased dynamic level, and a more energetic [[Groove]] compared to the preceding sections. In this curriculum, [[Letter B (Rehearsal Mark)]] functions as the chorus.

**Pro-Tip:** For drummers, the chorus is where you often "open up" the groove. This might involve a [[Playing Surface Switch]] to the [[Ride Cymbal]], a more driving [[Bass Drum]] pattern, or a [[Sloshy Hi-Hat]] sound to create a broader, more expansive feel. The key is to make the chorus feel like the peak of the song's energy and memorability, supporting the vocal and melodic hook.
