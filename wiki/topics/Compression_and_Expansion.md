# Compression and Expansion
Compression and expansion is a rhythmic concept used to develop [[Fill Development|fills]] and solo vocabulary by altering the density of a specific sticking pattern. 

### Core Concept
The technique involves taking a fixed sticking pattern—such as the [[Swiss Army Triplet]]—and transitioning it between different rhythmic subdivisions. 
- **Expansion**: Moving from a higher density (e.g., 16th notes) to a lower density (e.g., 8th note triplets).
- **Compression**: Moving from a lower density (8th note triplets) to a higher density (16th notes).

### Technical Application
In the Musicians Institute curriculum, this is practiced by playing one measure of [[Swiss Army Triplet|Swiss Army Triplets]] as 8th note triplets, followed immediately by the same sticking played as 16th notes. This requires the player to maintain a consistent [[Pulse]] while the internal rate of the notes increases.

### Pro-Tips
- **Foot Consistency**: Practice with both feet tapping quarter notes in unison to ensure the transition doesn't affect the underlying [[Tempo]].
- **BPM Range**: Start at 80 BPM to master the "shift" in feel before working up to 100 BPM.
- **Common Pitfall**: Avoid rushing the 16th notes; the transition should feel like a gear shift, not a loss of control.
