# Hi-Hat Shoulder Strike (Edge)
The [[Hi-Hat_Shoulder_Strike_Edge]] is a hi-hat technique where the shoulder of the drumstick (the part just below the tip) strikes the *edge* of the hi-hat cymbals. This contrasts with striking the top surface or the bell of the hi-hat.

This technique produces a softer, thicker, and more controlled sound with a less aggressive attack compared to other hi-hat articulations. It is ideal for:
*   Achieving lower dynamic levels (e.g., piano).
*   Creating a smooth, flowing rhythmic texture.
*   Blending seamlessly with other instruments.

**Pro-Tip:** This technique is crucial for achieving nuanced hi-hat dynamics and textures, allowing the drummer to provide a subtle rhythmic pulse without overpowering the rest of the ensemble. It's particularly effective for the [[Hi-Hat_Articulation_Letter_A]] to create dynamic contrast.
