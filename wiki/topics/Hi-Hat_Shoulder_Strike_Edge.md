# [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] Shoulder Strike (Edge)
The [[Hi-Hat_Shoulder_Strike_Edge]] is a [[Hi-Hat_Technique|hi-hat technique]] where the shoulder of the [[Drumstick|drumstick]] (the part just below the tip) strikes the *edge* of the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] cymbals. This contrasts with striking the top surface or the bell of the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]].

This technique produces a softer, thicker, and more controlled sound with a less aggressive attack compared to other [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] [[Articulations|articulations]]. It is ideal for:
*   Achieving lower dynamic levels (e.g., piano).
*   Creating a smooth, flowing rhythmic texture.
*   Blending seamlessly with other instruments.

**Pro-Tip:** This technique is crucial for achieving nuanced [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] [[Dynamics|dynamics]] and textures, allowing the drummer to provide a subtle rhythmic [[Pulse|pulse]] without overpowering the rest of the ensemble. It's particularly effective for the [[Hi-Hat_Articulation_Letter_A]] to create dynamic contrast.
