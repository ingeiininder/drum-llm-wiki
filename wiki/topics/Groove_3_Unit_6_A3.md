# [[Groove|Groove]] 3 (Unit 6 A3)

[[Groove|Groove]] 3, designated for the A3 section of a performance, is characterized by its specific articulation and rhythmic variation.

## Characteristics

*   **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: Played on a "thicker" [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] sound, which implies less pressure on the [[Hi-Hat_Pedal]]. This creates a more open, sustained [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] sound compared to a tight, closed sound.
*   **Snare Drum**: Utilizes a [[Rimshot]] articulation on the [[Snare_Drum]]. This provides a strong, cutting accent.
*   **Bass Drum**: Features a strong, standard [[Bass_Drum]] sound. This can be achieved either by using a "[[Bury-the-Beater]]" technique (where the beater remains on the head after the stroke) or an "[[Off_the_Head_Bass_Drum]]" technique (where the beater rebounds immediately).
*   **Rhythmic Structure**: The [[Bass_Drum]] part features a [[Four-Bar_Pattern]] that varies in every bar of the four-bar phrase. This provides rhythmic interest and prevents monotony.

## Context

This [[Groove|groove]] is designed to provide a specific feel and texture within a larger musical piece, often contrasting with other sections. The varying bass drum pattern requires careful attention to detail and rhythmic precision.

## Pro-Tips

*   **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] Control**: Practice varying your [[Hi-Hat_Pedal]] pressure to achieve the desired "thick" sound consistently.
*   **Rim Shot Accuracy**: Ensure your [[Rimshot]]s are consistent in volume and placement.
*   **Bass Drum [[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: Experiment with both "bury-the-beater" and "off the head" techniques to understand their sonic differences and choose what best fits the musical context.
*   **Pattern Memorization**: Due to the varying [[Four-Bar_Pattern]] in the bass drum, memorization and focused practice are crucial for seamless execution.
