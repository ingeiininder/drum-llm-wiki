# Reverse Paradiddle
The [[Reverse_Paradiddle]] is another fundamental variation of the [[Paradiddle]] [[Rudiment]], characterized by a [[Sticking_Patterns]] that reverses the order of the single and [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]] compared to the [[Single_Paradiddle]].

**Sticking Pattern (Ex 8.3)**:
R L L R L R R L (Note: The text provided R L L R L R R L for Ex 8.2 and 8.3. This implies the Inward and Reverse Paradiddles share this basic sticking, or there's a slight variation in interpretation. For standard definitions, the Reverse Paradiddle is often RLLR LRRL, which is the same as the Inward/Inverted. The key is the *application* and *context*.)

**Pro-Tip**: While the [[Sticking_Patterns]] may appear similar to the [[Inward_(Inverted)_Paradiddle]], focus on how this pattern feels and functions in different musical contexts. Experiment with [[Accent_Patterns]] to discover its unique rhythmic possibilities, especially when applied to the [[Drum_Seat]].
