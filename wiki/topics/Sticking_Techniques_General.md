# Sticking Techniques (General)
Sticking Techniques (General) refers to the broader array of methods and approaches drummers use to apply [[Sticking Patterns]] to rhythms. Beyond the foundational [[Assigned Sticking]] and [[Double Strokes]], the drumming community recognizes numerous other techniques, each with its own advantages depending on the musical context, tempo, meter, and instrumentation.

Commonly endorsed sticking techniques include:
*   **Alternate Sticking**: A strict R L R L pattern, regardless of accents or phrasing. It's excellent for developing evenness and speed.
*   **Natural Sticking**: This approach prioritizes what feels most comfortable and efficient for a given rhythm, often allowing accents to fall on the dominant hand or using specific hand combinations to facilitate movement around the drum set.
*   **Straight Sticking**: Often implies a consistent, non-alternating pattern applied to a phrase, or a very basic alternating pattern.
*   **Hybrid Stickings**: Combinations of various techniques to navigate complex or unusual rhythmic figures. These often involve mixing single strokes, double strokes, and accents in creative ways.

**Factors Influencing Sticking Choices**:
*   **Tempo**: Faster tempos might necessitate more [[Double Strokes]] or efficient [[Alternating_Hands_Sticking]].
*   **Meter**: Different time signatures can influence natural groupings and preferred stickings.
*   **Instrumentation/Orchestration**: Moving between different drums and cymbals on the drum set will often dictate specific hand placements.
*   **Musical Style**: Certain genres or feels might favor particular sticking approaches.

**Pro-Tip**: A versatile drummer understands and can apply multiple sticking techniques. While [[Assigned Sticking]] and [[Double Strokes]] are excellent starting points for [[Drum Reading]] development, exploring other techniques will expand your vocabulary and problem-solving abilities on the drum set. Always choose the sticking that allows for the cleanest, most musical, and most comfortable execution.
