# Sticking Techniques (General)
Sticking Techniques (General) refers to the broader array of methods and approaches drummers use to apply [[Sticking_Patterns]] to rhythms. Beyond the foundational [[Assigned_Sticking]] and [[Double_Strokes]], the drumming community recognizes numerous other techniques, each with its own advantages depending on the musical context, [[Tempo|tempo]], [[Meter|meter]], and instrumentation.

Commonly endorsed sticking techniques include:
*   **Alternate Sticking**: A strict R L R L pattern, regardless of [[Accents|accents]] or [[Phrasing|phrasing]]. It's excellent for developing evenness and speed.
*   **Natural Sticking**: This approach prioritizes what feels most comfortable and efficient for a given rhythm, often allowing [[Accents|accents]] to fall on the dominant hand or using specific hand combinations to facilitate movement around the drum set.
*   **Straight Sticking**: Often implies a consistent, non-alternating pattern applied to a phrase, or a very basic alternating pattern.
*   **Hybrid Stickings**: Combinations of various techniques to navigate complex or unusual rhythmic figures. These often involve mixing single strokes, double strokes, and [[Accents|accents]] in creative ways.

**Factors Influencing Sticking Choices**:
*   **[[Tempo|Tempo]]**: Faster tempos might necessitate more [[Double_Strokes]] or efficient [[Alternating_Hands_Sticking]].
*   **[[Meter|Meter]]**: Different time signatures can influence natural groupings and preferred stickings.
*   **Instrumentation/Orchestration**: Moving between different drums and cymbals on the drum set will often dictate specific hand placements.
*   **Musical Style**: Certain genres or feels might favor particular sticking approaches.

**Pro-Tip**: A versatile drummer understands and can apply multiple sticking techniques. While [[Assigned_Sticking]] and [[Double_Strokes]] are excellent starting points for [[Drum_Reading]] development, exploring other techniques will expand your vocabulary and problem-solving abilities on the drum set. Always choose the sticking that allows for the cleanest, most musical, and most comfortable execution.
