# Bass Drum and Hi-Hat Coordination Part 2
This chapter is designed to further develop the foundational [[Bass Drum and Hi-Hat Coordination Part 1]] introduced in the previous unit. It introduces more complex rhythmic variations for the bass drum while maintaining a steady hi-hat ostinato.

## Core Content
*   **Single 16th-Note Bass Drum Variations**: The exercises will introduce various single 16th-note patterns for the [[Bass Drum]].
*   **Quarter-Note Hi-Hat Ostinato**: These bass drum variations will be played against a steady quarter-note [[Hi-Hat]] [[Ostinato]].
*   **Counting**: Use four-part counting (e.g., "1-e-&-a") as you play and practice these exercises to ensure proper 16th-note subdivisions and rhythmic accuracy.

## Bass Drum Syncopation
This section introduces [[Bass Drum Syncopation]], where rhythms are intentionally placed off the main beats to create rhythmic tension and interest.
*   **Practice Method**:
    1.  First, practice the syncopated bass drum rhythms with an eighth-note hi-hat stepping ostinato.
    2.  Then, apply the same bass drum rhythms with a quarter-note hi-hat ostinato.
*   **Exercise**: Ex 7.33

### Pro-Tip
When working on syncopated bass drum patterns, it's crucial to maintain a rock-solid hi-hat pulse. The hi-hat acts as your internal metronome, allowing the bass drum to "dance" around it. Start very slowly, ensuring each bass drum note is precisely placed in relation to the hi-hat. Gradually increase tempo only when accuracy is consistent.
