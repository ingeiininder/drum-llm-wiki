# Four-Way Coordination: Triplet [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]] Permutations

This study focuses on developing independence between an alternating [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] pattern and various [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] permutations within an [[Eighth_Note_Triplet]] feel.

### Technical Framework
- **Hands**: Constant alternating sticking (RLRLRL) playing eighth-note [[Triplets|triplets]].
- **[[Accents|Accents]]**: Specific snare notes are accented to create rhythmic interest.
- **Feet**: The [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] plays a steady [[Ostinato]] (typically on beats 2 and 4, or all four [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]]), while the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] performs "double" triplet positions.

### Permutation Logic
The exercises (Ex 8.16 - 8.19) move the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] through different "slots" of the triplet:
- **Single Positions**: [[Bass_Drum|Bass drum]] on the "1", the "trip", or the "let".
- **Double Positions**: [[Bass_Drum|Bass drum]] playing two out of the three [[Triplet_Partials|triplet partials]] (e.g., the "1" and the "trip").

### Practice Objectives
1. **Dynamic Separation**: Create a "large and distinct" dynamic gap between accented snare notes and unaccented "ghost" notes.
2. **Three-Part Counting**: Count "1-trip-let, 2-trip-let" out loud while playing. This is critical for aligning the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] with the hand pattern.
3. **Ostinato Integration**: Once the snare and [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] are synchronized, add the [[Hi-Hat_Ostinato]] B to achieve true [[Four-Way_Coordination]].

### Common Pitfalls
- **Flamming**: The [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] and snare/[[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] must land exactly together. If they are slightly offset, it creates a "flam" effect that ruins the [[Groove|groove]]'s pocket.
- **Rushing [[Triplets|Triplets]]**: Ensure the [[Triplets|triplets]] remain wide and even; do not let them collapse into a "dotted-eighth sixteenth" feel.
