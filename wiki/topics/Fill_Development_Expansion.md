# [[Fill|Fill]] Development: Expansion

**Expansion** (or "stretching") is the rhythmic inverse of [[Fill_Development_Compression]]. It involves taking a rhythmic idea and slowing it down by placing it into a larger [[Subdivision|subdivision]]. This creates a powerful musical illusion, making it seem as though the performer has slowed down the [[Tempo|tempo]] while the underlying [[Pulse|pulse]] remains constant.

### Execution Strategies
*   **16th to 8th Note Expansion**: Taking a 16th-note hand/foot pattern and playing it as 8th notes. While technically simpler, the challenge lies in maintaining the [[Momentum]] and space.
*   **Triplet Expansion**: Transforming a 16th-note-based idea into [[8th-Note_Triplets]]. This often results in a "pivoting" feel between voices, such as the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] and floor tom.

### Technical Focus: Momentum Control
Expansion is a critical tool for developing **Momentum Control**. It requires the drummer to resist the urge to rush when the note density decreases. 

### Professional Application
Expansion is highly effective for creating dramatic tension during a solo or a transition into a slower section of a song. By "stretching" a [[Fill|fill]], you provide the listener with a sense of breath and space before returning to a high-density [[Groove|groove]].
