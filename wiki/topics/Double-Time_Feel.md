# Double-Time Feel

**Double-time feel** is a rhythmic concept where the pulse of the music feels twice as fast as the actual tempo, though the underlying beat (BPM) remains the same. This is a staple technique in [[Punk-Pop Drumming]] and various forms of extreme metal and hardcore.

### Construction of the Groove
In a standard 4/4 "half-time" or "standard-time" groove, the snare drum typically falls on beats 2 and 4. In a **Double-Time Feel**:
*   **Snare Drum**: Moves to the **"&" of every beat** (1 **&** 2 **&** 3 **&** 4 **&**).
*   **Hi-Hat**: Usually maintains eighth notes, often played "sloshy" or half-open.
*   **Bass Drum**: Often employs 16th-note patterns that may or may not align with the hi-hat, adding to the "busy" and driving nature of the feel.

### Musical Effect
This feel is used to increase the "energy" of a song, typically during a chorus or a bridge, without requiring the entire band to actually speed up the metronome. It creates a sense of urgency and forward motion.
