# Swung Reggae Ostinato

The **Swung Reggae Ostinato** is a coordination template used to practice reading rhythms against a consistent "one-drop" or reggae-style feel. This requires a [[Swung Feel Interpretation]], where eighth notes are played with a triplet underlying pulse.

### Ostinato Patterns (Ex 8.11)
1. **Hand Ostinato**: Typically involves a specific hi-hat or ride cymbal pattern characteristic of reggae (e.g., emphasizing the "and" of the beat or the "let" of the triplet).
2. **Bass Drum Independence**: Reading a 32-bar rhythmic chart (Ex 8.12) using the bass drum while the hands maintain the reggae ostinato.
3. **Left Foot Independence**: Reading the same 32-bar chart using the hi-hat foot while the right hand plays the ostinato on the ride cymbal.

### Technical Requirements
- **The "Let" of the Triplet**: In a swung feel, any note written as an "&" must be interpreted as the third part of a triplet (the "let").
- **Counting**: It is critical to count "1-trip-let, 2-trip-let" out loud to maintain the spacing between the downbeat and the swung upbeat.
