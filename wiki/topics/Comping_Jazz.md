# Comping (Jazz)
"Comping" (short for accompanying) is the rhythmic support provided by the drummer to the melody or soloist. It involves playing syncopated accents on the snare and bass drum while maintaining a consistent [[Jazz Ride Cymbal Technique|Ride Cymbal]] pattern and [[Jazz Hi-Hat Technique|Hi-Hat]] backbeat.

### Two Roles of Comping
1.  **Comping the Head**: The drummer supports the melody by accentuating specific rhythmic "hits" written in the lead sheet. The goal is to make the melody sound "thick" and reinforced.
2.  **Comping the Solo**: The drummer reacts to the soloist's phrases. This is a conversation. It requires "tasteful" independence—avoiding overly complex patterns that distract from the soloist.

### Technical Requirement: Independence
To comp effectively, the drummer must develop 4-way independence. The right hand (Ride) and left foot (Hi-hat) act as the "constant," while the left hand (Snare) and right foot (Bass Drum) provide the "variables."
