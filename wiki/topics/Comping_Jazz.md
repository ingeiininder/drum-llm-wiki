# Comping ([[Jazz|Jazz]])
"Comping" (short for accompanying) is the rhythmic support provided by the drummer to the melody or soloist. It involves playing syncopated [[Accents|accents]] on the snare and [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] while maintaining a consistent [[Jazz_Ride_Cymbal_Technique|Ride Cymbal]] pattern and [[Jazz_Hi-Hat_Technique|Hi-Hat]] backbeat.

### Two Roles of Comping
1.  **Comping the Head**: The drummer supports the melody by accentuating specific rhythmic "hits" written in the lead sheet. The goal is to make the melody sound "thick" and reinforced.
2.  **Comping the Solo**: The drummer reacts to the soloist's phrases. This is a conversation. It requires "tasteful" independence—avoiding overly complex patterns that distract from the soloist.

### Technical Requirement: Independence
To comp effectively, the drummer must develop 4-way independence. The right hand (Ride) and left foot ([[Hi-Hat|Hi-hat]]) act as the "constant," while the left hand (Snare) and right foot ([[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]) provide the "variables."
