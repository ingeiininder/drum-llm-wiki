# New Groove
A "New Groove" refers to a distinct rhythmic pattern or feel introduced at a specific point in a song, often to mark a change in [[Song Structure]], mood, or energy. It provides a fresh rhythmic foundation that contrasts with previous sections.

In this curriculum, a new groove is mentioned as a characteristic of the [[Bridge (Song Section)]] within the [[Coda (Song Section)]], indicating a shift in the drum pattern and overall rhythmic feel.

**Pro-Tip:** Introducing a new groove is a powerful tool for drummers to shape the song's narrative. When a new groove is called for, consider how it should differ from the previous one in terms of [[Playing Surface Switch|instrumentation]], density, dynamics, and rhythmic complexity. The goal is to create a clear and intentional shift that supports the song's arrangement.
