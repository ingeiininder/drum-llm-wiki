# ABAB 32-Bar Form
The `[[ABAB 32-Bar Form]]` is a common musical structure used in songwriting and composition, similar in length to the `[[AABA 32-Bar Form]]` but with a different arrangement of its sections. This form is comprised of two distinct sections, labeled 'A' and 'B', which alternate throughout the 32 bars.

**Key Attributes of Each Section (A or B):**
1.  **Different Rhythm (or Groove)**: Each section features a unique rhythmic pattern or `[[Groove]]`. For drummers, this means adapting to distinct `[[Bass Drum]]` patterns, `[[Snare Drum]]` accents, and `[[Hi-Hat]]` articulations.
2.  **Eight-Bar Phrase**: Both the 'A' and 'B' sections are typically structured as an `[[Eight-Bar Phrase]]`, providing a clear, digestible musical statement.
3.  **Particular Guitar Riff and Chord Structure**: Beyond rhythm, each section is often defined by a specific `[[Guitar Riff]]` and underlying `[[Chord Structure]]`, which influences the drum part's feel and dynamics.

For example, in the context of `[[Intro to Two-Handed 16ths]]`, the 'A' section might feature a `[[Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove]]` with a `[[Four-Bar Bass Drum Pattern]]` (as in `[[Ex 4.1]]`), while the 'B' section might introduce a different `[[Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove]]` with a `[[Two-Bar Bass Drum Pattern]]` and a `[[Hi-Hat Opening]]` (as in `[[Ex 4.2]]`).
