# ABAB 32-Bar Form
The `[[ABAB_32-Bar_Form]]` is a common musical structure used in songwriting and composition, similar in length to the `[[AABA_32-Bar_Form]]` but with a different arrangement of its sections. This form is comprised of two distinct sections, labeled 'A' and 'B', which alternate throughout the 32 bars.

**Key Attributes of Each Section (A or B):**
1.  **Different Rhythm (or Groove)**: Each section features a unique rhythmic pattern or `[[Groove]]`. For drummers, this means adapting to distinct `[[Bass_Drum]]` patterns, `[[Snare_Drum]]` [[Accents|accents]], and `[[Hi-Hat]]` [[Articulations|articulations]].
2.  **Eight-Bar Phrase**: Both the 'A' and 'B' sections are typically structured as an `[[Eight-Bar_Phrase]]`, providing a clear, digestible musical statement.
3.  **[[Particular_Guitar_Riff_and_Chord_Structure|Particular Guitar Riff and Chord Structure]]**: Beyond rhythm, each section is often defined by a specific `[[Guitar_Riff]]` and underlying `[[Chord_Structure]]`, which influences the drum part's feel and [[Dynamics|dynamics]].

For example, in the context of `[[Intro_to_Two-Handed_16ths]]`, the 'A' section might feature a `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]` with a `[[Four-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` (as in `[[Ex_4.1]]`), while the 'B' section might introduce a different `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]` with a `[[Two-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` and a `[[Hi-Hat_Opening]]` (as in `[[Ex_4.2]]`). The full structure is illustrated in `[[Ex_4.3]]`.
