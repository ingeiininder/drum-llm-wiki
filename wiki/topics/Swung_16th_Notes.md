# Swung 16th Notes
**Swung 16th notes** are a rhythmic feel widely utilized in modern [[Rock|Rock]], [[Funk|Funk]], and Hip-Hop. Unlike the [[Shuffle_Feel]] (which is based on [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]]), the [[Swung_16th_Feel|swung 16th feel]] is based on the [[Subdivision|subdivision]] of [[Sixteenth_Note_Triplets]].

### The "Broken Triplet" Concept
To create the [[Swung_16th_Feel|swung 16th feel]], drummers employ a "broken" 16th-note triplet. This is achieved by removing the second and fifth partials of the sextuplet. 
*   **Straight 16th [[Triplets|Triplets]]**: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6
*   **Swung 16th Interpretation**: 1, (rest), 3, 4, (rest), 6

### Notation vs. Interpretation
To avoid cluttered charts filled with triplet brackets and [[Rests|rests]], composers typically write standard [[16th_Notes]] and provide a **Swung 16th Indicator** at the top of the page. 
*   **Indicator**: A small musical equation showing two 16th notes equaling a tied triplet figure.
*   **Text Instruction**: Simply writing "Swung 16ths" or "Half-time [[Shuffle|Shuffle]] feel."

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Blurring the Feel**: Many students fail to distinguish between a "straight" feel and a "swung" feel. 
*   **Inconsistency**: Ensure the "gap" between the first and second note of the swung pair remains consistent. If the second note is too late, it sounds like a double-stroke; if too early, it sounds straight.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> When reading [[Ensemble_Figures]] in a swung context, every written 16th note must be adjusted to the triplet grid unless otherwise specified.
