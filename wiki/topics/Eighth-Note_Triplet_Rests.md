# Eighth-Note Triplet [[Rests|Rests]]
Just as [[Eighth_Note]] can be replaced by [[Eighth_Rests]] in straight subdivisions, [[Eighth_Note_Triplets]] can also incorporate [[Rests|rests]]. An [[Eighth-Note_Triplet_Rests]] occupies the [[Duration|duration]] of one [[Triplet_Partials]]. To maintain clarity in notation, a bracket is often used (in addition to the number three) to house combinations of notes and [[Rests|rests]] within a triplet grouping.

**Combinations of Eighth-Note [[Triplets|Triplets]] and [[Rests|Rests]]:**
The following are common combinations, demonstrating how [[Rests|rests]] can be placed on different [[Triplet_Partials]]:

1.  **Eighth Rest on the First Triplet Partial:**
    *   The first partial is silent, while the second and third partials are played.
    *   Counting: "rest-trip-let"
2.  **Eighth Rest on the Second Triplet Partial:**
    *   The first and third partials are played, with the second partial silent.
    *   Counting: "1-rest-let"
3.  **Eighth Rest on the Third Triplet Partial:**
    *   The first and second partials are played, with the third partial silent.
    *   Counting: "1-trip-rest"
4.  **[[Eighth_Rests|Eighth Rests]] on the First and Second Partials:**
    *   Only the third partial is played.
    *   Counting: "rest-rest-let"
5.  **[[Eighth_Rests|Eighth Rests]] on the First and Third Partials:**
    *   Only the second partial is played.
    *   Counting: "rest-trip-rest"
6.  **[[Eighth_Rests|Eighth Rests]] on the Second and Third Partials:**
    *   Only the first partial is played.
    *   Counting: "1-rest-rest"

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing these combinations, focus on the *silence* as much as the sound. The precise placement and [[Duration|duration]] of the [[Rests|rests]] are crucial for the rhythmic integrity of the triplet. Use the "1-trip-let" counting method, replacing the syllable with "rest" when a rest occurs, to ensure accuracy.
