# Eighth-Note Triplet Rests
Just as [[Eighth Notes]] can be replaced by [[Eighth Rests]] in straight subdivisions, [[Eighth-Note Triplets]] can also incorporate rests. An [[Eighth-Note_Triplet_Rests]] occupies the duration of one [[Triplet_Partials]]. To maintain clarity in notation, a bracket is often used (in addition to the number three) to house combinations of notes and rests within a triplet grouping.

**Combinations of Eighth-Note Triplets and Rests:**
The following are common combinations, demonstrating how rests can be placed on different [[Triplet Partials]]:

1.  **Eighth Rest on the First Triplet Partial:**
    *   The first partial is silent, while the second and third partials are played.
    *   Counting: "rest-trip-let"
2.  **Eighth Rest on the Second Triplet Partial:**
    *   The first and third partials are played, with the second partial silent.
    *   Counting: "1-rest-let"
3.  **Eighth Rest on the Third Triplet Partial:**
    *   The first and second partials are played, with the third partial silent.
    *   Counting: "1-trip-rest"
4.  **Eighth Rests on the First and Second Partials:**
    *   Only the third partial is played.
    *   Counting: "rest-rest-let"
5.  **Eighth Rests on the First and Third Partials:**
    *   Only the second partial is played.
    *   Counting: "rest-trip-rest"
6.  **Eighth Rests on the Second and Third Partials:**
    *   Only the first partial is played.
    *   Counting: "1-rest-rest"

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing these combinations, focus on the *silence* as much as the sound. The precise placement and duration of the rests are crucial for the rhythmic integrity of the triplet. Use the "1-trip-let" counting method, replacing the syllable with "rest" when a rest occurs, to ensure accuracy.
