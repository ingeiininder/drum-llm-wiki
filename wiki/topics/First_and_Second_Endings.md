# First and Second Endings

In musical notation, **First and Second Endings** (also known as "Volta Brackets") are used to indicate repeated sections of music that have different conclusions. This technique allows for efficient notation of forms like the [[AABA_32-Bar_Form]] without unnecessary repetition of common parts.

## Navigation Logic
1.  **First Pass**: Play through the section until you reach the **First Ending** bracket (marked with a "1."). Follow the instructions (usually a repeat sign) to return to the beginning of the section.
2.  **Second Pass**: Upon repeating the section, play precisely as before, but **skip the entire First Ending** bracket and jump directly to the **Second Ending** (marked with a "2.") to continue the piece.

## Professional Practice
- **Repeats are Good**: If you see a [[D.C._al_Fine]] or [[D.S._al_Coda]] instruction that includes "repeats are good," you must observe these first and second endings during that repeat.
- **Anticipation**: Pay close attention to these markings, as they often signal a change in [[Groove]], dynamic shifts, or the addition of a [[Fill]]. Misreading a volta bracket can cause the entire band to get lost.
