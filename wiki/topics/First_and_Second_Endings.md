# First and Second Endings
In musical notation, first and second endings (also known as "volta brackets") are used to indicate repeated sections of music that have different conclusions. The first ending is played during the initial pass through a section, and upon repetition, the performer skips the first ending and plays the second ending instead.

**Pro-Tip:** Drummers must pay close attention to these markings, as they often dictate changes in groove, dynamics, or even the addition of [[Drum Fill]]s. Misreading them can lead to significant ensemble errors. Always scan your chart for these navigational cues before playing.
