# [[Duration|Duration]] and Sustain
In music, **[[Duration|duration]]** refers to the length of time a note or rest is held, symbolized by its [[Note_Value]] (e.g., [[Whole_Note]], [[Half_Note]], [[Quarter_Note]]). **Sustain**, on the other hand, denotes the period of time a sound remains audible after its initial attack before it fades into silence.

While note value dictates the *relative* [[Duration|duration]] in notation, sustain describes the *actual* audible length of the sound. Drum and percussion instruments typically have a relatively short sustain compared to instruments like woodwinds, brass, or a pipe organ, which can have (theoretically) unlimited sustain.

**Key Concepts:**
*   **Note Value**: The symbolic representation of a note's length in relation to the [[Beat|beat]] and other notes.
*   **Sustain**: The physical characteristic of a sound's decay.

**Pro-Tip for Drummers:** Even though many drum sounds have a short sustain, it's crucial for drummers to *feel* the full notated [[Duration|duration]] of a note. This internal awareness helps maintain accurate [[Phrasing]] and ensures that the rhythmic space is correctly filled, even if the sound itself has already decayed. For example, a [[Half_Note]] on a [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]], though short in sustain, still occupies two beats of rhythmic space.
