# Triple Paradiddle
The [[Triple Paradiddle]] is a further extension of the [[Single Paradiddle]], consisting of eight notes. It features six [[Single Stroke]]s followed by one [[Double Stroke Technique]], with the sticking alternating between hands. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 8 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking:**
RLRLRLRR LRLRLRLL

**Breakdown:**
*   **R L R L R L R R:** Right, Left, Right, Left, Right, Left, Right, Right
*   **L R L R L R L L:** Left, Right, Left, Right, Left, Right, Left, Left

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Eight-Note Pattern:** The eight-note grouping provides a longer, more flowing rhythmic phrase.
*   **Alternating Lead:** The leading hand alternates every eight notes.
*   **Extended Single-Double Combination:** Further develops control over extended sequences of single strokes leading into a double stroke.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Advanced Stick Control:** Challenges drummers to maintain precision and evenness over a longer, more complex rudiment.
*   **Endurance:** Builds endurance for playing continuous patterns.
*   **Fills and Linear Phrasing:** Highly effective for creating long, flowing fills and for developing [[Linear Phrasing]] concepts around the [[Drum Kit]].

**Pro-Tip:** The [[Triple Paradiddle]] requires significant focus on maintaining evenness throughout the six single strokes before the double. Practice slowly with a metronome, ensuring each note is distinct and consistent. Pay attention to the transition from the single strokes to the double stroke, making it smooth and controlled. This rudiment is excellent for developing advanced coordination and fluidity.
