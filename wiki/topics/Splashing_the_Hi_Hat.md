# Splashing the Hi-Hat

Splashing the [[Hi-Hat]] with the foot is a technique used to create a "metallic pad" or a shimmering "sheen" of sound, particularly effective during fills or underneath a [[Ride Cymbal]] pattern in jazz.

### Technique: The Heel Kick
1.  **The Strike**: Give the hi-hat pedal a terse, quick kick with the heel.
2.  **The Release**: Immediately allow the cymbals to clang together.
3.  **The Control**: Gently place the foot back on the pedal, leaving just enough space between the cymbals to allow them to continue vibrating without choking the sound.

### Musical Use
The hi-hat splash adds a layer of high-frequency texture that fills the sonic space. It is often used on beat 1 of a new section or as a "wash" during a [[Drum Solo]].
