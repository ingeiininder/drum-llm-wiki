# Double-Stroke Roll (Open Roll)

The **Double-Stroke Roll**, specifically performed as an **Open Roll**, is one of the most critical [[Rudiment]] for any percussionist. Unlike the [[Closed_Roll]] (or press roll), every stroke in an open roll must be clearly audible and distinct.

### Technical Execution
- **Rebound Mechanics**: The second note of each double should be a [[Rebound]]—one wrist motion followed by a controlled bounce.
- **Consistency**: The goal is to achieve a clean sound where there is no audible difference between the first and second stroke of the double, nor between single-stroke and double-stroke transitions.
- **Relaxation**: Tension is the enemy of the roll. Drummers must rely on the natural energy created by the stick's interaction with the playing surface.

### Practice Method
- **Transitioning**: Practice alternating between one [[Measure|measure]] of various [[Sticking]] patterns (like [[Single_Strokes]] or [[Paradiddle]]) and one [[Measure|measure]] of [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]].
- **[[Tempo|Tempo]]**: A target [[Tempo|tempo]] of **120 BPM** is suggested for developing the "rebound" feel in [[16th_Notes]].

### Pro-Tips
- **Foot Integration**: Always tap both feet with the [[Metronome]] to ensure the hands are not "floating" or rushing against the [[Pulse|pulse]].
- **Surface Awareness**: Practice on both a high-rebound surface (like a practice pad) and a low-rebound surface (like a floor tom) to master the finger control required for the second stroke.
