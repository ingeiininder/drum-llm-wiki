# Percussion Clef
The Percussion Clef, also referred to as the **Neutral Clef**, is a specialized [[Clef_Music_Notation|clef]] used in [[Music_Notation]] specifically for non-melodic instruments.

## Function
*   **Non-Melodic Instruments**: Unlike the [[Treble_Clef]] or [[Bass_Clef]] which designate specific pitches, the percussion clef indicates that the music written on [[The_Staff]] is for instruments that do not produce definite pitches, such as:
    *   [[Drum_Seat]]
    *   Various [[Percussion_Instrument]]s (e.g., wood blocks, cowbells, shakers)
*   **Component Assignment**: For instruments like the [[Drum_Seat]], the lines and spaces of the staff are assigned to represent specific components of the kit (e.g., [[Snare_Drum_Notation]], [[Bass_Drum_Notation]], [[Hi_Hat_Cymbal_Notation]]) rather than musical pitches. The specific assignments are typically detailed in a [[Drum_Set_Notation_Key]] at the beginning of the score.

## Appearance
The percussion clef often appears as two parallel vertical lines, sometimes with a small rectangle or other simple symbol between them, or simply as a standard staff without a traditional clef symbol. Its primary purpose is to signal to the performer that the notation should be interpreted percussively, not melodically.
