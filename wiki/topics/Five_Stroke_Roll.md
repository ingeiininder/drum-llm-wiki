# Five-Stroke Roll
The [[Five_Stroke_Roll]] is a fundamental drum rudiment and one of the most common [[Double_Stroke_Roll]]s. It consists of two [[Double_Strokes]]s followed by a single accented stroke, totaling five notes. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 7 of "[[Drum_Technique_1_(MI_Curriculum_Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
RRLLR (or LLRRL for Left Hand Lead)

**Breakdown:**
*   **RR:** Two strokes with the right hand (a [[Double_Stroke_Technique]]).
*   **LL:** Two strokes with the left hand (a [[Double_Stroke_Technique]]).
*   **R:** A single, accented stroke with the right hand, which typically marks the end of the roll and leads into the next [[Beat|beat]] or phrase.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Roll Development:** Essential for developing control over the [[Double_Stroke_Technique]] and creating smooth, sustained sounds.
*   **Fills and Embellishments:** Widely used in drum fills, transitions, and to embellish rhythmic patterns across various musical styles.
*   **Dynamic Control:** Allows for dynamic shaping, with the initial double strokes often softer and the final single stroke accented.

**Pro-Tip:** The key to a good [[Five_Stroke_Roll]] is to ensure that all four initial double strokes are even in volume and spacing, and that the final single stroke is clearly accented. Practice slowly, focusing on the rebound for the second stroke of each pair. Gradually increase [[Tempo|tempo]], aiming for a smooth, continuous sound rather than five distinct individual notes.
