# Different Rhythm (or Groove)
Within musical structures like the `[[ABAB_32-Bar_Form]]`, a "Different Rhythm (or Groove)" refers to the distinct rhythmic patterns that define individual sections (e.g., 'A' versus 'B'). This variation is a fundamental element of musical composition, providing contrast and progression throughout a piece.

For drummers, this means:
*   **Adapting Patterns**: Switching between different `[[Bass_Drum]]` patterns, `[[Snare_Drum]]` [[Accents|accents]], and `[[Hi-Hat]]` [[Articulations|articulations]].
*   **Creating Contrast**: Using a new `[[Groove]]` to signify a change in mood, energy, or section within the song.
*   **Developing Versatility**: The ability to seamlessly transition between different rhythmic feels is a hallmark of a skilled drummer.

For example, an 'A' section might feature a driving `[[Two-Handed_16th-Note_Groove]]` with a `[[Four-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]`, while a 'B' section might introduce a more open feel with a `[[Two-Bar_Bass_Drum_Pattern]]` and `[[Hi-Hat_Opening]]`.
