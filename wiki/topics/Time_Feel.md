# Time Feel

Time Feel (or simply "Feel") refers to the underlying pulse and rhythmic character of a performance. While a [[Metronome|metronome]] provides a rigid tempo, the "feel" is how a drummer interprets that tempo to create a specific musical atmosphere, such as making a song sound faster or slower without changing the actual BPM.

## Variations in Time Feel

- **Double-Time Feel**: Creates the illusion that the music has sped up by moving the backbeat ([[Snare_Drum|snare drum]]) from beats 2 and 4 to the "**&**" of every [[Beat|beat]]. This generates musical excitement and is often used in high-energy B-sections or guitar solos.
- **Half-Time Feel**: Creates the illusion that the music is half its original speed by placing the backbeat only on **[[Beat|beat]] 3** (in 4/4 time). It is common in genres like [[Reggae|Reggae]] (the "one-drop"), Hip-Hop, and slowed-down Ballads.
- **Stop-Time Feel**: A section where the regular timekeeping stops, and the drummer only plays specific rhythmic [[Accents|accents]]/figures alongside the ensemble.
- **Double-Time Feel (Sloshy)**: A [[Rock|rock]] technique where the drummer uses a "sloshy" [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] (hitting both top and bottom cymbals with the shoulder of the stick) to add force and drive to a double-time section.

## Foundations of Feel

- **[[Jazz|Jazz]] Time Feel**: The foundation of [[Jazz|jazz]] is the **Ride Cymbal**. A good feel starts with a solid quarter-note pulse on the ride, followed by the addition of the "skip note" to complete the swing pattern.
- **Maintaining Momentum**: Filling the space between ensemble figures with [[Triplets|triplets]] or [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]] is a key strategy for maintaining a consistent time feel and keeping the [[Chart|chart]] moving forward.
- **[[Check_Patterns|Check Patterns]]**: Exercises used to establish and maintain an even, consistent time feel, often incorporating both triplet and 16th-note forms.

## Professional Indicators

- **[[Timekeeping_Figures|Timekeeping Figures]]**: Rhythms written above [[The_Staff|the staff]] (with [[Time_Slashes|time slashes]] below) that show what the rest of the band is playing. These guide the drummer in choosing a "musical" and complimentary time feel without interrupting the general [[Groove|groove]].
- **Sound Production**: A "legato" feel on the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] (leaving them slightly open) can avoid a rigid, "vertical" time feel during solos.
