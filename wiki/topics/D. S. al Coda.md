# D. S. al Coda
"D. S. al Coda" is a common musical instruction, an abbreviation for "Dal Segno al Coda," which translates from Italian as "from the sign to the coda." It is a crucial navigation tool in sheet music, especially in songs with repeated sections and specific endings.

The instruction dictates the following sequence:
1.  **D. S. (Dal Segno):** Go back to the [[Dal Segno (D.S.)]] sign (often a stylized 'S' with a slash and dots).
2.  **Play until the Coda Sign:** Continue playing from the [[Dal Segno (D.S.)]] sign until you encounter the [[Coda Sign]] (a circle with a crosshair).
3.  **al Coda:** At the [[Coda Sign]], jump directly to the [[Coda (Song Section)]] section, which is typically marked with the same [[Coda Sign]].

This instruction allows for efficient notation of complex song forms, ensuring that specific sections are repeated or skipped as intended.

**Pro-Tip:** Navigating D. S. al Coda requires careful chart reading. Always mentally (or physically) trace the path through the music before playing, especially in performance. Missing the [[Coda Sign]] or jumping to the wrong section can lead to significant musical errors. Pay attention to any dynamic or [[Groove]] changes that might occur upon returning to the [[Dal Segno (D.S.)]] or entering the [[Coda (Song Section)]].
