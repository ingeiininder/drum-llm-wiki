# D.S. al Coda (Repeats are Good)
The directive "D.S. al Coda (repeats are good)" is a specific instruction for navigating a [[D.S. al Coda]] when [[First Ending]]s and [[Second Ending]]s (or other repeat signs) are present.

*   **Meaning**: When you encounter the "D.S. al Coda" directive, you return to the "segno" sign. On this return, you are instructed to play *everything as written*, including observing all repeat signs, [[First Ending]]s, and then proceeding to the [[Second Ending]] before jumping to the Coda.
*   **Execution**:
    1.  Play through the music until you reach the "D.S. al Coda" instruction.
    2.  Go back to the "segno" sign.
    3.  Play the section again, observing the [[First Ending]] on the first pass of the repeated section.
    4.  Repeat back to the beginning of the repeated section (if indicated).
    5.  On the subsequent pass, skip the [[First Ending]] and proceed to the [[Second Ending]].
    6.  Continue playing until you reach the "To Coda" sign, then jump to the Coda section.

**Pro-Tip**: This instruction ensures that the full musical structure, including all repetitions, is honored on the return. It's crucial to mentally map out the navigation path before playing, especially in complex arrangements.
