# [[Jazz|Jazz]] Style Triplet Independence

This study focuses on developing [[Coordinated_Independence]] using eighth-note triplet [[Phrasing|phrasing]], which is the foundation of the "swing" feel in [[Jazz|jazz]].

### The Four-Level System
The curriculum utilizes a progressive learning structure:
- **Levels 1-4**: Pre-written exercises of increasing complexity involving all four limbs.
- **Level 5 (Creative)**: The student must compose their own triplet-based independence phrases. This is a graded component of the final exam.

### Key Components
- **Ride Pattern**: Often involves the standard "spang-a-lang" [[Jazz|jazz]] rhythm or variations thereof.
- **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: Strictly maintained on beats 2 and 4.
- **Snare/Bass Comping**: Triplet-based [[Accents|accents]] distributed between the snare and [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]].
- **32nd Note Variations**: Advanced levels (Unit 10) introduce 32nd note "doubles" on the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] (the "e" and "&" of the triplet) to add an "exciting edge" to the [[Phrasing|phrasing]].

### Technical Focus
- **Articulation**: Precise execution of the triplet grid is essential. This concentration directly improves [[Jazz_Phrasing]].
- **No Flamming**: All voices (hands and feet) must be perfectly aligned.
- **Rephrasing**: Exercises often involve taking a triplet phrase and re-notating/re-feeling it as 16th or 32nd notes to expand the player's rhythmic vocabulary.

### Pro-Tips
- **Notation Skills**: Developing a high skill level in rhythmic notation is just as important as the physical execution for the Level 5 requirements.
- **Slow [[Tempo|Tempo]] Investment**: Keep tempos between 60-80 BPM initially to ensure the "internal clock" is perfectly aligned with the triplet subdivisions.
