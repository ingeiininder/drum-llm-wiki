# Three-Stroke Ruff
The [[Three-Stroke Ruff]] is a drum rudiment belonging to the "Drag" or "Ruff" family. It consists of two grace notes (drags) preceding a primary stroke. It is mentioned as a rudiment in Unit 4 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
rrL (or LLr for Left Hand Lead)

**Breakdown:**
*   **rr:** Two quiet, quick grace notes (drags) played with the right hand. These are typically [[Double_Strokes]]s or very quick bounces.
*   **L:** A louder, primary stroke played with the left hand.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Embellishment:** Ruffs are used to embellish single strokes, adding a "fuzzy" or "dragged" sound.
*   **Dynamic Contrast:** The grace notes are soft, while the primary note is accented, creating dynamic contrast.
*   **Rhythmic Interest:** Adds texture and rhythmic complexity to patterns.

**Pro-Tip:** The key to a good [[Three-Stroke Ruff]] is to make the grace notes very quiet, close together, and immediately preceding the main stroke. The grace notes should sound like a "buzz" or "drag" rather than distinct individual notes. Practice slowly to ensure the grace notes are consistent and then gradually increase tempo, maintaining the dynamic contrast.
