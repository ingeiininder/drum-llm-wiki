# Multiple Bounce Stroke
The **Multiple Bounce Stroke** is the building block of the [[Closed Roll]]. Unlike a [[Double Stroke Roll]], where the drummer plays two distinct notes, the multiple bounce stroke aims for an indeterminate number of rebounds.

### Mechanics
- **The Push**: The stick is pushed slightly into the head to initiate the "buzz."
- **Fulcrum Control**: The pivot point (usually between the thumb and first or second finger) acts as a regulator for the speed and duration of the bounces.
- **Relaxation**: The wrist must remain supple to allow the stick to vibrate freely.

### Conceptualizing Density
A helpful way to conceptualize a high-quality bounce is to try and produce **32nd-note sextuplets** with every single stroke. While the notes aren't counted individually, aiming for that level of density helps in achieving a smooth texture.

### Related Techniques
- [[Press Roll]]: Often used in orchestral or traditional jazz contexts.
- [[Fulcrum]]: The essential anatomical component for controlling bounce density.
