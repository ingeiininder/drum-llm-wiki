# Counting Odd Meters

Accurate counting is the foundation of performing in **[[Odd Meter]]**. Because these signatures are asymmetrical, the counting method often changes based on tempo and musical context.

### Basic Method: Sequential Counting
For beginners, it is essential to count every beat number in the measure to solidify the mathematical structure.
*   **5/8**: Count "1, 2, 3, 4, 5"
*   **7/8**: Count "1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, Sev" (Note: Use **"Sev"** for 7 to keep it monosyllabic).

### Advanced Method: Grouped Counting
As tempos increase, counting every beat becomes physically difficult. Drummers then switch to counting the **[[Asymmetrical Groupings]]**.
*   **5/8 (3+2)**: Count "1 - 2 - 3, 1 - 2" or "1, 2" (where 1 is long and 2 is short).
*   **7/8 (2+2+3)**: Count "1 - 2, 1 - 2, 1 - 2 - 3" or "1, 2, 3" (short, short, long).

### Factors Influencing Counting Choice
1.  **Tempo**: Faster tempos require grouped counting.
2.  **Instrumentation**: If the bass player is playing a specific riff, your counting should mirror their phrasing.
3.  **Notation**: How the notes are beamed in the chart often dictates the intended grouping.

### Pro-Tip: Verbalization
Always practice counting out loud while playing. This bridges the gap between the intellectual understanding of the meter and the physical coordination required to play the **[[Groove]]**.
