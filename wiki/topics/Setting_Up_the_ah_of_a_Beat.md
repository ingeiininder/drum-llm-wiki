# Setting Up the "ah" of a Beat

Ensemble figures landing on the "ah" of a beat (the fourth 16th note) are technically referred to as **"pushes."** This terminology stems from the feeling that the figure is "pushing" into the following downbeat, making it feel as though the next beat arrives early.

### The Target Area
Unlike figures on the "e," which target a single downbeat, figures on the "ah" target the **three 16th notes prior** (the downbeat, the "e," and the "&"). 

### Set-up Fill Requirements
To prevent the ensemble from rushing the "ah," the drummer should provide a three-note 16th-note sequence leading directly into the figure.
*   **Standard Set-up**: If the figure is on the "ah" of beat 1, the fill should consist of 1-e-&.
*   **Extended Set-up**: Fills can be expanded to start earlier (e.g., starting on the beat prior) to provide more momentum.

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Rushing**: Because the "ah" is so close to the following downbeat, there is a high risk of the ensemble "tripping" into the next measure. The three-note set-up fill is critical for stabilizing the tempo.
*   **Weak Subdivision**: If the 16th notes of the fill are not even, the "ah" will feel displaced. Focus on "grid-accurate" [[Subdivision]].

**Pro-Tip**: Think of the "ah" as the resolution of a four-note 16th-note cell where the first three notes are played by you and the fourth is played by the whole band.
