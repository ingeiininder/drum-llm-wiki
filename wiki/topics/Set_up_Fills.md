# Set-up Fills

A **Set-up Fill** is a short rhythmic statement played by the drummer immediately preceding an [[Ensemble_Figure_Interpretation|Ensemble Figure]]. Its primary purpose is to "telegraph" the upcoming rhythm to the rest of the band, ensuring a unified execution.

### Mechanics of a Set-up
A set-up fill must conclude exactly before the ensemble figure begins. The fill typically starts on a designated [[Target Beats|Target Beat]].

- **Simple Set-up**: A basic rhythmic lead-in (e.g., 8th notes on the snare).
- **Complex Set-up**: A more syncopated or melodic fill that still respects the target beat.

### General Rules for Length
The length of the fill often corresponds to the length of the note being set up:
- **Quarter Notes**: Typically require a one-beat set-up.
- **Half Notes**: Typically require a two-beat set-up.
- **Whole Notes**: Typically require a four-beat set-up.

### Pro-Tips
- **Orchestration**: A common professional technique is to use the [[Snare Drum]] for the fill and the [[Crash Cymbal]] supported by the [[Bass Drum]] for the actual ensemble figure.
- **Volume**: The set-up should be loud enough to be heard by the ensemble but should not overshadow the figure itself.
- **The "Lead-in"**: Think of the set-up as a physical breath. It prepares the band to "exhale" the ensemble figure together.
