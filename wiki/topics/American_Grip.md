# American Grip
[[American_Grip]] describes the angle at which the hands are positioned in a resting posture while holding the [[Drumstick]]. It is one of the fundamental grip types in drumming, alongside [[German_Grip]] and [[French_Grip]].

**Key Characteristics:**
1.  **Thumb Angle:** The thumbs should be positioned at approximately a 45-degree angle upward.
2.  **Stick Angle:** The sticks should point at a slight angle down toward the drum.
3.  **Purpose:** This hand and stick positioning is crucial for facilitating [[Tap_Stroke]] and [[Up_Stroke]] applications, allowing for dynamic control and efficient movement.

**Relationship to [[Fulcrum|Fulcrum]]:**
The [[American_Grip]] works in conjunction with a properly established [[Fulcrum_(Drumming)]] to provide a balanced and versatile foundation for various drumming techniques.

**Pro-Tip:** Maintaining the correct angles in [[American_Grip]] helps prevent tension and allows for a natural range of motion, which is essential for developing speed, power, and dynamic subtlety. Regularly check your grip in a mirror to ensure consistency.
