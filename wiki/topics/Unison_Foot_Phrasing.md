# Unison Foot [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]

**Unison Foot [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]** is an advanced drumming technique where the bass drums (typically via [[Double_Pedal]]) replicate the exact rhythmic subdivisions of a melodic instrument, such as a guitar or bass, while the hands maintain a steady time-keeping pattern.

### Application in "Dog"
In the composition [[Dog_Composition]], the drummer is required to play the guitar solo's rhythmic melody on the kick drums. This creates a "thickened" rhythmic texture where the percussion and melody are locked in a singular rhythmic identity.

### Technical Requirements
1.  **Independence**: The ability to decouple the feet from the hands. The hands must remain "relaxed" and "straight" to guide the band, while the feet execute complex, syncopated bursts.
2.  **Transcription/Reading**: Often requires the drummer to read a melodic [[Chart|chart]] or "lead sheet" rather than a standard drum part.
3.  **Orchestration**: Occasionally, these unison parts can be moved from the kick drums to the [[Tom-Toms]] (e.g., using 8th-note or quarter-note [[Triplets|triplets]]) to add dynamic variety.

### Pro-Tips
*   **The "Blasé" Feel**: As noted by Conrad Schrenk, the goal is to play these difficult phrases in a relaxed manner. If the drummer looks or sounds like they are struggling, the "[[Groove|groove]]" of the solo section will be lost.
