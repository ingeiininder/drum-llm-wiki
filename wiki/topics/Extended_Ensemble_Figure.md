# Extended Ensemble Figure
An "Extended Ensemble Figure" refers to a musical passage where multiple instruments in an ensemble play a synchronized, often rhythmically complex, figure together for a sustained [[Duration|duration]]. These figures are typically written out precisely in the notation and require tight coordination among musicians.

In this curriculum, an extended ensemble figure is noted within the five-[[Measure|measure]] [[Bridge_(Song_Section)]] section of the [[Coda_(Song_Section)]]. This suggests a moment where the drums, along with other instruments, play a specific, pre-arranged rhythmic pattern.

**Pro-Tip:** When encountering [[Ensemble_Figures|ensemble figures]], precision is paramount. Practice these sections slowly with a [[Metronome|metronome]], ensuring your part locks perfectly with the rest of the band. Listen intently to the other instruments to ensure rhythmic alignment and dynamic balance. These moments are crucial for creating impact and demonstrating the band's tightness.
