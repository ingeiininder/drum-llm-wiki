# Single Paradiddle
The [[Single Paradiddle]] is one of the most fundamental and widely used drum rudiments. It is a four-note pattern consisting of two [[Single Stroke]]s and one [[Double Stroke Technique]], with the sticking alternating between hands. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 8 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking:**
RLRR LRLL

**Breakdown:**
*   **R L R R:** Right, Left, Right, Right
*   **L R L L:** Left, Right, Left, Left

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Alternating Lead:** The pattern naturally alternates the leading hand every four notes, making it excellent for developing ambidexterity.
*   **Single-Double Combination:** The combination of single and double strokes makes it highly versatile for creating grooves and fills.
*   **Natural Flow:** When played correctly, the paradiddle has a natural, flowing feel due to the rebound of the double strokes.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Stick Control:** Develops precision, evenness, and control between single and double strokes.
*   **Coordination:** Improves coordination between hands and can be easily orchestrated around the [[Drum Kit]].
*   **Grooves and Fills:** Forms the basis for countless drum grooves and fills in various musical styles. Its alternating nature makes it particularly useful for moving around the kit.
*   **Foundation for Other Paradiddles:** It is the building block for more complex paradiddle variations like the [[Double Paradiddle]], [[Triple Paradiddle]], and [[Paradiddle-diddle]].

**Pro-Tip:** Practice the [[Single Paradiddle]] slowly with a metronome, focusing on making all notes even in volume and spacing. Pay close attention to the [[Double Stroke Technique]] part (RR or LL) to ensure the two notes are distinct and controlled, not rushed. Once comfortable, experiment with orchestrating it around the [[Drum Kit]] to create musical patterns.
