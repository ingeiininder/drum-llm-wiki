# Single [[Paradiddle|Paradiddle]]

The **Single [[Paradiddle|Paradiddle]]** is one of the most fundamental and versatile [[Rudiments]] in drumming. It combines single strokes and [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]] into a single four-note pattern.

## Sticking Pattern
The pattern is most commonly practiced as:
- **R - L - R - R**
- **L - R - L - L**

## Characteristics and Benefits
- **Hand Switching**: The beauty of the [[Paradiddle|paradiddle]] is that it naturally switches the lead hand after each four-note grouping.
- **[[Dynamics|Dynamics]]**: It is often practiced with an accent on the first note of each grouping (**R**-l-r-r **L**-r-l-l).
- **Orchestration**: On the drum set, paradiddles are used to move between different playing surfaces (e.g., [[Accents|accents]] on cymbals/toms, unaccented notes on the snare).

## Pro-Tip
Focus on the "double" (the -rr or -ll). Ensure the volume and timing of the [[Double_Strokes|double strokes]] match the single strokes for a perfectly even roll.
