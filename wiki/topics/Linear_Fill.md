# Linear [[Fill|Fill]]

A **Linear [[Fill|Fill]]** is a drum set technique where no two notes are played simultaneously. Every stroke (hands or feet) occupies its own rhythmic space, creating a continuous stream of single notes.

### Technical Requirements
- **Coordination**: Requires high independence between the hands and the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]].
- **Dynamic Contrast**: To make linear fills musical, one must exaggerate the difference between **accented** notes and **[[Ghost_Notes|ghost notes]]**.
- **Note Placement**: Evenness is paramount. Any "flamming" or uneven gaps between notes will destroy the linear effect.

### Practice Strategy
1. **Isolate the Pattern**: Practice the sticking/kicking pattern slowly without a [[Metronome|metronome]] to build muscle memory.
2. **Subdivide**: Use a 16th-note or triplet grid to ensure every note is perfectly placed.
3. **Orchestration**: Move the pattern around the kit (Snare to Toms to Cymbals) while keeping the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] consistent.

### Professional Context
Linear fills are staples in [[Dubstep_Drumming]], Gospel Chops, and Modern Fusion because they cut through dense mixes without the "clutter" of simultaneous cymbal crashes and snare hits.
