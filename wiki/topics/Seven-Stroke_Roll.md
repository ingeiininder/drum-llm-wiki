# Seven-Stroke Roll
The [[Seven-Stroke Roll]] is a drum rudiment belonging to the [[Double Stroke Roll]] family. It consists of three [[Double_Strokes]]s followed by a single accented stroke, totaling seven notes. It is mentioned as a key topic in Unit 7 of "[[Drum Technique 1 (MI Curriculum Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
RRLLRR L (or LLRRLL R for Left Hand Lead)

**Breakdown:**
*   **RR:** Two strokes with the right hand.
*   **LL:** Two strokes with the left hand.
*   **RR:** Two strokes with the right hand.
*   **L:** A single, accented stroke with the left hand, which typically marks the end of the roll.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Roll Development:** Further develops control over the [[Double Stroke Technique]] and the ability to sustain a roll for a longer duration.
*   **Fills and Embellishments:** Used for longer, more flowing drum fills and to create a sustained "buzz" or "press" roll effect.
*   **Dynamic Control:** Allows for nuanced dynamic shaping, with the initial double strokes often softer and the final single stroke accented.

**Pro-Tip:** The challenge with the [[Seven-Stroke Roll]] is maintaining evenness and consistency across all six initial double strokes before the final accent. Focus on a relaxed approach to the [[Double Stroke Technique]] for each pair, allowing for natural rebound. Practice slowly with a metronome to ensure precision and a smooth transition into the final accented note.
