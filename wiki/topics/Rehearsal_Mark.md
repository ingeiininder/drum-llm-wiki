# Rehearsal Mark
A "Rehearsal Mark" is a letter (e.g., A, B, C) or sometimes a number, placed prominently in sheet music to designate specific sections of a song. These marks serve as essential navigational tools, allowing musicians to quickly locate and refer to particular parts of a piece during rehearsals, recording sessions, and live performances.

Rehearsal marks often correspond to significant changes in [[Song_Structure]], such as the beginning of a [[Verse_(Song_Section)]], [[Pre-Chorus]], [[Chorus_(Song_Section)]], or [[Bridge_(Song_Section)]]. Sometimes, a [[Measure|measure]] number is added to the letter (e.g., [[A9_(Rehearsal_Mark)]]) for even more precise identification.

**Pro-Tip:** Always scan your [[Chart|chart]] for rehearsal marks before playing. They are your roadmap. During rehearsals, listen for the bandleader or conductor calling out these marks. Being able to jump instantly to the correct section is a hallmark of a professional musician.
