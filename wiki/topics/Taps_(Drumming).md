# Taps (Drumming)
In drumming, "taps" refer to unaccented notes, which are played at a softer dynamic level compared to [[Accents_(Drumming)]]. They are typically executed with a lower [[Stick_Height|stick height]], generally one to two inches above the drumhead.

## Purpose
*   **Dynamic Contrast**: Taps provide the softer dynamic counterpoint to [[Accents|accents]], creating a varied and expressive rhythmic texture.
*   **[[Fill|Fill]]-in Notes**: They are used to [[Fill|fill]] in the spaces between [[Accents|accents]], maintaining rhythmic continuity without overpowering the emphasized notes.
*   **Control and Precision**: Practicing taps helps develop fine motor control and precision in stick placement and [[Dynamics|dynamics]].

## Execution
Taps are usually played with a minimal amount of force and a low [[Stick_Height|stick height]]. The goal is to produce a consistent, soft sound that blends into the background while allowing accented notes to project.

## Relationship with Moeller Technique
The [[Moeller_Technique]] is highly effective for integrating taps into complex patterns. The [[Moeller_Up_Stroke]] is essentially a tap stroke that efficiently sets up an upcoming accent, demonstrating the seamless interplay between soft and loud [[Dynamics|dynamics]] within the Moeller system.

## Pro-Tips
*   **Consistent Height**: Maintain a consistent [[Stick_Height|stick height]] (1-2 inches) for all taps to ensure an even dynamic level.
*   **Relaxation**: Keep your grip and wrist relaxed to allow for a light touch and natural rebound.
*   **Listen**: Pay close attention to the sound of your taps. They should be clear and consistent, not muffled or weak.
*   **Dynamic Gap**: Focus on creating a clear dynamic gap between your taps and your [[Accents|accents]]. This is crucial for expressive drumming.
