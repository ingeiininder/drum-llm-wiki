# Comparison: 6/8 vs. 3/4 Time

While both **6/8** and **3/4** time signatures contain six [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] per [[Measure|measure]], they are distinguished by their mathematical grouping and musical "feel."

### Structural Differences
| Feature | 3/4 Time | 6/8 Time |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Grouping** | 3 groups of 2 [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] | 2 groups of 3 [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] |
| **Strong Beats** | 1, 2, 3 | 1, 4 |
| **Counting** | 1 & 2 & 3 & | 1 2 3 4 5 6 |
| **Feel** | Simple Triple (Waltz) | Compound Duple (Folk/[[Blues|Blues]]) |

### Musical Context
- **3/4 Application**: Most commonly found in Country Waltzes and classical Minuets.
- **6/8 Application**: Found in Celtic folk tunes, marches, and African-influenced rhythms.

### Pro-Tip
To identify the difference by ear, listen to the [[Bass_Drum]] and [[Snare_Drum]]. In 3/4, you often hear a "Boom-Chik-Chik" (1, 2, 3). In 6/8, you hear a "Boom-trip-let, Chik-trip-let" (1, 4).
