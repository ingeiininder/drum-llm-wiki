# Jazz

Jazz is a sophisticated musical genre born at the beginning of the 20th century from the blending of rich African rhythms with European harmony. For drummers, Jazz is a high-level discipline centered on **improvisation**, group interaction, and a unique approach to sound balance.

## Foundational Concepts

- **Improvisation**: Unlike many other genres, jazz relies on the spontaneous creation of musical ideas, both in soloing and "comping."
- **Group Interaction**: Musicians react in real-time to one another's [[Phrasing|phrasing]]. The drummer's role is to support the soloist while engaging in a musical "conversation."
- **Note Interpretation**: In jazz, [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] are almost always interpreted as **[[Swing_Eighth_Notes|Swing Eighth Notes]]** (triplet-based), even when written as straight eighths.

## Structural Terminology

- **The Head**: The thematic melody of a jazz tune. It is conventionally played at the start and end of the performance.
- **Chorus**: A single pass through the song's entire harmonic form (e.g., one full 12-bar blues cycle).
- **Comping**: "Accompanying" or "Complementing." It refers to the snare and [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] patterns played to react to and support a soloist's melody.
- **The Cue**: A physical signal (like patting the top of the head) used to indicate the return to the melody.

## The Jazz Sound Balance

In jazz, the traditional "pop-[[Rock|rock]]" balance is reversed. The priority of volume and presence is as follows:
1.  **Ride Cymbal**: The loudest and most influential voice.
2.  **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: Second in importance, defining the backbeat.
3.  **[[Snare_Drum|Snare Drum]]**: Placed just below the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] for comping.
4.  **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: The softest voice, often "felt but not heard."

## Specialized Techniques

- **Feathering**: Playing the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] on straight [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] at an extremely quiet dynamic to add "heartbeat" and support the walking bass line. 
- **Shouldering the Ride**: Using the shoulder of the stick to strike the ride cymbal's surface for a full, washy sound that "covers" the entire band.
- **Brushes**: Metal or plastic wires used in place of sticks to create a legato, sweeping texture on the drumhead.
- **[[Stick_Shot|Stick Shot]]**: A wood-tone accent created by pressing the bead of one stick into the head and striking it with the other.

## Influential Legends
Traditional jazz drumming concepts are modeled after masters such as:
- **Max Roach**
- **Art Blakey**
- **Philly Joe Jones**
- **Elvin Jones**
