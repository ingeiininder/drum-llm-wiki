# 7/8 Time Signature

The **7/8 time signature** contains seven eighth notes per measure. Like 5/8, it is asymmetrical and relies on internal groupings of 2s and 3s to define its feel.

### Common Groupings
The most frequent rhythmic phrasings for 7/8 are:
1.  **2 + 2 + 3**: Two groups of two eighth notes followed by one group of three.
2.  **3 + 2 + 2**: One group of three eighth notes followed by two groups of two.
3.  **2 + 3 + 2**: (Less common but used) A group of three sandwiched between two groups of two.

### Counting and Phonetics
When counting 7/8, the eighth note receives the beat. 
*   **The "Sev" Rule**: To maintain a consistent rhythmic pulse, the number seven should be verbalized as a single syllable: **"Sev"**. This prevents the extra syllable in "sev-en" from disrupting the timing.
*   **Full Count**: 1 - 2 - 3 - 4 - 5 - 6 - Sev.
*   **Sixteenth Notes**: Counted as 1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 & Sev &.

### Groove Development
In **[[7/8 Grooves]]**, the drummer must navigate the "extra" or "missing" eighth note compared to a standard 4/4 (8/8) feel. 
*   **Template**: Use a 2-2-3 grouping as a default unless the chart specifies otherwise.
*   **Orchestration**: Often, the ride cymbal or hi-hat will play all seven eighth notes, while the bass drum and snare highlight the dominant pulses of the 2s and 3s.
