# 12-Bar Blues Structure
The "12-bar blues" is a fundamental and widely recognized musical form, referring to a specific number of measures used to express the theme of a typical [[Blues]] song.

**Structure Breakdown**:
*   **Measures**: Consists of 12 musical bars.
*   **Segments**: Divided into three four-bar segments.
*   **Chord Progression**: A standard blues progression typically features three chords, based on the first (I), fourth (IV), and fifth notes of an eight-note scale. This is known as the [[I-IV-V Chord Progression]].
    *   **First Four Bars**: Dominated by the I chord (e.g., B7).
    *   **Second Four Bars**: Typically features the IV chord (e.g., E7).
    *   **Third Four Bars**: Features the V chord (e.g., F#7) and often returns to the I chord.

**Key Insight**: While broken into four-bar phrases outlining the I-IV-V chord structure, it's crucial to understand that you are ultimately dealing with a single, cohesive 12-bar phrase that repeats.

**Pro-Tip**: Internalize the sound and feel of the 12-bar progression. Practice playing simple [[Grooves]] over the chord changes, focusing on how the drum part supports the harmonic movement. This will help you anticipate changes and play more musically.
