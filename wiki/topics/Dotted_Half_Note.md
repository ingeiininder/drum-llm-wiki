# Dotted Half Note
A **dotted half note** is a [[Half Note]] with a dot placed immediately to its right. This dot increases the [[Duration]] of the half note by one-half of its original value.

**Value Calculation:**
*   A [[Half Note]] is typically worth 2 [[Beat]]s (in [[4/4 Time Signature]]).
*   Half of a half note's value is 1 beat (equivalent to a [[Quarter Note]]).
*   Therefore, a dotted half note is worth 2 + 1 = **3 beats**.

**Implication for Rhythmic Placement:**
In 4/4 time, if a dotted half note starts on beat 1, it will sustain through beats 1, 2, and 3. The next available place for a note or rest would then be on beat 4.

**Pro-Tip:** While a dotted half note on beat 1 technically "obscures" beat 3 (a violation of the strict [[2/3 Rule]]), it is a commonly accepted notation in many musical contexts. Drummers should be prepared to read and interpret this figure accurately, feeling the full three-beat duration. When in doubt, always prioritize the musical flow and the intended duration.
