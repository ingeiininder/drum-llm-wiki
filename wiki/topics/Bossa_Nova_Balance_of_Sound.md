# Bossa Nova Balance of Sound

Achieving the correct "internal mix" is critical for a stylistically authentic [[Bossa_Nova]] sound. The hierarchy of volume differs significantly from [[Rock|rock]] or pop.

### The Volume Hierarchy (Loudest to Softest)
1.  **[[Ride_Cymbal|Ride Cymbal]] / [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: The primary voice, providing the rhythmic [[Pulse|pulse]].
2.  **[[Snare_Drum|Snare Drum]] / Cross-Stick**: Equal to or slightly below the cymbal volume.
3.  **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: The softest voice in the mix.

### Dynamic Comparison
While [[Jazz_Drumming]] also features a soft [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] ("feathering"), the Bossa Nova balance is even more compressed. The overall dynamic is usually **mezzo-piano (mp)**. 

### Implementation
*   **Cymbal Articulation**: Use the "shouldering" technique to create a full, washy sound that controls overtones.
*   **Cross-Stick**: Ensure the click is woody and consistent, cutting through the cymbal wash without being abrasive.
