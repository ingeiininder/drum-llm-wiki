# [[Groove|Groove]] #2 Syncopated Shuffle
"[[Groove|Groove]] #2" is described as a "[[Syncopated]]" [[Shuffle]] [[Groove|groove]]. Unlike the more foundational [[Groove_#1_Four_on_the_Floor_Shuffle]], this pattern introduces rhythmic displacements, particularly in the [[Bass_Drum]] and [[Snare_Drum]] parts, to create a more intricate and dynamic feel.

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **[[Syncopation|Syncopation]]:** Rhythmic [[Accents|accents]] fall on off-beats or unexpected parts of the [[Measure|measure]], creating a push-and-pull feel against the underlying [[Shuffle]] [[Pulse|pulse]].
*   **Interplay:** The [[Bass_Drum]], [[Snare_Drum]], and [[Hi-Hat]] parts are likely to be more independent, requiring greater coordination.
*   **[[Shuffle_Feel|Shuffle Feel]]:** Despite the [[Syncopation|syncopation]], the inherent [[Swing]] or triplet-based [[Shuffle]] feel must be maintained.

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing [[Syncopated]] grooves, [[Subdividing]] is crucial. Count or sing the smallest [[Subdivision|subdivision]] (e.g., [[Triplets|triplets]] for a [[Shuffle]]) to ensure accurate placement of the syncopated notes. Start slowly with a [[Metronome]] and gradually increase [[Tempo|tempo]]. Focus on the "space" between the notes as much as the notes themselves to truly capture the syncopated feel. This [[Groove|groove]] will add significant character to a [[Shuffle_12-Bar_Blues_Play-Along_Unit_7_Ex_7.11]].
