# Groove #2 Syncopated Shuffle
"Groove #2" is described as a "[[Syncopated]]" [[Shuffle]] groove. Unlike the more foundational [[Groove_#1_Four_on_the_Floor_Shuffle]], this pattern introduces rhythmic displacements, particularly in the [[Bass Drum]] and [[Snare Drum]] parts, to create a more intricate and dynamic feel.

**Key Characteristics:**
*   **Syncopation:** Rhythmic accents fall on off-beats or unexpected parts of the measure, creating a push-and-pull feel against the underlying [[Shuffle]] pulse.
*   **Interplay:** The [[Bass Drum]], [[Snare Drum]], and [[Hi-Hat]] parts are likely to be more independent, requiring greater coordination.
*   **Shuffle Feel:** Despite the syncopation, the inherent [[Swing]] or triplet-based [[Shuffle]] feel must be maintained.

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing [[Syncopated]] grooves, [[Subdividing]] is crucial. Count or sing the smallest subdivision (e.g., triplets for a [[Shuffle]]) to ensure accurate placement of the syncopated notes. Start slowly with a [[Metronome]] and gradually increase tempo. Focus on the "space" between the notes as much as the notes themselves to truly capture the syncopated feel. This groove will add significant character to a [[Shuffle_12-Bar_Blues_Play-Along_Unit_7_Ex_7.11]].
