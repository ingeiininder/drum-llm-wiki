# Stick Shot
The stick shot is a specialized [[Jazz|jazz]] articulation used for sharp, wood-toned [[Accents|accents]] during solos or transition fills. It produces a dry, high-pitched "crack" that cuts through the ensemble.

### Execution
1.  **Setup**: Place the tip of the left stick against the drumhead at a 45-degree angle.
2.  **Pressure**: Press the bead firmly into the head to mute the resonance and prevent buzzing.
3.  **The Strike**: Strike the shaft of the stationary left stick with the right stick.

### Usage
*   Commonly associated with the solo styles of **Philly Joe Jones** and **Max Roach**.
*   Used to mark the end of a phrase or to provide a "punctuation mark" in a 12-bar [[Blues|blues]] cycle.
