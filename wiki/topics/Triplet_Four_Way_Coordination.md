# Triplet Four-Way Coordination
This concept focuses on developing independence between all four limbs using an **eighth-note triplet** foundation.

### The Foundation
- **Hands**: Play continuous eighth-note triplets on the snare drum using alternating sticking (R L R L R L).
- **Feet**: Apply [[Bass Drum]] permutations against various [[Hi-Hat Ostinatos]].

### Hi-Hat Ostinatos
- **Ostinato A**: Quarter notes (1, 2, 3, 4).
- **Ostinato B**: The "&" of every beat (upbeats).
- **Ostinato C**: "Down-up" motion or eighth-note patterns.

### Advanced Application: Accented Snare (Unit 7)
Once the basic coordination is mastered, apply accents to the snare drum triplets (e.g., accenting the first, second, or third partial of the triplet).
- **Goal**: Achieve perfect **unison placement** between the accented snare notes and the bass drum hits. 
- **Warning**: Avoid "flamming" between the hands and feet.

### Pro-Tip
Use "three-part counting" (1-trip-let, 2-trip-let) out loud while playing to ensure the subdivisions remain accurate.
