# Rebound Stroke
The [[Rebound_Stroke]] refers to the natural phenomenon where a [[Drumstick]] bounces back after striking a drumhead or practice pad due to the elasticity of the stick and the playing surface. It is a fundamental concept in drumming technique, particularly central to the [[Gladstone_Technique]] and [[Free_Stroke]].

**Key Principles:**
*   **Natural Physics:** Drummers leverage the natural physics of rebound to minimize muscular effort. Instead of actively lifting the stick after each stroke, the stick is allowed to bounce back.
*   **Efficiency:** Utilizing rebound conserves energy, allowing for greater speed, endurance, and fluidity in playing.
*   **Control:** While the rebound is natural, controlling its height and timing is a key aspect of [[Stick_Control]] and dynamic articulation.
*   **Relationship to Grip:** A relaxed [[Fulcrum_(Drumming)]] and grip are essential for allowing the stick to rebound freely. Any tension or squeezing will inhibit the natural bounce.

**Applications:**
*   **[[Free_Stroke]] / [[Gladstone_Technique]]:** These techniques are built entirely around maximizing and controlling the natural rebound.
*   **Rolls:** Many drum rolls, such as the [[Double_Stroke_Technique]] and various [[Drum_Roll]] [[Rudiments|rudiments]], rely heavily on controlled rebound to produce multiple strokes with minimal effort.
*   **Dynamic Control:** The height of the rebound can be manipulated to achieve different [[Dynamics|dynamics]]. A higher rebound typically results in a louder stroke, while a lower rebound is used for softer, faster passages.

**Pro-Tip:** To truly understand the [[Rebound_Stroke]], practice isolation exercises where you simply drop the stick and allow it to bounce back into your hand without any active lifting. This helps to internalize the feeling of the stick's natural motion and reduces unnecessary muscular tension.
