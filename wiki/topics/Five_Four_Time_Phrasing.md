# 5/4 Time Signature Phrasing

Playing in **5/4 Time** requires a departure from standard 4/4 groupings. Phrasing within the bar is critical to maintaining the "groove" without losing the listener.

### Common Phrasing
- **The 3+2 Split**: A common way to phrase 5/4 is two dotted quarter notes (3 eighths) followed by two quarter notes (2+2 eighths).
- **Polyrhythmic Overlays**: A sophisticated technique involves playing a 4/4 [[Ride Cymbal]] pattern (alternating bell and body) over a 5/4 [[Bass Drum]] and [[Snare Drum]] pattern. This creates a shifting relationship between the cymbal and the ground beat.

### The Coda Section
In the context of the [[Musicians Institute]] curriculum, the Coda often features:
- **Creative Fills**: Fills that respect the 5/4 internal phrasing.
- **Ride Cymbal Morphing**: Transitioning from a 4/4 ride pattern to a 5/4 bell pattern that accents every quarter note, while the bottom limbs maintain a rock groove.

### Pro-Tips
- **Counting**: Always count "1, 2, 3, 4, 5" explicitly when learning these sections to avoid falling back into a "familiar" 4/4 feel.
- **Ensemble Figures**: Watch for figures that end on beat 5, which can be jarring if the drummer is used to resolving on beat 1.
