# 5/4 [[Time_Signature|Time Signature]] [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]

Playing in **5/4 Time** requires a departure from standard 4/4 groupings. [[Phrasing|Phrasing]] within the bar is critical to maintaining the "[[Groove|groove]]" without losing the listener.

### Common [[Phrasing|Phrasing]]
- **The 3+2 Split**: A common way to phrase 5/4 is two dotted [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] (3 eighths) followed by two [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] (2+2 eighths).
- **Polyrhythmic Overlays**: A sophisticated technique involves playing a 4/4 [[Ride_Cymbal]] pattern (alternating bell and body) over a 5/4 [[Bass_Drum]] and [[Snare_Drum]] pattern. This creates a shifting relationship between the cymbal and the ground [[Beat|beat]].

### The Coda Section
In the context of the [[Musicians_Institute]] curriculum, the Coda often features:
- **Creative Fills**: Fills that respect the 5/4 internal [[Phrasing|phrasing]].
- **Ride Cymbal Morphing**: Transitioning from a 4/4 ride pattern to a 5/4 bell pattern that [[Accents|accents]] every quarter note, while the bottom limbs maintain a [[Rock|rock]] [[Groove|groove]].

### Pro-Tips
- **Counting**: Always count "1, 2, 3, 4, 5" explicitly when learning these sections to avoid falling back into a "familiar" 4/4 feel.
- **[[Ensemble_Figures|Ensemble Figures]]**: Watch for figures that end on [[Beat|beat]] 5, which can be jarring if the drummer is used to resolving on [[Beat|beat]] 1.
