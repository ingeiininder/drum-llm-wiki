# Bad to the Bone: Song Study

"Bad to the Bone" is a study in [[Syncopation]] and [[Horn_Comping]]. The track is driven by a busy, syncopated bass guitar line in the A section that requires the drummer to establish a solid [[Pocket]] while navigating complex rhythmic figures.

### Performance Key Points
*   **Bass/Drum Relationship**: The bass line is highly active. In the initial pass, maintain a simple [[Backbeat]] (Kick on 1 & 3, Snare on 2 & 4) to ensure the groove is locked before attempting variations.
*   **Horn Figures**: The arrangement features prominent horn parts. Drummers should "comp" (accompany/complement) these figures. Specifically, listen for the staccato accented horn shot in the 4th bar of section B1.
*   **Dynamic Control**: The song concludes with a gradual decrescendo. Mastering the [[Fade_Out]] requires consistent time-keeping while reducing velocity.

### Technical Tips
*   **Pick-ups**: Allow the horns to play the opening pick-up line solo to establish the melody.
*   **Bass Lick Comping**: Before section D2, there is a specific bass lick. You can either comp this rhythm or maintain time on the [[Hi-Hat]] to provide a stable foundation.
*   **Fills**: Short, one- or two-beat [[Drum_Fills]] are most effective for this style, ensuring the flow of the groove is not interrupted.

### Groove Variations
The curriculum provides Easy, Intermediate, and Advanced variations. Advanced variations often incorporate [[Splash_Cymbal]] and [[China_Cymbal]] accents to highlight specific horn stabs.
