# R&B Drumming Sound
The classic R&B sound is characterized by a warm, blended texture where no single voice—[[Bass Drum]], [[Snare Drum]], or [[Hi-Hat]]—overpowers the groove. Unlike the aggressive "crack" of modern rock, R&B requires a balanced "player's mix" that supports the vocal and bass line.

### Voice Specifications
*   **Snare Drum**: Played primarily in the center of the head for a fat, dry tone.
*   **Bass Drum**: Played "off the head" (allowing the beater to rebound) to create an open, resonant sound that blends with the upright or electric bass.
*   **Hi-Hat**: Kept light and even.

### Sectional Dynamics
*   **Verse/Letter A**: The hi-hat should be very tight and quiet. Use the tip of the stick or the upper shaft just below the tip to achieve a delicate "tick" sound.
*   **Chorus/Letter B**: The hi-hat pressure is slightly released from the pedal to create a broader sound. The snare drum often moves to "all downbeats" (quarter notes) played with consistent velocity, avoiding heavy accents on 2 and 4 to maintain a driving, hypnotic pulse.

> [!TIP]
> To achieve the "upper shaft" sound on the hi-hat, angle your stick slightly so the shoulder grazes the top cymbal while the tip remains in contact. This provides more body than the tip alone without the volume of a full shoulder hit.
