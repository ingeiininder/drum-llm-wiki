# Alternating Hands (Sticking)
Alternating Hands (Sticking) is a fundamental [[Sticking Patterns]] principle where the right (R) and left (L) hands play successive notes in an alternating fashion (R L R L...). This is one of the most basic and widely used sticking approaches in drumming.

**Key Characteristics**:
*   **Efficiency**: For single strokes, alternating hands is generally the most efficient way to play continuous notes, especially at faster tempos.
*   **Evenness**: Promotes evenness between the hands, as each hand gets an equal opportunity to play.
*   **Foundation**: It forms the basis for many other [[Sticking_Techniques_General]], including [[Assigned Sticking]] for [[16th Note]]s.

**Applications**:
*   **Continuous [[16th Note]]s**: The most common application, where each 16th note is played with an alternating hand (1=R, e=L, &=R, a=L).
*   **[[Eighth Note]]s**: Similarly, alternating hands are used for continuous eighth notes (1=R, &=L, 2=R, &=L).
*   **[[Double_Strokes_Alternating_Method]]**: While double strokes involve two notes per hand, the *lead* hand can alternate (RRL LRR LL), providing a similar benefit of preparation.

**Pro-Tip**: Develop strong, even strokes with both hands when practicing alternating patterns. A common pitfall is having one hand stronger or faster than the other. Use a mirror or video recording to observe your technique and ensure symmetrical motion and sound quality. This foundational skill is critical for developing speed and control across the drum set.
