# Tenuto
**Tenuto** (Italian for "to hold") is an articulation marking that instructs the drummer to play a note for its full rhythmic duration.

### Notation
Represented by a horizontal line (—) placed above or below the note head.

### Application on Drum Set
*   **Cymbals**: Let the cymbal ring for the entire value of the note. Do not choke.
*   **Drums**: Play with a "full" stroke, allowing the drum to resonate naturally.
*   **Legato Phrasing**: When applied to a whole phrase, it implies a smooth, connected flow between notes.

### Pro-Tip
While sometimes redundant to the rhythmic notation, a tenuto mark is a clear signal from the arranger that they do *not* want a dry or choked sound.
