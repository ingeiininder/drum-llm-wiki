# The Eighth-Note Rule
In jazz and triplet-based contemporary music, standard notation often fails to capture the intended phrasing. The **Eighth-Note Rule** provides a guideline for interpreting [[Ensemble Figures]].

### The Rule Defined
1.  **Freestanding Eighth Notes**: If you see an eighth note with nothing immediately following it, it is **accented**.
2.  **Strings of Eighth Notes**: In a group of eighth notes, the **last note** of the group is accented. The preceding notes function as "lead-in" tones (often played slightly softer to emphasize the destination).

### Application
When converting a melodic line (like a guitar or horn line) into drum figures:
*   Catch the accents on the crashes or snare.
*   You do not necessarily need to play every written note; focus on the "target" accented notes to support the ensemble's phrasing.
