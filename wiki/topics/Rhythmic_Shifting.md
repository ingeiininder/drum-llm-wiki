# Rhythmic Shifting

**Rhythmic Shifting** is the ability to transition fluidly between different subdivisions of the [[Beat|beat]]. This is often conceptualized as "shifting gears" in a manual transmission vehicle.

### The Gear System
- **2nd Gear**: [[Eighth_Note]] (dividing the [[Beat|beat]] into two).
- **3rd Gear**: [[Triplets]] (dividing the [[Beat|beat]] into three).
- **4th Gear**: [[16th_Notes]] (dividing the [[Beat|beat]] into four).

### Technical Execution
The Musicians Institute curriculum emphasizes **Hand-to-Hand Sticking** (alternating R-L-R-L) regardless of the rhythm. This forces the student to lead different subdivisions with different hands, which is essential for developing a balanced [[Internal_Clock]].

### Training Progression
1. **Two-Bar Shifting**: Provides ample time to settle into the new "gear."
2. **One-Bar Shifting**: Increases the frequency of the transition.
3. **One-[[Beat|Beat]] Shifting**: The most advanced level, requiring the drummer to subdivide the upcoming [[Beat|beat]] while still playing the current one.

### Pro-Tips
- **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]] Pre-hearing**: You must "hear" the new [[Subdivision|subdivision]] in your head one [[Beat|beat]] before you actually play it.
- **[[Metronome|Metronome]] Alignment**: The "downbeat" (the 1, 2, 3, 4) must remain constant. If the [[Tempo|tempo]] of the quarter note changes during the shift, the "gears" are slipping.
