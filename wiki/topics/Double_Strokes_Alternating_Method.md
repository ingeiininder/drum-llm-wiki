# Double Strokes: Alternating Method
The Double Strokes: Alternating Method is an application of [[Double_Strokes]] where the lead hand alternates, rather than a single hand consistently leading. This approach is particularly beneficial for maintaining fluidity and speed, as it allows each hand a brief moment to prepare for its next double stroke.

*   **Concept**: Instead of a continuous RRLL RRLL pattern, the lead hand switches. For example, a common pattern might be RRL LRR LL...
*   **Benefits**:
    *   **Faster Tempos**: The alternating lead can make it easier to execute double strokes cleanly at higher speeds.
    *   **Reduced Fatigue**: By distributing the "lead" responsibility, it can help reduce fatigue in one hand.
    *   **Versatility**: Offers more options for orchestrating patterns around the drum set.

*   **Example**: The [[Five_Stroke_Roll]] (RRLLR or LLRRL) is a classic rudiment that exemplifies the alternating method, as the lead hand changes with each iteration of the roll.

**Pro-Tip**: When practicing this method, focus on the smooth transition between the lead hands. The goal is to make the change imperceptible, maintaining an even flow of notes. This requires strong control over both the initial double stroke and the preparation for the next.
