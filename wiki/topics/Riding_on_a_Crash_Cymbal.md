# Riding on a Crash Cymbal

**Riding on a crash cymbal** is a technique used primarily in high-energy music like [[Hard_Rock_Drumming]] and [[Punk-Pop_Drumming]] to create a "washy," sustained wall of white noise that fills the frequency spectrum.

### Technical Execution
*   **Stick Contact**: Use the **shoulder** of the stick (the area just below the tip).
*   **Angle of Attack**: Strike the edge of the cymbal at approximately a **45-degree angle**. This allows the cymbal to vibrate freely and produce a "wash" rather than a sharp "crash."
*   **Wrist Motion**: The wrist must remain relaxed. Each stroke should be consistent in velocity to maintain an even volume level without unintended [[Accents|accents]].

### Musical Considerations
*   **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]]**: While the hand may be playing [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]], the goal is a "washy" sound with minimum perceived [[Subdivision|subdivision]].
*   **Balance**: The "wash" of the crash cymbal is very loud. It is crucial to ensure this sound does not overpower the [[Snare_Drum]] backbeat or the [[Bass_Drum]] patterns.
*   **Tension Warning**: Playing aggressively on a crash cymbal often leads to physical tension. Periodically check your jaw, hands, and toes to ensure you are not "choking" the sound through body stiffness.
