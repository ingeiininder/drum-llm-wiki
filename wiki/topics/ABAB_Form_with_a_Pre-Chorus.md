# ABAB Form with a Pre-Chorus
This study employs a musical [[Song Form]] similar to the [[ABAB 32 Bar Form]] but is more condensed due to shorter section lengths. It is comprised of four distinct sections, each assigned a rehearsal letter: A, A', B, and Coda. The inclusion of a "Pre-Chorus" (implied as part of the A or A' section leading into B) is a common feature in modern [[Pop]] and [[Country-Pop Genre]] arrangements, building anticipation for the main chorus.

*   **A**: Typically the [[Verse Section (Letter A)]].
*   **A'**: A variation of the verse, often with slight lyrical or musical changes.
*   **B**: The Chorus, the main hook of the song.
*   **Coda**: The outro or ending section.

**Pro-Tip:** Understanding song forms is crucial for drummers to anticipate changes, build dynamics, and place [[Drum Fill]]s effectively. In forms with a pre-chorus, the drumming often builds intensity subtly to propel the listener into the main chorus.
