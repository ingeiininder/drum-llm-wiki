# [[ABAB_Form|ABAB Form]] with a [[Pre-Chorus|Pre-Chorus]]
This study employs a musical [[Song_Form]] similar to the [[ABAB_32_Bar_Form]] but is more condensed due to shorter section lengths. It is comprised of four distinct sections, each assigned a rehearsal letter: A, A', B, and Coda. The inclusion of a "[[Pre-Chorus|Pre-Chorus]]" (implied as part of the A or A' section leading into B) is a common feature in modern [[Pop]] and [[Country-Pop_Genre]] arrangements, building anticipation for the main chorus.

*   **A**: Typically the [[Verse_Section_Letter_A]].
*   **A'**: A variation of the verse, often with slight lyrical or musical changes.
*   **B**: The Chorus, the main hook of the song.
*   **Coda**: The outro or ending section.

**Pro-Tip:** Understanding song forms is crucial for drummers to anticipate changes, build [[Dynamics|dynamics]], and place [[Drum_Fill]]s effectively. In forms with a [[Pre-Chorus|pre-chorus]], the drumming often builds intensity subtly to propel the listener into the main chorus.
