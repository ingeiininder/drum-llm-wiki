# Linear Hand/Foot Development (Triplet)

This is the second phase of [[Linear Drumming]] development, shifting the focus from 16th notes to [[Eighth-Note Triplets]]. 

### Objective
The goal is to build a linear vocabulary based on groups of three, which is fundamental for jazz, blues, and shuffle-based [[Fills]]. The objective remains the equal subdivision of all limbs.

### Exercise Progression (Unit 9)
- **Basic Idea (Ex 9.6 - 9.15)**: Patterns are established using R, L, and K (Kick) in triplet groupings.
- **Leading**: The curriculum emphasizes practicing these patterns leading with both the right and left hands.
- **Orchestration**: Once the "Basic Idea" is "owned" (memorized and fluid), the student should apply it to different drums.

### Technical Challenges
- **Triplet Evenness**: In a linear context, it is easy to "swing" the triplets or make them uneven. Constant use of a metronome is required.
- **Four-Way Integration**: While these are linear, the non-playing limbs (like the hi-hat foot) should eventually maintain a steady pulse to ground the time.

### Pro-Tip
Explore "voicing options" beyond the standard snare and toms. Try orchestrating the hand parts between the bell of the ride and the snare for a more contemporary "linear gospel" sound.
