# Prep Notes

**Prep notes** are subtle, often unwritten (or lightly ghosted) [[Bass_Drum]] hits used to stabilize the drummer's balance before executing a syncopated figure.

### Application
In Unit 7, Chapter 1 of the Musicians Institute curriculum, prep notes are specifically recommended when navigating the space between a backbeat and a syncopated figure. 

**Example**:
When playing an ensemble figure on the **"e" of [[Beat|beat]] 3**, a subtle "prep" in the bass drum on the downbeat of 3 can help the drummer bridge the gap from the snare hit on [[Beat|beat]] 2. This provides a physical "launchpad" for the syncopated hit.

### Pro-Tip
While prep notes are useful for stability, they should not be played so loudly that they interfere with the intended rhythm of the [[Ensemble_Figures]]. They are a tool for internal coordination and physical balance.
