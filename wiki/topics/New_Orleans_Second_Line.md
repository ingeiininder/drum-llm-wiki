# New Orleans Second Line (Drum Set)

**Second Line** drumming is a unique style originating from New Orleans brass band traditions. It blends military marching cadences with African syncopation and a "swung-sixteenth" feel.

### The Groove Characteristics
- **Feel**: A "lazy" or "in-between" feel that sits between straight and swung sixteenth notes.
- **Snare Drum**: The heart of the groove. It features heavy use of syncopated accents and "crushed" rolls (buzz rolls). Accents are often played as light [[Snare_Drum_Rim_Shot_Articulations|rim shots]].
- **Bass Drum**: Often follows the "Big Drum" tradition, playing syncopated patterns that dance around the snare accents.
- **Hi-Hat**: Frequently features an upbeat-eighth note pattern played with the foot.

### Key Elements
- **The "Push"**: Anticipating a chord or beat by playing on the "&" of 4.
- **Ride Bell**: In modern interpretations (e.g., Dr. John or Dirty Dozen Brass Band), the bell of the ride cymbal is used to drive the groove with eighth-note upbeat accents.
- **Crushed Rolls**: Rolls on the "&" of 4 should be "crushed" (closed/pressed) rather than open rudimental rolls to maintain the funky, street-beat aesthetic.
