# New Orleans Second Line (Drum Set)

**Second Line** drumming is a unique style originating from New Orleans brass band traditions. It blends military marching cadences with African [[Syncopation|syncopation]] and a "swung-sixteenth" feel.

### The [[Groove|Groove]] Characteristics
- **Feel**: A "lazy" or "in-between" feel that sits between straight and swung [[Sixteenth_Notes|sixteenth notes]].
- **[[Snare_Drum|Snare Drum]]**: The heart of the [[Groove|groove]]. It features heavy use of syncopated [[Accents|accents]] and "crushed" rolls (buzz rolls). [[Accents|Accents]] are often played as light [[Snare_Drum_Rim_Shot_Articulations|rim shots]].
- **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: Often follows the "Big Drum" tradition, playing syncopated patterns that dance around the snare [[Accents|accents]].
- **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: Frequently features an upbeat-eighth note pattern played with the foot.

### Key Elements
- **The "Push"**: Anticipating a chord or [[Beat|beat]] by playing on the "&" of 4.
- **Ride Bell**: In modern interpretations (e.g., Dr. John or Dirty Dozen Brass Band), the bell of the [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]] is used to drive the [[Groove|groove]] with eighth-note upbeat [[Accents|accents]].
- **Crushed Rolls**: Rolls on the "&" of 4 should be "crushed" (closed/pressed) rather than open rudimental rolls to maintain the funky, street-[[Beat|beat]] aesthetic.
