# Clef (Music Notation)
A clef is a symbol placed at the beginning of [[The_Staff]] in [[Music_Notation]] that designates one particular line as a specific pitch. This reference point then allows all other notes on the staff to be mapped in ascending and descending order relative to that designated line.

## Function
*   **Pitch Reference**: The primary function of a clef is to establish the pitch range for the notes written on the staff. Without a clef, the lines and spaces of the staff would not have specific pitch meanings.
*   **Instrument Specificity**: Different clefs are used for different instruments or vocal ranges to ensure that the majority of their notes fall comfortably within the five lines of the staff, minimizing the need for [[Ledger_Line]]s.

## Common Types of Clefs
*   **[[Treble_Clef]] (G Clef)**: Commonly used for higher-pitched instruments like guitars, keyboards (right hand), violins, and horns. It designates the second line from the bottom as G above middle C.
*   **[[Bass_Clef]] (F Clef)**: Commonly used for lower-pitched instruments like bass guitars, keyboards (left hand), cellos, and trombones. It designates the fourth line from the bottom as F below middle C.
*   **[[Percussion_Clef]] (Neutral Clef)**: Specifically used for non-melodic instruments such as drums and other percussion. It does not designate specific pitches but rather specific drum or cymbal components on [[The_Staff]].

Understanding the clef is the first step in interpreting the pitch or instrument assignment of notes on a musical staff.
