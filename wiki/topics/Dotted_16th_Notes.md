# Dotted [[16th_Notes|16th Notes]]

A **dotted 16th note** follows the standard rule of [[Dotted_Notes]]: the dot adds half the value of the note it is attached to. 

### Rhythmic Value
- **16th Note** = 1/4 of a [[Beat|beat]] (in 4/4).
- **Dot** = 1/8 of a [[Beat|beat]] (half of a 16th).
- **Total Value** = 3/8 of a [[Beat|beat]].
- **Equivalency**: One dotted 16th note is exactly equal to three [[32nd_Notes]].

### Comparison Table
| Note Type | Value in 32nd Notes |
| :--- | :--- |
| 16th Note | 2 |
| Dotted 16th Note | 3 |
| 8th Note | 4 |

### Performance and Subdivision
Dotted [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] are most common in slower tempos or "ballad" charts. To ensure proper placement, the performer must subdivide the [[Beat|beat]] into 32nd notes. 
- On non-sustaining instruments like the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]], a dotted 16th note followed by a 32nd note sounds identical to a 16th note tied to a 32nd note.
- **The "Gallop" Feel**: A common pattern is the dotted 16th followed by a single 32nd note, creating a sharp, syncopated "long-short" feel.

### Dotted 16th [[Rests|Rests]]
The **dotted 16th rest** also carries a value of 3/8 of a [[Beat|beat]]. It is used to precisely offset the start of a note by three 32nd-note subdivisions.

> [!PRO-TIP]
> When practicing [[Dotted_16th_Note_Exercises]], keep a steady flow of 8th notes with your lead hand (usually the right hand) on the [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]] or Ride. This acts as a "grid" to help you place the 32nd-note "tail" of the dotted figure accurately.
