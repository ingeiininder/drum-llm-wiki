# Accented Eighth-Note Triplets

Developing control over **Accented Eighth-Note Triplets** is essential for mastering the [[Swing Feel]] and jazz-influenced popular music styles.

### Technical Execution
- **Up Motion**: The note immediately preceding an accent should be played as an [[Upstroke]]. This prepares the hand for the velocity required for the accent.
- **Clean Articulation**: All subdivisions must be precise. There should be no "flamming" between the snare drum accents and the [[Bass Drum]] or [[Hi-Hat]] ostinato.
- **Ostinato**: Typically practiced over a "feathered" bass drum (all four quarter notes) and hi-hat on 2 and 4.

### Practice Variations
To achieve total mastery, play triplet exercises in four ways:
1. **Right Hand Lead**: (R L R) (L R L)
2. **Left Hand Lead**: (L R L) (R L R)
3. **Right Hand Only**: (R R R) (R R R) - Focuses on speed and wrist endurance.
4. **Left Hand Only**: (L L L) (L L L) - Critical for strengthening the weak hand.

### Pro-Tips
- **Dynamic Contrast**: Make the difference between the "taps" and the "accents" as wide as possible.
- **Avoid Bass Drum Accents**: Do not unintentionally accent the bass drum when you accent the snare drum. Keep the foot steady and independent.
