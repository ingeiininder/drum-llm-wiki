# Staccato and Tenuto Articulations
In drum notation, articulations help the drummer interpret [[Ensemble Figures]] to match the phrasing of the horn section or rhythm section.

### Staccato (Short)
*   **Notation**: Indicated by a dot placed above or below the note head.
*   **Drumset Interpretation**: Play these notes as short as possible. 
    *   *Orchestration Tip*: Use the [[Snare Drum]] and [[Hi-Hat]] (closed) to achieve a "tight" sound. Avoid using crashing cymbals that ring.
*   **Purpose**: To provide rhythmic clarity and "punch."

### Tenuto (Long)
*   **Notation**: Indicated by a horizontal line over the note head.
*   **Drumset Interpretation**: Play these rhythms as "long" as possible.
    *   *Orchestration Tip*: Use [[Crash_Cymbal]], open hi-hats, or let the [[Bass Drum]] resonate. The goal is to fill the duration of the note with sound.
*   **Purpose**: To emphasize the weight and duration of a specific beat.

### Comparison Table
| Feature | Staccato | Tenuto |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sound** | Choked, tight, dry | Ringing, sustained, wet |
| **Common Voice** | Snare/Closed Hats | Crash/Bass Drum/Open Hats |
| **Visual** | Dot (.) | Line (-) |
