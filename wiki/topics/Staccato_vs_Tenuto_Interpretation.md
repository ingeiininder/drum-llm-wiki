# [[Staccato|Staccato]] and [[Tenuto|Tenuto]] [[Articulations|Articulations]]
In drum notation, [[Articulations|articulations]] help the drummer interpret [[Ensemble_Figures]] to match the [[Phrasing|phrasing]] of the horn section or rhythm section.

### [[Staccato|Staccato]] (Short)
*   **Notation**: Indicated by a dot placed above or below the [[Note_Head|note head]].
*   **Drumset Interpretation**: Play these notes as short as possible. 
    *   *Orchestration Tip*: Use the [[Snare_Drum]] and [[Hi-Hat]] (closed) to achieve a "tight" sound. Avoid using crashing cymbals that ring.
*   **Purpose**: To provide rhythmic clarity and "punch."

### [[Tenuto|Tenuto]] (Long)
*   **Notation**: Indicated by a horizontal line over the [[Note_Head|note head]].
*   **Drumset Interpretation**: Play these rhythms as "long" as possible.
    *   *Orchestration Tip*: Use [[Crash_Cymbal]], open hi-hats, or let the [[Bass_Drum]] resonate. The goal is to [[Fill|fill]] the [[Duration|duration]] of the note with sound.
*   **Purpose**: To emphasize the weight and [[Duration|duration]] of a specific [[Beat|beat]].

| Feature | [[Staccato|Staccato]] | [[Tenuto|Tenuto]] |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
