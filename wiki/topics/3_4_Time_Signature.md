# 3/4 [[Time_Signature|Time Signature]]
The `[[3_4_Time_Signature]]` is a common `[[Meter]]` used across virtually all genres of music, including `[[Modern_Rock]]` by bands like `[[Paramore]]`, `[[Muse]]`, and `[[Radiohead]]`. In 3/4, there are three `[[Quarter_Note]]` per [[Measure|measure]], with the quarter note receiving one [[Beat|beat]].

From a drummer's perspective, the technical aspects of playing in 3/4 are similar to other time signatures. However, a significant challenge is preventing the `[[Tempo]]` from `[[Rush]]`ing. The `[[Downbeat]]` of [[Beat|beat]] 1 is often perceived as very strong, leading inexperienced players to neglect the importance of beats 2 and 3.

**Pro-Tip:** To develop a solid `[[3/4_Time_Feel]]`, practice consciously emphasizing (or at least acknowledging) beats 2 and 3. Avoid the tendency to treat 3/4 as a fast 1/4 or to rush towards the next downbeat. `[[Subdividing]]` the [[Measure|measure]] into `[[Eighth_Note]]` or `[[16th_Notes]]` can help maintain an even `[[Internal_Pulse]]` across all three beats. Listen to diverse musical examples in 3/4 to internalize its unique rhythmic flow.
