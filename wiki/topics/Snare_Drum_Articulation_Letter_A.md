# Snare Drum Articulation (Letter A)
For the 'A' section of a song, the snare drum should be played with a specific articulation to provide a consistent and strong backbeat. This involves:

*   **Consistency:** The snare drum should be played very consistently, ensuring each hit has a similar attack and decay.
*   **Dynamic:** A dynamic of forte (loud) is recommended.
*   **Rimshot:** Additionally, each snare hit should be a [[Rimshot Technique]], executed by hitting the center of the drumhead and the rim at the exact same time.

When playing a two-handed 16th-note pattern, the rimshot is typically played with the right hand to maintain the flow of the hi-hat pattern.

**Pro-Tip:** Consistent, powerful rimshots are a hallmark of many rock and funk grooves, providing a strong, cutting backbeat that drives the 'A' section. Practice achieving uniform rimshots at a forte dynamic.
