# Roll Applications in Ensemble Figures
Rolls are a vital resource for creating [[Set-up_Fill]] and sustaining the sound of an ensemble figure. They allow the drummer to "elongate" the snare drum sound.

### Types of Rolls for Interpretation
*   **Single Stroke Roll**: Used for high-energy, melodic movement.
*   **Open Double Stroke Roll**: Provides a clear, rhythmic texture.
*   **Closed (Buzz) Roll**: Creates a seamless, sustained "wall of sound."

### Melodic Accents
Rolls can be broken into smaller pieces (e.g., Five-Stroke Rolls) to match specific rhythmic figures. By placing accents within the roll, you can mirror the ensemble's rhythm while maintaining the "connected" feel of the roll.

### Orchestration
While figures are traditionally voiced on cymbals, using a **Snare Drum Roll** (often reinforced with the **Bass Drum** on the accents) provides a sophisticated alternative that ties multiple figures together.

**Technical Requirement**: Maintain the primary stroke (wrist movement) regardless of the roll type; the difference between an open and closed roll is primarily the finger pressure applied to the stick.
