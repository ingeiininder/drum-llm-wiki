# Roll Applications in [[Ensemble_Figures|Ensemble Figures]]
Rolls are a vital resource for creating [[Set-up_Fill]] and sustaining the sound of an ensemble figure. They allow the drummer to "elongate" the [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] sound.

### Types of Rolls for Interpretation
*   **Single Stroke Roll**: Used for high-energy, melodic movement.
*   **Open [[Double_Stroke_Roll|Double Stroke Roll]]**: Provides a clear, rhythmic texture.
*   **Closed (Buzz) Roll**: Creates a seamless, sustained "wall of sound."

### Melodic [[Accents|Accents]]
Rolls can be broken into smaller pieces (e.g., Five-Stroke Rolls) to match specific rhythmic figures. By placing [[Accents|accents]] within the roll, you can mirror the ensemble's rhythm while maintaining the "connected" feel of the roll.

### Orchestration
While figures are traditionally voiced on cymbals, using a **[[Snare_Drum|Snare Drum]] Roll** (often reinforced with the **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]** on the [[Accents|accents]]) provides a sophisticated alternative that [[Ties|ties]] multiple figures together.

**Technical Requirement**: Maintain the primary stroke (wrist movement) regardless of the roll type; the difference between an open and [[Closed_Roll|closed roll]] is primarily the finger pressure applied to the stick.
