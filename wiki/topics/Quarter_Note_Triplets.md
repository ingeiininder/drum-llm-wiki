# Quarter-Note Triplets

Quarter-note triplets (also known as "half-note triplets" in some contexts, though technically three notes over the span of two quarter notes) are a subdivision that creates a "three-against-two" feel.

### Derivation from Eighth-Note Triplets
The most accurate way to learn quarter-note triplets is to play every other note of an [[Eighth-Note Triplets]] sequence:
*   **Eighth-Note Triplet Grid**: 1 - trip - let - 2 - trip - let
*   **Quarter-Note Triplet Placement**: **1** - (trip) - **let** - (2) - **trip** - (let)

This results in the notes falling on:
1.  The "1"
2.  The "let" of beat 1
3.  The "trip" of beat 2

### Technical Execution
When using alternating sticking (R-L-R L-R-L) for eighth-note triplets, the quarter-note triplets will naturally be played by the **Right Hand** only (Ex 2.3). Removing the left hand (Ex 2.4) reveals the quarter-note triplet pulse.

### Rests and Combinations
Quarter-note triplet rests follow the same logic as standard quarter rests but exist within the triplet grid. Common combinations (Ex 2.5) include:
*   Note - Rest - Note
*   Rest - Note - Note
*   Note - Note - Rest
