# Closed Triplet Rolls
Closed triplet rolls are essential for developing touch and [[Finger_Control]], particularly within [[Jazz_Drumming]] and other triplet-based styles.

### Technical Execution
The goal is to produce a smooth, continuous sound by applying [[Multiple_Rebound]] (buzz) strokes to an 8th-note triplet base.
1. **Primary [[Pulse|Pulse]]**: Maintain a steady alternating 8th-note triplet "skeleton."
2. **Diddles**: Transition to 16th-note [[Triplets|triplets]] by doubling every stroke ([[Diddles]]).
3. **The Buzz**: Apply finger pressure to produce a [[Closed_Roll]] (buzz). 

### Developing the "Touch"
- **Short vs. Long**: Adjust hand pressure to shorten or elongate the length of the multiple rebounds.
- **Eliminating the Break**: At slower tempos, there is a tendency for a "gap" to appear between strokes. Focus on sustaining the buzz of the first note until the second note begins.

### Practice Progression
- [[Measure|Measure]] 1: Single stroke [[Triplets|triplets]].
- [[Measure|Measure]] 2: Double stroke (open) rolls.
- [[Measure|Measure]] 3: Single stroke [[Triplets|triplets]].
- [[Measure|Measure]] 4: Closed (buzz) rolls.

### Pro-Tips
- **[[Jazz|Jazz]] Context**: Use these rolls for subtle [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] "comping" behind a soloist.
- **Common Pitfall**: Do not let the "buzz" tension travel up into the wrist or forearm. The pressure should be localized in the fingers and [[Fulcrum|fulcrum]].
