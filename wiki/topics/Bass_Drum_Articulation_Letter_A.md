# [[Bass_Drum_Articulation|Bass Drum Articulation]] (Letter A)
For the 'A' section of a song, the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] pattern should be played with a specific articulation to establish a strong, driving foundation. This involves:

*   **Forceful and [[Staccato|Staccato]]:** The [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] should be played very forcefully, with a short, detached ([[Staccato|staccato]]) articulation.
*   **Dynamic:** A dynamic of forte (loud) is recommended.

These characteristics are achieved by utilizing three important strategies:
1.  **Beater Choice:** Using the [[Plastic_Bass_Drum_Beater]] (or any [[Hard-Surfaced_Bass_Drum_Beater|hard-surfaced bass drum beater]]) to achieve a brighter, more articulate attack.
2.  **Beater Contact:** [[Burying_the_Bass_Drum_Beater]] into the [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] head after the stroke, which mutes the head and creates the [[Staccato|staccato]] sound.
3.  **Power Generation:** [[Using_Entire_Leg_and_Foot_for_Bass_Drum_Stroke]] to generate maximum power and consistency.

**Pro-Tip:** This combination creates a punchy, defined [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] sound that effectively drives the 'A' section with authority, providing a solid rhythmic anchor for the ensemble.
