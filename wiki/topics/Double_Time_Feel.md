# Double-Time Feel

A **Double-Time Feel** is a rhythmic device that gives the illusion that the music has doubled in speed, even though the underlying pulse (BPM) and chord progression remain the same.

### Execution
In a standard 4/4 rock context, the "feel" is altered by shifting the [[Snare Drum]] backbeat:
*   **Standard Feel**: Backbeat on beats 2 and 4.
*   **Double-Time Feel**: Backbeat moves to the "&" of every beat (1 **&** 2 **&** 3 **&** 4 **&**).

### Technical Application
*   **Hi-Hat**: Often played as straight eighth notes using a [[Sloshy Hi-Hat]] technique to increase the wall of sound.
*   **Transitions**: To effectively move into a double-time section, drummers should use a [[Transition Fill]] (often involving 16th or 32nd notes) that foreshadows the increased rhythmic density.

---PITFALL---
Avoid the "shoulder/tip" motion on the hi-hat during double-time sections; using this motion will inadvertently accent the downbeats and diminish the "driving" wall-of-sound effect required for the feel.
