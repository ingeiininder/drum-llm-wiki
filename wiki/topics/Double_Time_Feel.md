# Double-[[Time_Feel|Time Feel]]

**Double-[[Time_Feel|Time Feel]]** is a rhythmic device that gives the illusion that the music has doubled in speed, even though the underlying [[Pulse|pulse]] (BPM) and chord progression remain the same. It is a staple technique in [[Punk-Pop_Drumming]], extreme metal, and hardcore genres.

## Construction of the Groove
In a standard 4/4 [[Rock|rock]] context, the "feel" is altered by shifting the [[Snare_Drum]] backbeat's placement:
*   **Standard Feel**: Backbeat on beats 2 and 4.
*   **Double-[[Time_Feel|Time Feel]]**: The backbeat moves to the **"&" of every [[Beat|beat]]** (1 **&** 2 **&** 3 **&** 4 **&**).

## Technical Execution and Style
- **[[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]**: Usually played as straight [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] using a [[Sloshy_Hi_Hat]] (half-open) technique to increase the "wall of sound."
- **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: Often employs busy 16th-note patterns that provide constant forward motion.
- **Sectional Energy**: This feel is used to increase the "energy" of a song during a chorus or bridge without changing the actual tempo.

---PITFALL---
**Avoid the "shoulder/tip" motion** on the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] during double-time sections. Using this alternating motion will inadvertently accent the downbeats, which diminishes the "driving" wall-of-sound effect required for a powerful double-[[Time_Feel|time feel]]. Use the shoulder on the edge consistently.

## Transitions
To effectively move into a double-time section, drummers should use a [[Transition_Fill]] (often involving 16th or 32nd notes) that foreshadows the increased rhythmic density of the upcoming section.
