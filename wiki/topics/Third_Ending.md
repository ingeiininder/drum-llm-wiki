# Third Ending
A `[[Third Ending]]` is a `[[Musical Form]]` tool used in `[[Musical Notation]]` to provide different conclusions to a repeated section, typically after the section has been played through multiple times.

To navigate a third ending:
1.  Play through the section and take the `[[First Ending]]`.
2.  Repeat back to the beginning of the section (or designated repeat point).
3.  Play through the section again and take the `[[Second Ending]]`.
4.  Repeat back for a third pass of the particular section or passage.
5.  After this third pass, proceed directly to the `[[Third Ending]]`, which then continues to the next section of the piece.

In the `[[A Section]]` example `[[Ex 9.5]]`, the first and second endings are identical and housed under the same bracket, while the third ending features a `[[Written Fill]]`.

**Pro-Tip:** `[[Chart Reading]]` with multiple endings requires careful attention. Always scan ahead to understand the full `[[Form Navigation]]` before you start playing. Mentally (or lightly with a pencil) mark your path through the repeats and endings. This prevents confusion and ensures smooth `[[Transitions]]` between sections. The presence of a `[[Fill]]` in a third ending often signals a significant change or a build-up to a new part of the song.
