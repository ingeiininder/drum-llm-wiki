# Rudiment Families
All of the drum rudiments, which are fundamental building blocks of [[Rudimental Drumming]], are categorized into five distinct "families" or groups. This classification helps in understanding their underlying structure and how they relate to each other.

**The Five Rudiment Families:**
1.  **[[Ruffs_Rudiment_Family]]:** These rudiments involve a short single-stroke roll, typically played either before or after an [[Accents (Drumming)|accent]]. Examples include the 3-Stroke, 4-Stroke, 5-Stroke, and 7-Stroke Ruffs.
2.  **[[Rolls_Rudiment_Family]]:** Characterized by sustained sounds created by multiple bounces or presses of the stick. Examples include the Long Roll, 5-Stroke Roll, 6-Stroke Roll, and 7-Stroke Roll.
3.  **[[Diddles_Rudiment_Family]]:** These rudiments incorporate "diddles," which are two consecutive notes played with the same hand. The most common example is the [[Paradiddle]], along with the Double Paradiddle.
4.  **[[Flams_Rudiment_Family]]:** Flams involve two notes played almost simultaneously, with one note (the grace note) preceding the main note. Examples include the Flam Accent, Flam Tap, and Swiss Triplet.
5.  **[[Drags_Rudiment_Family]]:** Drags are similar to ruffs but typically involve two grace notes preceding a main note, often played as a double bounce. Examples include the Single Drag, Double Drag, and Ratamacue.

**Curriculum Focus:**
In the current unit, the [[Ruffs_Rudiment_Family]] will be introduced and developed, specifically focusing on the 3-, 4-, 5-, and 7-stroke ruffs. Subsequent units will explore the other rudiment families.

**Pro-Tip:** Understanding the family structure can help you learn new rudiments more quickly by recognizing common patterns and sticking principles. Each family offers unique rhythmic and textural possibilities, and mastering them all will significantly expand your drumming vocabulary.
