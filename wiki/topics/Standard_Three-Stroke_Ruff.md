# Standard Three-Stroke Ruff
The [[Standard Three-Stroke Ruff]] is a variation of the [[Three-Stroke Ruff]] where the rudiment consists of three alternating strokes: the first two are unaccented grace notes, and the third stroke is accented.

**Execution:**
The basic sticking pattern for a [[Standard Three-Stroke Ruff]] is typically:
*   **R L R** (first two soft, third loud)
*   **L R L** (first two soft, third loud)

**Practice Variations:**
1.  **Eighth Notes and Sixteenth Notes:** Practice the rudiment using these note values to develop rhythmic precision.
    *   *Example (Ex 4.1):* R L R L R L R L (first two soft, third loud, repeating)
2.  **Accent on the Quarter Note:** Practice shifting the accent to fall on the quarter note pulse, which helps in internalizing the rhythmic placement.
    *   *Example (Ex 4.2):* (Accent on 1, then 2, then 3, then 4)
3.  **Individual Hand Development:** For concentrated wrist development, practice the rudiment with each hand individually (non-alternating).
    *   *Example (based on Ex 4.1):* R R R R R R R R (first two soft, third loud, repeating with only the right hand)

**Pro-Tip:** Ensure the grace notes are played very softly and quickly, almost as a single "brush" sound leading into the main accent. The accent should be clear and strong. Maintain a relaxed grip to allow for natural rebound and fluid motion, especially when practicing with individual hands.
