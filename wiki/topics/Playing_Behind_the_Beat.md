# Playing Behind the [[Beat|Beat]]

Playing "behind the [[Beat|beat]]" (or "behind the click") is a stylistic choice used to create a relaxed, "fat," or "laid-back" feel. It is a hallmark of [[Ballad]] playing and certain styles of [[Funk]] and [[Blues]].

### Key Concepts
*   **Micro-Timing**: The drummer is not slowing down the [[Tempo|tempo]]; rather, they are placing the notes at the very back edge of the metronomic [[Pulse|pulse]].
*   **Emotional Impact**: As noted by [[Todd_Sucherman]] in [[Center_of_Your_Heart_Song_Study]], playing behind the [[Beat|beat]] can make a song feel more emotional. Moving from "behind" to "on top" of the [[Beat|beat]] can create a sense of urgency and build energy without an actual BPM increase.
*   **The "Pocket"**: This technique is essential for finding the [[Pocket]] in tunes like [[Calif-Horn-Ya_Song_Study]].

**Common Pitfall**: Beginners often confuse "playing behind the [[Beat|beat]]" with "dragging." Dragging is a continuous deceleration of [[Tempo|tempo]], whereas playing behind the [[Beat|beat]] is a consistent placement of notes relative to a steady [[Pulse|pulse]].
