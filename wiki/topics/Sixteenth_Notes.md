# Sixteenth Notes

A **Sixteenth Note** (or semiquaver) is a musical note played for half the duration of an [[Eighth Note]].

## Characteristics
- **Notation**: Drawn with an oval, filled-in note head and a straight note stem with two flags. Consecutive sixteenth notes are connected with a double beam.
- **Value**: Four sixteenth notes equal one [[Quarter Note]]. In a standard [[4-4_Time_Signature]], there are sixteen sixteenth notes per [[Measure]].

## Counting
Sixteenth notes are counted evenly: "1 e & a 2 e & a 3 e & a 4 e & a".

## Application
Sixteenth notes form the basis of funk [[Groove]]s, complex [[Drum Fill]]s, and advanced [[Rudiment]] such as the Paradiddle and Double Stroke Roll.
