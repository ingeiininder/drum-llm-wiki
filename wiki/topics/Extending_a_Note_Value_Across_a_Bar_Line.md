# Extending a Note Value Across a [[Bar_Line|Bar Line]]
One of the primary uses of [[Ties]] in musical notation is to allow a [[Note_Value]] to extend beyond the allowable number of beats within a single [[Measure]]. This is essential for rhythmic continuity and for creating [[Phrasing]] that transcends the visual breaks of [[Bar_Lines]]s.

**Why it's Used:**
*   **Rhythmic Continuity**: Prevents a long note from being prematurely cut off by a [[Bar_Line|bar line]].
*   **Complex Phrasing**: Enables the notation of rhythms that naturally flow across measures, creating a sense of forward motion.
*   **Adherence to [[Meter|Meter]]**: Allows notes to occupy their full [[Duration|duration]] without violating the [[Time_Signature]] of individual measures.

**Example:**
In [[4-4_Time_Signature]], a [[Whole_Note]] cannot be held for more than four beats within a single measure. If a composer desires a note to last for five beats, it would be notated as a [[Quarter_Note]] tied to a [[Whole_Note]] across a [[Bar_Line|bar line]], where the quarter note is in the first measure and the whole note is in the second.

**Pro-Tip:** When reading tied notes across a [[Bar_Line|bar line]], mentally "connect" the notes as one continuous sound. For drummers, this means maintaining the internal [[Pulse|pulse]] and feeling the [[Duration|duration]] of the combined note, even though the attack only occurs on the first note. This is vital for accurate [[Sight_Reading]] and musical interpretation.
