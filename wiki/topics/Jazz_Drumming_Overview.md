# [[Jazz|Jazz]] Drumming Overview
[[Jazz|Jazz]] emerged at the start of the 20th century, blending African rhythmic sensibilities with European harmonic structures. For the drummer, [[Jazz|jazz]] is defined by **improvisation**, **group interaction**, and a fundamental shift in the [[Jazz_Balance_of_Sound]].

### Structural Foundation: The 12-Bar [[Blues|Blues]]
Most introductory [[Jazz|jazz]] studies focus on the 12-bar [[Blues|blues]] (I-IV-V progression). 
*   **Bars 1-4**: I Chord (e.g., G7)
*   **Bars 5-8**: IV Chord (e.g., C7)
*   **Bars 9-12**: V Chord (e.g., D7) leading back to the I chord.

### Key Concepts
*   **[[The_Head_(Jazz)]]**: The main melody of the tune.
*   **[[Comping_Jazz]]**: The art of accompanying a soloist.
*   **[[Swing_Eighth_Notes]]**: The rhythmic "feel" that defines the genre.
*   **Open Form**: A structure where the 12-bar cycle is repeated indefinitely for solos until a "cue" is given to return to the head.
