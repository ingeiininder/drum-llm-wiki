# Half-Time Shuffle
The Half-Time Shuffle is a sophisticated groove based on a [[Triplets|triplet]] subdivision where the [[Back-Beat]] occurs only on beat 3 (in 4/4 time), rather than the traditional beats 2 and 4.

### Stylistic Application: Indie Rock
While famously associated with Bernard Purdie and Jeff Porcaro, a "relaxed" version of this feel is prevalent in [[Indie_Rock_Drumming|Indie Rock]].
*   **Reference Artists**: Mutemath, Radiohead, Death Cab for Cutie.
*   **Feel**: Less "busy" on the ride surfaces compared to traditional blues shuffles, focusing more on the "wash" and internal snare ghosting.

### Technical Requirements
1.  **Swung 8th Notes**: Every 8th note is interpreted as the first and third notes of a triplet.
2.  **Intricate Ghost Notes**: The snare drum fills the "middle" triplet partials with very quiet ghost notes. If these are misplaced, the groove will sound "sloppy" rather than "swinging."
3.  **Sound Profile**: 
    *   **Snare**: Center-snare hits for the backbeat.
    *   **Bass Drum**: Beater off the head (resonant).
    *   **Ride**: A "washy" ride cymbal is preferred to create a wall of sound.

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Rushing the "3"**: Because the backbeat is delayed until beat 3, drummers often have a tendency to rush the transition from beat 2 to 3.
*   **Straightening the Feel**: Ensure the 16th-note ghost notes (when used) also adhere to the triplet/swung grid.
