# Half-Time [[Shuffle|Shuffle]]
The Half-Time [[Shuffle|Shuffle]] is a sophisticated [[Groove|groove]] based on a [[Triplets|triplet]] [[Subdivision|subdivision]] where the [[Back-Beat]] occurs only on [[Beat|beat]] 3 (in 4/4 time), rather than the traditional beats 2 and 4.

### Stylistic Application: Indie [[Rock|Rock]]
While famously associated with Bernard Purdie and Jeff Porcaro, a "relaxed" version of this feel is prevalent in [[Indie_Rock_Drumming|Indie Rock]].
*   **Reference Artists**: Mutemath, Radiohead, Death Cab for Cutie.
*   **Feel**: Less "busy" on the ride surfaces compared to traditional [[Blues|blues]] shuffles, focusing more on the "wash" and internal snare ghosting.

### Technical Requirements
1.  **Swung 8th Notes**: Every 8th note is interpreted as the first and third notes of a triplet.
2.  **Intricate [[Ghost_Notes|Ghost Notes]]**: The [[Snare_Drum|snare drum]] fills the "middle" [[Triplet_Partials|triplet partials]] with very quiet [[Ghost_Notes|ghost notes]]. If these are misplaced, the [[Groove|groove]] will sound "sloppy" rather than "swinging."
3.  **Sound Profile**: 
    *   **Snare**: Center-snare hits for the backbeat.
    *   **[[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: Beater off the head (resonant).
    *   **Ride**: A "washy" [[Ride_Cymbal|ride cymbal]] is preferred to create a wall of sound.

### Common Pitfalls
*   **Rushing the "3"**: Because the backbeat is delayed until [[Beat|beat]] 3, drummers often have a tendency to rush the transition from [[Beat|beat]] 2 to 3.
*   **Straightening the Feel**: Ensure the 16th-note [[Ghost_Notes|ghost notes]] (when used) also adhere to the triplet/swung grid.
