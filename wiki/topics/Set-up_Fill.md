# Set-up Fill
A **Set-up Fill** is a rhythmic interjection played by the drummer to signal an upcoming [[Ensemble Figures]] to the rest of the band. It bridges the gap between the [[Groove]] and the figure.

### Repertoire of Set-ups
As drummers progress, they build a "vocabulary" of fills ranging from simple to complex:
1.  **Minimal**: A single snare or bass drum hit on the [[Target Beat]].
2.  **Eighth Note Additions**: Adding eighth notes on the snare or toms leading to the figure.
3.  **Sixteenth Note Variations**: Using 16th note runs or varied stickings (RLLR, RLRL) for more energy.
4.  **Double Stops**: Playing two drums (e.g., Snare and Floor Tom) simultaneously for a thicker texture.
5.  **Complex Triplets**: Using 16th-note triplets to create a sophisticated, "pro" sound.

### Practice Method
To master set-ups, practice at three specific tempos: **85 BPM, 100 BPM, and 115 BPM**. The goal is to maintain momentum and recover from mistakes smoothly without breaking the time.
