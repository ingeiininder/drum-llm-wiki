# Seven-Stroke Ruff
The [[Seven-Stroke_Ruff]] is a drum rudiment belonging to the "Drag" or "Ruff" family. It consists of six grace notes (drags) preceding a primary stroke. It is mentioned as a rudiment in Unit 4 of "[[Drum_Technique_1_(MI_Curriculum_Series)]]".

**Standard Sticking (Right Hand Lead):**
rrLLrrL (or LLrrLLr for Left Hand Lead)

**Breakdown:**
*   **rrLLrr:** Six quiet, quick grace notes (drags) played as three [[Double_Strokes]]s (rr, LL, rr).
*   **L:** A louder, primary stroke played with the left hand.

**Purpose and Application:**
*   **Embellishment:** Provides a longer, more sustained "drag" sound before a main stroke.
*   **Dynamic Contrast:** The grace notes are soft, while the primary note is accented, creating a clear dynamic shape.
*   **Roll Development:** Further develops control over multiple bounces and transitions, building on skills from shorter ruffs and rolls.

**Pro-Tip:** The challenge with the [[Seven-Stroke_Ruff]] is maintaining evenness and consistency across all six grace notes, ensuring they are quiet and tightly spaced. Focus on a relaxed approach to the [[Double_Stroke_Technique]] for the grace notes, allowing for natural rebound. Practice slowly with a [[Metronome|metronome]] to ensure precision before attempting higher tempos.
