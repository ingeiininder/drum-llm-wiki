# Shouldered Hi-Hat Technique

The **Shouldered Hi-Hat** technique is used to create a "chunky," aggressive, and thick tone, often required in dance-pop and rock grooves to create forward motion.

### Execution
- **Stick Placement**: Use the **shoulder** of the stick (the tapered area mid-way down from the tip) rather than the tip.
- **Striking Point**: Strike the **edge** of the hi-hat cymbals, not the top surface.
- **Foot Tension**: The hi-hats should be closed but not "choked" tight. This allows the cymbals to vibrate against each other slightly, creating a "chunk-chunk" sound rather than a thin "chick-chick."

### Musical Effect
This technique creates a rhythmic illusion of "forward motion," making a groove feel faster and more syncopated than its actual BPM. It is particularly effective for eighth-note patterns in contemporary "Top 40" music.
