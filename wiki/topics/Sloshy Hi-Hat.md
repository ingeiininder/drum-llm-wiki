# Sloshy Hi-Hat
"Sloshy Hi-Hat" describes a looser, more open sound produced on the [[Hi-Hat]] cymbals, typically achieved by releasing some pressure on the hi-hat pedal, allowing the cymbals to partially open and resonate. This creates a fuller, more sustained, and often "washier" sound compared to a [[Tight Hi-Hat]].

This articulation is often employed in more energetic sections like a [[Chorus (Song Section)]] (e.g., [[Letter B (Rehearsal Mark)]]) to provide a broader, more expansive feel to the [[Groove]]. It can add a sense of excitement and fullness to the overall drum sound.

**Pro-Tip:** Achieving a good "sloshy" sound involves finding the right balance of pedal pressure. Too open, and it becomes a full crash; too closed, and it loses its wash. Experiment with varying degrees of foot pressure to control the amount of "slosh." This technique is excellent for adding dynamic contrast and texture to different song sections.
