# Snare Drum Articulation
The placement of the stick on the snare drum head drastically alters the frequency response and "feel" of a groove, especially in [[Country_Rock_Ballad]] and R&B styles.

### Center Snare
*   **Technique**: Striking the exact center of the drum head.
*   **Sound**: Produces a warm, dry, and focused tone.
*   **Application**: Used for gentle verses and subtle [[Back-Beat|backbeats]] at a *piano* (p) dynamic.

### Rim Shot
*   **Technique**: Striking the head and the rim of the drum simultaneously.
*   **Fat Rim Shot**: The tip of the stick is just past the center, putting more "wood" on the drum. This produces a deep, full, and authoritative sound.
*   **Thin Rim Shot**: The tip of the stick is closer to the rim. This produces a higher-pitched, "pingy" sound that lacks body.
*   **Application**: Used for choruses (Letter B) and high-energy sections to provide a strong backbeat.

### Ghost Notes
When playing backbeats, special care must be taken with **[[Ghost Notes]]**. These should be kept extremely quiet to maintain the "fat" sound of the primary backbeat without cluttering the frequency space.
