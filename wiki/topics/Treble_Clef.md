# Treble Clef
The Treble Clef, also known as the G Clef, is one of the most commonly used [[Clef_Music_Notation|clefs]] in [[Music_Notation]]. It is characterized by its ornate, stylized 'G' shape.

## Function
*   **Pitch Designation**: The curl of the treble clef encircles the second line from the bottom of [[The_Staff]], thereby designating that line as **G above middle C**. All other notes on the staff are then interpreted relative to this G.
*   **Instrument Range**: It is primarily used for instruments and vocal ranges that produce higher pitches.

## Common Instruments
The treble clef is typically used for:
*   Guitars
*   Keyboards (for the right hand)
*   Violins
*   Flutes
*   Trumpets
*   Female vocal ranges (soprano, alto)

While drummers primarily use the [[Percussion_Clef]], understanding the treble clef is beneficial for reading scores that include melodic parts or for general musical literacy.
