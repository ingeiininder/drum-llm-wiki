# Articulations

Articulation refers to the specific performance method or stroke used to achieve a desired sound on the drum set. It is not enough to play the correct rhythm; a professional drummer must apply the correct articulation (and corresponding feel) for the genre and song section.

## Key Drum Articulations

### Bass Drum
- **Bury the Beater**: Keeping the beater pressed against the head. Produces a punchy, [[Staccato|staccato]] sound with heavy attack.
- **Off the Head (Open)**: Allowing the beater to rebound. Produces a round, resonant, and low tone.
- **Feathering**: Extremely quiet, consistent quarter-note pulses (common in [[Jazz|Jazz]]).

### Snare Drum
- **[[Rimshot|Rimshot]]**: Striking the center of the head and the rim simultaneously for a sharp, cutting accent.
- **Center Stroke**: Striking only the center for a warm, deep sound.
- **Cross-Stick**: Placing the stick across the head and hitting the rim to create a "wood-click" percussive sound.
- **[[Stick_Shot|Stick Shot]]**: Pressing one stick into the head and striking it with the other for a unique wood-tone attack.

### Hi-Hat
- **Tight vs. Sloshy**: Controlling the pedal pressure to vary between a crisp "chick" and a thick "chunk."
- **Shouldered Strokes**: Using the shoulder of the stick on the edge of the hi-hat for a thick, dynamically controlled tone.
- **Hi-Hat Openings**: Briefly lifting the pedal on specific [[Syncopated_Beats|syncopated beats]] to "lift" the [[Groove|groove]].

## Cymbal Articulations
- **Shouldering the Ride**: Striking the ride cymbal surface with the stick's shoulder for a full "wash" sound that covers the band.
- **Bell [[Accents|Accents]]**: Hitting the bell of the ride or crash for clear, high-frequency pulses.
- **Cymbal Muting**: Grabbing the cymbal immediately after a strike to stop the sound abruptly, creating dramatic silence.

## Marking the Form
Individual song sections are often defined by their articulations. For example:
- **Verse**: Might use a [[Tight_Hi-Hat|tight hi-hat]] and cross-stick.
- **Chorus**: Might switch to a "sloshy" hi-hat and heavy snare rimshots to heighten energy.

## Orchestration and Sound
Articulations are also used to support **Ensemble Figures**. For instance, a "long" note in a melody might be supported by a "shouldered" crash that sustains, while a "short" note might be played as a dry snare [[Rimshot|rimshot]].
