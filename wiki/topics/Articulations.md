# Articulations
**Articulations** are symbols placed above or below notes to provide specific performance instructions regarding the attack, sustain, and character of a sound. They go beyond simple rhythmic placement to define the "shape" of the note.

### Primary Drum Articulations
*   **[[Staccato]]**: Indicated by a dot. Means "short" or "detached."
*   **[[Tenuto]]**: Indicated by a horizontal line. Means "long," "held," or "legato."
*   **[[Martellato]]**: Indicated by a "roof-top" symbol (^). Means "hammered" or "fat."

### Summary Table

### Phrase Indicators
If an entire passage requires the same feel, a composer may use a bracketed word (e.g., *Staccato* or *Legato*) rather than marking every individual note.
