# Left Hand Lead (Double Strokes)
Left Hand Lead (Double Strokes) is a specific application of the [[Double Strokes: Lead Method]] where the left hand consistently initiates the double stroke pattern.

*   **Pattern**: When playing continuous [[16th Note]]s, the sticking would be LL RR LL RR...
*   **Execution**: The left hand plays two consecutive strokes (LL), followed by the right hand playing two consecutive strokes (RR), and this sequence repeats.
*   **Purpose**: This method helps develop the left hand's ability to execute clean, even double strokes consistently, which is crucial for overall hand balance and ambidexterity.

**Pro-Tip**: Many drummers have a dominant right hand, so developing the left hand's double stroke can be more challenging. Dedicate extra practice to the left hand, focusing on rebound control and evenness. A strong left-hand double stroke significantly enhances overall technique and allows for more complex [[Sticking Patterns]].
