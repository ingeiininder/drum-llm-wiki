# 32nd Note Counting Systems

While it is rare to verbally count every 32nd note at high tempos, a specific counting system is utilized during the developmental phase to ensure mathematical precision.

### The "e-&-a" Expansion
The most effective system for counting 32nd notes involves doubling the standard [[16th_Note]] syllables. Each 16th note partial (1, e, &, ah) is subdivided into two 32nd notes:

**1 - e - & - a - & - e - & - a**

In this system:
*   The first four 32nd notes (the first eighth-note [[Pulse|pulse]]) are counted: `1 e & a`
*   The second four 32nd notes (the second eighth-note [[Pulse|pulse]]) are counted: `& e & a`

### Practical Application
When practicing [[32nd_Note_Notation]], use a [[Metronome|metronome]] and focus on the larger [[Subdivision|subdivision]] of [[Eighth_Note]] pulses. Each eighth note "house" contains four 32nd notes.
