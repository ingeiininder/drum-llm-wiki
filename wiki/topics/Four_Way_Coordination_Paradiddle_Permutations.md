# Four-Way Coordination: Paradiddle Permutations

This advanced coordination study applies [[Bass Drum Permutations]] to the four standard [[Paradiddle]] variations. This method is considered a "building block for much of contemporary drumming."

### The Four Paradiddle Variations
To master these exercises, the student must first be proficient in the following stickings:
1. **Standard Paradiddle**: RLRR LRLL
2. **Inward Paradiddle**: RLLR LRRL
3. **Reverse Paradiddle**: RR LR LL RL
4. **Outward Paradiddle**: RLR L LRL R

### Application
The exercises (Ex 9.16 - 9.19) overlay these stickings on top of moving bass drum patterns. This creates a complex polyphonic texture where the hands are playing a rudiment while the foot provides a syncopated foundation.

### Practice Strategy
1. **Review Stickings**: Ensure the hand patterns are automatic before adding the feet.
2. **Isolate Permutations**: Focus on one bass drum pattern at a time. Do not try to play the full 6-bar exercise until the individual components are mastered.
3. **Add Ostinato**: The final stage is adding the [[Hi-Hat Ostinato]] (usually stepping on 2 and 4).
4. **Counting**: Count the 16th notes (1-e-&-a) out loud to ensure the bass drum notes land precisely between or on the hand strikes.

### Professional Context
These exercises are not just for technical gain; they are the basis for creating [[Advanced Fills]] and "broken" time grooves used in fusion and progressive styles.
