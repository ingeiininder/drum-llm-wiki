# New_Orleans_Contemporary_Jazz_Sound
Achieving an authentic New Orleans contemporary [[Jazz|jazz]] sound requires a departure from modern fusion or [[Rock|rock]] techniques, focusing instead on legato tones and specific drumhead [[Articulations|articulations]].

### Key Technical Components
1. **Open [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]**: To mimic the small, resonant bass drums of traditional street beats, the player must use a "non-buried" beater technique. [[Burying_the_Beater]] produces a [[Staccato|staccato]] "thud" associated with fusion, whereas an open stroke allows the drum to ring legato.
2. **Light Rim Shots**: Unlike the high-volume rim shots used in [[Rock|rock]], [[Jazz|jazz]] rim shots are played at lower [[Dynamics|dynamics]]. The goal is to make the drum "speak" and add character to the [[Snare_Drum]] tone without overpowering the ensemble.
3. **Ride Cymbal Articulation**: The [[Ride_Cymbal]] should maintain a long, open tone. [[Accents|Accents]] should be "shouldered" (using the shoulder of the stick on the bow or edge) rather than played with the tip to create a broader wash.
