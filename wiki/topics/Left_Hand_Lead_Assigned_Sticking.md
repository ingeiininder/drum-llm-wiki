# Left Hand Lead (Assigned Sticking)
In [[Assigned_Sticking]], the concept of Left Hand Lead dictates that when playing a continuous stream of [[16th_Note]]s with [[Alternating_Hands_Sticking]], the left hand is "assigned" to play the subdivisions that fall between the [[Right_Hand_Lead_Assigned_Sticking]] strokes.

*   **Pattern**: R L R L R L R L (for 1 e & a 2 e & a)
*   **Left Hand's Role**: The left hand strikes on the "e's" and "ah's" subdivisions. It effectively "fills in the spaces" created by the right hand.
*   **Visualization**: If you isolate just the left hand's part, it plays a pattern on the "e" and "ah" of each [[Beat|beat]].

This consistent assignment helps drummers internalize the rhythm and develop [[Muscle_Memory_Drumming]] for accurate [[Drum_Reading]] and performance.

**Pro-Tip**: While the right hand often carries the primary [[Pulse|pulse]] in many grooves, the left hand's precision on the "e's" and "ah's" is equally vital for a tight, even 16th-note feel. Practice developing equal strength and control in both hands to avoid a "lopsided" sound.
