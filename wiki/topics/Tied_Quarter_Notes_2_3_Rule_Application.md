# [[Tied_Quarter_Notes|Tied Quarter Notes]] (2/3 Rule Application)
The [[2_3_Rule]] often necessitates the use of [[Ties]] with [[Quarter_Note]] to maintain clarity in notation, particularly in [[4-4_Time_Signature]]. This ensures that the [[Downbeat]] of [[Beat|beat]] 3 is never obscured.

**Scenario:**
If a sound needs to last for two beats, starting on [[Beat|beat]] 2 and extending through [[Beat|beat]] 3, it cannot be notated as a single [[Half_Note]] on [[Beat|beat]] 2. A half note on [[Beat|beat]] 2 would visually "cover" [[Beat|beat]] 3, making the downbeat of 3 unclear.

**Solution:**
To correctly represent this two-[[Beat|beat]] [[Duration|duration]] while adhering to the 2/3 rule, two [[Quarter_Note]] are used, tied together:
*   A quarter note on [[Beat|beat]] 2.
*   This quarter note is tied to another quarter note on [[Beat|beat]] 3.

This notation clearly shows the attack on [[Beat|beat]] 2 and explicitly marks the downbeat of [[Beat|beat]] 3, even though the sound is continuous.

**Pro-Tip:** While the sound produced by a half note on [[Beat|beat]] 2 and two [[Tied_Quarter_Notes|tied quarter notes]] (on beats 2 and 3) is identical on a drum kit (due to short [[Sustain]]), the notation difference is crucial for [[Sight_Reading]] and understanding the rhythmic structure. Always prioritize clear notation for the performer.
