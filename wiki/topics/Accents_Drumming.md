# [[Accents|Accents]] (Drumming)
In drumming, an accent refers to a note that is played louder than the surrounding notes. [[Accents|Accents]] are crucial elements of a drummer's vocabulary, as they are one of the primary ways to introduce expression, color, and rhythmic emphasis into a performance. They make specific notes "stand apart from the rest," guiding the listener's ear and defining rhythmic patterns.

**Importance:**
*   **Expression and Color:** [[Accents|Accents]] add dynamic contrast and emotional depth to drumming.
*   **Rhythmic Definition:** They highlight specific beats or subdivisions, clarifying the rhythmic structure.
*   **[[Groove|Groove]] Enhancement:** Strategic placement of [[Accents|accents]] can significantly enhance the feel and [[Groove|groove]] of a musical passage.

**Technique for [[Accents|Accents]]:**
While various techniques can produce [[Accents|accents]], the [[Moeller_Technique]] is particularly effective due to its inherent dynamic control. An accented note typically involves a larger stroke height and more force than unaccented notes (often referred to as [[Taps_(Drumming)]]).

**Relationship with Taps and Up Strokes:**
*   **Taps:** Unaccented notes, generally played with a lower [[Stick_Height|stick height]] (e.g., one to two inches above the drumhead).
*   **Moeller Up Stroke:** A specific technique that allows a tap to precede an accented [[Moeller_Up_Stroke]], efficiently setting up the accent.

**Pro-Tip:** Practice exaggerating the dynamic difference between accented and unaccented notes. This "dynamic gap" is key to making your [[Accents|accents]] truly impactful and your drumming expressive. Ensure that the accent is produced with a relaxed, controlled motion, not brute force, to maintain good tone and prevent injury.
