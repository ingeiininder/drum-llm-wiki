# Displaced Grooves

**Displaced Grooves** involve taking a specific rhythmic sequence or [[Groove]] and shifting its starting point by a set [[Note_Value|note value]]—typically one eighth note or one 16th note—either before or after the primary downbeat.

### Conceptual Overview
The challenge of displacement is more mental than physical. It requires the drummer to maintain the internal "[[Pulse|pulse]]" while the external pattern suggests a different downbeat. This creates the illusion of an [[Odd_Meter]] shift, even though the underlying [[Time_Signature|time signature]] remains constant.

### Types of Displacement
*   **Displacement "After"**: Shifting the pattern forward in time (e.g., starting a [[Beat|beat]] on the "and" of 1).
*   **Displacement "Before"**: Shifting the pattern backward in time (e.g., starting a [[Beat|beat]] on the "ah" of the previous [[Measure|measure]]).
*   **Triplet Displacement**: Shifting patterns by an eighth-note triplet value, which is common in [[Jazz|jazz]] and fusion contexts.

### Key Principles
1.  **Know Where You Are**: Counting is non-negotiable. You must be able to resolve the displaced [[Beat|beat]] back to the original "1."
2.  **Set-ups**: Pay close attention to the transitions (fills or lead-ins) when moving from a standard groove to a displaced version.
3.  **The Illusion of Time**: Displacement is a primary tool for creating "metric tension" without actually changing the [[Tempo|tempo]] or [[Meter|meter]].

> [!TIP]
> **Pro-Tip**: When first practicing displacement, use a loud [[Metronome|metronome]] with a distinct downbeat (bell) to ensure you don't accidentally "reset" your internal clock to the new, displaced downbeat.
