# [[Funk|Funk]] and R&B Drumming

**[[Funk|Funk]] and R&B (Rhythm and [[Blues|Blues]])** are genres centered on "The Pocket"—a deep, infectious [[Groove|groove]] characterized by repetitive rhythms, intricate syncopation, and a sophisticated balance of sound. It evolved from soul and [[Blues|blues]], eventually giving birth to disco, hip-hop, and modern pop.

## The Foundation: Subdividing the [[Pulse|Pulse]]
The most critical skill for [[Funk|Funk]] drumming is **[[Subdividing]]**. To maintain a precise "pocket," drummers count or sing the smallest [[Subdivision|subdivision]] (typically **[[16th_Notes|16th notes]]**) throughout the [[Groove|groove]]. This ensures even note placement and prevents rushing or dragging during complex syncopations.

## Key [[Articulations|Articulations]] & Sounds

### 1. The Snare: Ghost Notes and Backbeats
[[Funk|Funk]] drumming is defined by its layered snare textures.
- **[[Ghost_Notes]]**: Extremely soft strokes played between the main backbeats. These "bubbles" [[Fill|fill]] the rhythmic space and provide a sense of internal momentum.
- **[[Snare_Drum_Articulation|Center Head Backbeat]]**: A warm, consistent "pop" on beats 2 and 4.
- **[[Syncopated_Backbeat]]**: [[Funk|Funk]] often moves the backbeat to unexpected locations (e.g., beats 2, 3, and the "&" of 4) to challenge the listener's expectations.

### 2. The [[Bass_Drum|Bass Drum]]: Syncopated Foundation
- **[[Syncopation|Syncopated Bass Drum Patterns]]**: Placing the kick on 16th-note upbeats (the "e" and "a") that do not necessarily align with the [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] hits.
- **[[Bass_Drum_Technique|Off the Head vs. Buried]]**: 
    - **[[Motown]]/[[Stax]] Style**: Open, resonant [[Bass_Drum|bass drum]] that blends with the bass guitar.
    - **Modern [[Funk|Funk]]-[[Rock|Rock]]**: Punchy and "[[Staccato|staccato]]" for a more aggressive, dry attack.

### 3. The [[Hi-Hat|Hi-Hat]]: Crisp & Dynamic
- **[[Hi-Hat_Technique|One-Handed 16ths]]**: Playing continuous [[16th_Notes|16th notes]] with one hand for a consistent, driving texture.
- **[[Hi-Hat_Technique|Tight vs. Loose]]**: Using a tightly closed [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] for "clean" R&B, and a "sloshy" or "half-open" [[Hi-Hat|hi-hat]] for aggressive [[Funk|Funk]]-[[Rock|Rock]].

## Stylistic Sub-Genres

### Motown & Stax (1960s-70s)
- **Sound**: Warm, balanced, and dance-oriented. No single drum should overpower the group.
- **Drummers**: **[[Benny_Benjamin]]**, **[[Al_Jackson_Jr]]**.

### [[Funk|Funk]]-[[Rock|Rock]] (Modern)
- **Sound**: Aggressive, loud, and riff-oriented. Fusion of [[Blues-Rock|blues-rock]] volume with [[Funk|funk]] syncopation.
- **Drummers**: **[[Steve_Jordan]]**, **[[Shawn_Pelton]]**.

### Indie-Pop [[Funk|Funk]] (Purdie [[Shuffle|Shuffle]])
- **Concept**: Utilizing the **[[Half-Time_Shuffle]]** (or "Purdie [[Shuffle|Shuffle]]") to create a swinging, sophisticated feel.
- **Drummers**: **[[Jason_McGerr]]**, **[[Bernard_Purdie]]**.
