# Modern Rock Drumming

**Modern Rock** is a broad stylistic category describing the rock music format prevalent on commercial radio from the 1980s through the 2000s. It synthesizes the melodic structures and guitar riffs of classic rock with the aggressive textures and "attitude" of post-Nirvana alternative rock.

### Stylistic Characteristics
- **Instrumentation**: Often utilizes the traditional guitar, bass, and drums trio format.
- **Production**: Characterized by "overblown" or high-fidelity production, featuring fat, syncopated grooves and catchy melodies.
- **Key Artists**: Muse, Green Day, Paramore, Alice in Chains, 311, and Jesus and Mary Chain.

### Performance Focus
In a Modern Rock context, the drummer must balance power with precision. The grooves often utilize [[Swung 16th Phrasing]] and complex [[Two-Handed 16th-Note Groove]] patterns to create a "fat" feel that supports heavy guitar layers.

> [!TIP]
> To achieve the "Modern Rock" sound, focus on a consistent [[Snare Rim Shot]] and "burying the beater" in the bass drum for a punchy, compressed attack.
