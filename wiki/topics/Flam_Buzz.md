# Flam-Buzz
The **Flam-Buzz** is a foundational element of [[New Orleans Second Line Drumming]]. It is characterized by a loose, unmeasured "rolled feel" rather than a strictly timed rudimental roll.

### Execution and Technique
- **The Mechanic**: Both hands perform a [[Flam]] (typically without a rim shot) just above the center of the snare head. 
- **The Flutter**: Immediately following the initial impact, the stick tips are allowed to bounce and flutter haphazardly.
- **Stylistic Application**: In a [[Swung Eighth-Note]] context, this is often used on the final beat of a measure (e.g., beat 7 in 7/4 or beat 4 in 4/4) to "bounce" or lead into a [[Five-Stroke Roll]] on beat 1 of the subsequent measure.

### Pro-Tips
- **Dynamic Control**: Maintain a loose grip to allow the sticks to vibrate freely.
- **Tempo Perspective**: At higher tempos (e.g., 173 BPM), the haphazard nature of the bounce creates a dense texture that sounds intentional and stylistic rather than sloppy.
- **Placement**: Avoid using a [[Rim Shot]] for this technique to keep the texture "thick" and "spread" rather than sharp.
