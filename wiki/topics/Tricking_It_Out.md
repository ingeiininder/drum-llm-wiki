# Tricking It Out

"Tricking it Out" refers to the process of taking a basic groove and adding technical embellishments to increase its complexity, "feel," or "flavor" without changing the fundamental structure of the beat.

### Common Applications
- **Bass Drum Doubles**: Adding quick double strokes on the [[Bass_Drum]] (e.g., on the "1" and the "2") to create a more driving, modern funk or R&B feel.
- **Tom Orchestration**: Moving the standard snare or hi-hat patterns to the toms to create a "tribal" or "jungle" feel.
- **Linear Embellishments**: Inserting [[Ghost_Notes]] or quick hand-to-foot combinations to fill the rhythmic space.

### Context
In the MI curriculum, this concept is applied to basic grooves to transition them from "Easy" to "Intermediate" or "Advanced" levels. It encourages students to experiment with [[Coordination]] while maintaining the core pulse.
