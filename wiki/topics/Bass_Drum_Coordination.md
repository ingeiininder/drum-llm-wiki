# Bass_Drum_Coordination

**Bass Drum and Hi-Hat Coordination** is the study of layered independence between the lower and upper extremities. In the MI curriculum, this is developed through progressive parts (Part 3, 4, and 5) focusing on 16th-note variations and unison playing.

### Core Concepts
*   **[[Four-Part_Counting|Four-Part Counting]]**: Essential for maintaining proper [[Subdivision]] (1-e-&-a).
*   **Ostinatos**: Fixed patterns played by the [[Hi-Hat]] (e.g., [[Quarter_Notes|quarter notes]] or 8th notes) while the bass drum plays syncopated figures.
*   **Unison Playing**: The goal of playing the [[Snare_Drum]] and [[Bass_Drum]] at the exact same time without "flamming."

### Progression
1.  **Basic Coordination**: Simple 8th and 16th note bass drum patterns against a steady hi-hat.
2.  **Layered Snare Integration**: Adding alternating snare drum strokes over the foot patterns.
3.  **Accented Unisons**: Playing [[Accents|accents]] on the snare drum that align perfectly with every bass drum note to develop "locked" coordination.

### Pro-Tip
Focus on your balance on the drum throne. If you are leaning too far forward or back, your feet will lose the ability to play "chippy" hi-hat sounds and authoritative bass drum strokes. Use a mirror to ensure your posture is centered.
