# Orchestrating Ensemble Figures
When reading a drum chart, "orchestration" refers to the drummer's choice of which voices to use to execute written rhythmic figures. 

### Key Considerations
1.  **Note Length**: Look at the duration of the written notes. 
    *   **Long Notes**: Usually played on [[Crash Cymbal]]s with a [[Bass Drum]] hit to provide sustain.
    *   **Short Notes**: Usually played on the [[Snare Drum]] or [[Hi-Hat]] (closed) to provide a "staccato" feel.
2.  **Subdivision**: For syncopated figures, it is critical to subdivide (usually 16th notes in R&B/Funk or triplets in [[Jazz Drumming Overview]]) to ensure the figure sits perfectly within the groove.
3.  **Mimicry**: Often, drum fills are written to mimic a recurring rhythmic figure played by the guitar and bass. In these cases, play the figure exactly as written without alteration to reinforce the ensemble's "hit."

### Common Directives
*   **On D.S. Only**: This instruction indicates that a specific figure or fill should only be played during the "Dal Segno" (repeat) section, not the first time through. This is a common tool used to keep charts concise.
*   **Build-Up Fills**: Often involve a crescendo using the snare and toms (e.g., eighth-note builds) to transition between sections. Avoid "flamming" (unintentional double hits) between the hands and the bass drum during these builds.
