# Drag Rudiments Overview

Drag-based rudiments are essential for developing "inner-beat" dexterity and control over [[Diddle]] quality. The goal is not immediate speed, but the ability to maintain consistent stick heights between grace notes and primary strokes.

### Key Drag Rudiments
1. **Single Drag Tap**: A drag followed by an accented tap on the opposite hand.
2. **Double Drag Tap**: Incorporates two drags before the primary accented tap.
3. **Lesson 25**: A specific rhythmic pattern (often counted as "1-and-a-2") starting with a drag on the upbeat.
4. **Single Dragadiddle**: Unique among drag rudiments because the diddle itself is accented.
5. **Drag Paradiddle #1**: A variation of the [[Paradiddle]] incorporating a drag on the first note.
6. **Single Ratamacue**: A triplet-based rudiment (llRLRL) that is a staple of rudimental drumming.

### Practice Strategy
- **Isolation**: Practice the grace notes (diddles) on a different surface (e.g., a pillow or a different drum) than the primary note to isolate the responsibility of each hand.
- **Subdivision**: Ensure you are evenly subdividing the drag as 32nd notes rather than a crushed buzz stroke.
