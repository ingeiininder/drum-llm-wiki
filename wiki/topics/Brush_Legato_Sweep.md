# Brush Legato Sweep

The foundation of [[Brush_Technique]] is the smooth, legato sweep. This motion creates the "sandpaper" sustain that defines jazz ballad and swing playing.

### Execution Guidelines
1.  **Motion**: The motion should be more horizontal than vertical. Avoid "pecking" at the drum head.
2.  **Pressure**: Let the brush rest lightly against the head. Avoid pushing into the head unless a specific "accent" or "crunch" effect is desired.
3.  **Subdivision**: Always feel the underlying [[Triplet_Subdivision]] beneath the quarter-note pulse to ensure the sweep remains "in the pocket."
4.  **The Pattern**:
    *   **Left Hand (LH)**: Typically handles the continuous circular or lateral sweep to provide the "swish" sound.
    *   **Right Hand (RH)**: Often provides the rhythmic pulse (quarter notes or the [[Ride_Pattern]]).

**Pro-Tip**: Practice the sweep alone with no pulse or accents first. The goal is a seamless, fluid sound where the change in direction is inaudible.
