# Punk-Pop Performance Style

The Punk-Pop style is characterized by high-energy, authoritative playing and clear structural shifts between song sections. In this curriculum, the style is explored through an [[ABAB_Form|ABAB form]] augmented by a [[Fermata]] introduction and a [[Double_Time_Feel]] in the B sections.

### Structural Characteristics
*   **Section A**: Typically features an [[Eight-Bar_Phrase|eight-bar phrase]] with a medium-heavy eighth-note [[Hi-Hat]] [[Groove|groove]]. It utilizes "four-on-the-floor" [[Bass_Drum]] patterns and strong [[Snare_Drum]] backbeats.
*   **Section B**: Shifts to a [[Double_Time_Feel]] using a [[Sloshy_Hi_Hat]] (half-open) articulation. This section creates musical excitement by moving the backbeat to the "&" of each [[Beat|beat]].
*   **Section C**: Often used for guitar solos, this section maintains the double-[[Time_Feel|time feel]] but shifts the time-keeping surface to the [[Ride_Cymbal_Bell]].

### Performance [[Dynamics|Dynamics]]
The style demands a consistent **Forte** (loud) dynamic. Precision in [[Articulations]] is required to ensure the "punchy" nature of the genre is maintained, specifically through the use of [[Rimshot|Rim Shots]] and heavy bass drum strokes.

---PRO-TIP---
To propel the music forward in Section A, ensure the hi-hat openings on the "&" of each [[Beat|beat]] are "choked" quickly by closing the hats precisely on the following downbeat. This creates a "karate-chop" rhythmic effect.
