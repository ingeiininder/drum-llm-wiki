# Shouldering Technique

**Shouldering** is a cymbal striking technique used to create a full, "washy" sound and control overtones. It is a staple in both [[Jazz_Drumming]] and [[Bossa_Nova]].

### Execution
*   **Ride Cymbal**: Use the shoulder of the drumstick to strike the top surface of the cymbal rather than the tip.
*   **Crash Cymbal**: Use the shoulder to strike the outermost edge of the cymbal.

### Stylistic Application
In a Brazilian context, shouldering is used to accentuate "long notes" in a melody. It provides a broader sonic footprint that mimics the duration of tied notes or half notes played by melodic instruments like the guitar or saxophone.

> [!TIP]
> **Pro-Tip**: When shouldering, do not hit harder to get more volume. The goal is a change in *texture* and *duration*, not necessarily intensity.
