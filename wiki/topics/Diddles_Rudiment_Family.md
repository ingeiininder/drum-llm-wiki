# Diddles (Rudiment Family)
The [[Diddles_Rudiment_Family]] is one of the five [[Rudiment_Families]] and is characterized by the use of "diddles," which are two consecutive notes played with the same hand. These [[Rudiments|rudiments]] are crucial for developing stick control, hand-to-hand coordination, and creating intricate rhythmic patterns.

**Core Concept:**
A diddle involves a double stroke played by a single hand (e.g., RR or LL). The arrangement of these diddles with single strokes forms the various [[Rudiments|rudiments]] within this family.

**Examples (as mentioned in OCR):**
*   [[Paradiddle]]
*   [[Double_Paradiddle|Double Paradiddle]]

**Application:**
Diddles are incredibly versatile and form the basis for many grooves, fills, and solo ideas. The [[Paradiddle]], in particular, is one of the most fundamental and widely used [[Rudiments|rudiments]], offering a natural alternation of leading hands while maintaining a consistent rhythmic flow.

**Pro-Tip:** When practicing diddles, focus on making the two strokes from the same hand sound even in volume and spacing. The second stroke of a diddle often relies on rebound, so ensure your grip is relaxed enough to allow the stick to bounce freely. This will help prevent tension and promote fluidity.
