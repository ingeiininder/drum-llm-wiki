# Herta (Hairta)

The **Herta** (spelled "Hairta" in some MI curricula) is a modern hybrid [[Rudiment]] that has become a staple of contemporary drumset vocabulary. It is an alternating-stroke rudiment where one hand plays a double stroke while the other plays singles, creating a rapid "galloping" effect.

### Sticking Pattern
The Herta is essentially a three-note motive played within a four-note or triplet grouping:
*   **Standard Sticking**: R L R L (where the first two notes are twice as fast) or **R L R L** alternating.
*   **MI Definition**: An alternating-stroke rudiment where one hand plays doubles and the other plays singles.

### Rhythmic Forms
*   **Triplet Herta**: Played within a triplet grid, often used in fills.
*   **Sixteenth-Note Herta**: Played within a 16th-note grid, creating a highly syncopated feel.

### Usage
Hertas are frequently used in:
*   **Grooves**: Adding excitement to a standard backbeat.
*   **Fills/Solos**: Creating high-velocity bursts that are easier to execute than pure [[Single Stroke Roll]]s.

### Common Pitfalls
The most common mistake is "crushing" the double strokes, which causes the rudiment to sound like a [[Flam]]. The goal is an "open" and "breathy" sound where every note is distinct.

### Pro-Tip
Practice Hertas to a 16th-note click. Ensure the double strokes are not rushed. This rudiment is equally effective when orchestrated between the hands and the [[Bass Drum]].
