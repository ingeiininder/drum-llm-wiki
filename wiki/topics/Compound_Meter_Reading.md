# Compound [[Meter|Meter]] Reading

Compound [[Meter|meter]] refers to time signatures where the basic pulse is divisible by three. In the context of [[Drum_Reading]], this primarily focuses on [[6/8_Time]], [[3/8_Time]], and [[12/8_Time]].

### Core Concepts
In these meters, the [[Eighth_Note]] typically serves as the underlying pulse, while the [[Dotted_Quarter_Note]] represents the primary [[Beat|beat]] (the "macro-[[Beat|beat]]").

- **[[3/8_Time]]**: A single [[Beat|beat]] of compound time (one dotted quarter [[Note_Value|note value]]).
- **[[6/8_Time]]**: Two beats of compound time.
- **[[12/8_Time]]**: Four beats of compound time, often feeling like a "triplet-based" version of 4/4.

### [[Phrasing|Phrasing]] Levels
Developing proficiency in compound [[Meter|meter]] requires practicing rhythms across various phrase lengths to improve musical "sight-span":
1. **One-Bar Phrases**: Focus on internalizing the two-[[Beat|beat]] feel of 6/8.
2. **Two-Bar Phrases**: Developing the ability to read across the [[Bar_Line|bar line]].
3. **Four-Bar Phrases**: Essential for understanding standard musical periods and song structures.

### Practice Methodology
When practicing exercises such as **Ex 3.11** through **Ex 3.15**, students should utilize a dual-[[Metronome|metronome]] approach:
- **Eighth Note Click**: Set between 150–190 BPM to ensure subdivision accuracy.
- **Dotted Quarter Note Click**: Set between 60–100 BPM to develop the "big [[Beat|beat]]" feel and internalize the [[Pulse]].

> [!TIP]
> Always count the [[Eighth_Notes|eighth notes]] out loud (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6) when first approaching these exercises to ensure the [[Subdivision]] is perfectly even before transitioning to counting only the dotted quarter pulses.
