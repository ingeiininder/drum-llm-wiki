# Compound Meter Reading

Compound meter refers to time signatures where the basic pulse is divisible by three. In the context of [[Drum Reading 2]], this primarily focuses on [[6/8 Time]], [[3/8 Time]], and [[12/8 Time]].

### Core Concepts
In these meters, the [[Eighth Note]] typically serves as the underlying pulse, while the [[Dotted Quarter Note]] represents the primary beat (the "macro-beat").

- **[[3/8 Time]]**: A single beat of compound time (one dotted quarter note value).
- **[[6/8 Time]]**: Two beats of compound time.
- **[[12/8 Time]]**: Four beats of compound time, often feeling like a "triplet-based" version of 4/4.

### Phrasing Levels
Developing proficiency in compound meter requires practicing rhythms across various phrase lengths to improve musical "sight-span":
1. **One-Bar Phrases**: Focus on internalizing the two-beat feel of 6/8.
2. **Two-Bar Phrases**: Developing the ability to read across the bar line.
3. **Four-Bar Phrases**: Essential for understanding standard musical periods and song structures.

### Practice Methodology
When practicing exercises such as **Ex 3.11** through **Ex 3.15**, students should utilize a dual-metronome approach:
- **Eighth Note Click**: Set between 150–190 BPM to ensure subdivision accuracy.
- **Dotted Quarter Note Click**: Set between 60–100 BPM to develop the "big beat" feel and internalize the [[Pulse]].

> [!TIP]
> Always count the eighth notes out loud (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6) when first approaching these exercises to ensure the [[Subdivision]] is perfectly even before transitioning to counting only the dotted quarter pulses.
