# Standard Four-Stroke Ruff
The [[Standard Four-Stroke Ruff]] is a variation of the [[Four-Stroke Ruff]] where the rudiment consists of four alternating strokes: the first three are unaccented grace notes, and the fourth stroke is accented.

**Execution:**
The basic sticking pattern for a [[Standard Four-Stroke Ruff]] is typically:
*   **R L R L** (first three soft, fourth loud)
*   **L R L R** (first three soft, fourth loud)

**Practice Variations:**
1.  **Eighth Note Triplets and Quarter Notes:** Practice the rudiment using these note values to develop rhythmic precision.
    *   *Example (Ex 4.5):* R L R L R L R L (first three soft, fourth loud, repeating)
2.  **Accents on Beats 1 and 3:** Practice shifting the accents to fall on specific beats (e.g., beats 1 and 3) to develop rhythmic placement and internal clock.
    *   *Example (Ex 4.6):* (Accent on 1, then 3)
3.  **Wrist Isolation:** Just as with the [[Three-Stroke Ruff]], these exercises can be used to isolate wrist movement by practicing with each hand individually.
    *   *Example (based on Ex 4.5):* R R R R R R R R (first three soft, fourth loud, repeating with only the right hand)

**Pro-Tip:** The challenge with three grace notes is maintaining their softness and evenness before the accent. Think of them as a quick "scramble" leading into the main note. Ensure your grip is loose enough to allow the stick to bounce for the grace notes, and then snap down for the accent.
