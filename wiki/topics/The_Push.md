# The Push (Rhythmic Anticipation)

A **"Push"** occurs when an [[Ensemble_Figures|Ensemble Figure]] is placed on the "ah" (the fourth partial) of a 16th-note [[Subdivision|subdivision]], or the "&" of an eighth-note [[Subdivision|subdivision]], just before a new bar or a chord change.

### Musical Effect
The "push" creates the illusion that the upcoming downbeat or chord change has arrived early. It adds forward momentum and energy to a transition. 

### Execution Tips
- **[[Subdivision|Subdivision]] is Key**: Because the "ah" is so close to the following downbeat, it is easy to "crush" the note or play it too late. Constant 16th-note mental counting is required.
- **Ghosting the Downbeat**: In some notations (e.g., IMG_0957), the downbeat following a push is written in parentheses (a [[Ghost_Notes]]). This indicates that the note is tied over from the "ah" and should not be re-struck on the repeat.
- **Coordination**: When playing a push on the "ah" of [[Beat|beat]] 4, the drummer must ensure the [[Crash_Cymbal]] and [[Bass_Drum]] align perfectly to "push" the band into the next section.
