# Downbeat [[Ensemble_Figures|Ensemble Figures]]

Downbeat [[Ensemble_Figures|Ensemble Figures]] are rhythmic hits that occur on the primary pulses of a [[Measure|measure]] (1, 2, 3, or 4). These are the most common figures found in standard drum charts.

### Execution by [[Note_Value|Note Value]]

#### [[Quarter_Notes|Quarter Notes]]
Quarter-note figures on downbeats are usually supported by a [[Bass_Drum]] and [[Crash_Cymbal]]. 
- **[[Beat|Beat]] 2 and 4**: These often coincide with the standard [[Back-Beat]] ([[Snare_Drum|Snare Drum]]). In these cases, the snare is paired with the cymbal crash.

#### Half Notes and Whole Notes
These longer note values require longer [[Set-up_Fill]].
- **Half Note**: Worth two beats; should be preceded by a two-[[Beat|beat]] [[Fill|fill]].
- **Whole Note**: Worth four beats; should be preceded by a four-[[Beat|beat]] [[Fill|fill]].

### The 2/3 Imaginary [[Bar_Line|Bar Line]] Rule
When notating a half note starting on [[Beat|Beat]] 2, it is often written as two [[Tied_Quarter_Notes|tied quarter notes]]. This adheres to the **2/3 Imaginary [[Bar_Line|Bar Line]] Rule**, which dictates that the middle of a 4/4 [[Measure|measure]] (the "invisible wall" between beats 2 and 3) should be visible to make the music easier to read.

### Summary of Set-ups
- **Figure on [[Beat|Beat]] 1**: [[Target_Beats|Target beats]] 3 and 4 (for a half note) or [[Beat|beat]] 4 (for a quarter note).
- **Figure on [[Beat|Beat]] 3**: [[Target_Beats|Target beats]] 1 and 2 (for a half note) or [[Beat|beat]] 2 (for a quarter note).
