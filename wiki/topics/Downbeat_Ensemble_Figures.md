# Downbeat Ensemble Figures

Downbeat Ensemble Figures are rhythmic hits that occur on the primary pulses of a measure (1, 2, 3, or 4). These are the most common figures found in standard drum charts.

### Execution by Note Value

#### Quarter Notes
Quarter-note figures on downbeats are usually supported by a [[Bass Drum]] and [[Crash Cymbal]]. 
- **Beat 2 and 4**: These often coincide with the standard [[Backbeat]] (Snare Drum). In these cases, the snare is paired with the cymbal crash.

#### Half Notes and Whole Notes
These longer note values require longer [[Set-up Fills]].
- **Half Note**: Worth two beats; should be preceded by a two-beat fill.
- **Whole Note**: Worth four beats; should be preceded by a four-beat fill.

### The 2/3 Imaginary Bar Line Rule
When notating a half note starting on Beat 2, it is often written as two tied quarter notes. This adheres to the **2/3 Imaginary Bar Line Rule**, which dictates that the middle of a 4/4 measure (the "invisible wall" between beats 2 and 3) should be visible to make the music easier to read.

### Summary of Set-ups
- **Figure on Beat 1**: Target beats 3 and 4 (for a half note) or beat 4 (for a quarter note).
- **Figure on Beat 3**: Target beats 1 and 2 (for a half note) or beat 2 (for a quarter note).
