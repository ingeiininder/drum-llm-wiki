# Progressive Metal Drumming

**Progressive Metal** (Prog Metal) is a sub-genre that fuses the intensity of heavy metal with the complexity of progressive rock. Key pioneers include Queensryche and King's X, with modern evolution by bands like Periphery and Opeth.

### Technical Hallmarks
- **Polyrhythms**: Playing two or more conflicting rhythms simultaneously (e.g., groupings of three over a 4/4 pulse).
- **Metric Modulation**: Shifting the pulse based on a previous subdivision.
- **Odd Time Signatures**: Frequent use of 5/4, 7/8, or shifting meters.
- **Counter-Rhythms**: The drums often play patterns that contrast with the guitar and bass riffs rather than simply mirroring them.

### Groupings of Three in 4/4
A common Prog Metal technique involves playing a pattern based on **three quarter notes** within a 4/4 frame.
- **The Challenge**: Because 3 does not divide evenly into 4, the "1" of the pattern will shift its position in the bar across a multi-bar phrase.
- **Critical Skill**: You must be able to count the underlying 4/4 pulse while playing the "3-based" pattern. If you switch to counting in 3/4, you will lose your place in the chart and miss subsequent section cues.
