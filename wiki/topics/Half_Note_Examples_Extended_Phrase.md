# Half Note Examples Extended Phrase
Extended phrases often utilize [[Dotted_Half_Note]] and [[Ties]] to create rhythmic patterns that span across multiple measures, commonly referred to as "over the bar" phrasing. This is particularly effective when a three-beat pattern is systematically repeated in [[4-4_Time_Signature]].

**How it Works:**
*   A [[Dotted Half Note]] is worth three [[Quarter Note]]s (three beats).
*   When a three-beat pattern is repeated in 4/4 time, it will naturally shift its starting point relative to the [[Downbeat]] of each measure.
*   For example, a pattern starting on beat 1 will then start on beat 4 (of the previous measure, tied over), then beat 3, then beat 2, and so on, as it "travels over the bar line."

**Notation Methods:**
*   **With Dotted Half Notes and Ties**: This method combines the efficiency of dotted notes with the clarity of ties to show the three-beat groupings while maintaining readability across measures.
*   **With Ties Exclusively**: This method uses only [[Ties]] to connect notes, explicitly showing each beat and subdivision. While sometimes more visually complex, it strictly adheres to rules like the [[2_3_Rule]] by ensuring all downbeats are clear.

**Pro-Tip:** When playing "over the bar" phrases, it's crucial to feel the repeating rhythmic motif (e.g., the three-beat pattern) as a continuous entity, rather than restarting your mental count at each bar line. This helps create a fluid, driving feel and prevents the rhythm from sounding choppy.
