# Half Note Examples Extended Phrase
Extended phrases often utilize [[Dotted_Half_Note]] and [[Ties]] to create rhythmic patterns that span across multiple measures, commonly referred to as "over the bar" [[Phrasing|phrasing]]. This is particularly effective when a three-[[Beat|beat]] pattern is systematically repeated in [[4-4_Time_Signature]].

**How it Works:**
*   A [[Dotted_Half_Note]] is worth three [[Quarter_Note]]s (three beats).
*   When a three-[[Beat|beat]] pattern is repeated in 4/4 time, it will naturally shift its starting point relative to the [[Downbeat]] of each [[Measure|measure]].
*   For example, a pattern starting on [[Beat|beat]] 1 will then start on [[Beat|beat]] 4 (of the previous [[Measure|measure]], tied over), then [[Beat|beat]] 3, then [[Beat|beat]] 2, and so on, as it "travels over the [[Bar_Line|bar line]]."

**Notation Methods:**
*   **With Dotted Half Notes and Ties**: This method combines the efficiency of [[Dotted_Notes|dotted notes]] with the clarity of ties to show the three-[[Beat|beat]] groupings while maintaining readability across measures.
*   **With Ties Exclusively**: This method uses only [[Ties]] to connect notes, explicitly showing each [[Beat|beat]] and [[Subdivision|subdivision]]. While sometimes more visually complex, it strictly adheres to rules like the [[2_3_Rule]] by ensuring all downbeats are clear.

**Pro-Tip:** When playing "over the bar" phrases, it's crucial to feel the repeating rhythmic motif (e.g., the three-[[Beat|beat]] pattern) as a continuous entity, rather than restarting your mental count at each [[Bar_Line|bar line]]. This helps create a fluid, driving feel and prevents the rhythm from sounding choppy.
