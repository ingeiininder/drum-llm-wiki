Eighth notes are equal to one half of a beat: By dividing quarter note (equal to one beat) in half; the byproduct is now two eighth notes (or two half beats): Ex 2.2

To further clarify; by splitting every quarter note within a measure (i.e: four beats) in half, we receive eight eighth notes In other words, it is called an eighth note because one eighth note takes up one eighth of a measure (and there can be a maximum of eight within a measure).
