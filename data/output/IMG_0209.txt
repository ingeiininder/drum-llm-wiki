Chapter 2: Performance Notes and Sound The focal point of the particular rock shuffle is the use of the floor tom for a riding surface: It is very important that you not strike the floor tom too hard: You want to find the sweet spot where the drum rumbles at an even keel without any notes standing out: If this is not achieved, then the groove will sound lopsided and sloppy The challenge with this concept is that while you are maintaining a medium volume on the floor tom you also need to hit the snare drum hard on beats 2 and 4. Ex 10.2 Snare drum loud on 2 and 4
Floor tom medium volume every beat
Flams and Double Stops A recurring fill appears three times (bars 8, 16 and 25). This fill consists ofthe bass drum maintaining the four- on-the-floor and the hands filling in the upbeats on the toms (and/or snare/cymbals) Technically speaking; the fill has flams on the toms on the & of 1,2 and 3. On beat 4 the flams come together and form double stops between the snare and crash cymbal (Note: In measure 25 this part is played between the floor tom and rack tom) The flams should be played as right hand flams (right hand is the primary stroke) and should be very wide and open: Ex 10.3
