Unit eight reading | techNIQUe perfOrmance Chapter 1: Basic Concepts Sixteenth note-based ensemble figures (short and/or long notes beginning on the"e" or"ah"ofa beat) need clear set-up fills in order for the rest ofan ensemble to execute the figures with precision In this unit we will look at the general target beats for setting up ensemble figures on the "e" and "ah" of all beats
The "e" of a Beat When setting up ensemble figures on the"e"of a beat; a clearly played downbeat must be present just prior to the ensemble figure. When reading music; everyone in the ensemble is reading ahead to prepare for the next sections, phrases and rhythms When a figure on the "e" of a beat arises, the natural tendency for the performer is to slightly rush this figure With a clear downbeat played just before the figure (on the"e"), the proper amount of space gets occupied, making all performers wait just long enough before executing the figure: In addition, a 16th-note-based fill will also help all performers subdivide 16th notes, again assisting in proper beat placement:
