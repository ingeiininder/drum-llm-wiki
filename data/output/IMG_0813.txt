Unit twO reading | techNIque perfOrmance Chapter 1: Drag Technique Drags are two grace notes that are tied to a primary note Traditionally, drags do not have a specific note value associated with them, and they can be interpreted as a tight buzz or an open diddle. For our purposes in studying drumset; we will play drags as open diddles with a corresponding note value: Much like the flam technique of alternating the hands with up-stroke and down-stroke positioning; alternating drags apply the same sequential motion (Ex 2.1). Rather than playing one grace note in an up- stroke position, you will now be playing a low diddle: Use the"catch and release" method discussed in Drum Technique 1, Unit 7 to execute the low diddles Once the diddle has been played, raise the hand into a down-stroke position: Following the diddle will be the primary note played by the opposite hand coming from its down-stroke position: Ex 2.1
Much like the flams; hand-to-hand drags can be very challenging because of the various strokes they incorporate: Each hand will be playing an accent followed by a low diddle; this will utilize the Moeller down and up stroke as well as the"catch and release" method for the diddles As each hand alternates; focus on the position of each corresponding down-stroke and up-stroke position, as they: should look the same Keep the diddles low and the primary notes high.
