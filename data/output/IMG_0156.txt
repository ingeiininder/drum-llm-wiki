In this chart; both sections utilize the same drum sound: an open (yet syncopated) bass drum alongside a dark-sounding snare backbeat and chunky 16th-note hi-hat groove Let's take a look at how you can achieve these articulations:
Bass Drum The bass drum pattern should be played very forcefully,with an open articulation This will be accomplished by utilizing two important strategies They are:
1 Using the felt side of (or a soft-surfaced) bass drum beater: This gives each pedal strokes impact a round sound (with less attack and punch) This is the perfect option for adding a full, low tone to a song:
2. Playing the bass drum with an open "off the head" (non- buried) stroke. After each stroke it is also very important to keep the beater away from (not buried into) the drumhead Not only does this allow the drum to resonate more; but it also allows the pitch of the drum to remain lower as well  Important: you must make sure to control the bass-drum beater and footboard, and avoid any unwanted "felt-flutter" between the beater and drum head You must "rest and release"both your foot and the beater between strokes This is very different from the buried approach, because you cannot keep your leg raised in "attack" position between strokes.
