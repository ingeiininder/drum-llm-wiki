Chapter 2: Performance Notes and Sound It is not enough to merely play the correct patterns; you must play the correct patterns with the correct sound; feel, and form: With this in mind, we will break down each section, demonstrating the appropriate hi-hat; snare; and bass drum articulations: Ex 8.6 Letter A
Light; Tight Closed Hi-Hat
Just as the A section has a sonic treatment; phrase length, and chord progression,it will also have a particular drum sound. Therefore, this section features a light; two-bar eighth-note hi-hat groove alongside very staccato/syncopated bass drum and strong snare backbeat Let's take a look at howyou can achieve these articulations:
Bass Drum The bass drum pattern should be played very forcefully;with a staccato articulation and a dynamic of mezzo- forte (medium-loud) This will be accomplished by utilizing three important strategies They are as follows: 1 Using the felt side of (or a soft-surfaced) bass drum beater: Many pop drummers make this choice because it gives each pedal strokes impact a round sound This is the perfect option for adding a full and low tone to a song; while still providing the rhythmic foundation: 2_ Burying the bass drum beater into the bass drum head: After each stroke it is also very important to keep the beater pushed (or buried) into the drum head: Not only does this muffle the drum slightly, but it adds a slight element of attack to the initial stroke (and compliments the soft-surfaced bass-drum beater). However; you must make sure to firmly press the beater into the head to avoid any unwanted "felt flutter" between the beater and drum head. 3. Use a combination of your leg and ankle: Now that you have correct attack articulations, it is also important to get a loud and powerful tone from the drum: Most drummers use their ankle for each stroke; which results in a fast and accurate tone. However; this technique is not appropriate for loud rock and metal styles because it lacks projection; force, and power: In order to remedy this, it is suggested that you use your entire leg (and foot) to make each stroke:
