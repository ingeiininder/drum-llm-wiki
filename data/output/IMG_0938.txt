In this example (Ex. 6.7), at letter B, there is a shorthand indicator that instructs to reader to play eight bars ("Play 8 ). Since this is a solo section; the drummer is given the freedom to play an appropriate part and interact accordingly with the soloist: No bar lines, time slashes or rhythms are used:
This also allows the drummer to relax from all the terminology and notation traditionally used in a chart: A common mistake young drummers make (when developing their reading skills) is to get fixated on the chart and stop listening to what is going on around them: A skilled reader can glance at a chart; retain the information and continue to interact with the band.
