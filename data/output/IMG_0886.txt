Note Length Note length is another essential aspect of ensemble figure interpretation While it is vitally important to know when a note begins, it is equally important to be aware of when the note ends
Most drummers are introduced to playing and reading rhythms on a practice pad. While a pad is a great tool to get the essentials (rudiments, grips and strokes) together; it is not a great device to help one fully understand note length: This should not be confused with playing a snare drum roll to elongate a note: Roll usage will be addressed in Unit 6.

You will notice that every note played sounded the same: a sound on the downbeat of with no sustain: Now play the same rhythms on a cymbal, grabbing (choking) the cymbal on each rest. Ex 1.16

As you can hear; the various downbeat based note lengths are quite different when played with a cymbal.
In the example below (Ex 1.17) the dotted quarter note on beat one lasts (or sounds) for one and a half beats (three eighth notes) and the eighth note tied to a half note lasts for two and a half beats (five eighth notes). Obviously; subdividing is essential when reading ensemble figures. Since both notes last for over one beat, you will need to play these figures on an instruments capable of duration: cymbals! Ex 1.17
