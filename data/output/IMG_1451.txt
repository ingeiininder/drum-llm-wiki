Chapter 2: Drum Soloing Concepts Drum solos are a chance for the drummer to shine; be creative and add excitement to a piece of music: When taking a drum solo it is very easy to get overwhelmed with ideas, therefore you must think ahead and have a seed of an idea to start your solo. You can then develop your idea in similar fashion to the concepts presented in this book (compression/expansion; over-the-bar ideas, etc ).
One way to build your own repertoire of"go to"ideas is to research past drummers""go to"ideas. Drummers such as Philly Joe Jones, Steve Gadd, Tony Williams, John Bonham, Neil Peart and Steve Jordan have provided the drumming community with classic concepts that are essential to the development of great drum soloing:
Drum solos can be categorized as follows: The "open" solo: An extended, unaccompanied drum solo that typically changes tempos and has rubato (free-form, out-of-time) sections Soloing over the form: Common in the jazz idiom, this style of solo follows the form (AABA, ABAB, Blues; etc) of the tune just as a melodic instrumentalist would: Soloing over a rhythmic vamp: A drum solo over a set of ensemble figures, usually in a two or four-bar pattern: Groove-based solo: This drum solo is primarily a groove with variations and fill ideas either unaccompanied or over a bass line:
