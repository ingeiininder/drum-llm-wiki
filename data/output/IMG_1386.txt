Unit three reading | techNIque perfOrMance Chapter 1: Developing a Closed Roll The closed roll consists of consecutive and alternating multiple bounce strokes Each stroke has an undefined number of notes that are produced by pushing the stick into the drumhead (or practice pad) using careful amount of fulcrum pressure: The number of bounces is generated by the pressure you use on your fulcrum- the more pressure you use; the less amount of bounces Please note that"pressure" does not mean "rigidity" or"tension"Therefore,it is important that you focus on maintaining even pressure within the hands when developing closed rolls. The closed roll is another rudiment that once mastered-_will open up your ability to be very creative and expressive in your drumming: There are many names for the closed roll: multiple bounce roll; buzz roll and press roll are a few common terms:
Note: closed rolls are notated in different styles For our purposes, the closed roll will be indicated with a"z" attached to the stem as follows: Ex 3.1
To begin developing a smooth closed roll; play one bar of quarter notes with alternating sticking; followed by a bar of closed quarter-note rolls also with alternating sticking: The goal is to try and make each rolled quarter note "bleed" into the next one by producing as many rebounds as possible with each roll stroke: Play at 60 BPM: Ex 3.2
