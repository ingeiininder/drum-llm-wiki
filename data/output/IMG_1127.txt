Half Open Hi-Hat Sound Over the past two books we have explored closed (+) (with varying degrees of sound) and open (0) hi-hat sounds In this chart we now see a new symbol: 0. This symbol instructs us to play the hi-hat half open and very"sloshy". A common characteristic in R&B/hip-hop drumming is to play open-handed with the left hand on the hi-hat and the right hand travelling around the toms This is a great effect to use when you need to "build" into a bigger fill. Drop-Outs Another common technique within this genre is the concept of"drop-outs" Drop-outs are short interruptions ofa groove that last for about a beat or two. This concept developed along with DJs'use of drum machines, MPCs, loops and vinyl records. The producer would push "mute" on the mixing console (while the groove was playing) and upon its return, the groove did not restart; but rather continue (as if it has never stopped) There is one drop-out in this chart; in bar 10 of letter A (bar 15 of the entire chart) For example: Ex 3.12 Regular Groove Drop-out
