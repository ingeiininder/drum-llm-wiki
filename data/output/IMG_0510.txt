Unit ONe reading | techNIQUe perfOrmance Chapter 1: Fulcrum & American Grip Fulcrum The fulcrum is the pivot point for the drumstick within your grip. The thumb and the forefinger should be across from each other (Ex 1.1), not wrapped (Ex 1.2). This fulcrum should be positioned about one-third up from the bottom of the stick_-this will be the"sweet spot" ofthe stick Ifthe fulcrum is too far up the stick, it will not be able to develop enough momentum: If the fulcrum is too far back, the stick will feel top-heavy and will have a very sluggish response: An easy way to discover the sweet spot is to use your hand to physically bounce the stick against the head while maintaining a loose fulcrum: Ex 1.1 Ex 1.2
The rest ofthe fingers should be gently wrapped around the stick, resting on the first crease (knuckle) of each respective finger (Ex: 1.3). This positions the stick for proper finger technique; which will be addressed in future units. Ex 1.3
