Splashing the Hi-Hat Splashing the hi-hat with the left foot helps create a metallic pad of sound (or sheen) underneath fills and grooves This is notated by a small "open" circle below the notehead; like this: Ex 6.10
To splash the hi-hat; simply give the hi-hat pedal a terse kick with the heel. This will allow the cymbals to clang together in a gentle but stern fashion. After the initial kick, you then gently place your foot back on the pedal leaving just enough space between the cymbals to maintain the sound:

Practice Note: Subdividing at Slow Tempos At an extremely slow tempo; many (ifnot all) professional drummers subdivide a smaller subdivision/note value than the main rhythms notated in the chart This allows them to stay in time and not rush the quarter note pulse; while maintaining a consistent sound (and, of course, accurately reading the chart) Therefore, it is extremely important that you practice this section (and entire chart) while subdividing eighth-note triplets Please spend equal amounts of time playing the chart with your click track on eighth-note triplets and on quarter notes while counting eighth-note triplets to yourself Together; these practice techniques will help you to master this tempo, swing feel and chart.
