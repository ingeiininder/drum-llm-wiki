Chapter 2: Performance Notes and Sound Groove 1 The A1 12-bar form is played on the ride cymbal and utilizes a rimshot articulation on the snare drum, alongside strong standard (either "bury-the-beater" or "off the head") bass drum sound. The ride plays continuous eighth note triplets, the snare drum is on beats 2 and 4,and the bass drum plays a one-bar pattern: For example: Ex 6.11
Al Ride Cymbal 3
While on the ride cymbal, please experiment with different hi-hat foot permutations such as closing onbeats 2and4 (matching the snare) or closing on all eighth note triplets (matching the ride pattern)
This eighth-note triplet ride cymbal groove has been played on thousands of popular recordings Although it is technically simple to play; it is quite difficult to make this groove feel and sound good. You must pay particular attention to make sure that each limb lines up with one another: No "flams" should be present: Please focus on the groups ofthree within the eighth note pulse as well Ifthe time straightens out to groups of 2 or four (eighths or 16ths), you are not playing the correct groove.
Groove 2 The A2 section is played on a tightly closed hi-hat and utilizes a cross-stick articulation on the snare drum, alongside strong standard (either "bury-the-beater" or "off the head") bass drum sound. This section features a two-bar pattern from the bass drum by adding a bar to the pattern from A1. For example: Ex 6.12
