Chapter 3: Control Stroke/Down Stroke A control stroke occurs when you deny the natural rebound of the stick It is very similar to the free stroke, but just after the stick strikes the drum you will use the back two fingers to stop it from rebounding (Ex 1.14). This will place the stick in the resting position of 1-2 inches above the head (Ex: 1.15). Ex 1.14 Ex 1.15
Remember to catch the stick low and after the impact Don't' catch and choke the stick before impact this will not only choke the open sound of the drum but will also transfer all of the energy back into your wrist: This is not ideal if you want to avoid injury:
Also, as general rule, keep your sticks in a down position when youre not using them. When in an up position, the sticks can only go down: But down position allows for more options; and this is generally preferred.
The following exercise will incorporate both free stokes and down strokes. The idea is to apply the down stokes only as a transition from each sequence of isolated free strokes.
