Chapter 2: Drumset Mobility: Paradiddles The paradiddle is one of the most popular rudiments because it is comprised of the two main strokes of drumming: the single stroke and the double stroke. Combining these two strokes offers endless application and functionality in terms of drumset mobility: Also, the paradiddles natural ability to alternate the leading hand will offer you more options for orchestrations and rhythmic composition:
In the following exercises; we will explore how the paradiddle can be applied to the kit by using various voicing options and note values. One of the goals of applying rudiments to the drumset is to no make them sound like rudiments; we want to create music, not perform exercises This can be difficult to achieve every time you apply a rudiment; however; the following exercises are designed to get you thinking outside of the basic approach:
