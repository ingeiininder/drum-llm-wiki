As you begin to play these exercises faster you will notice the motion will start to minimize and involve less shoulder and elbow: Using this technique at a fast tempo will mostly utilize the wrist to produce the whipping motion; this is referred to as "low Moeller"
The low Moeller is accomplished by allowing the wrist to come up while the hand stays down (Ex 3.6). As you keep the hand relaxed and the fulcrum slightly squeezing the stick, snap the wrist down (Ex. 3.7). The tip of the stick will come up as the butt end of the stick goes down: Allow the natural flow of energy to engage the stick and bring the stroke to completion (Ex. 3.8).
