"12/8 Feel" within a 4/4 Time Signature: The definition of a triplet is three notes of the same value occupying the same space as two notes of the same value: Basically, each beat; instead of being divided by twos or fours, is divided by thirds Dividing each beat into three eighth note triplets creates 12 eighth notes within one measure of 4/4 time (3X4-12). Rather than writing four groups of three note sub-phrases within 12/8 time signature (6/8 and 12/8 will be addressed in Reading 2) , many composers prefer to write four groups of triplets within 4/4 time. This is called a"12/8 Feel" Maintaining the time signature of 4/4 allows for the downbeats to remain as 1, 2,3,and 4.This weeks drum chart is written in 4/4,with a tempo of a quarter note equaling 60 beats per minute: For example: Ex 6.3
