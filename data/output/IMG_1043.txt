Chapter 3: 5/8 Grooves and Ensemble Figures Now that you understand how to count and read basic rhythms in 5/8,the next step is to develop the ability to play grooves in 5/8. In addition; incorporating ensemble-figure interpretation amid grooves is essential for any working drummer:
When developing grooves in 5/8,you will need to use the overall rhythm template of the tune or use the bass part as a guide: As mentioned earlier; 5/8 is normally felt as two groups of eighth notes: 1) a group of two and a group of three eighth notes or 2) a group of three and a group of two eighth notes: Play the following basic 5/8 grooves while counting eighth notes out loud: Start at a tempo of eighth notes equal 150 BPM:
