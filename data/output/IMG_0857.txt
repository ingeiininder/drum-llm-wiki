Chapter 2: Linear Hand/Foot Development Having a strong foundation of hand and foot coordination and/or combinations are paramount in developing a complete drumset understanding and vocabulary: Being able to combine various hand/foot combinations are not only essential to fill and solo development; but more importantly they: give each limb an opportunity to feel and understand time in a greater capacity. Since these are linear (i.e: no two or more limbs/voices are playing at the same time) exercises, each limb cannot depend on another limb to "guide"it: Each limb is now solely responsible for subdividing the time evenly; that is what will make all of these hand/foot combinations sound intentional and applicable
Each of the following exercises will be presented as a basic idea, first as eighth notes, then applied to 16th notes The same coordination is required, only the note value has changed. Count each respective note value out loud,
Following the basic idea will be two separate ways to orchestrate the basic idea Once you become comfortable with these orchestrations; explore and find your unique way of applying these concepts to your individual style: Hand/Foot Combinations
