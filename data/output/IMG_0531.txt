Chapter 5: Seat Height Seat height is subject to the length of your legs: To prescribe the same measurement from the floor to the top of the stool for every drummer would be very ineffective. Rather; base the height of your seat to the relationship of your hips to your knees:
Generally, your hips should be slightly higher than your knees (Ex 3.21). If your hips drop below your knees, your center of gravity will shift and apply much unneeded pressure to your lower back and hips (Ex 3.22). If a seat is too high you may run the risk of only being able to use a full-leg bass drum technique; thus leaving the desirable ankle technique aside (Ex. 3.23). Ex. 3.21 Ex 3.22
