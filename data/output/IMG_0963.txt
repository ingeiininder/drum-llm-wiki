The "ah" of a Beat When setting up ensemble figures on the "ah" of a beat; the target beat (or target area, in this case) is essentially the three 16th notes prior to the "ah" of the beat (the downbeat; the "e" and the "&"). Again, a figure on the "ah" of a beat creates a natural tendency for the performer to slightly rush this figure: As mentioned in Unit 7,a figure on the "ah" of the beat is regularly referred to as a "push" due to the fact that this action creates a feeling as if the next downbeat is occurring early:
