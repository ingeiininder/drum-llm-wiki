This means that the song (or chart) is played (and subsequently repeated) in 12-bar increments without a specifically stated ending: Therefore; the 12-bar form (chart) will be performed in 12-bar increments (12-24-36-48-60-72 bars): With this fact in mind; there are two additional terms that modify, add to and accompany this form: They are: (1) the head and (2) a chorus Lets take a look at each in greater detail:
The Head The term 'head" is most often used in jazz or blues-related contexts, and it refers to the thematic melody within a song's form. This melody is usually played at the beginning and at the end of a song's performance: Thus, if a bandleader requests one head (in a 12-bar form), that would mean that the band would play through 12 bars ofthe melody.
