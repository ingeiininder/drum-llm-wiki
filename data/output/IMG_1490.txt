2  MmmPick It Up A Little
This is a nice 12-bar blues at a medium swinging tempo. This is a perfect tempo to practice some of the comp- ing exercises from the book, and also work on feathering the bass drum:
Try playing quarter notes on the ride cymbal with your eyes closed to really feel the pulse with the bass player: Are your quarter notes even, solid, and balanced? Are you comfortable and balanced on the set? Is the bass drum feathered lightly and controlled? Is there good balance between the bass drum, hi-hat, and ride cymbal?
Try to outline the form of the 12-bar blues. Play a turnaround figure to mark the top of each new 12-bar section.
During the bass solo, try playing time on the hi-hat: Realize that you don't want to totally close the cymbals, but leave them slightly open to avoid a "vertical" time feel. The great masters often used the left thumb and first finger to slightly close the cymbals to create more legato feel.
During the four-bar solo trades (trading fours) , understand that the form keeps moving, so that on the first cho- rus Of "4s, you would play the second four bars of the 12-bar tune_ On the second chorus, you will play the first and third sets of four bars.
This tune has a classic ending, so memorize it!
