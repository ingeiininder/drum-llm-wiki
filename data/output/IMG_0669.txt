Unit eight reading | techNIQUe pertOrMaNce Chapter 1: Triplets Mixed with Eighth and 16th Notes In this unit you will read combinations of triplets, eighth notes and 16th notes You must first become comfortable playing them back-to-back before you mix them together Think of eighth notes as groups of twos, triplets as groups of threes and 16th notes as groups of fours. You are now going to shift between twos; threes and fours Practice with a metronome, and you will begin to feel how this shifting works. Think of it as driving stick-shift car and you are shifting from second gear to third gear to fourth gear: Stick these exercises hand-to-hand Begin with your right hand,and no matter what happens rhythmically, continue to alternate R-L-R-L for the entire exercise BPM checklist: 70 80 90 Shifting Between Twos, Threes and Fours Ex 8.1

Once you are comfortable with this, let's shift up then down: So, we will shift from twos to threes to fours, then back down to threes to twos This will begin to give you the feeling that you will have when reading combinations of these rhythms Make sure you are always subdividing, but it is much more difficult to do when the rhythms are changing from beat to beat: You will get very good at dividing time up into various numeric pieces; and at hearing the new subdivision in your head before you play it: BPM checklist: 70 80 90 Up and Down Shifting Ex 8.2
