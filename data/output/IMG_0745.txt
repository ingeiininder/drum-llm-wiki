Motown Berry Gordy founded the Motown record label during 1959 in Detroit; Michigan. Motown was named after Detroit's most successful product: motor cars. (i.e: the moniker Mo-Town/Motor Town). Employing R&B and pop qualities, Motown saw its greatest success from 1959-1972,and they used a small group of studio musicians to back each of their featured artists. This small group of musicians eventually became known as The Funk Brothers and helped define an ageless sound. The drummers of Motown were Benny Benjamin, Richard "Pistol" Allen and Uriel Jones:
Stax Jim Stewart and Estelle Axton founded the Stax record label in 1957. Based in Memphis, Tennessee; Stax was pop-influenced R&B label that also (like Motown) used one group of musicians to back their entire artist roster This backing band was well-known under the name Booker T and the MG's Their drummer was the influential Al Jackson, Jr:
