Drum charts really do run the gamut; and in the end it is usually up to the drummer to decide what needs to be played: Here is a list of some possible types of charts you could be presented with in your career:
Fully notated: Contains detailed drum notation with specific drum fills, orchestration performance directives and dynamics Skeletal: Provides the form and basic groove; possibly with some ensemble figures: Lead sheet: A chart that provides the form, chords, melody and basic feel (example: 3/4 jazz waltz) but no specific drum parts Piano, bass or trumpet chart: Many times the drum chart goes missing, and you'll be handed piano or bass chart (lead trumpet if in a big band situation) that at least provides the form and essential rhythms: Shorthand: Many times you may have to chart tune out yourself: This method has endless possibilities
