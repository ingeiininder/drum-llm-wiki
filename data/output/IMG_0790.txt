Letter A Ex 10.4
Tight Hi-hat
Just as the A section has a particular groove, phrase length, and chord progression, it will also have a particular drum sound. Therefore, this section features a light (yet consistent) two-handed 16th-note hi-hat groove alongside a very staccato-syncopated bass drum and strong snare backbeat Let's take a lookat how you can achieve these articulations:
Bass Drum The bass drum pattern should be played very forcefully, with a staccato articulation and a dynamic of forte (loud) . As mentioned in Performance 1, Unit 1, this sound is accomplished by burying the beater into the bass drum head.
Snare Drum The snare drum should be played very consistently and ata dynamic of forte (loud). Additionally, each snare hit should be a rim shot: As mentioned in Performance 1, Unit 1,this sound is accomplished by striking the snare drum and rim simultaneously with power and conviction:
Hi-Hat This two-handed alternating I6th pattern should be played evenly and softly at a dynamic of piano (soft). In order to achieve this sound (as mentioned in Performance 1, Unit 4), you will use the shoulder of the stick just beneath the tip. Use this part ofthe stick to strike the edge (not the top) ofthe hi-hat cymbals This will give you a very thick, yet dynamically controlled hi-hat tone. In comparison, by bringing the hi-hat volume lower; it also creates a dynamic illusion, making the bass drum and snare drum appear to sound louder within the overall drum mix
