Unit ten reading | techNIque pertOrMaNce Chapter 1: Developing an Ostinato-Based Drum Solo As drummers, we play ostinatos all of the time, primarily with our hi-hat stepping pattern and our ride cymbal pattern. The main reason for playing an ostinato is to establish a constant: something to which the listener can quickly understand This is imperative because once the drummer begins adding variations and improvises over (or along with) the ostinato, the listener will have a"home base" they can relate to. From Max Roach to John Bonham; solo-based ostinatos have been a very effective and exciting way to make a solo have a unique sound and/or characteristic But it wasn't until- Terry ' Bozzio began using this tool as a primary agent to compose highly sophisticated drum solos that this concept reached its full potential. The ostinatos that Bozzio was incorporating were very specialized and specific to his original drum compositions and his large setup. This paved the way to exploring the drum set as a solo instrument like never before and has influenced countless drummers to create without boundaries
Just as you've practiced groove-based ostinatos (.e. ride and hi-hat stepping patterns) we will use the same systematic methodology to develop improvisational freedom with this units solo-based ostinato.
