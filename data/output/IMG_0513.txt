Once this feels somewhat comfortable; begin to move your wrist with the natural motion of the stick After you throw the stick, bend the wrist back and follow the stick as it returns into your hand (Ex: 1.9). Don't lift the stick with your hand; allow the stick to naturally bounce back into your hand. Catch the stick with your hand, not your fingers. Ex, 1.9
Now that weve established the relationship between our wrist and natural motion of the stick, we are going to play continuous strokes with each hand individually Throughout the exercise the stick will not stop; it will stay in constant motion. The lateral movement of the stick should be very even and consistent There should be no accents. Allow your wrist to move in sync with the rebounding stick; but make sure it does not become the primary force that is enabling the continuously rebounding strokes: Ex 1.10
The following exercise will reveal how the height of the rebound will be determined by how fast the stroke is. To display this, we will play one bar of eighth notes, followed by one bar of sixteenth notes. For the eighth notes the stick height should be bouncing back to a full stroke position (Ex: 1.12), while the sixteenth notes should be at a half stroke position (Ex. 1.13).
