Groove 3 The A3 section is played on a thicker (less pressure on the pedal) hi-hat and utilizes a rim shot articulation on the snare drum alongside a strong standard (either"bury-the-beater" or"off the head") bass drum sound: This section features a four-bar pattern from the bass drum that varies in every bar of the four-bar phrase For example: Ex 6.15
A3 Thick Hi-Hat
Groove 4 The groove for the final section, 44, features a two-bar pattern; the first bar of the phrase employs a very syncopated bass drum strike on the third partial of the triplet on beat 2. Special attention must be paid to this particular hit since is does not "land"on a downbeat Make sure to "feel" (on the ride cymbal or perhaps with your hi-hat foot) the downbeat of beat 3 immediately following this bass drum hit The second bar of this two-bar pattern is identical to the second bar of the two-bar pattern in A2. The groove also gets "lifted" by moving from the hi-hat back to the ride as previously played during A1. For example: Ex. 6.16
