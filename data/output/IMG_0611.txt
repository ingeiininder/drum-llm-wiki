The Difference Between 6/8 and 3/4 The musical genre and harmonic movement (chord changes) will help determine if a chart should be written in 6/8 or 3/4. For example; most country waltzes are written in 3/4 whereas a Celtic folk tune will be written in 6/8. Both the 6/8and 3/4 time signatures have six eighth notes within one measure However; 3/4 divides the six eighth notes into three groups of two while 6/8 divides the six eighth notes into two groups ofthree: Also, in 6/8 you count every eighth note as a downbeat (1, 2,3,4,5,6) with the strong beats being on beats 1 and 4,In 3/4 the eighth notes are counted as downbeats and upbeats (1, &,.2,& 3,&) with the strong beats being on beats 1, 2 and 3.
