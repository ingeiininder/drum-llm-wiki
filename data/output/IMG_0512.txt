Chapter 2: Gladstone Technique/Free Stroke Technique is defined as: a The way in which the fundamentals, as of an artistic work, are handled: b Skill or command in handling such fundamentals
There is no one "right" technique in playing the drums. Technique serves two purposes: one; to allow you to freely express yourself without encumbrance, and two, avoiding unnecessary injury: However; the musicians and athletes that seem to display super-human physical ability have been able to tap into the natural physical properties that enable them to display such virtuosity; this is technique worth exploring:
The first technique we will be focusing on is the Gladstone technique, also known as the free stroke. This technique is vitally important,as it will serve as the foundation for most of the techniques you will use:
The main objective of this technique is to allow the stick to naturally bounce back to where the stroke began: Just as you would bounce a ball, you allow the sticks to bounce back into your hands, free of any restriction:
To become familiar with this concept; we are going to start from an up position (Ex: 1.7), and simply throw the stick down. Allow it to bounce back in your hand while leaving your fingers extended downward (Ex 1.8). This is an isolation exercise:
