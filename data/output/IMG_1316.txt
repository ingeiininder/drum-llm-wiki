Chapter 3: Single-Stroke Rolls Performing a smooth single-stroke roll of any length and at any tempo is a most important and challenging drumming skill: In the following exercises each roll stroke begins and ends with the metronome as noted. You are adding one note to each successive roll stroke:
Maintaining equal pressure throughout all motions and strokes is ofthe utmost importance: Tension should be kept to a minimum as you (a) increase the number of notes per beat and (b) increase tempo: If you feel tension in your hands, do not raise the tempo of the exercise: Conversely, lower the tempo in order to feel the relaxation in your hands; wrists and forearms:
Metronome marking for these exercises: BPM = 40...48
