Cross-Stick In order to get a strong; full and warm cross stick sound, turn the stick around and use the butt end. The bead (tip) ofyour stick should be placed just "off-center;" between the eight and nine oclock position. The butt end will be off the drum between the three and four Oclock position as follows: Ex. 6.13 (right):
In order to make the stroke, grip the stick in your normal fashion. Then "throw"it into the drum and take your four fingers off the stick At this point your fingers should flatten and you will apply pressure to keep the stick on the head (and rim). To make the next cross-stick stroke, grab the stick with your fingers again and repeat: For example: Ex 6.14
Although this will get you in the general area and enable you to get a solid cross stick sound, each snare drum is quite unique and has its own "sweet spot" Therefore; it is extremely important to experiment and find the stick position that sounds best
