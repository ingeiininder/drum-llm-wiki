Chapter 2: Performance Notes and Sound We will break down each section and describe the appropriate hi-hat, snare and bass drum articulations: Letter A Ex 2.5
Medium Hi-hat
Just as the A section has a particular groove, phrase length; and chord progression; it will also have a particular drum sound. This section features a medium-heavy (and consistent) eighth-note hi-hat groove (with open accents on the"&"s of each beat) alongside a very staccato four-on-the-floor bass drum pattern and strong snare backbeat: Lets take a look at howyou can achieve these articulations:
Bass Drum The bass drum pattern should be played very forcefully; with a staccato articulation and a dynamic of forte (loud) This will be accomplished by utilizing three important strategies:
1 _ Using the plastic side of (or a hard-surfaced) bass drum beater: 2 Burying the bass drum beater into the bass drum head. 3. Using your entire leg (and foot) to make the stroke
Snare Drum The snare drum should be played very consistently at a dynamic of forte (loud). Each snare hit should be a rim shot: This stroke is executed by hitting the center of the drumhead and rim at the same exact time:
Hi-Hat This medium-heavy hi-hat pattern should be played evenly and forcefully at a dynamic of mezzo-forte (medium loud) In order to achieve this sound, you will use the upper shoulder of the stick (just below the tip) for the unaccented notes and the shoulder of the stick for each "&"accented hi-hat opening: Ex 2.6
