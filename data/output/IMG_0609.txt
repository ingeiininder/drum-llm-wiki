Now that you understand the primary aspects of notation (note values, rest values; flags, beams, ties and dots), we can apply these to different time signatures
Another very common time signature is 6/8. In 6/8 time there are six beats to every measure and the eighth note gets the beat as follows: Ex 3.1 Six beats in every measure
Eighth note is worth one beat
The six eighth notes within a measure of 6/8 are usually grouped into two groups ofthree eighth notes The following is a list of how notes within a measure of 6/8 are divided. Ex 3.2
Half note six beats/one per measure
Dotted quarter note three beats/two per measure
Quarter note two beats/three per measure
