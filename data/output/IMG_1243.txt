Letter B: Ensemble Figures The last three bars of letter B feature a series of ensemble figures. Although the figures within the first two bars of this sequence start on the same beats, the note lengths are very different; in the first measure the figures are short in length and in the second measure the notes are long: For example: Ex 5.15

You will also notice some new articulations above these figures. First; we have dots placed on top of the figures in the first measure of this sequence. These dots mark these rhythms as staccato, which means you should play these notes as short as possible. The other figures in bar 2 of this sequence have lines over them marking them as tenuto, meaning to play these rhythms as long as possible: These markings are very common, and becoming familiar with them will help you to interpret ensemble figures properly (and musically).
