Mixing 16th Notes and 16th Rests By mixing both 16th notes and 16th rests, we can create endless rhythmic possibilities and permutations Thus (and to keep the notation clear and concise), a double beam is used to maintain a grouping of four 16ths (notes; rests or a combination thereof) within every beat: In many cases, these beams extend over rests; and in others they do not.
