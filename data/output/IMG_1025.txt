Chapter 3: Double Dotted Notes To continue our study of advanced rhythmic notation, we will now take a look at notes that are double dotted: Although double dotted notes are not very common, it is important that you are familiar with this concept A second dot adds half of the value of the first dot to the note: For example; in the example below (Ex 3.11),a half note is worth two beats Adding a single dot will add half of the value of the half note (one beat: half of two is one) making a dotted half note worth three beats.
