Chapter 2: Accents, Taps and Up Strokes One of the greatest advantages of learning the Moeller technique is its ability to offer the drummer an effective way to play accents within a series of notes Accents and taps are crucial to a drummer's vocabulary, as they are one of the few ways we can allow expression and color to contribute within our performance
An accented note is played louder than the other notes; this will make it stand apart from the rest: For the sake of this study, taps will be unaccented notes, generally played one to two inches above the drumhead (Ex. 3.8).

To effectively utilize this technique, start with the stick in a tap position and with a loose grip (Ex. 3.9).
