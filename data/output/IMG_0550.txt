Unit SeveN reading | techNIque perfOrmance Chapter 1: Double Stroke Technique The ability to play clean and even double-stroke rolls is essential for all drummers, although initially; it can be deceptively difficult: By slowing down the required motion and focusing on the basic mechanics, we will make the development of this technique much easier: Ex 7.1
To begin, play one free stroke; remember to allow the stick to bounce back naturally (Ex 7.1). Next; play a down stroke; using your back fingers to stop the rebound (Ex 7.2). Both strokes should have the same dynamic integrity:
Typically, drummers will accent the first note of the double; which is not desirable: The goal in producing an open, clean and evenly subdivided roll is focusing on both notes sounding equal in tone and dynamic This is accomplished by even stick heights and a relaxed grip for both strokes. Allow the sticks to breathe within the hand, applying just enough pressure in the fulcrum to control the natural motion of the stick while not micro-managing it: Ex 7.2
As you begin to increase the speed, you will notice the free stroke will begin to get smaller; and the natural rebound of the stick will be controlled primarily with the fingers The fingers will be "catching and releasing" the second stroke, but don't focus too much on this It will eventually be a natural reaction:
