Jazz Terminology Review As mentioned in Performance 1,_ Unit 7,there are a few key - terms and concepts related to jazz and blues music that are essential to understand.
Open Form The term open form means that the song is played (and repeated) in 12-bar increments, without a specifically stated ending:
Chorus Unlike a pop music chorus, which refers to "the hook" of a song,a jazz or blues chorus is a term that is slang for "one-time through the form:" If a bandleader says they want to play one chorus of guitar solo (in a 12- bar blues form) , that would be mean one pass through the 12-bar blues form; but with a guitar solo (in place of the melody).
Cue Jazz and blues musicians often give each other the "head" or"top" cue by patting their hand on top of their head,which is usually meant to make sure everybody goes back to the melody the next time the top e ofthe form comes around;
