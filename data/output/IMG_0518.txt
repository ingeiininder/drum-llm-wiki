Chapter 3: Traditional Grip
All of the grips we've discussed so far are considered matched grip, where both hands are identical. Traditional grip breaks this model by using two separate grips For a right-handed drummer; the right hand will incorporate an overhanded, matched grip technique; generally American or German grip. However; the left hand will incorporate an underhanded grip.
This technique descends from military marching drummers who played drum slung from their shoulder At that time it was difficult to march with a flat drum so they positioned the drum to ride closer to one hip and at an angle (Ex 2.8), which forced them to alter their grip.
