Cue: As stated within the open form terminology, there is no specific ending within this form. Thus, a cue is employed. A cue is a notice made from one band member to the rest of the band in order to signal a break; rest or ending: Thus, in the chart you will see the text "on cue" This will tell you to get the band's attention by playing a short fill or counting them into the coda ending:

Shuffle Feel: A shuffle feel is a triplet-based groove written in 4/4 time It is very common in blues songs, medium tempo jazz pieces and in rock music The shuffle usually refers to a "broken triplet feel;" where there is no middle triplet present: For example: Ex 7.5 3
It is also usually written (in chart form) as eighth notes, although triplets are implied This is recognized at the beginning of the chart with the following symbol: Ex 7.6 3
Played as eighth note triplets
This lets the reader of the chart know that every time they read an eighth note, they are to perform it as a "broken" eighth-note triplet:
