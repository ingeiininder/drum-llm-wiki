Tuplets and False Notation False notation refers to the small number written above or below a beamed group of notes This number is a note to the performer that this particular group of notes (commonly triplet-based notes) involves an irregular division of a beat; which is normally divided in half or in fourths. These notes are also referred to as tuplets Tuplets are used to write rhythms beyond the beat divisions usually permitted by the time signature: Ex 2.16
