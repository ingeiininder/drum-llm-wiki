"The B Groove" In order to properly mark the song's form (and draw attention to a new section), this eighth-note rock groove (within letter B) now includes a different bass drum pattern, and also utilizes the ride cymbal (rather than the hi-hat) Just as in the letter A hi-hat pattern, this cymbal pattern now also employs an accent on every quarter note (beats 1,2,3 and 4). This accent is played on the bell of the cymbal Therefore; the accents are on the bell and the remaining notes are on the body of the cymbal. Furthermore (and because the ride is marking the pulse), the hi-hat foot is closing on all four-quarter note pulses (i.e: beats 1, 2, 3 and 4).
