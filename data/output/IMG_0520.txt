Chapter 4: Second-Finger Fulcrum In Unit we developed a fulcrum using our thumb and forefinger: However; another popular position for the fulcrum is substituting the second finger for the forefinger: To establish this, we are going to drop the forefinger slightly down and allow the stick to rest in between the first and second crease of the second finger (Ex. 2.12).The thumb will remain on the stick; positioned slightlyin between the forefinger and second finger (Ex 2.13).

A second-finger fulcrum will coincide more with a wrist technique and allow power to be more accessible This fulcrum is also popular with heavier back-beat drumming; as the stick will naturally have a more open feel, and the impact of each stoke will be felt considerably less
Because both fulcrums have their advantages and disadvantages, a proper understanding of both is essential for a professional drummer: Depending on the dynamic; intensity and/or speed of the music, one of these fulcrums will allow you to express yourself more fully and be free of injury:
