Chapter 2: Performance Notes and Sound Intro Vamp (and Letter A) Groove Interpretation: Closed Roll or Five-Stroke Roll The groove for the vamp and letter A has a swung eighth-note feel, and there is a five-stroke roll on beat 1. With this in mind, you can phrase this first beat as a closed roll or an open five-stroke rudimental roll as follows: Ex 2.11 Open Roll JRX R   R L L  R R L   R   LL   R L   R  L RIL
Closed Roll
3= R L R R LL   R L  R L RIL
Flam-Buzz on Beat 7 In addition; beat 7 features a loose "flam-buzz" This is a staple of New Orleans-style drumming technique; where both hands flam and then the sticks flutter and bounce/buzz for the remainder of the beat. It is not measured roll but rather a rolled "feel" that is used in this style: In this case the flam-buzz will occur on beat 7 and"bounce" into the five-stroke roll on beat 1 of the next measure: Ex 2.12
7 R R L L R L R L   R   LL   R R
RIL
In order to execute this flam-buzz, play a normal flam (without a rim shot) just above the center of the snare head: Immediately afterward; let the stick tips bounce and flutter haphazardly into the next beat Don't worry, this will not sound sloppy at 173 BPM! Ex 2.13a Ex. 2.13b
