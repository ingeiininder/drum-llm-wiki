Unit Six reading | techNIque pertOrMaNce Chapter 1: Slow Swing Grooves and Subdividing Swing blues is a jazz-tinged style of blues that came to prominence in the mid-to-late 1940s. Usually featuring a vocalist in front of a large; horn-driven orchestra, the style is earmarked by intensely shouted vocals,honking tenor saxophone solos and a driving rhythm section. The lyrics are celebratory in nature,and the music was the bridge between the older styles of small band blues and the big band jazz sound of the 1940s.
