Sound Review 2: Snare Drum: Center of the Head (No Rim Shot) The snare drum should be played very consistently,in the center of the drumhead. Additionally, each snare hit should be played without a rim shot; which will make the drum sound deep and open in tonality. This stroke is executed by hitting the center of the drumhead with the butt end of the stick
Most drummers think that this is an easier stroke to execute than a rim shot: However; this could not be farther from the truth: In actuality, this motion (and sound) takes a great deal of accuracy and concentration: Let's take a look both steps:

Bass Drum:"Off the Head" The bass drum pattern should be played very forcefully,with an open articulation This will be accomplished by utilizing two important strategies They are:
1 Using the felt side of (or a soft-surfaced) bass drum beater: 2. Playing the bass drum with an open "off-the-head" (non-buried) stroke: After each stroke it is also very important to keep the beater away from (not buried into) the drumhead: Not only does this allow the drum to resonate more; but it also allows the pitch of the drum to remain lower as well (as it does not get raised by the beater getting buried into the head)
Important:you must make sure to control the bass drum beater and footboard and avoid any unwanted "felt-flutter" between the beater and drum head: In order to play this stroke properly, you must "rest and release" both your foot and the beater between strokes.
