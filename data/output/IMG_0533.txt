Unit fOUr reading | techNIque perfOrmance Chapter 1: Rudimental Drumming A rudiment is defined as: fundamental element; principle; or skill, as of a field of learning: To create and/or perform anything of significant value requires a certain amount of technique: In playing drums, studying the rudiments is a very effective way to develop technique. Just as a pianist or guitarist will diligently practice scales to gain the freedom to which they can effortlessly express themselves; the practicing of rudiments to the drummer will offer the same results
Every time you pickup a pair of sticks to play any percussion instrument; you will inevitably be playing some kind of established rudiment Rudiments organize; in the most basic forms, the various stickings and rhythms we use to percussively express ourselves. Therefore, any serious student who desires to play to the best of their ability cannot overlook the importance of studying the rudiments:
All of the drum rudiments fall within one of the five rudimental "families" or groups They are:

Ruffs (3-Stroke, 4-Stroke; etc) 2 Rolls (Long; 5,6,7, etc) 3. Diddles (Paraddidle; Double Paradiddle; etc) 4. Flams (Flam Accent; Flam Tap; = Swiss Triplet; etc ) 5_ Drags (Single; Double; Ratamacue; etc)
All of the rudimental families will be covered in the following units In this unit; the ruff family will be introduced and developed: A ruff is a short single-stroke roll that is generally played either before or after an accent The 3-,4-, 5-,and 7-stroke ruff will be the rudiments focused on for this unit.
One ofthe main"understood" concepts while analytically studying any technique is to focus while you practice but forgetitwhen you perform. The performance should be where all ofthe focused practice naturally surfaces without you thinking about it: Drum rudiments are just that; they require focused, diligent study and, when developed, offer an endless amount of possibilities to your rhythmic vocabulary:
