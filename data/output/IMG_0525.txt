Unit three reading | techNIque perfOrmance Chapter 1: Moeller Technique The Moeller technique was formally introduced by Sanford A "Gus" Moeller; an influential drum teacher in the early 1900s. Since then; drum icons Jim Chapin and Joe Morello have championed and taught this technique to scores of today's top drummers. The main purpose of this technique is to provide speed and power with less energy and tension, thus allowing the ease of playing accented patterns free of injury:
Ex 3.1
The Moeller technique is based on wind-up movement from the arm followed by a whipping motion generated from the elbow: To get started, use American or German grip and focus more on a second finger fulcrum with a minimum amount of tension; this will allow the energy to flow through the stick easier Move the stick from the 4-oclock position to the 3-oclock position (Ex. 3.1). Now, lift your wrist up, with a loose grip, so the stick is pointed down directly to the center of the drum but not touching (Ex. 3.2). Make sure to keep your palm facing- towards yourself; don't turn it out and away: Finally; while keeping your wrist completely relaxed, quickly drop the elbow as you bring the stick back to the original 4-oclock position (Ex. 3.3).

One ofthe most challenging aspects in executing this technique properly is not allowing the wrist to tense up. As drummers, we are conditioned to use our wrist for most of the strokes we play. However; any amount of tension from the wrist or forearm will reduce the power and speed this motion naturally generates.
