Unit five reading | techNIQUe pertOrMaNce Chapter 1: Hand-to-Hand 16th Figures As you have learned in Drum Performance 1, there are many spots within a drum chart that allow for interpretation and creative development: Drummers must develop the ability to read a stagnant ensemble figure and add musicality to a particular phrase In Reading you learned that using certain sticking patterns help to solidify your ability to read recurring rhythms This same technique can be applied to ensemble figure reading; interpretation and orchestration:
Two common methods used to help develop interpretation and orchestration skills are 1) Hand-to-hand and 2) Right-hand lead. Both techniques will be explained in this unit using I6th notes as a template:
Hand-to-Hand This technique is based on the"assigned sticking" method from Drum Reading 1, where all downbeats and upbeats are played with the right hand and all"es" and"ahs" are played with the left hand Taking a series of 16th note-based ensemble figures, play continuous hand-to-hand 16th notes while accenting the written rhythms For example; in the example below (Ex. 5.1), the top line shows the ensemble figure while the bottom staff shows how to play this figure with accented hand-to-hand I6th notes Ex 5.1 Ensemble figure
