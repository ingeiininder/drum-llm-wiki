5. Cmon Now
To match the style and tempo of this song, lighten up the feel and keep the time "floating' over the top of the tune. Comp less and let the time breathe more_ Listen to Tony Williams on some of the classic uptempo Miles Davis tracks, and notice how his time breathes and how he lets his ride cymbal carry the time_ Motion is a key ingredient to helping the feel_ If you consciously lighten up your cymbal motion and think of a legato flowing stroke when playing figures (i.e: the intro), it will reflect in your time. Also, try to be aware of letting the stick do more and more of the work when playing faster tempos.
Practice the introduction figure over and over until it is very comfortable Use variety of fills to work in and out of the rhythms. Play this song in very relaxed and comfortable manner and if you have to think about playing the song's figures, they will sound stiff .
During the intro, all of the figures are long and legato, so they should be phrased long with "shouldered" cym- bal, rather than short on a snare drum or bass drum only: When you crash your ride cymbal, try to drop the shoul- der of the stick against the bOw of the cymbal; don't crash it on the edge like a normal crash:
Listen to the soloists during this tune and notice how their lines ascend and descend: Usually, when ascends, you should increase in volume, and when it descends, you should decrease in volume_
