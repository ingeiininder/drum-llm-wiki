Chapter 2: Linear 16th Note Concept Now that you have a basic understanding of what linear means, the next step is to apply these concepts to 16th-note groove ideas  Most linear grooves are fairly complex with heavy syncopated elements which, again, will require a strong ability to mentally subdivide:
Basic Linear Trainer System The following exercises are designed to help you feel a repeated linear pattern of three I6th notes that travel over the bar: By doing this exercise, you will be able to feel syncopated linear patterns in all possible 16th note subdivisions
Start by playing the following four-bar pattern between the hi-hat, snare drum and bass drum: Play = allnotes at a mezzo-forte dynamic; avoiding accents any notes Take notice of the "turnaround" bass drum double between the last bar of the pattern (the "ah" of beat 4) and the downbeat of bar 1. You must subdivide 16th notes verbally (out loud) while playing this pattern. Start at 60 BPM: Ex 7.4
Next, take notice of what voice (hi-hat; snare drum or bass drum) lands on each downbeat: Switch your counting from I6th-note subdivision to quarter-note subdivision (count out loud on every downbeat: "1-2-3-4". Ex 7.5
