To bury or not to bury . that is the question. Another; and often highly debated, aspect of bass drum technique is whether or not to bury the beater. Burying the beater is leaving the beater pressed against the bass drum head after it has been played. Not burying the beater is allowing the beater to naturally rebound off the head after it has been played:
This is primarily based on personal sonic preference. Just like any drum, pressing something against the head after it has been struck will quickly stop the natural resonance: If you want that quick and/or muted sound, then bury the beater; if you want the drum to resonate; don't bury the beater: However; if you do decide to bury the beater; pay attention to any residual "dribble"that the beater may softly play against the head if there is not enough pressure holding it to the surface: This is rarely a desirable sound, and one you should be able to control:
Dynamic Bass Drum Exercises The following exercises are designed to help you explore the heel-down and heel-up positions. One ofthe most effective methods to develop complete control is through dynamic exercises; dynamic meaning being able to play at various levels of soft and loud.
