Unit eight reading | techNIque pertOrMaNce Chapter 1: Reggae Reggae is music genre developed in Jamaica during the late 1960s and popularized internationally by Bob Marley and the Wailers Within this style;, Carlton Barrett (drummer for the Wailers) created the one-drop and four-on-the-floor rhythms, which he used alongside very syncopated cross-stick and hi-hat groove embellishments Both grooves can be performed with a straight or shuffle feel: Later; in the 1970s and early 1980s, DJs in the UK began to play reggae songs during their sets. Soon thereafter; The Clash; Fishbone; UB4O and The Police began to incorporate reggaes one-drop and four-on- the-floor rhythms into their music: In recent years, reggae has been revitalized by many mainstream acts; including Sublime; No Doubt; Audiodub and the B-Foundation With this in mind, this week's chart is in the modern tradition of Sublime: It features both the one-drop and four-on-the-floor grooves (and their embellishments) within a shuffle feel,
One-Drop The one-drop is named after the Wailers' song ofthe same name; and this groove shifts the snare cross-stick backbeat and bass drum to the third beat of the bar: Beat is left empty, which allows for large open rhythmic and melodic improvisations Ex 8.1
