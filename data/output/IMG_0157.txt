Snare Drum: Center of the head (non-rimshot) stroke The snare drum should be played very consistently,in the center of the drumhead Each hit should be played without a rimshot; which will make the drum sound deep and open: This stroke is executed by hitting the center of the drumhead with the butt end of the stick
Most drummers think that this is an easier stroke to execute (than a rimshot) In actuality, this motion (and sound) takes a great deal of accuracy and concentration Lets take a look at the steps: 1 _ Locate the center of the head and turn the stick so that the butt end is facing outward. 2 Next, while aiming for this spot on the snare head, start the stroke with your wrist: 3_ Now, just before hitting the lower half of the center dot; grab the stick with your back three fingers This is lends a nice crisp attack to an open snare tone:

Hi-Hat: Chunky; "shouldered" 16ths You should use the shoulder of the stick (mid-way down from the tip). In addition; the hi-hat should be closed with your foot, but not too tightlyl Rather than an articulation of"chick-chick-chick-chick"think ofyour hi-hat saying; "chunk-chunk-chunk-chunk" Ex 3.18 (left)
Next; use this part ofthe stick to strike the edge (not the top) of thehi-hat cymbals. This will give you the very thick and chunky hi-hat tone: These performance methods create a rhythmically thick (and tempo-based) illusion, which will make the groove feel fat; with more forward motion and syncopation.
