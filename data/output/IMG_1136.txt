Hand Position Many drummers play the same hi-hat and snare drum hand position for every groove. However; this cannot possibly work for every rhythmic pattern or sonic articulation. In order to effectively play a strong backbeat within this week's jazz ride hi-hat pattern, you may need to alter your normal "grooving" hand position:
Rather than crossing your hands over each other in the normal position (Ex 4.13, Fig.3), move your hi-hat hand slightly off- center while keeping your elbows in. Next, strike the hi-hatat a slight angle (Fig.4). This will allow you to stay relaxed and still allow for your left hand to lift up and play a strong backbeat (Fig.5).
