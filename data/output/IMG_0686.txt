Chapter 2: Note Length: Short/Long Up to this point the lessons in this book have focused on proper note placement of rhythms within a beat or measure: In this chapter we will take an introductory look at note length and how this affects ensemble figure interpretation and drum set orchestration The first step in honing your interpretive skills is to assign various note lengths to specific drum set voices:
Short/Long In these first exercises you will play continual eighth notes on the ride cymbal and quarter notes on the hi- hat (with foot) while reading eighth note-based rhythms The following orchestration rules will apply: Play all notes longer than an eighth note (quarter note; dotted quarter; half note; dotted half note; whole note and tied rhythms equaling the equivalent of these values) on the bass drum: Play all notes equal to an eighth note (or shorter) on the snare drum: Orchestration example: In this example the upper staff provides the rhythmic ensemble figures.
