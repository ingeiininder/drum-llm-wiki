Unit SevEN reading | techNIque pertOrMaNce Chapter 1: Bossa Nova Bossa nova is a style of Brazilian music popularized by Antonio Carlos Jobim, Vinicius de Moraes and Joao Gilberto. The bossa nova movement (which is Portuguese for "new trend") lasted six years (1958-63),and it contributed a number of songs to the standard jazz repertoire Today's chart will focus on a classic bossa nova drumming method, which is characterized by continuous eighths on the hi-hat (or ride cymbal), a typical two-bar cross-stick pattern and the bass drum playing eighth notes on "1-83-&1" Ex 7.1
Bossa Nova
Notice that this is a two-bar cross-stick phrase The first bar has the pattern being played on beat 1, the"&" of 2 and beat 4.The second bar has the cross-stick pattern on beats 2 and the"&"of 3.It is also important to keep the bass drum pattern consistent; without any rhythmic variations
AAB Open Form The form of this chart is AAB with each section being eight bars in length: This form is open (similar to the open 12-bar blues form), which means the AAB (24-bar) form circulates after the head (melody over AAB the first time) for solo choruses. With these facts in mind; the normal usages of the head and chorus (from Unit 6) still apply: Head = melody Chorus one time through the form

The phrase "vamp until cue"is used to instruct you to continue to play the four-bar intro until you receive a visual cue (usually a confirming head nod). Since the guitar plays the melody at A,the guitar player will be in charge of giving the cue You must make sure you are keeping an eye on the guitar player during the intro.
