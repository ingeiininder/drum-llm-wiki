Chapter 6: Hi-Hat Technique There are two popular approaches in hi-hat stepping technique: The heel/toe method and the heel-up method. Both serve valuable purposes and are worthy of investigation.
The heel/toe method is primarily used for stepping the hi-hat on beats 2 and 4. The natural motion of this method allows this rhythm to be played without having to focus on it. To utilize this technique begin with your foot resting flat on the pedal (Ex. 4.16), this is beat 1. Next; roll the weight ofyour foot up to the ball/toe area of your foot (Ex 4.17); this is beat 2. As the weight shifts up to the toes; the pressure on the pedal will pull the top hi-hat cymbal down to the bottom cymbal creating a "chick" sound. Aim for a tight; articulate "chick"; weak or "fluffy" sounds are not the goal at this point: Roll back to your heel for beat 3, then repeat for beat 4.
