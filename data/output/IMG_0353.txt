Muscle Memory Muscle memory is not a memory stored in your muscles; but rather memories stored in your brain that are similar to a hard drive of frequently-enacted tasks Through immense repetition, it's a form of procedural memory that can help you become very good at a given task For instance; baseball players develop their batting and pitching skills through hours of detailed practice and game time performance As a result; their muscles grow accustomed to certain types of movement; which that ultimately become an unconscious process and fluid routine This procedure can also be applied to musicians and their repetitive movements as well:
As a performer visualizes musical notation; the brain files the various patterns in its storage banks for future reference Thereafter (and by applying a physical motion to these common musical patterns), muscle memories are formed. With these facts in mind, let's take a look at various sticking patterns (i.e. physical manifestations of our notation), which you can use to help develop your reading skills (and muscle memory) more quickly:
