Sound Addition: Stick Shot In bars 5-8 and bar 12 ofthis solo, a stick shot is used. A stick shot is usually performed by placing the left stickata 45-degree angle (to the drumhead) Thereafter; you must immediately press the bead (of the stick) into the drumhead: You should apply enough pressure to avoid a buzz sound and slightly mute the head as well The right stick then strikes the left stick and a wood-tone accent is heard This articulation is very common in jazz solo phrases and transition fills (Both traditional and matched grip can be used for this technique) Ex 6.15
