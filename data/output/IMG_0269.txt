Sixteenth notes are equal to one 16th of a measure in 4/4.By dividing a quarter note (equal to one beat) into four equal parts, the byproduct is now four I6th notes There are four quarter notes in one measure and therefore sixteen I6th notes in one measure (4x4=16). You can also think of 16th notes as an eighth note divided into two equal parts Thus, one-quarter note (1/4) is equal to two eighth notes (2/8), which are also equal to four I6th notes (4/16) all of which are equal to one beat in 4/4 time (i.e. 1/4-2/8-4/16).
