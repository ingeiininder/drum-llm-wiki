Tenuto (Long or Legato) Tenuto means to hold in Italian. In musical terms,a tenuto marking instructs the reader to play a note as long as possible This articulation is also often referred to as legato, although legato usually affects an entire phrase. Placing a line above the note head or stem marks a tenuto articulation For example: Ex 3.3
Sometimes these articulations are redundant to the rhythmic notation and other times these articulations may be difficult to actually play: In the example above (Ex. 3.3) you may play the phrase the same way _ i.e. regardless of the articulation; but having the articulation present reinforces that each note must be played to its fullest duration (in this case one beat).
Martellato (Roof Top Accent) Martellato means hammered in Italian: In musical terms, a Martellato marking instructs the reader to accent a note in a wider fashion (i.e: than a traditional accent) This articulation is also often referred to as "roof top" accent: If a regularly accented note sounds like "dat"then a rooftop accented note sounds like "daht" Ex 3.4
