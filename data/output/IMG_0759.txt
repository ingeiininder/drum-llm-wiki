Jazz Drumming: Marking a Song's Form A drummer's most important job is to play a great groove with steady time However; these attributes are not enough to make music. We have another very important duty to perform: marking a songs form. Marking form is outlining (and drawing attention to) each different section within a song: Here; these sections are the head and chorus ofthe 12-bar blues This is accomplished by employing sonic changes to the drumset by changing ride cymbals for each new section of the song: (In jazz, all cymbals are considered rides ) The head should have a particular cymbal/texture and the choruses of solo should have different cymbal/texture:
