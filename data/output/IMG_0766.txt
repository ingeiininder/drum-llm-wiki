The Hi-Hat: The Bossa Backbeat Just as in straight-ahead jazz drumming; the bossa nova hi-hat is also played on counts 2 and 4 of the bar: This is very similar to pop music's backbeat function. There are at least three techniques that may be employed: 1) heel up 2) heel down 3) bouncing the whole leg: Please use whichever is most comfortable for you: Ex 7.7
Important: You will play on the ride cymbal and close the hi-hat foot on beats 2 and 4 during the choruses of guitar solo
Bass Drum: Proper Bossa Sound The bossa nova bass drum sound features the dotted-quarter/eighth rhythm at an extremely quiet dynamic (see example 7.4). This articulation should fall beneath all of the other drumset voices Please be careful to make both notes even in volume: Many drummers mistakenly accent the second note of each grouping; which is not authentic or appropriate.
