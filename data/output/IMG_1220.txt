Drumset Technique: Splashing the Hi-Hat to Mimic a Dedicated Cymbal Player The hi-hat should be played very consistently, with an open and splashing sound This mimics the hand-held crash cymbals that are played by a dedicated cymbal player in a New Orleans street-beat marching/funeral procession: Additionally, each hi-hat note should remain open and ring into the next half note: This technique is executed by hitting the heel plate and foot pedal of the hi-hat stand in unison:
How to Splash the Hi-Hat Cymbals: As presented in Drum Performance 3, Unit 6, to perform the splash; simply push down quickly on the footplate with the ball of your foot, and as soon as the cymbals make contact; remove your foot from the footplate so the cymbals touch gently: Because we want the cymbals to ring; you must be sure that they don't remain in contact with each other for more than a fraction of a second. It is also acceptable to have the cymbals "sizzle" or "rattle" together at times: Ex 3.5
