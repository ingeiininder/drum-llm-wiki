All Together The end result will be a strong; punchy and very authoritative bass drum sound alongside a karate-chop- like rim shot and thick (yet dynamically controlled) hi-hat: This approach perfectly compliments the arpeggiated guitar patterns and rhythmically-driven synth textures within letter A
Letter B Ex 10.5
BJLoose Hi-hat
In addition to letter A's bass drum and snare drum sounds, this section features a wide-open sloshy eighth- note hi-hat groove. Let's take a look at how you can add this new hi-hat articulation to your drumming arsenal:
Hi-Hat This one-handed wide-open sloshy eighth-note hi-hat pattern should be played evenly and forcefully,at a dynamic of fortissimo (very loudly). As stated in Performance 1, Unit 4,in order to achieve this sound, you will use the middle-shoulder of the stick: This sound (and technique) gives the illusion that the B section speeds up slightly and therefore it generates a large amount of musical excitement:
Important: Do not use the shoulder/tip motion on the hi-hat because it will only accent the downbeats! This will sound very old-fashioned, and it will not sound exciting!
